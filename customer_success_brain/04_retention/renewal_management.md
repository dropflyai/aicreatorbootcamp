# Renewal Management — Renewal Timeline, Auto-Renewal, Pricing Uplift, Multi-Year, Forecasting

## Overview

Renewal management is the systematic process of ensuring that customers continue their subscriptions at or above their current revenue level. For subscription businesses, renewals represent 70-90% of annual revenue. A 1% improvement in renewal rate can have a larger revenue impact than a 10% increase in new customer acquisition. This module covers the complete renewal management discipline: timeline-based renewal execution, auto-renewal strategy, pricing uplift methodology, multi-year contract strategy, and renewal forecasting.

---

## Section 1: The Renewal Timeline

### The 120/90/60/30 Day Framework

Renewal management begins 120 days before the contract expiration date. Each milestone has specific activities, owners, and deliverables.

**Day -120: Renewal Assessment**

Owner: CSM
Activities:
- Review health score and engagement trends
- Assess renewal risk (green/yellow/red classification)
- Review success plan: were defined outcomes achieved?
- Identify expansion opportunities
- Check contract terms (auto-renewal clauses, notice periods)
- Verify billing and contact information accuracy

Deliverables:
- Internal renewal assessment document
- Risk classification with justification
- Expansion or contraction forecast

**Day -90: Strategic Alignment**

Owner: CSM (with manager involvement for at-risk accounts)
Activities:
- Schedule renewal planning meeting with customer stakeholders
- Prepare value summary (ROI delivered, outcomes achieved, usage data)
- Develop renewal proposal (pricing, terms, expansion recommendations)
- For at-risk accounts: Develop remediation plan before renewal discussion
- For expansion opportunities: Prepare business case with ROI projections

Deliverables:
- Customer-facing value summary document
- Internal renewal strategy (pricing, terms, negotiation boundaries)
- Expansion proposal (if applicable)

**Day -60: Renewal Engagement**

Owner: CSM + Account Executive (for complex or at-risk renewals)
Activities:
- Present value summary and renewal proposal to customer
- Address any concerns or objections
- Negotiate terms (pricing, scope, duration)
- Secure verbal commitment or identify remaining blockers
- For at-risk: Escalate to executive sponsor if needed

Deliverables:
- Customer commitment status (committed, negotiating, at-risk)
- Updated renewal forecast
- List of open items and owners

**Day -30: Closure**

Owner: CSM + Deal Desk / Finance
Activities:
- Finalize renewal terms and generate contract
- Route through customer's procurement process
- Follow up on outstanding signatures
- Confirm payment method and billing details
- Update CRM with renewal details

Deliverables:
- Signed renewal contract
- Updated CRM records
- Finance notification for invoicing

**Day 0: Renewal Processed**

Activities:
- Confirm billing processed successfully
- Send renewal confirmation to customer
- Update success plan for new contract period
- Schedule first check-in for new contract period
- Document any commitments made during renewal

## Section 2: Auto-Renewal Strategy

### Auto-Renewal Mechanics

Auto-renewal clauses automatically extend the subscription at the current terms unless the customer provides written notice of cancellation within a specified window (typically 30-90 days before expiration).

**Benefits of Auto-Renewal:**
- Reduces administrative burden (no manual renewal for every contract)
- Improves renewal rates by 5-15% (default effect)
- Reduces the cancellation window (customer must actively opt out)
- Simplifies revenue forecasting

**Auto-Renewal Best Practices:**
- Transparency: Clearly communicate auto-renewal terms at initial sale
- Reminders: Send automated reminders at 90, 60, and 30 days before renewal (legal requirement in many jurisdictions)
- Easy cancellation: Make the opt-out process straightforward (hiding it damages trust and may violate regulations)
- Price increase notice: If pricing increases on auto-renewal, provide explicit written notice with the new price

### Auto-Renewal by Segment

| Segment | Auto-Renewal Default | Notice Period | CSM Involvement |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|-----------------|
| Self-Serve SMB | Yes (default on) | 30 days | Automated only |
| Managed SMB | Yes (default on) | 30 days | CSM email at -60 days |
| Mid-Market | Yes (negotiable) | 60 days | Full renewal process |
| Enterprise | Negotiated per contract | 90 days | Full renewal process with executive |

## Section 3: Pricing Uplift at Renewal

### The Case for Renewal Price Increases

Annual price increases at renewal are standard practice in SaaS and reflect:
- Increased product value (new features, improvements since last contract)
- Inflation and cost increases
- Market rate alignment
- Revenue growth from existing customer base (NRR component)

### Uplift Strategy

**Standard Uplift Range:** 3-7% annually for contracted customers

**Uplift Justification Framework:**
1. Value delivered: Quantify ROI, time saved, outcomes achieved
2. Product improvements: List features added during the contract period
3. Market positioning: Show how pricing compares to alternatives
4. Usage growth: If customer's usage increased, higher pricing is justified

**Uplift Communication:**

```
Subject: Your [Product] Renewal — Value Summary and Updated Terms

Dear [Name],

Over the past [X months], your team has achieved impressive results
with [Product]:
- [Outcome 1 with specific metrics]
- [Outcome 2 with specific metrics]
- [Outcome 3 with specific metrics]

During this period, we've added [X] new features including [top 3
features relevant to their use case].

Your renewal terms reflect a [X%] adjustment to align with the expanded
value and capabilities now available to your team.

[Updated pricing details]

I'd love to discuss how we can help you achieve even more in the next
contract period. Can we schedule a call this week?
```

### Pricing Uplift by Health Score

| Health Score | Recommended Uplift | Rationale |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| 80-100 | 5-7% (or higher with expansion) | Strong value perception supports increase |
| 60-79 | 3-5% (standard) | Maintain relationship, standard increase |
| 40-59 | 0-3% (minimal or flat) | Preserve the renewal, avoid adding price friction |
| 0-39 | Flat or discount | Retain at any cost; address root issues |

## Section 4: Multi-Year Contract Strategy

### Why Multi-Year Matters

Multi-year contracts provide:
- Revenue predictability (2-3 years of committed revenue)
- Reduced churn risk (locked in for the contract period)
- Customer commitment (deeper investment signals stronger partnership)
- Cash flow (upfront payment or guaranteed payment schedule)

### Multi-Year Incentive Structure

| Term | Discount off Annual Price | Payment Terms |
|------|--------------------------|--------------|
| 1 year | 0% (standard) | Annual or quarterly |
| 2 years | 5-10% | Annual or upfront |
| 3 years | 10-15% | Annual or upfront |

**Additional Multi-Year Incentives:**
- Price lock (no increases during the contract term)
- Premium support tier included
- Additional seats or usage at no extra cost
- Early access to new features or beta programs
- Dedicated implementation or success resources

### When to Offer Multi-Year

**Strong candidates:**
- Health score 80+ (high value realization)
- Usage growing (expanding engagement)
- Multiple stakeholders engaged (organizational commitment)
- Strategic initiative alignment (product supports a multi-year initiative)

**Weak candidates:**
- Health score below 60 (risk of buyer's remorse)
- Single champion dependency (relationship fragile)
- New customer (insufficient track record)
- Industry volatility (customer may not exist in 3 years)

## Section 5: Renewal Forecasting

### Forecast Methodology

**Weighted Pipeline Forecasting:**

```
Expected Renewal Revenue = Sum of (Account ARR x Renewal Probability)

Where Renewal Probability is based on:
- Health score (quantitative)
- CSM assessment (qualitative)
- Historical renewal rate for segment
```

| Risk Category | Renewal Probability | Health Score Range |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Committed | 95-100% | 80-100 with verbal/written confirmation |
| Likely | 80-90% | 70-79, engaged, no known risks |
| At Risk | 50-70% | 50-69, some concerns identified |
| Unlikely | 20-40% | 30-49, significant issues |
| Lost | 0-10% | 0-29, customer has communicated intent to leave |

### Forecast Cadence

| Timeframe | Frequency | Accuracy Target |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| 12 months out | Quarterly | Within 10% |
| 6 months out | Monthly | Within 7% |
| 3 months out | Monthly | Within 5% |
| 1 month out | Weekly | Within 3% |
| Current month | Weekly | Within 1% |

### Forecast Accuracy Tracking

```

                          / Forecasted Renewals

```

### Early Warning Indicators

Monitor these signals that predict renewal risk 90+ days out:
- Usage decline of 20%+ over 30 days
- Primary contact non-responsive for 30+ days
- Support ticket sentiment trending negative
- Health score dropping below 60
- No QBR conducted in last 90 days
- Key stakeholder departure
- Customer company layoffs or restructuring news
- Competitor evaluation signals (feature comparison requests)

## Summary

Renewal management is a structured, timeline-driven discipline that begins 120 days before contract expiration. The 120/90/60/30 framework ensures that every renewal receives appropriate assessment, engagement, negotiation, and closure. Auto-renewal improves baseline renewal rates through default effects but requires transparent communication. Pricing uplift at renewal is justified by value delivered and should be calibrated to health score. Multi-year contracts reduce churn risk and improve revenue predictability when offered to the right customers. Renewal forecasting provides the forward visibility that enables proactive intervention and accurate financial planning. The Customer Success Brain treats every renewal as an opportunity to deepen the partnership, not merely extend the contract.

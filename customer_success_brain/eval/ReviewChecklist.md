# Customer Success Review Checklist — Pre-Ship Verification (Authoritative)

Every CS program, success plan, playbook, and customer intervention must pass this
checklist before it is launched, delivered, or executed.

No exceptions. No shortcuts. Incomplete checklist = cannot deploy.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

1. Complete every section relevant to the artifact type
2. Mark each item: PASS, FAIL, or N/A (with justification for N/A)
3. All FAIL items must be resolved before shipping
4. Reviewer must sign off at the bottom
5. Checklist is stored with the program documentation

## SECTION 1: STRATEGIC ALIGNMENT

### 1.1 Business Objective Alignment
- [ ] CS initiative clearly maps to a company-level objective (GRR, NRR, NPS, LTV)
- [ ] Expected impact on the target metric is quantified
- [ ] Timeline for impact is realistic and documented
- [ ] Initiative does not conflict with other team priorities
- [ ] Budget and resource requirements are documented and approved

### 1.2 Customer Segment Alignment
- [ ] Target customer segment is defined (Enterprise, Mid-Market, SMB, all)
- [ ] Segment-specific needs are understood and addressed
- [ ] Initiative accounts for segment differences in behavior and expectations
- [ ] Segmentation criteria are clear (ARR, employee count, industry, use case)

### 1.3 Baseline Measurement
- [ ] Current state metrics documented (before intervention)
- [ ] Baseline is statistically reliable (not a single data point)
- [ ] Comparison group or benchmark defined
- [ ] Measurement methodology is consistent with how success will be measured

## SECTION 2: ONBOARDING REVIEW

### 2.1 Onboarding Design
- [ ] Time-to-first-value target defined for each customer segment
- [ ] Onboarding milestones documented with clear completion criteria
- [ ] Stakeholder mapping template included (who needs to be involved)
- [ ] Technical implementation plan included for each integration type
- [ ] Onboarding kickoff meeting agenda is standardized
- [ ] Customer-facing onboarding guide exists (not just internal process doc)

### 2.2 Onboarding Execution
- [ ] Handoff from Sales to CS is documented with required information
- [ ] Sales handoff includes: goals, stakeholders, technical requirements, timeline commitments
- [ ] Kickoff meeting scheduled within SLA (48 hours for SMB, 5 days for Enterprise)
- [ ] All stakeholder groups identified and engaged (not just the buyer)
- [ ] Technical prerequisites verified before kickoff
- [ ] Escalation path defined if onboarding stalls

### 2.3 Onboarding Measurement
- [ ] Time-to-first-value tracked and reported
- [ ] Onboarding completion rate tracked
- [ ] Onboarding NPS or CSAT collected
- [ ] Correlation between onboarding quality and retention validated
- [ ] Onboarding bottlenecks identified and addressed

### 2.4 Onboarding Failure Handling
- [ ] Red flags during onboarding are defined (unresponsive customer, missing stakeholders, technical blockers)
- [ ] Escalation process for stalled onboardings is documented
- [ ] Re-onboarding process exists for customers who failed initial onboarding
- [ ] Root cause analysis required for every onboarding failure

---

## SECTION 3: SUCCESS PLAN REVIEW

### 3.1 Success Plan Structure
- [ ] Success plan exists for every account above ARR threshold ($50K+)
- [ ] Success plan is co-created with the customer (not imposed)
- [ ] Customer's business objectives are documented in their words
- [ ] Measurable outcomes defined (KPIs that prove value)
- [ ] Milestones defined with timelines
- [ ] Risks identified with mitigation plans
- [ ] Stakeholder map included with roles and engagement plan

### 3.2 Success Plan Content
- [ ] Includes customer's definition of success (not vendor's)
- [ ] Links product usage to business outcomes
- [ ] Defines what "fully adopted" means for this customer
- [ ] Includes expansion opportunities aligned with customer goals
- [ ] Includes renewal timeline and approach

### 3.3 Success Plan Maintenance
- [ ] Review cadence defined (quarterly minimum for Enterprise)
- [ ] Success plan updated after every QBR
- [ ] Milestone progress tracked and reported to customer
- [ ] Success plan shared with customer (not just internal)
- [ ] Plan adjustments documented with reasons

### 3.4 Success Plan Gaps
- [ ] Every account >$50K ARR has a success plan (verify, no exceptions)
- [ ] Every account >$100K ARR has an executive sponsor identified
- [ ] No success plan is >6 months old without an update
- [ ] No success plan has all milestones "in progress" with none completed

---

## SECTION 4: QBR AND ENGAGEMENT REVIEW

### 4.1 QBR Program
- [ ] QBR cadence defined by segment (quarterly for Enterprise/Mid-Market, semi-annual for SMB)
- [ ] QBR template is outcome-focused (not feature-focused)
- [ ] QBR prep checklist exists and is followed
- [ ] Executive attendance tracked and targets set
- [ ] QBR action items tracked to completion
- [ ] QBR feedback collected from customers

### 4.2 Check-in Cadence
- [ ] Regular check-in cadence defined by segment and health
- [ ] Check-in agenda includes value review, not just status update
- [ ] Check-in notes logged in CRM/CS tool
- [ ] Missed check-ins flagged and followed up on
- [ ] Check-in frequency increases for at-risk accounts

### 4.3 Communication Standards
- [ ] Response time SLAs defined by account tier
- [ ] Email templates exist for common touchpoints
- [ ] All customer-facing communications reviewed for quality
- [ ] Multi-channel engagement strategy (email, call, in-app, video)
- [ ] Internal escalation paths are clear and documented

---

## SECTION 5: RISK AND RETENTION REVIEW

### 5.1 Risk Detection
- [ ] Risk signals are defined and automated where possible
- [ ] Risk alert response time SLAs are defined
- [ ] Risk playbooks exist for all common risk types
- [ ] Risk is categorized by type and severity
- [ ] Historical risk signals are validated against actual churn

### 5.2 Risk Response
- [ ] Every risk alert has a defined first response action
- [ ] Escalation criteria are clear (when CSM vs CSM manager vs VP)
- [ ] Save offers or concessions are pre-approved by tier
- [ ] Executive involvement triggers are defined
- [ ] Risk resolution is tracked and documented

### 5.3 Churn Process
- [ ] Churn reason is captured for every lost account
- [ ] Churn reason categorization is standardized
- [ ] Churn post-mortem required for accounts >$25K ARR
- [ ] Churn data is shared with Product, Sales, and Marketing monthly
- [ ] Win-back process exists for churned accounts
- [ ] Involuntary churn (payment failure) has a recovery process

### 5.4 Retention Plays
- [ ] Proactive retention plays exist for common risk scenarios
- [ ] Retention plays are triggered by health score changes
- [ ] Retention play effectiveness is measured (save rate)
- [ ] Retention plays are updated quarterly based on results
- [ ] At-risk renewal accounts identified 120+ days before expiration

---

## SECTION 6: HEALTH SCORE REVIEW

### 6.1 Health Score Model
- [ ] Health score formula is documented and transparent
- [ ] Health score uses 4+ signal categories (usage, engagement, sentiment, financial)
- [ ] Weights are based on empirical churn correlation (not gut feel)
- [ ] Health score updates automatically (not manual entry only)
- [ ] CSM override capability exists with required justification
- [ ] Health score history is preserved (not just current snapshot)

### 6.2 Health Score Validation
- [ ] Health score accuracy validated against actual churn data
- [ ] False positive rate measured and below threshold (<20%)
- [ ] False negative rate measured and below threshold (<15%)
- [ ] Validation performed at least quarterly
- [ ] Recalibration schedule defined and followed

### 6.3 Health Score Operationalization
- [ ] Health score drives automated workflows (alerts, task creation)
- [ ] Health score is visible in CRM and CS tool
- [ ] Health score changes trigger notifications to CSM
- [ ] Red health scores have mandatory action requirements
- [ ] Health score trends (improving/declining) are tracked, not just snapshots

---

## SECTION 7: EXPANSION REVIEW

### 7.1 Expansion Process
- [ ] Expansion signals are defined and tracked
- [ ] CSM/Sales handoff for expansion is documented
- [ ] CSMs are trained on identifying expansion opportunities
- [ ] Expansion conversations are value-led (not discount-led)
- [ ] Expansion pipeline is visible and managed
- [ ] Cross-sell and upsell motions are differentiated

### 7.2 Expansion Execution
- [ ] Expansion conversations happen after value is demonstrated (not before)
- [ ] Expansion proposals include ROI justification
- [ ] Commercial terms for expansion are pre-approved
- [ ] Expansion close rate is tracked
- [ ] Average expansion revenue is tracked by segment

### 7.3 Expansion Guardrails
- [ ] Expansion is not attempted on at-risk accounts
- [ ] Customer satisfaction verified before expansion conversation
- [ ] Expansion does not compromise customer relationship
- [ ] Failed expansion attempts are documented with learnings
- [ ] NPS/CSAT monitored post-expansion to verify no negative impact

---

## SECTION 8: DATA AND TOOLING REVIEW

### 8.1 Data Quality
- [ ] Customer data in CRM is complete and accurate (>90% field completion for key fields)
- [ ] Product usage data is flowing correctly to CS tool
- [ ] Data discrepancies between systems are resolved
- [ ] Single source of truth defined for each metric
- [ ] Data refresh frequency meets operational needs

### 8.2 Tooling
- [ ] CS platform configured and adopted by team (>80% DAU)
- [ ] Key workflows automated (alerts, tasks, emails)
- [ ] Reporting dashboards exist for all key CS metrics
- [ ] Integration between CS tool, CRM, product analytics, and support is working
- [ ] CSMs trained on tools and processes

### 8.3 Reporting
- [ ] Weekly metrics report is automated
- [ ] Monthly CS scorecard is produced
- [ ] Quarterly business review for CS leadership is prepared
- [ ] Metrics definitions are documented and consistent
- [ ] Historical trends are visible (not just point-in-time)

---

## SECTION 9: TEAM AND PROCESS REVIEW

### 9.1 Team Structure
- [ ] CSM-to-account ratio is appropriate by segment
- [ ] Account assignment considers CSM skills and customer needs
- [ ] Backup coverage plan exists for CSM absence
- [ ] Onboarding program exists for new CSMs
- [ ] Career development path defined for CS team

### 9.2 Skills and Training
- [ ] CSMs trained on product (deep product knowledge required)
- [ ] CSMs trained on commercial skills (expansion conversations)
- [ ] CSMs trained on executive communication
- [ ] CSMs certified on health score model and risk playbooks
- [ ] Ongoing training cadence established (monthly minimum)

### 9.3 Process Compliance
- [ ] Playbook adherence tracked (are CSMs following processes?)
- [ ] CRM hygiene score tracked per CSM
- [ ] Activity minimums defined and monitored
- [ ] Process exceptions documented with justification
- [ ] Process improvement feedback loop exists

---

## SECTION 10: MEASUREMENT AND ACCOUNTABILITY

### 10.1 Success Metrics
- [ ] Primary success metric for the initiative is defined
- [ ] Baseline measurement completed
- [ ] Target improvement quantified
- [ ] Measurement methodology documented
- [ ] Reporting cadence defined

### 10.2 Accountability
- [ ] Owner assigned for the initiative
- [ ] Review cadence established
- [ ] Escalation triggers defined
- [ ] Failure criteria defined (when to stop or pivot)
- [ ] Learnings will be documented regardless of outcome

### 10.3 Continuous Improvement
- [ ] Feedback mechanism exists (from CSMs, from customers)
- [ ] Regular retrospectives scheduled
- [ ] Best practices captured and shared
- [ ] Process updates require change management (not ad-hoc)
- [ ] Benchmarking against industry standards

## COMMON REJECTION REASONS

| Reason | Frequency | Fix |
|--------|-----------|-----|
| No success plan for large account | Very Common | Create within 5 business days of contract |
| Health score not validated | Common | Run quarterly validation against churn data |
| QBR without executive attendance | Common | Escalate invite, tie to exec priorities |
| No churn reason captured | Common | Mandatory field in CRM at churn event |
| Expansion attempted on at-risk account | Occasional | Check health score before commercial conversation |
| Onboarding SLA missed | Occasional | Automate kickoff scheduling, add alerts |
| Risk signal ignored | Periodic | Review alert fatigue, prioritize signals |

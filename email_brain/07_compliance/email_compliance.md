# Email Compliance — CAN-SPAM, GDPR, CASL, and Global Regulations

## 1. CAN-SPAM Act (United States)

The Controlling the Assault of Non-Solicited Pornography And Marketing Act (2003) establishes rules for commercial email in the United States. Despite its name, CAN-SPAM does not require prior consent for commercial email — it regulates how commercial email must be sent.

### CAN-SPAM Requirements

**1. Accurate Header Information**
- The "From," "To," and "Reply-To" fields must accurately identify the person or business sending the message
- The originating domain name and email address must be accurate
- Violation: Spoofing sender identity, using misleading domain names

**2. Non-Deceptive Subject Lines**
- Subject lines must accurately reflect the content of the email
- Misleading subject lines are a violation even if the body contains accurate information
- Example violation: Subject "RE: Your recent order" when there was no order or prior conversation

**3. Identification as Advertisement**
- The email must clearly identify itself as an advertisement or solicitation
- The law allows flexibility in how this disclosure is made
- Common approaches: "This is a promotional email" or clear commercial context
- Exception: If recipient has given affirmative consent, this disclosure is not required

**4. Physical Postal Address**
- Every commercial email must include the sender's valid physical postal address
- This can be: current street address, PO Box registered with USPS, or private mailbox registered with a commercial mail receiving agency
- The address must be valid for at least 30 days after sending

**5. Opt-Out Mechanism**
- Every commercial email must include a clear, conspicuous mechanism to opt out of future email
- The opt-out must be functional for at least 30 days after the email is sent
- Common approaches: Unsubscribe link, reply-to instruction
- The opt-out mechanism cannot require payment, personal information beyond email address, or multiple steps beyond confirming the request

**6. Honor Opt-Out Requests**
- Opt-out requests must be processed within 10 business days
- After opt-out, you cannot send commercial email to that address
- You cannot sell, transfer, or share the email address for others to email
- The opt-out applies to the sender, not individual lists or campaigns

**7. Monitoring Third Parties**
- If you hire a company to send email on your behalf, you are still legally responsible
- Both the company whose product is promoted AND the company that sends the email can be held liable

### CAN-SPAM Penalties

- Each individual email in violation can incur penalties up to $50,120 (adjusted for inflation)
- Penalties can be assessed per recipient, per violation — making mass violations extremely expensive
- Criminal penalties possible for certain violations (accessing others' computers, falsifying headers)

### CAN-SPAM Practical Compliance Checklist

- [ ] From address accurately identifies sender
- [ ] Subject line accurately reflects content
- [ ] Commercial nature of email is clear
- [ ] Valid physical address included in every email
- [ ] Unsubscribe link is present, visible, and functional
- [ ] Opt-outs are processed within 10 business days
- [ ] Third-party senders are monitored for compliance

## 2. GDPR Email Requirements (European Union)

The General Data Protection Regulation (2018) fundamentally changed email marketing in the EU and for any organization communicating with EU residents. GDPR is a data protection law, not an email law — but its requirements profoundly impact email marketing.

### Lawful Basis for Email Marketing Under GDPR

GDPR requires a lawful basis for processing personal data. For email marketing, two bases are relevant:

**Consent (Article 6(1)(a))**
- Must be freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
- Requires an affirmative action (opt-in, not pre-checked box)
- Must be separate from other consents or terms
- Must be easy to withdraw
- Records of consent must be maintained
- Cannot be a condition of service (no "subscribe to get access")

**Legitimate Interest (Article 6(1)(f))**
- Can be used for marketing to existing customers about similar products/services (soft opt-in)
- Requires a documented Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA)
- Must balance business interest against individual privacy rights
- Must still provide opt-out mechanism
- Cannot be used for cold email to people with no prior relationship

### GDPR Consent Requirements (Detailed)

**What constitutes valid consent**:
- Active opt-in (checkbox they must check, not pre-checked)
- Clear description of what they are consenting to
- Identity of the data controller clearly stated
- Each purpose for data processing listed separately
- Informed about the right to withdraw consent
- Not bundled with other terms or consents

**What does NOT constitute valid consent**:
- Pre-checked boxes
- Inactivity or silence
- "By using this service you agree to receive marketing"
- Consent buried in terms and conditions
- Single consent for multiple unrelated purposes

### GDPR Data Subject Rights (Email-Relevant)

**Right to Access (Article 15)**:
- Subscribers can request a copy of all data you hold about them
- You must respond within 30 days
- Must include: data held, purposes of processing, recipients, retention period

**Right to Erasure (Article 17)**:
- Subscribers can request deletion of their personal data
- Must be completed "without undue delay"
- Exceptions: Legal obligations, public interest, archiving
- Note: Add email to suppression list BEFORE deleting data (to prevent re-collection)

**Right to Data Portability (Article 20)**:
- Subscribers can request their data in a machine-readable format
- Must provide data in structured, commonly used format (CSV, JSON)

**Right to Object (Article 21)**:
- For marketing purposes, the right to object is ABSOLUTE
- If subscriber objects to marketing, you MUST stop processing immediately
- No balancing test, no exceptions

### GDPR Penalties

- Tier 1: Up to 10 million euros or 2% of global annual turnover
- Tier 2: Up to 20 million euros or 4% of global annual turnover
- Plus potential claims for damages from affected individuals

## 3. CASL — Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation

CASL is among the strictest email marketing laws globally. Unlike CAN-SPAM, CASL requires PRIOR consent before sending commercial email.

### CASL Consent Types

**Express Consent**:
- Recipient explicitly agreed to receive commercial email
- Consent must be obtained with clear identification of the sender
- Description of the type of messages to be sent
- Statement that consent can be withdrawn at any time
- Express consent does not expire (but best practice: re-confirm periodically)

**Implied Consent** (time-limited):
- Existing business relationship: Purchased within 2 years, or contract within 2 years
- Existing non-business relationship: Membership, volunteer, donation within 2 years
- Inquiry: Inquiry within 6 months
- Published address: Address published without "no spam" note (very narrow exception)
- Referral: One-time message to a referred contact

### CASL Requirements

- Identify the sender clearly (name, physical address, contact info)
- Include a functioning unsubscribe mechanism
- Process unsubscribes within 10 business days
- Maintain records of consent (who, when, how, what was consented to)
- Include sender's contact information in every message

### CASL Penalties

- Up to $1 million CAD per violation for individuals
- Up to $10 million CAD per violation for organizations
- Private right of action allows individuals to sue

## 4. UK PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations)

Post-Brexit, the UK maintained GDPR through the UK GDPR but retains PECR specifically for electronic communications.

### PECR Key Requirements for Email

- Consent required for unsolicited marketing email
- "Soft opt-in" exception: Existing customers can receive marketing about similar products/services IF given the opportunity to opt out at the time of data collection AND in every subsequent communication
- Unsubscribe mechanism required in every marketing email
- Sender must be identifiable

### The Soft Opt-In (PECR Regulation 22)

Conditions for soft opt-in:
1. Email address was obtained during a sale or negotiation for a sale
2. The marketing relates to similar products or services
3. The recipient was given the opportunity to opt out when the address was collected
4. The recipient is given the opportunity to opt out in every subsequent email

## 5. California Email Laws

### CalOPPA (California Online Privacy Protection Act)
- Requires a privacy policy accessible from any page that collects personal information
- Must disclose email collection and usage practices
- Must describe opt-out procedures

### CCPA/CPRA (California Consumer Privacy Act / California Privacy Rights Act)
- Right to know what personal data is collected (including email engagement data)
- Right to delete personal data
- Right to opt out of sale of personal data
- "Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link required if selling data
- Does not require consent for marketing email (unlike GDPR) but affects data handling

### Global Compliance Strategy

For organizations with subscribers in multiple jurisdictions, the pragmatic approach is to comply with the strictest applicable law:
1. Obtain explicit opt-in consent for all subscribers (satisfies GDPR, CASL, and all opt-in jurisdictions)
2. Maintain detailed consent records (who, when, how, what)
3. Include physical address and clear sender identification in every email
4. Provide one-click unsubscribe processed within 48 hours
5. Maintain preference center and data access/deletion request process
6. Document all processing activities

## 7. Consent Collection Best Practices

### Double Opt-In vs. Single Opt-In

**Single opt-in**: Subscriber enters email → added to list immediately
- Pros: Higher signup rates (15-20% more signups), faster list growth
- Cons: Higher invalid addresses, higher complaint risk, weaker consent evidence

**Double opt-in (confirmed opt-in)**: Subscriber enters email → confirmation email sent → subscriber clicks confirmation link → added to list
- Pros: Verified email addresses, stronger consent evidence, lower complaint rates, better deliverability
- Cons: 20-30% of signups do not complete confirmation

**Recommendation**: Double opt-in is the gold standard for GDPR compliance and deliverability health. Use single opt-in only in jurisdictions that do not require it AND when you have real-time email validation at signup.

## 8. Preference Centers

### Preference Center Components

A preference center gives subscribers granular control:

**Content preferences**: Which topics or categories they want to receive
**Frequency preferences**: How often (daily, weekly, monthly)
**Channel preferences**: Email, SMS, push, or combination
**Format preferences**: HTML or plain text
**Pause option**: Temporarily stop emails for a defined period

### Preference Center Design Principles

1. Pre-populate current selections based on actual behavior and stored preferences
2. Save changes instantly with visible confirmation
3. Accessible from every email footer and from the subscriber's account page
4. Offer "reduce frequency" as a prominent alternative to unsubscribing
5. Changes must be reflected in the email program within 24 hours (ideally immediately)

## 9. Compliance Monitoring and Audit

### Monthly Compliance Checks

- [ ] All active email templates include physical address
- [ ] All active email templates include working unsubscribe link
- [ ] Unsubscribe requests are processed within regulatory timeframe
- [ ] Consent records are being maintained for new subscribers
- [ ] Suppression lists are being enforced across all sending systems
- [ ] New email types/campaigns have been reviewed for compliance

### Annual Compliance Audit

- [ ] Review all consent collection mechanisms for current legal requirements
- [ ] Verify consent records are complete and accessible
- [ ] Audit data processing agreements with ESPs and third parties
- [ ] Review data retention policies and enforce deletion schedules
- [ ] Update privacy policy to reflect current email practices
- [ ] Verify DSAR (Data Subject Access Request) process is functional
- [ ] Review employee training on email compliance

### Record Retention Requirements

| Record Type | Retention Period | Purpose |
|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| Consent records | Duration of consent + 3 years | Proof of lawful basis |
| Unsubscribe requests | 7 years minimum | Proof of compliance |
| Suppression lists | Indefinitely | Prevent re-contact |
| Email send logs | 3-7 years | Audit trail |
| Complaint records | 3-7 years | Pattern analysis, defense |
| Privacy policy versions | Indefinitely | Historical reference |

## 10. Emerging Compliance Considerations

### AI and Email Compliance

- AI-generated email content must still comply with all regulations
- AI-driven personalization must respect consent boundaries (only use data consented for)
- Automated decision-making that significantly affects individuals may trigger GDPR Article 22 rights
- AI must not circumvent unsubscribe requests or suppression lists

### Privacy-First Email Architecture

The trend toward privacy-first technology impacts email:
- **Apple MPP**: Mail Privacy Protection inflates open rates with proxy opens
- **Browser privacy**: Third-party cookie deprecation affects cross-site tracking for email
- **Email privacy**: ProtonMail, Tutanota strip tracking pixels
- **Regulatory trend**: More countries adopting GDPR-like regulations

### Preparing for the Future

1. Reduce reliance on open-rate metrics (use clicks, conversions, revenue)
2. Build first-party data relationships (direct preference collection)
3. Invest in zero-party data (data subscribers voluntarily provide)
4. Design email programs that work without tracking (value-driven, not surveillance-driven)
5. Monitor regulatory developments in all jurisdictions where you have subscribers

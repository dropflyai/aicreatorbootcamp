# HR Brain — Scope and Boundaries

## Jurisdictional Authority

The HR Brain has **authoritative jurisdiction** over the following domains. No other brain may override HR Brain decisions in these areas without explicit conflict resolution.

---

## In-Scope: Full Authority

- Job architecture and role definition
- Sourcing strategy and channel optimization
- Interview design and candidate assessment
- Offer construction and negotiation strategy
- Onboarding program design and execution
- Workforce planning models and headcount forecasting
- Employer brand strategy and employee value proposition (EVP)
- Internal mobility and talent marketplace design

### 2. Compensation, Benefits, and Total Rewards
- Pay philosophy definition (lead, match, lag market)
- Job leveling and career architecture
- Salary band design and market pricing methodology
- Equity compensation design (options, RSUs, ESOP)
- Benefits strategy and plan selection
- Total rewards statement design
- Pay equity analysis and remediation
- Executive compensation frameworks

### 3. Performance Management and Development
- Performance review system design (continuous feedback, calibration)
- Goal-setting frameworks (OKRs, SMART, cascading objectives)
- Career ladder and leveling framework design
- Individual development plan (IDP) methodology
- Mentorship and sponsorship program design
- Succession planning and high-potential identification
- Performance improvement plan (PIP) design and process
- Leadership development programs

### 4. Culture, Engagement, and Employee Experience
- Culture definition and values operationalization
- Engagement measurement (Gallup Q12, pulse surveys, eNPS)
- Employee experience mapping and improvement
- Recognition and rewards program design
- Remote/hybrid culture frameworks
- Onboarding experience design
- Offboarding and alumni program design
- Internal communications strategy

### 5. Organizational Design and Change Management
- Organizational structure design (functional, matrix, pods, etc.)
- Team topology and composition
- Span of control optimization
- Reporting structure design
- Change management process (Kotter, ADKAR)
- Restructuring and reorganization planning
- Role consolidation and elimination planning
- Cross-functional collaboration frameworks

### 6. DEIB (Diversity, Equity, Inclusion, Belonging)
- DEIB strategy and goal-setting
- Inclusive hiring process design
- Bias reduction in assessment and promotion
- ERG (Employee Resource Group) program design
- Pay equity and representation analytics
- Inclusive leadership development
- Belonging measurement and improvement
- Accommodation and accessibility in HR processes

### 7. HR Operations and Compliance
- HRIS selection and configuration strategy
- Employee records and documentation standards
- Compliance with labor law (FLSA, ADA, FMLA, Title VII, etc.)
- Progressive discipline process design
- Investigation procedures and documentation
- Termination process and documentation
- Required training program design
- Policy creation and maintenance

### 8. People Analytics
- HR metrics definition and dashboarding
- Turnover analysis and prediction models
- Engagement driver analysis
- Compensation equity modeling
- Workforce demographic analysis
- Recruiting funnel analytics
- Performance distribution analysis
- Organizational network analysis (ONA)

## Out of Scope: Explicit Boundaries

The HR Brain does NOT have authority over:

### Legal Determinations
- The HR Brain does not practice law
- It identifies compliance risks and recommends process
- Final legal determinations require licensed counsel
- **Boundary**: HR Brain flags risk; Legal Brain (or external counsel) adjudicates

### Final Business Decisions
- Headcount approval (HR recommends; business approves)
- Budget allocation (HR models; finance/leadership approves)
- Business strategy (HR aligns to; MBA Brain or leadership sets)
- Product roadmap (HR provides talent feasibility; Product Brain owns)
- **Boundary**: HR Brain provides people-informed recommendations; business leadership decides

### Clinical/Medical Decisions
- Mental health diagnosis or treatment
- Disability determination (medical professionals determine; HR accommodates)
- Workers' compensation medical determinations
- Drug testing interpretation (MRO role)
- **Boundary**: HR Brain designs accommodation processes; clinical professionals make medical determinations

### Individual Employee Disputes
- The HR Brain designs fair processes, not individual adjudications
- Specific employee complaints require human investigation
- Arbitration and mediation require trained neutrals
- **Boundary**: HR Brain provides frameworks; trained HR professionals execute

## Escalation Protocol

When the HR Brain encounters issues at the boundary of its scope:

```
Level 1: Within Scope
  → Apply HR Brain frameworks directly
  → Reference relevant module documentation

Level 2: Shared Authority
  → Identify the collaborating brain
  → Define the HR Brain's contribution clearly
  → Request input from the appropriate specialist brain
  → Synthesize recommendations jointly

Level 3: Out of Scope
  → Clearly state the boundary being reached
  → Identify the appropriate authority (legal, medical, business leadership)
  → Provide HR Brain's analysis up to the boundary
  → Recommend specific next steps for the appropriate authority

Level 4: Compliance Risk
  → STOP all recommendations in the affected area
  → Flag the specific compliance risk with citations
  → Recommend immediate legal/compliance review
  → Document the issue for the Memory system
```

## Geographic and Regulatory Scope

The HR Brain's recommendations account for jurisdictional variation:

### Federal (US)
- FLSA (Fair Labor Standards Act) — wage and hour
- Title VII (Civil Rights Act) — discrimination
- ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act) — accommodation
- FMLA (Family and Medical Leave Act) — leave
- ADEA (Age Discrimination in Employment Act)
- WARN Act — mass layoff notification
- ERISA — benefits and retirement plans
- OSHA — workplace safety
- NLRA — collective bargaining rights

### State-Level Considerations
- State-specific minimum wage and overtime rules
- Pay transparency laws (CO, CA, NY, WA, etc.)
- Ban-the-box and fair chance hiring laws
- Non-compete enforceability variations
- State family leave laws (often more generous than FMLA)
- At-will employment exceptions by state

### International Considerations
- The HR Brain flags when recommendations may not apply outside the US
- GDPR implications for employee data (EU)
- Country-specific termination requirements
- Statutory benefits requirements by country
- Works council and employee representation requirements

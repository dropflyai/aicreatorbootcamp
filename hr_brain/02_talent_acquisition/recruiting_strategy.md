# Recruiting Strategy

## Purpose

This module defines the strategic framework for talent acquisition. Recruiting is not a transactional activity -- it is the organization's primary mechanism for acquiring human capital. The quality of hiring decisions compounds over time, making talent acquisition the highest-leverage activity in the HR function. Research from the SHRM and the Corporate Leadership Council consistently demonstrates that top-quartile talent produces 2-4x the output of bottom-quartile talent in knowledge work.

---

## 1. Employer Brand

### 1.1 Definition and Strategic Importance

Employer brand is the organization's reputation and value proposition as an employer. It is the intersection of:

- **Identity**: Who we are as an employer (values, culture, work environment)
- **Image**: How external candidates perceive us
- **Experience**: What current employees actually experience

Research from LinkedIn indicates that a strong employer brand reduces cost per hire by up to 50% and reduces turnover by up to 28%. Employer brand is not marketing fiction -- it is the authentic representation of the employee experience.

### 1.2 Employee Value Proposition (EVP) Framework

The EVP is the total offering an employer provides in exchange for the employee's skills, capabilities, and experiences. The Corporate Leadership Council identifies five pillars:

| Pillar | Components | Measurement |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| **Compensation** | Base salary, variable pay, equity, signing bonus | Market positioning (percentile), compa-ratio distribution |
| **Benefits** | Health, retirement, PTO, parental leave, wellness | Total benefits value, utilization rates |
| **Career** | Growth opportunity, learning, promotion velocity, internal mobility | Promotion rate, learning hours, internal fill rate |
| **Work environment** | Culture, work-life balance, flexibility, physical/virtual workspace | Engagement scores, eNPS, flexibility satisfaction |
| **Purpose** | Mission alignment, impact, social responsibility, pride in company | Purpose alignment survey items, Glassdoor ratings |

### 1.3 Employer Brand Audit

Conduct annually. Assess across channels:

- **Glassdoor**: Overall rating (target >4.0), CEO approval, "recommend to a friend" percentage, review themes
- **LinkedIn**: Talent brand index, follower growth, content engagement, InMail acceptance rate
- **Career site**: Traffic, application conversion rate, bounce rate, time on site
- **Offer acceptance rate**: By source, level, department (declining rates signal brand erosion)
- **Candidate experience survey**: Post-interview NPS, communication satisfaction, process fairness perception
- **Employee referral rate**: High referral rates indicate employees are willing to stake personal reputation

### 1.4 Employer Brand Content Strategy

```
Awareness stage:   Company culture videos, employee stories, thought leadership
Interest stage:    Day-in-the-life content, team introductions, tech blog posts
Application stage: Clear job descriptions, salary transparency, process explanation
Interview stage:   Interviewer bios, preparation guides, timeline communication
Offer stage:       Personalized total compensation breakdown, team welcome message
Onboarding stage:  Pre-boarding content, first-week schedule, buddy introduction
```

## 2. Sourcing Strategy

### 2.1 Channel Strategy

Effective sourcing uses a portfolio approach. No single channel should represent more than 40% of total hires to avoid concentration risk.

| Channel | Typical Quality | Typical Cost | Best For |
|---------|----------------|-------------|----------|
| Employee referrals | Highest (referrals have 25% higher retention at 2 years) | Low-medium ($1-5K referral bonus) | All roles; culture-fit advantage |
| Direct sourcing (outbound) | High (targeting passive candidates) | Medium-high (recruiter time + tools) | Senior/specialized roles |
| LinkedIn Recruiter | Medium-high | High ($8-12K/seat annually) | Professional/technical roles |
| Job boards (Indeed, etc.) | Medium | Medium ($200-500/posting) | Volume hiring, early-career |
| University recruiting | Medium (high potential, lower experience) | Medium (career fairs, campus visits) | Entry-level, intern pipelines |
| Agencies/search firms | Variable | Very high (20-33% of first-year salary) | Executive search, niche roles, urgent fills |
| Internal mobility | Highest retention, known performance | Low (internal posting, development) | All roles where internal talent exists |
| Diversity-focused sources | Targeted | Medium | Underrepresented talent pipelines |

### 2.2 Passive vs. Active Candidate Strategy

According to LinkedIn data, approximately 70% of the global workforce is passive (not actively job-seeking). Reaching passive candidates requires different tactics:

**Active candidates** (applying to postings):
- Optimize job descriptions for search (SEO, clear titles, keywords)
- Streamline application process (target <15 minutes)
- Ensure mobile-friendly application
- Respond within 48 hours

**Passive candidates** (must be attracted):
- Personalized outreach (reference specific work, mutual connections, genuine interest)
- Content marketing to build awareness over time
- Networking at industry events, meetups, conferences
- Alumni networks and boomerang programs
- Maintain talent community / CRM for future opportunities

### 2.3 Sourcing Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Source mix | % of hires from each channel | No channel >40% |
| Source yield | Hires from source / Candidates from source | Track by channel |
| Source quality | Avg performance rating by source at 12 months | Referrals and direct sourcing typically highest |
| Response rate (outbound) | Responses / Outreach messages sent | >25% for well-targeted outreach |
| Referral participation | Employees who referred / Total employees | >30% participation annually |
| Pipeline diversity | % underrepresented candidates at top of funnel | Meet or exceed market availability |

## 3. Applicant Tracking System (ATS) Strategy

### 3.1 ATS Selection Criteria

The ATS is the central nervous system of recruiting. Evaluate based on:

- **Workflow flexibility**: Configurable stages, scorecards, and approval flows
- **Candidate experience**: Clean apply flow, mobile-optimized, status portal
- **Integration ecosystem**: HRIS, background check, assessment, scheduling tools
- **Reporting and analytics**: Funnel metrics, source tracking, EEO reporting, time-in-stage
- **Collaboration features**: Interviewer scorecards, @ mentions, Slack integration
- **Compliance**: EEO data collection, OFCCP audit support, data retention policies
- **AI/automation**: Resume parsing, candidate matching, automated scheduling (audit for bias)

### 3.2 ATS Market Landscape

| Tier | Tools | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Enterprise | Workday Recruiting, SAP SuccessFactors, iCIMS | 5,000+ employees, complex compliance needs |
| Mid-market | Greenhouse, Lever, Ashby | 200-5,000 employees, data-driven recruiting |
| SMB | BambooHR, JazzHR, Breezy | <200 employees, simplicity and cost |
| Startup | Ashby, Lever, Notion (manual) | <50 employees, early-stage flexibility |

### 3.3 ATS Data Hygiene

- Require disposition codes for every rejected candidate (compliance and analytics)
- Standardize stage names across all requisitions
- Enforce source attribution at point of candidate creation
- Archive (do not delete) requisitions and candidates for historical analysis
- Regular duplicate candidate cleanup
- EEO self-identification data collected per OFCCP requirements

## 4. Recruiting Metrics Dashboard

### 4.1 Operational Metrics (Weekly Review)

| Metric | Definition | Alert Threshold |
|--------|-----------|-----------------|
| Open requisitions | Active unfilled positions | >15% of total headcount |
| Requisition aging | Days since requisition opened | >45 days (standard), >60 days (executive) |
| Pipeline coverage | Candidates in active stages / Open requisitions | <3:1 per requisition |
| Interview-to-offer ratio | Interviews conducted / Offers extended | >8:1 signals process inefficiency |
| Offer-to-acceptance rate | Offers accepted / Offers extended | <85% |
| Recruiter workload | Open requisitions per recruiter | >20 signals capacity constraint |

### 4.2 Strategic Metrics (Quarterly Review)

| Metric | Definition | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Quality of hire | Composite: 12-month performance + retention + manager satisfaction | Top 2 quartiles |
| Hiring manager satisfaction | Survey of hiring managers on recruiting process | >4.0/5.0 |
| Candidate experience NPS | Post-process survey of all candidates (including rejected) | >40 |
| Diversity of hires | % underrepresented groups hired vs. pipeline vs. market | Positive trend |
| Internal fill rate | Internal placements / Total placements | >20% |
| Cost per hire by level | Total recruiting costs / Hires by level | Track trend |

## 5. Diversity Recruiting

### 5.1 Strategic Framework

Diversity recruiting is not separate from recruiting -- it is an integral component of excellent recruiting. Research from McKinsey ("Diversity Wins," 2020) demonstrates that companies in the top quartile for ethnic and cultural diversity are 36% more likely to achieve above-average profitability.

### 5.2 Inclusive Sourcing Practices

**Pipeline development:**
- Partner with HBCUs, HSIs, tribal colleges, and women's colleges
- Sponsor and attend conferences for underrepresented groups (Grace Hopper, AfroTech, Lesbians Who Tech, Out in Tech, DisabilityIN)
- Develop relationships with diversity-focused organizations (NSBE, SHPE, AISES, Anita Borg Institute)
- Establish returner programs for career-break professionals
- Create apprenticeship and alternative pathway programs (non-traditional backgrounds)

**Job description optimization:**
- Use gender-neutral language (audit with tools like Textio or Gender Decoder)
- Limit "requirements" to true must-haves (research shows women apply when meeting 100% of requirements; men apply at 60%)
- Include salary range (transparency attracts diverse candidates; mandated in many jurisdictions)
- State commitment to inclusion and accommodation
- Avoid unnecessary degree requirements that screen out qualified candidates

**Process equity:**
- Blind resume review (remove names, photos, university names) where feasible
- Standardized evaluation criteria applied equally to all candidates
- Diverse interview panels
- Structured interviews with consistent questions and scoring rubrics
- Monitor conversion rates by demographic group at each funnel stage

### 5.3 Diversity Recruiting Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Diagnostic Use |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Pipeline diversity | % underrepresented by funnel stage | Identify where diverse candidates drop off |
| Application-to-interview conversion by group | Interview invitations / Applications by demographic | Screen stage bias detection |
| Interview-to-offer conversion by group | Offers / Interviews by demographic | Interview stage bias detection |
| Offer acceptance by group | Acceptances / Offers by demographic | Competitiveness gap detection |
| Diversity hiring rate vs. market | Hire rate compared to labor market availability | Overall program effectiveness |

## 6. Recruiting Operations Excellence

### 6.1 Recruiter Capacity Model

```
Available recruiter hours per month: 160
- Administrative/meetings: 30 hours
- Sourcing per requisition: 5 hours/week x 4 weeks = 20 hours
- Screening per requisition: 3 hours/week x 4 weeks = 12 hours
- Coordination per requisition: 2 hours/week x 4 weeks = 8 hours
Total per requisition per month: ~40 hours

Sustainable capacity: 160 available / 40 per req = ~3-4 active requisitions
Peak capacity (with coordinator support): 6-8 active requisitions
```

### 6.2 Requisition Intake Meeting

Every new requisition requires a structured intake meeting between recruiter and hiring manager. Non-negotiable agenda:

1. Business case for the role (why now? what happens if unfilled?)
2. Role requirements: must-have vs. nice-to-have (challenge inflated requirements)
3. Ideal candidate profile (specific examples of people who would succeed)
4. Compensation range (pre-approved, market-validated)
5. Interview process design (panel, timeline, assessment methods)
6. Sourcing strategy and candidate persona
7. Timeline expectations and communication cadence
8. Diversity goals for the pipeline

### 6.3 Service-Level Agreements (SLAs)

| Stage | SLA | Owner |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Requisition intake to posting | 3 business days | Recruiter |
| Application acknowledgment | 24 hours (automated) | ATS |
| Resume screen to disposition | 5 business days | Recruiter |
| Interview scheduling | 3 business days from request | Recruiting coordinator |
| Interviewer feedback submission | 24 hours post-interview | Interviewer |
| Debrief to decision | 2 business days | Hiring manager + recruiter |
| Offer approval to verbal offer | 2 business days | Recruiter |
| Candidate rejection notification | 5 business days from decision | Recruiter (personalized for finalists) |

## 7. Candidate Experience Design

### 7.1 Touchpoint Mapping

Every interaction shapes candidate perception. Map and optimize each touchpoint:

```
Job discovery → Application → Acknowledgment → Screen → Interview scheduling
→ Interview preparation → Interview day → Debrief wait → Decision communication
→ Offer/Rejection → Negotiation → Acceptance → Pre-boarding → Day 1
```

### 7.2 Candidate Communication Standards

- **Transparency**: Share timeline, process steps, and evaluation criteria upfront
- **Timeliness**: No candidate should be without communication for >5 business days
- **Personalization**: Finalists receive personalized rejection with specific feedback (when legally permissible)
- **Respect**: Every interaction assumes the candidate is evaluating us as much as we are evaluating them
- **Feedback loop**: Post-process survey for all candidates; results reviewed monthly

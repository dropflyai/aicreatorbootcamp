# HR Review Checklist — Pre-Ship Verification (Authoritative)

This checklist must be completed before any HR initiative ships.
Every section must pass. Partial completion is not acceptable.

If an item cannot be verified, it has not been done.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

1. Complete every section relevant to the initiative type
2. Mark each item: PASS / FAIL / N/A
3. All FAIL items must be resolved before shipping
4. Reviewer must sign off with name, date, and initiative reference
5. Archive completed checklists for compliance record-keeping

## SECTION 1: HIRING PROCESS VERIFICATION

### Job Description Quality
- [ ] Job description focuses on outcomes, not just requirements
- [ ] Required qualifications are genuinely required (not wish list as requirements)
- [ ] Preferred qualifications are clearly separated from required
- [ ] Compensation range is included (where legally required, always recommended)
- [ ] Job description has been reviewed for exclusionary language
- [ ] Growth potential and learning opportunities are described
- [ ] Team and manager information is included
- [ ] Application process is clear and reasonable

### Interview Process
- [ ] Structured interview guide exists for the role
- [ ] Interview questions are standardized across candidates
- [ ] Interviewers are trained on structured interviewing
- [ ] Evaluation criteria are defined before interviews begin
- [ ] Technical assessments (if any) are relevant to the actual work
- [ ] Take-home assignments (if any) are reasonable in scope (< 3 hours)
- [ ] Interview panel includes diverse perspectives
- [ ] Candidate experience is mapped and optimized

### Legal Compliance
- [ ] No illegal interview questions (age, marital status, religion, disability, etc.)
- [ ] Background check process complies with FCRA and state requirements
- [ ] Ban-the-box compliance verified (if applicable by jurisdiction)
- [ ] EEO/AA data collection is compliant
- [ ] Adverse impact analysis conducted for selection criteria
- [ ] Reasonable accommodations offered for interviews
- [ ] Immigration status questions limited to legal requirements
- [ ] Consistent process documented for all candidates

### Decision-Making
- [ ] Hiring decision based on documented evaluation criteria
- [ ] All interviewers submit independent written feedback before debrief
- [ ] Debrief process is structured to prevent anchoring bias
- [ ] Compensation offer is within band and justified
- [ ] Reference checks completed (if part of process)
- [ ] Offer approval chain followed
- [ ] Rejection communication is respectful and timely

---

## SECTION 2: COMPENSATION VERIFICATION

### Market Data
- [ ] Compensation benchmarking data is current (< 6 months old)
- [ ] Data sources are reputable and relevant to the talent market
- [ ] Job matching is accurate (not just title matching)
- [ ] Geographic differentials are applied consistently
- [ ] Role leveling is calibrated against benchmark data
- [ ] Total compensation is benchmarked (not just base salary)

### Pay Equity
- [ ] Pay equity analysis conducted within the last 12 months
- [ ] Statistical methodology is sound (multiple regression preferred)
- [ ] Legitimate factors identified and controlled for (tenure, performance, location, etc.)
- [ ] Unexplained gaps flagged and remediation plan exists
- [ ] Individual exceptions reviewed and justified
- [ ] Pay equity results reviewed by legal counsel
- [ ] Remediation budget allocated if gaps exist

### Pay Band Compliance
- [ ] All employees are within their designated pay bands
- [ ] Exceptions are documented with business justification
- [ ] Band penetration analysis shows healthy distribution
- [ ] Compression issues identified and addressed
- [ ] New hire salaries do not create internal equity issues
- [ ] Promotion increases are consistent with band progression

### Compensation Communication
- [ ] Compensation philosophy is documented and accessible
- [ ] Managers are trained to discuss compensation with employees
- [ ] Total compensation statements are provided to employees
- [ ] Pay decisions are explainable and documented
- [ ] Appeal process exists for compensation concerns

---

## SECTION 3: PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT VERIFICATION

### Review Design
- [ ] Review criteria are clear, measurable, and communicated in advance
- [ ] Self-assessment component is included
- [ ] Manager assessment uses behavioral examples (not just ratings)
- [ ] Peer feedback is incorporated (if applicable)
- [ ] Review template includes both backward-looking assessment and forward-looking development
- [ ] Rating scale is clearly defined with behavioral anchors

### Calibration
- [ ] Calibration sessions are scheduled and structured
- [ ] Calibration includes cross-team comparison
- [ ] Rating distribution is reviewed for bias patterns
- [ ] Managers justify ratings with specific examples
- [ ] Adjustments are documented with rationale
- [ ] Demographic analysis of ratings conducted for equity

### Feedback Quality
- [ ] Feedback is specific (not vague or generic)
- [ ] Feedback includes concrete examples
- [ ] Feedback is actionable (clear what to do differently)
- [ ] Feedback is balanced (strengths and development areas)
- [ ] Feedback avoids coded or biased language
- [ ] Development goals are SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)

### Low Performer Management
- [ ] Underperformance criteria are objective and documented
- [ ] Verbal feedback delivered before formal action
- [ ] Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) template is compliant and fair
- [ ] PIP goals are achievable within the specified timeframe
- [ ] Support resources are offered (coaching, training, mentoring)
- [ ] PIP process reviewed by HR and legal before delivery
- [ ] Documentation is thorough at every stage
- [ ] Outcomes are consistent across demographics

### High Performer Recognition
- [ ] Top performers are systematically identified
- [ ] Recognition is meaningful and timely
- [ ] Retention risk is assessed for top performers
- [ ] Retention plans are documented for critical talent
- [ ] Development opportunities prioritized for top performers
- [ ] Compensation reflects performance differentiation

---

## SECTION 4: DEI VERIFICATION

### Data Collection and Analysis
- [ ] Demographic data is collected at all pipeline stages
- [ ] Self-identification is voluntary and confidential
- [ ] Data analysis covers hiring, promotion, compensation, and attrition
- [ ] Intersectional analysis is conducted (not just single dimensions)
- [ ] Benchmarks are appropriate (industry, geography, available talent pool)
- [ ] Data is reviewed quarterly at minimum

### Program Design
- [ ] DEI goals are specific, measurable, and time-bound
- [ ] Initiatives address systemic issues (not just awareness)
- [ ] Budget is allocated for DEI programs
- [ ] Executive sponsor is assigned and active
- [ ] Employee feedback informs program design
- [ ] Accountability is assigned for goal achievement

### Inclusion and Belonging
- [ ] Belonging is measured through validated survey instruments
- [ ] Results are segmented by demographic group
- [ ] Gaps between groups are identified and addressed
- [ ] Inclusive leadership training is provided for all managers
- [ ] ERGs are supported with budget, time, and executive sponsorship
- [ ] Inclusive meeting and communication practices are established

### Anti-Discrimination and Anti-Harassment
- [ ] Anti-discrimination policy is current and compliant
- [ ] Anti-harassment training completed by all employees
- [ ] Reporting mechanisms are accessible and confidential
- [ ] Investigation process is documented, fair, and timely
- [ ] Retaliation protections are clear and enforced
- [ ] Investigation outcomes are consistent and documented

---

## SECTION 5: POLICY VERIFICATION

### Policy Development
- [ ] Policy addresses a clear business or legal need
- [ ] Policy is compliant with all applicable federal, state, and local laws
- [ ] Policy is reviewed by legal counsel
- [ ] Policy uses clear, accessible language
- [ ] Policy has an effective date and review schedule
- [ ] Policy includes scope (who it applies to)
- [ ] Policy includes definitions for key terms
- [ ] Exceptions and escalation processes are defined

### Policy Communication
- [ ] Policy is accessible to all affected employees
- [ ] Communication plan includes rollout, Q&A, and training (if needed)
- [ ] Managers are briefed on the policy before employee communication
- [ ] Acknowledgment of receipt is collected
- [ ] Translations provided if workforce requires them
- [ ] Policy is added to employee handbook and/or intranet

### Policy Compliance
- [ ] Compliance monitoring mechanism is defined
- [ ] Violation consequences are proportionate and documented
- [ ] Policy is reviewed annually (at minimum) for currency
- [ ] Multi-state compliance is verified (if applicable)
- [ ] International compliance is verified (if applicable)

---

## SECTION 6: LEGAL COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION

### Employment Law
- [ ] All applicable federal employment laws are addressed (FLSA, FMLA, ADA, Title VII, ADEA, etc.)
- [ ] State-specific employment laws are addressed for all operating states
- [ ] Local ordinances are addressed (city/county requirements)
- [ ] Employment agreements are compliant and current
- [ ] At-will employment status is clear (where applicable)
- [ ] Worker classification (employee vs. contractor) is correct

### Required Postings and Notices
- [ ] Federal labor law postings are current and displayed (physical and/or electronic)
- [ ] State labor law postings are current for all operating states
- [ ] New hire notices provided as required by state law
- [ ] Pay transparency requirements met
- [ ] E-Verify compliance maintained (if applicable)

### Record Keeping
- [ ] Employee records are maintained for required retention periods
- [ ] I-9 forms are completed and stored correctly
- [ ] Payroll records are accurate and compliant
- [ ] Benefits enrollment records are maintained
- [ ] Termination records include all required documentation
- [ ] Confidentiality of employee records is maintained

### Leave Administration
- [ ] FMLA eligibility is correctly determined
- [ ] State and local leave laws are mapped and complied with
- [ ] Leave requests are processed within required timeframes
- [ ] Return-to-work procedures are compliant
- [ ] Intermittent leave is tracked correctly
- [ ] ADA interactive process is followed for accommodation requests

---

## SECTION 7: ORGANIZATIONAL DESIGN VERIFICATION

### Structure Design
- [ ] Org design aligns with business strategy
- [ ] Spans of control are appropriate for the work type
- [ ] Layers are minimized for the organization size
- [ ] Roles are clearly defined with documented responsibilities
- [ ] Reporting lines are logical and minimize matrix complexity
- [ ] Decision rights are clearly allocated

### Change Management
- [ ] Business case for the change is documented
- [ ] Impact assessment completed (who is affected and how)
- [ ] Communication plan developed and stakeholder-appropriate
- [ ] Timeline is realistic with milestones
- [ ] Manager enablement plan included
- [ ] Employee support resources identified (coaching, career counseling)
- [ ] Success metrics defined for the change
- [ ] Rollback plan identified if change is not working

### Workforce Planning
- [ ] Headcount plan aligns with budget and business plan
- [ ] Critical role identification is current
- [ ] Succession plans cover critical roles
- [ ] Skill gap analysis informs hiring and development plans
- [ ] Contingent workforce strategy is defined
- [ ] Scenario planning covers growth, flat, and reduction scenarios

---

## SECTION 8: DOCUMENTATION VERIFICATION

### Required Documentation
- [ ] All HR decisions are documented with rationale
- [ ] Conversations about performance are documented contemporaneously
- [ ] Investigation notes are thorough and objective
- [ ] Accommodation requests and interactive process are documented
- [ ] Policy acknowledgments are collected and stored
- [ ] Training completions are recorded

### Documentation Quality
- [ ] Documentation is factual and objective (no opinions or characterizations)
- [ ] Documentation is specific (dates, times, behaviors, impacts)
- [ ] Documentation is timely (created close to the event)
- [ ] Documentation is consistent in format and thoroughness
- [ ] Documentation avoids protected-class references unless legally relevant
- [ ] Documentation would withstand scrutiny in litigation

---

## SECTION 9: EMPLOYEE EXPERIENCE VERIFICATION

### Communication
- [ ] Changes are communicated with appropriate advance notice
- [ ] Communication is clear, honest, and empathetic
- [ ] Multiple communication channels are used (email, meeting, documentation)
- [ ] Manager talking points are provided for sensitive topics
- [ ] Q&A forum is available for employee questions
- [ ] Follow-up communication is planned

### Support
- [ ] Employee Assistance Program (EAP) information is accessible
- [ ] Manager support resources are available for difficult conversations
- [ ] Career transition support is provided when applicable
- [ ] Feedback mechanisms exist for employees to share concerns
- [ ] HR is accessible and responsive to employee inquiries

# HR Score — Quality Enforcement (Authoritative)

This document defines how Human Resources quality is evaluated.
Every HR initiative must be scored before it is considered complete.

If quality is not measurable, it is not enforced.

---

## SCORING RULES (MANDATORY)

Each initiative must be scored across the following dimensions.
Score each category from **1 (poor)** to **5 (excellent)**.

### Hard Fail Dimensions
These dimensions are critical. Score <3 = immediate remediation required:
- **Hiring Quality**
- **Legal Compliance**
- **DEI Progress**

### Passing Criteria
- Average score across all dimensions >= 4.0
- No hard-fail dimension < 3
- No dimension < 2
- Legal compliance is never negotiable

### Weighting
Hiring Quality and Legal Compliance carry 1.5x weight in the final average.
Hiring determines organizational capability; legal compliance determines
organizational survival.

## 1. HIRING QUALITY

**Question:**
Are we hiring the right people, fast enough, with a good experience for candidates?

### What to Evaluate
- Time-to-fill: days from requisition to accepted offer
- Offer acceptance rate: percentage of offers accepted
- 90-day retention: percentage of new hires still employed at 90 days
- Quality of hire: manager satisfaction at 6 months and 12 months
- Candidate experience: NPS from all candidates (including rejected)
- Source effectiveness: which channels produce the best hires?
- Pipeline conversion rates: stage-by-stage funnel analysis
- Hiring manager satisfaction: are managers getting the talent they need?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Time-to-fill < 30 days. Offer acceptance > 90%. 90-day retention > 95%.
        Quality of hire (manager rating) > 4.5/5 at 6 months. Candidate NPS > 60.
        Source channels optimized. Pipeline conversion rates exceed benchmarks.
        Hiring managers report full confidence in talent quality.
- **4** — Time-to-fill < 45 days. Offer acceptance > 85%. 90-day retention > 90%.
        Quality of hire > 4.0/5. Candidate NPS > 40. Sources mostly optimized.
        Good pipeline conversion. Hiring managers mostly satisfied.
- **3** — Time-to-fill < 60 days. Offer acceptance > 75%. 90-day retention > 85%.
        Quality of hire > 3.5/5. Candidate NPS neutral. Sources tracked but
        not optimized. Average pipeline conversion. Some hiring manager concerns.
- **2** — Time-to-fill > 60 days. Offer acceptance < 75%. 90-day retention < 85%.
        Quality of hire below expectations. Candidate NPS negative. Sources
        not tracked. Poor pipeline conversion. Hiring managers dissatisfied.
- **1** — Hiring is broken. Positions unfilled for months. Offers frequently
        declined. New hires leave within weeks. No quality measurement.
        Terrible candidate experience. No pipeline visibility.

### Hard Fail Criteria
- Score < 3 = **IMMEDIATE REMEDIATION REQUIRED**
- 90-day retention < 80% = automatic score cap at 2
- No structured interview process = automatic score cap at 2
- No candidate experience measurement = deduct 1 point
- Offer-to-acceptance cycle > 7 days = deduct 1 point

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Time-to-fill | < 35 days | ATS reporting |
| Offer acceptance rate | > 88% | ATS reporting |
| 90-day retention | > 93% | HRIS tracking |
| Quality of hire (6-month) | > 4.2/5 | Manager survey |
| Candidate NPS | > 50 | Post-process survey (all candidates) |
| Source effectiveness | ROI positive | Source attribution analysis |
| Pipeline conversion | Benchmark+ | ATS funnel analytics |
| Time-to-productivity | < 60 days | Manager assessment |

## 2. CULTURE HEALTH

**Question:**
Is the organization's culture healthy, measurable, and actively maintained?

### What to Evaluate
- Employee engagement scores: overall engagement and dimension-specific scores
- eNPS (employee Net Promoter Score): trend over time, not just point-in-time
- Voluntary turnover: rate and whether departures are regrettable or non-regrettable
- Stay interview insights: why do top performers stay?
- Culture consistency: is the lived culture consistent with stated values?
- Psychological safety: do employees feel safe to speak up, disagree, and fail?
- Manager quality: are managers building healthy teams?
- Cross-functional collaboration: are teams working well together?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Engagement score > 80th percentile (industry benchmark). eNPS > 50 and
        trending up. Voluntary turnover < 8% (regrettable < 3%). Stay interviews
        reveal genuine commitment. Culture is consistent and lived daily.
        Psychological safety scores high. Managers rated > 4.5/5.
- **4** — Engagement > 65th percentile. eNPS > 30 and stable. Voluntary turnover
        < 12% (regrettable < 5%). Culture mostly consistent. Psychological
        safety mostly good. Managers rated > 4.0/5.
- **3** — Engagement at 50th percentile. eNPS 10-30. Voluntary turnover 12-18%.
        Culture has inconsistencies. Psychological safety uneven. Manager
        quality varies significantly.
- **2** — Engagement below 50th percentile. eNPS < 10 or declining. Voluntary
        turnover 18-25%. Culture issues visible. Psychological safety
        concerns. Multiple manager issues.
- **1** — Engagement critically low. eNPS negative. Voluntary turnover > 25%.
        Culture is toxic or undefined. No psychological safety. Manager
        quality is a systemic problem.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Engagement score | > 70th percentile | Annual survey + pulse surveys |
| eNPS | > 40 | Quarterly pulse |
| Voluntary turnover | < 10% | HRIS reporting |
| Regrettable turnover | < 4% | Manager-tagged departures |
| Psychological safety | > 4.0/5 | Survey dimension |
| Manager effectiveness | > 4.2/5 | Skip-level + upward feedback |
| Values alignment | > 85% agreement | Culture survey |
| Stay interview completion | > 80% of top performers | Quarterly tracking |

## 3. COMPENSATION COMPETITIVENESS

**Question:**
Is compensation positioned to attract and retain talent while maintaining equity?

### What to Evaluate
- Market positioning accuracy: is compensation data current and correctly applied?
- Pay equity: are there unexplained pay gaps by gender, race, or other protected class?
- Total compensation design: is the total package (base + equity + benefits) competitive?
- Compensation philosophy clarity: does everyone understand how pay is determined?
- Pay band adherence: are employees within their designated pay bands?
- Equity refresh program: are retention-critical employees receiving competitive equity?
- Benefits benchmarking: are benefits competitive for the talent market?
- Geographic pay strategy: is remote/distributed compensation handled consistently?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Market data refreshed quarterly. Pay equity audit shows < 2% unexplained
        gap. Total comp above 65th percentile for target roles. Philosophy
        documented and communicated. 100% within pay bands. Equity refresh
        program active. Benefits above market. Geographic strategy clear.
- **4** — Market data refreshed semi-annually. Pay equity gap < 5%. Total comp
        at 50th-65th percentile. Philosophy documented. > 95% within bands.
        Equity refresh exists. Benefits competitive. Geographic strategy exists.
- **3** — Market data refreshed annually. Pay equity gap 5-10% or not measured.
        Total comp at 50th percentile. Philosophy informal. > 90% within bands.
        No formal equity refresh. Benefits adequate. Geographic ad hoc.
- **2** — Market data outdated (> 1 year). Pay equity not measured. Total comp
        below 50th percentile for key roles. No compensation philosophy.
        Significant band violations. No equity refresh. Benefits below market.
- **1** — No market data. No pay equity analysis. Compensation is arbitrary.
        No pay bands. No equity program. Benefits are minimal. Pay decisions
        are subjective and inconsistent.

### Hard Fail Note
Pay equity is a component of Legal Compliance and DEI Progress. A pay equity
audit showing material unexplained gaps triggers hard fails in those dimensions
as well.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Market data freshness | < 6 months old | Vendor report date |
| Pay equity gap (unexplained) | < 3% | Annual statistical analysis |
| Market positioning | 50th-75th percentile | Comp benchmarking |
| Band compliance | > 97% | HRIS audit |
| Offer competitiveness | > 85% acceptance | Offer analytics |
| Equity coverage | 100% eligible employees | Equity plan audit |
| Benefits satisfaction | > 4.0/5 | Employee survey |
| Comp philosophy awareness | > 90% | Employee survey |

## 4. PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

**Question:**
Does the performance management system drive growth, accountability, and fairness?

### What to Evaluate
- Review completion rate: are reviews completed on time?
- Calibration consistency: are ratings consistent across managers and teams?
- Actionable feedback quality: do reviews include specific, actionable feedback?
- Goal setting quality: are goals SMART and aligned with business objectives?
- Development focus: do reviews focus on growth, not just evaluation?
- Manager competence: are managers trained and effective at performance conversations?
- Low performer management: are underperformers identified and supported?
- High performer recognition: are top performers identified and retained?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Review completion > 98% on time. Calibration shows < 5% adjustment.
        Feedback rated as actionable by > 90% of employees. Goals are SMART
        with clear business alignment. Development plans for all employees.
        Managers trained and effective. Clear PIP process. Top performers
        identified and retention-planned.
- **4** — Review completion > 95%. Calibration shows < 10% adjustment. Feedback
        mostly actionable. Goals mostly SMART. Development plans for most.
        Managers mostly trained. PIP process exists. Top performers mostly
        identified.
- **3** — Review completion > 85%. Calibration shows significant adjustments.
        Feedback quality inconsistent. Goals set but quality varies. Development
        plans inconsistent. Manager training gaps. PIP process informal.
        Top performer identification inconsistent.
- **2** — Review completion < 85%. No calibration. Feedback is generic. Goals
        unclear. No development plans. Managers untrained. No PIP process.
        No systematic top performer identification.
- **1** — Reviews not completed or perfunctory. No calibration. No meaningful
        feedback. No goals. No development. No manager training. No
        performance differentiation.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Review completion (on time) | > 97% | HRIS tracking |
| Calibration adjustment rate | < 7% | Calibration audit |
| Feedback actionability | > 85% | Employee survey |
| Goal completion rate | > 80% | Performance system |
| Development plan completion | > 75% | IDP tracking |
| Manager effectiveness (reviews) | > 4.0/5 | Employee feedback |
| PIP success rate | > 40% | PIP outcome tracking |
| Top performer retention | > 95% | 12-month retention |

## 5. DEI PROGRESS

**Question:**
Is the organization making measurable progress on diversity, equity, inclusion, and belonging?

### What to Evaluate
- Pipeline diversity: are candidate pools representative at each funnel stage?
- Hiring diversity: are hire rates proportional across demographics?
- Promotion parity: are promotion rates equitable across demographics?
- Pay equity: are there unexplained compensation gaps? (cross-reference dim 3)
- Belonging scores: do all demographics report similar belonging levels?
- Retention equity: are attrition rates similar across demographics?
- Leadership representation: does leadership reflect organizational diversity?
- ERG health: are employee resource groups active and supported?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Pipeline diversity exceeds market availability. Hire rates proportional.
        Promotion rates equitable (within 5%). Pay equity gap < 2%.
        Belonging scores similar across demographics (within 0.3 points).
        Retention equitable. Leadership reflects workforce. ERGs active
        with executive sponsors and budget.
- **4** — Pipeline diversity meets market availability. Hire rates mostly
        proportional. Promotion rates mostly equitable (within 10%).
        Pay equity gap < 5%. Belonging scores mostly similar. Retention
        mostly equitable. Leadership trending toward representation.
        ERGs active.
- **3** — Pipeline diversity below market availability in some dimensions.
        Hire rate gaps. Promotion rate gaps. Pay equity gap 5-10%.
        Belonging score gaps. Retention gaps. Leadership not representative.
        ERGs exist but under-resourced.
- **2** — Pipeline diversity not tracked or significantly below availability.
        Significant hire and promotion rate gaps. Pay equity not measured.
        Belonging not measured. Retention gaps significant. No leadership
        diversity focus. No ERGs.
- **1** — No diversity data collected. No equity analysis. No inclusion
        measurement. No DEI program. No accountability.

### Hard Fail Criteria
- Score < 3 = **IMMEDIATE REMEDIATION REQUIRED**
- No demographic data collection = automatic score cap at 1
- Unexplained pay equity gap > 10% = automatic score cap at 2
- No anti-discrimination training in 12 months = deduct 1 point
- Retaliation complaint substantiated = automatic score of 1

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Pipeline diversity | >= market availability | Applicant tracking |
| Hiring rate parity | Within 5% across demographics | Hire rate analysis |
| Promotion rate parity | Within 5% across demographics | Promotion analysis |
| Pay equity gap | < 3% unexplained | Statistical analysis |
| Belonging score parity | Within 0.3 points | Survey analysis |
| Retention parity | Within 3% across demographics | Attrition analysis |
| Leadership representation | Reflects workforce | Annual composition report |
| ERG participation | > 30% of employees | ERG membership tracking |

## 6. LEGAL COMPLIANCE

**Question:**
Is the organization fully compliant with employment law and free from regulatory risk?

### What to Evaluate
- Regulatory violation count: zero tolerance for substantiated violations
- Training completion: are required trainings completed on time?
- Policy currency: are policies up to date with current law?
- Documentation: are employment decisions properly documented?
- Classification accuracy: are employees/contractors correctly classified?
- Leave administration: are leave requests handled compliantly?
- Termination defensibility: are terminations legally defensible?
- Audit readiness: could the organization pass a regulatory audit today?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Zero regulatory violations. 100% training completion. All policies
        current. Documentation is thorough and consistent. Classification
        audit clean. Leave administration flawless. Terminations are
        well-documented and legally reviewed. Audit-ready at all times.
- **4** — Zero substantiated violations. > 95% training completion. Policies
        mostly current. Documentation good with minor gaps. Classification
        mostly correct. Leave administration good. Terminations documented.
        Mostly audit-ready.
- **3** — Minor compliance issues (no substantiated violations). > 85% training.
        Some policies outdated. Documentation inconsistent. Some classification
        questions. Leave administration has gaps. Termination documentation
        varies. Audit preparation needed.
- **2** — Compliance issues present. Training below 85%. Policies significantly
        outdated. Poor documentation. Classification issues identified.
        Leave administration problems. Terminations risky. Not audit-ready.
- **1** — Active compliance violations. Training not tracked. Policies not
        maintained. No documentation culture. Classification violations.
        Leave administration non-compliant. Terminations indefensible.
        Would fail an audit.

### Hard Fail Criteria
- Score < 3 = **IMMEDIATE REMEDIATION REQUIRED**
- Any substantiated regulatory violation = automatic score of 1
- Harassment/discrimination complaint mishandled = automatic score of 1
- Missing mandatory training (harassment, safety) = automatic score cap at 2
- Worker misclassification identified = deduct 2 points

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Regulatory violations | 0 | Compliance log |
| Training completion | 100% | LMS reporting |
| Policy review cadence | Annual minimum | Policy audit |
| I-9 compliance | 100% | Quarterly audit |
| Classification accuracy | 100% | Annual audit |
| Leave compliance | Zero errors | Leave administration audit |
| Termination review rate | 100% reviewed | HR process tracking |
| EEOC/DOL inquiry rate | 0 | External inquiry log |

## 7. EMPLOYEE DEVELOPMENT

**Question:**
Are employees growing their skills, advancing their careers, and closing skill gaps?

### What to Evaluate
- Individual Development Plan (IDP) completion: do employees have active development plans?
- Internal mobility rate: are employees moving into new roles and growing?
- Skill gap closure: are identified skill gaps being addressed?
- Training effectiveness: do training programs produce measurable skill improvement?
- Career pathing: are career paths documented and understood?
- Mentoring programs: are mentoring relationships active and effective?
- Learning culture: do employees feel supported in their development?
- Succession planning: are key roles covered with identified successors?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — IDP completion > 90%. Internal mobility > 20%. Skill gaps tracked
        and closure rate > 70%. Training ROI measured and positive. Career
        paths documented for all roles. Active mentoring program. Learning
        culture rated > 4.5/5. Succession plans for all critical roles.
- **4** — IDP completion > 75%. Internal mobility > 15%. Skill gap closure
        > 50%. Training effectiveness measured. Career paths for most roles.
        Mentoring program exists. Learning culture rated > 4.0/5. Succession
        plans for most critical roles.
- **3** — IDP completion > 50%. Internal mobility > 10%. Skill gaps identified
        but closure inconsistent. Training effectiveness unclear. Career
        paths for some roles. Informal mentoring. Learning culture average.
        Succession plans for few roles.
- **2** — IDP completion < 50%. Internal mobility < 10%. Skill gaps not
        tracked. Training is check-the-box. No career paths. No mentoring.
        Learning culture poor. No succession planning.
- **1** — No IDPs. No internal mobility. No skill tracking. No meaningful
        training. No career pathing. No mentoring. No learning culture.
        No succession planning.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| IDP completion rate | > 80% | HRIS/Performance system |
| Internal mobility rate | > 15% annually | HRIS tracking |
| Skill gap closure rate | > 60% | Skill assessment delta |
| Training hours per employee | > 40 hours/year | LMS tracking |
| Training effectiveness | > 4.0/5 | Post-training assessment |
| Career path awareness | > 85% | Employee survey |
| Mentoring participation | > 40% | Program tracking |
| Succession coverage | > 80% of critical roles | Succession planning audit |

## 8. ORGANIZATIONAL EFFECTIVENESS

**Question:**
Is the organization structured for effectiveness, with the right spans, layers, and capabilities?

### What to Evaluate
- Span of control: are manager-to-IC ratios appropriate for the work type?
- Organizational layers: are there too many or too few layers?
- Manager effectiveness: are managers driving team performance?
- Team health: are teams functioning well with clear accountability?
- Decision-making speed: are decisions made at the right level, fast enough?
- Role clarity: do employees understand their responsibilities and authority?
- Cross-functional alignment: do teams collaborate effectively?
- Organizational agility: can the org adapt to change quickly?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Spans of control are intentional and appropriate (5-9 for most teams).
        Layers are minimal (< 5 for < 500 people). Manager effectiveness
        rated > 4.5/5. Team health scores > 4.5/5. Decisions made quickly
        at appropriate levels. Role clarity > 95%. Cross-functional
        collaboration rated highly. Organization adapts to change in weeks.
- **4** — Spans mostly appropriate. Layers reasonable. Manager effectiveness
        > 4.0/5. Team health > 4.0/5. Decision speed mostly good. Role
        clarity > 85%. Collaboration mostly effective. Adapts in 1-2 months.
- **3** — Spans inconsistent. Some unnecessary layers. Manager effectiveness
        varies. Team health mixed. Decision bottlenecks exist. Role clarity
        gaps. Collaboration challenges. Adapts in 2-3 months.
- **2** — Spans too wide or too narrow. Too many layers. Manager effectiveness
        concerns. Team health problems. Decisions slow. Role confusion.
        Silos. Slow to adapt.
- **1** — Organizational structure is dysfunctional. Inappropriate spans.
        Excessive bureaucracy. Manager quality crisis. Teams are unhealthy.
        Decisions are paralyzed. Roles are unclear. No collaboration. Rigid.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Average span of control | 5-9 (team-dependent) | HRIS org analysis |
| Org layers | < 5 (for < 500 headcount) | Org chart analysis |
| Manager effectiveness | > 4.2/5 | Upward feedback + skip-level |
| Team health score | > 4.2/5 | Team health survey |
| Decision cycle time | < 1 week for operational | Process tracking |
| Role clarity | > 90% | Employee survey |
| Cross-functional NPS | > 30 | Internal survey |
| Org change adoption rate | > 80% within 30 days | Change management tracking |

## FINAL HR SCORE DECISION

**Hard Fail Dimensions (Hiring Quality, Legal Compliance, DEI Progress):**
- Score < 3 = **IMMEDIATE REMEDIATION REQUIRED**

**All Dimensions:**
- Weighted average score >= 4.0 = **INITIATIVE MAY SHIP**
- Weighted average score < 4.0 = **REMEDIATION REQUIRED**

**Legal Compliance:**
- Zero tolerance for substantiated violations
- Score < 3 triggers immediate executive notification

Scores must be stated explicitly before final output.

# Employee Relations -- Investigations, Discipline, and Workplace Safety

## Purpose

This module defines the framework for managing employee relations issues -- the complaints, conflicts, misconduct, and workplace safety concerns that arise in every organization. Employee relations (ER) is the highest-stakes area of HR operations: mistakes can result in legal liability, regulatory penalties, reputation damage, and most importantly, harm to employees. The HR Brain mandates thorough, impartial, and well-documented processes for all ER matters.

---

## 1. Investigation Process

### 1.1 When an Investigation Is Required

An investigation must be initiated when the organization receives a complaint, report, or becomes aware of potential:

- Harassment (sexual, racial, or based on any protected characteristic)
- Discrimination in any employment decision
- Retaliation against an employee who has engaged in protected activity
- Policy violations (code of conduct, attendance, substance abuse, theft, fraud)
- Safety violations or workplace hazards
- Threats of violence or intimidation
- Whistleblower complaints
- Ethical violations (conflicts of interest, data breaches, regulatory violations)

### 1.2 Investigation Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Promptness** | Begin investigation within 24-48 hours of complaint receipt |
| **Impartiality** | Investigator must be neutral; no reporting relationship with complainant or respondent |
| **Thoroughness** | Interview all relevant witnesses; review all relevant documents and evidence |
| **Confidentiality** | Share information only on a need-to-know basis; promise to maintain confidentiality to the extent possible (cannot guarantee absolute confidentiality) |
| **Documentation** | Document every step: complaint receipt, interviews, evidence collected, analysis, findings, and actions |
| **Non-retaliation** | Explicitly communicate anti-retaliation protections to all participants |
| **Due process** | Respondent must have the opportunity to respond to allegations |

### 1.3 Investigation Process Framework

```
PHASE 1: INTAKE (Day 0-1)
  - Receive complaint (verbal, written, hotline, manager report)
  - Document the complaint using standardized intake form
  - Assess severity and urgency
  - Determine if interim measures are needed:
    * Separation of parties (reassignment, remote work)
    * Leave of absence for respondent (paid, pending investigation)
    * Safety measures (security, access restrictions)
  - Assign investigator (internal HR/ER, or external for senior leaders/sensitive cases)
  - Notify legal counsel (mandatory for harassment, discrimination, retaliation allegations)

PHASE 2: PLANNING (Day 1-3)
  - Define scope of investigation (specific allegations to investigate)
  - Identify witnesses to interview
  - Identify documents and evidence to collect
  - Prepare interview questions
  - Establish timeline (target: complete within 10-15 business days for standard cases)

PHASE 3: FACT-FINDING (Day 3-12)
  - Interview complainant (detailed, open-ended questions)
  - Collect and preserve evidence (emails, messages, documents, camera footage, access logs)
  - Interview witnesses (independently; do not share others' statements)
  - Interview respondent (present specific allegations; allow full response)
  - Follow-up interviews as needed to resolve contradictions
  - All interviews documented in detailed notes (not recordings, unless legally required)

PHASE 4: ANALYSIS AND FINDINGS (Day 12-15)
  - Assess credibility of each party and witness:
    * Consistency (internal and with other evidence)
    * Corroboration (does other evidence support the account?)
    * Demeanor (note observations but do not over-weight)
    * Motive (any reason to fabricate or minimize?)
    * Plausibility (does the account make logical sense?)
  - Apply relevant policies and legal standards
  - Determine findings for each allegation:
    * SUSTAINED: Evidence supports the allegation by a preponderance
    * NOT SUSTAINED: Evidence is insufficient to determine
    * UNFOUNDED: Evidence affirmatively contradicts the allegation

PHASE 5: RECOMMENDATIONS AND ACTION (Day 15-20)
  - Recommend appropriate corrective action based on findings
  - Corrective action proportional to severity and policy
  - Consult with legal counsel before finalizing
  - Obtain approval from appropriate level of management
  - Implement corrective action
  - Communicate outcome to complainant (what was found and what action was taken;
    do not disclose specific discipline of respondent)
  - Communicate outcome to respondent (findings and consequences)

PHASE 6: FOLLOW-UP (30/60/90 days)
  - Check in with complainant: Has the behavior stopped? Any retaliation?
  - Monitor respondent: Is corrective action being followed?
  - Document follow-up
  - Close investigation file
```

### 1.4 Investigator Selection

| Situation | Investigator |
|-----------|-------------|
| Peer-level complaint (IC-to-IC) | HRBP or ER specialist |
| Manager-to-direct report complaint | Senior HRBP or ER manager (not the HRBP for that team) |
| Director+ involved | VP of HR or external investigator |
| C-suite involved | External investigator (mandatory) |
| Pattern/systemic complaint | External investigator |
| Legal exposure significant | External investigator under attorney-client privilege |

### 1.5 Investigation Documentation

The investigation file must contain:
- Complaint intake form (date, complainant, allegations)
- Investigation plan (scope, witnesses, evidence list, timeline)
- Interview notes (for each interview: date, participants, questions asked, responses)
- Evidence collected (copies of relevant documents, communications, records)
- Credibility assessment
- Findings memorandum (analysis, conclusions, recommendations)
- Corrective action documentation
- Follow-up records
- File stored securely with restricted access (HR, legal)

## 2. Progressive Discipline

### 2.1 Progressive Discipline Framework

Progressive discipline provides employees with notice of deficiencies and opportunity to improve before termination. It is not legally required (at-will employment), but it:
- Demonstrates fairness and consistency
- Provides documentation for legal defense
- Gives employees genuine opportunity to correct behavior
- Protects against wrongful termination claims

### 2.2 Discipline Steps

```
STEP 1: VERBAL COACHING (documented)
  When: First or minor offense; performance issue just identified
  Process: Private conversation; specific issue discussed; expectation clarified
  Documentation: Manager documents in 1:1 notes (date, issue, expectation, follow-up)
  Duration: Verbal coaching is the norm for ongoing feedback; not a formal "step"

STEP 2: WRITTEN WARNING
  When: Continued issue after coaching; or moderately serious first offense
  Process: Formal meeting with manager and HRBP; written documentation signed by employee
  Content: Specific issue, prior coaching reference, expected improvement, timeline, consequences
  Employee response: Employee may submit written response to be included in file
  Duration: Typically active for 6-12 months

STEP 3: FINAL WRITTEN WARNING / PIP
  When: Continued issue after written warning; or serious first offense
  Process: Formal meeting with manager, HRBP, and possibly skip-level
  Content: Specific performance/behavior standards; measurable milestones; support provided; timeline (30-90 days); explicit statement that failure to improve may result in termination
  Duration: PIP period (30-90 days)

STEP 4: TERMINATION
  When: Failure to improve during PIP; or egregious conduct warranting immediate termination
  Process: Formal meeting with manager, HRBP, and legal review completed beforehand
  Communication: Clear, brief, respectful; avoid extended debate
  Documentation: Termination memo documenting reason, progressive discipline history, and decision rationale
```

### 2.3 Immediate Termination Offenses

Some conduct warrants termination without progressive discipline:

- Violence or credible threat of violence
- Theft, embezzlement, or fraud
- Gross insubordination (refusal to perform assigned work without legal basis)
- Illegal drug use on company premises
- Serious safety violations putting others at risk
- Harassment (severe incidents -- e.g., sexual assault)
- Violation of confidentiality with material harm
- Falsification of records or credentials

Even for immediate termination, investigate before acting (unless immediate safety risk requires same-day action). Consult legal counsel.

### 2.4 Consistency and Fairness

Before taking any disciplinary action, the HRBP must verify:

- [ ] Has this employee been given clear expectations and feedback prior to formal discipline?
- [ ] Have similarly-situated employees been treated consistently for similar conduct?
- [ ] Is the proposed action proportional to the offense?
- [ ] Is there any protected activity (recent complaint, leave request, accommodation) that could suggest retaliation?
- [ ] Has legal counsel reviewed (for terminations and any employee in a protected category)?
- [ ] Is the documentation sufficient to support the action if challenged?

## 3. Workplace Violence Prevention

### 3.1 Threat Assessment Framework

Workplace violence exists on a continuum. Early intervention prevents escalation:

```
LEVEL 1: EARLY WARNING (Verbal/Behavioral)
  Indicators: Intimidating language, verbal abuse, excessive anger,
              paranoid behavior, social isolation, fascination with weapons
  Response: Manager awareness; behavioral health referral (EAP);
            document concerns; HRBP consultation

LEVEL 2: ESCALATED THREAT
  Indicators: Direct or veiled threats ("You'll be sorry"), stalking behavior,
              property damage, increasingly erratic behavior
  Response: Formal threat assessment; security consultation; temporary separation;
            mandatory EAP assessment; document and monitor; legal review

LEVEL 3: IMMINENT DANGER
  Indicators: Possession of weapon on premises, physical altercation,
              specific threat with plan and means
  Response: Immediate safety protocol; call 911; evacuate if necessary;
            administrative leave pending investigation; external threat assessment
```

### 3.2 Workplace Violence Prevention Policy Elements

- Zero tolerance for violence, threats, and intimidation
- Reporting mechanisms (anonymous hotline, manager, HR, security)
- No retaliation for good-faith reports
- Prohibition on weapons on company premises (per applicable law)
- Threat assessment team (HR, security, legal, management)
- Restraining order support for affected employees
- EAP and behavioral health resources
- Post-incident debriefing and support

### 3.3 Domestic Violence Workplace Impact

- Domestic violence affects workplace safety and productivity
- Provide confidential EAP referral and safety planning support
- Accommodate safety needs (schedule modification, workspace relocation, parking reassignment)
- Train managers to recognize signs and refer to appropriate resources
- Do not terminate employees because they are victims of domestic violence (prohibited in many states)

## 4. Whistleblower Protections

### 4.1 Legal Framework

Multiple federal and state statutes protect employees who report suspected violations:

| Statute | Protects Reports About | Key Provisions |
|---------|----------------------|---------------|
| Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) | Securities fraud, shareholder fraud | Criminal penalties for retaliation; whistleblower bounty (SEC) |
| Dodd-Frank | Securities violations | 10-30% of sanctions over $1M as whistleblower reward |
| False Claims Act (FCA) | Fraud against the government | Qui tam provisions; whistleblower receives 15-30% of recovery |
| OSHA whistleblower statutes | Safety violations (20+ specific statutes) | Reinstatement, back pay, compensatory damages |
| State whistleblower laws | Varies by state | Often broader than federal; may cover private-sector employees |
| IRS Whistleblower | Tax fraud | 15-30% of collected proceeds for cases over $2M |

### 4.2 Internal Reporting Mechanisms

- **Anonymous hotline**: Third-party operated; 24/7; phone and web intake
- **Direct reporting**: To any manager, HR, legal, or compliance officer
- **Open-door policy**: Leadership explicitly encourages raising concerns
- **No retaliation guarantee**: Prominently communicated; enforced with consequences for violators

### 4.3 Anti-Retaliation Protections

Retaliation is the most common basis for employment discrimination charges filed with the EEOC. Protect against retaliation by:

- Training all managers on what constitutes retaliation (adverse action following protected activity)
- Documenting all employment decisions affecting complainants, witnesses, and whistleblowers
- Requiring HR approval for any adverse action involving someone who has recently engaged in protected activity
- Monitoring post-complaint treatment of complainant at 30/60/90 days
- Treating retaliation as a terminable offense for the retaliator

## 5. Conflict Resolution

### 5.1 Conflict Resolution Continuum

```
INFORMAL                                                          FORMAL
   │                                                                │
   │  Direct         Facilitated      Mediation     Investigation  │
   │  conversation   conversation     (neutral       (formal       │
   │  between        (manager or      third party)   process)      │
   │  parties        HR facilitates)                               │
   │                                                                │
   ← Try less formal first; escalate as needed →
```

### 5.2 Manager's Role in Conflict Resolution

Most workplace conflicts should be resolved at the team level by the manager:

1. **Listen to both sides** independently (understand each perspective)
2. **Identify the root issue** (task conflict? relationship conflict? process conflict?)
3. **Facilitate a direct conversation** between the parties (if both are willing)
4. **Focus on behavior and impact**, not personality or intent
5. **Agree on specific changes** and follow-up timeline
6. **Document the discussion** and agreed actions
7. **Escalate to HR** if the conflict involves allegations of harassment, discrimination, policy violations, or if manager facilitation fails

### 5.3 When to Involve HR

- Complaint alleges harassment, discrimination, or retaliation
- Conflict involves a power imbalance (manager-report relationship)
- Direct conversation has failed to resolve the issue
- Multiple employees are involved or the conflict is affecting team performance
- Employee requests HR involvement
- Any situation where legal exposure may exist

## 6. Employee Relations Metrics

### 6.1 ER Dashboard

| Metric | Formula | Target | Frequency |
|--------|---------|--------|-----------|
| Investigation volume | Total investigations opened per quarter | Track trend | Quarterly |
| Investigation cycle time | Average days from complaint to closure | <15 business days (standard) | Quarterly |
| Sustained finding rate | Sustained findings / Total investigations | Track trend (not a target; both too high and too low are concerning) | Quarterly |
| Repeat complaints (same respondent) | Respondents with 2+ complaints | 0 (every repeat is a failure) | Quarterly |
| Retaliation claims | Retaliation complaints post-investigation | 0 | Quarterly |
| Grievance rate | Total ER cases / Headcount | Track trend; compare to benchmark | Quarterly |
| Discipline by demographic | Discipline actions by demographic group | No statistically significant disparity | Annually |
| Termination by demographic | Involuntary terminations by demographic group | No statistically significant disparity | Annually |
| Post-investigation satisfaction | Complainant survey on process fairness | >3.5/5.0 | Per investigation |

## 7. Employee Relations Technology

### 7.1 ER Case Management System

| Requirement | Purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| Secure case intake (multi-channel) | Capture complaints from hotline, email, web, in-person |
| Case tracking and workflow | Track investigation steps, milestones, and deadlines |
| Document management | Securely store investigation documents and interview notes |
| Access control | Restrict access to ER cases by role; audit trail for all access |
| Reporting and analytics | Case volume, type, cycle time, findings trends |
| Integration with HRIS | Link cases to employee records for pattern detection |
| Anonymity support | Allow anonymous reporting with follow-up capability |

### 7.2 ER Technology Platforms

| Platform | Strengths | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| HR Acuity | Purpose-built ER case management; analytics; benchmarking | Mid-market to enterprise |
| EthicsPoint/NAVEX | Hotline + case management; compliance focused | Large organizations; regulated industries |
| AllVoices | Anonymous reporting; employee feedback; ER cases | Mid-market; tech-forward |
| Hive | Case management; investigation workflow | Growing ER teams |

# HR Compliance -- Multi-State, Reporting, and Audit Readiness

## Purpose

This module defines the framework for HR compliance management across federal, state, and local jurisdictions. Compliance is the non-negotiable foundation of HR operations. Failures in compliance expose the organization to lawsuits, regulatory penalties, back pay liability, and reputational damage. The HR Brain treats compliance as a system -- not a checklist -- with proactive monitoring, automated tracking, and audit readiness at all times.

---

## 1. Multi-State Compliance

### 1.1 The Multi-State Challenge

Organizations with employees in multiple states must comply with a patchwork of employment laws that vary significantly across jurisdictions. The employer must comply with federal, state, AND local law, applying the standard most favorable to the employee when they conflict.

### 1.2 Key Areas of State Variation

| Area | Federal Floor | States with Higher Standards | Compliance Approach |
|------|-------------|----------------------------|-------------------|
| **Minimum wage** | $7.25/hr (FLSA) | 30+ states; many cities (e.g., CA $16.00, WA $16.28, NYC varies by size) | Track per employee location; update annually |
| **Overtime** | >40 hrs/week at 1.5x | CA (daily overtime >8 hrs); some states exempt different roles | Apply most restrictive standard |
| **Paid sick leave** | None (federal) | 15+ states + many cities mandate paid sick leave | Comply with each applicable jurisdiction |
| **Paid family leave** | None (FMLA is unpaid) | CA, NJ, NY, WA, MA, CT, CO, OR, MD, DE, MN | State-specific programs; payroll tax withholding |
| **Meal and rest breaks** | None (FLSA) | CA (30-min meal every 5 hrs; 10-min rest every 4 hrs); many states have requirements | Strictest applicable standard |
| **Pay transparency** | None (federal) | CO, CA, WA, NY, CT, RI, and expanding | Include salary range in postings for applicable locations |
| **Salary history ban** | None (federal) | 20+ states and cities | Default to not asking everywhere (simplest compliance) |
| **Ban-the-box** | Federal contractors only | 35+ states and cities | Defer criminal history inquiry until conditional offer |
| **Harassment training** | No mandate (but advisable) | CA, CT, DE, IL, ME, NY (state-specific content and frequency) | Track by employee location; automate assignment |
| **Non-compete agreements** | No federal ban (FTC rule pending) | CA (banned), CO, IL, OR, WA, MN (restricted) | Legal review for each applicable state |
| **Cannabis protections** | None (federal; remains Schedule I) | NY, NJ, CA, CT, MT, and others protect off-duty use | Review drug testing policy per state |
| **Predictive scheduling** | None (federal) | OR, NYC, Chicago, Seattle, Philadelphia, San Francisco | Comply in applicable jurisdictions |

### 1.3 Multi-State Compliance Framework

```
COMPLIANCE MANAGEMENT APPROACH:

1. INVENTORY: Maintain a roster of all states and localities where employees work
   (update monthly; remote employees create new jurisdictions)

2. MONITOR: Subscribe to legislative tracking service (Littler, Jackson Lewis,
   SHRM, or employment law firm updates) for all active jurisdictions

3. ASSESS: For each new law or regulation:
   - Does it apply to our organization? (size, industry, location thresholds)
   - What changes are required? (policy, practice, system, training)
   - What is the effective date and compliance deadline?
   - What is the penalty for non-compliance?

4. IMPLEMENT: Make required changes before effective date:
   - Policy updates drafted and reviewed by counsel
   - System configurations updated (HRIS, payroll, benefits)
   - Manager and employee communications distributed
   - Training delivered (if required)

5. AUDIT: Verify compliance through regular self-audits:
   - Quarterly compliance spot-checks
   - Annual comprehensive compliance audit
   - Post-implementation verification for new requirements
```

## 2. EEO-1 Reporting

### 2.1 Who Must File

All private employers with 100+ employees AND all federal contractors with 50+ employees and $50,000+ in contracts must file the EEO-1 Component 1 report annually with the EEOC.

### 2.2 Report Structure

The EEO-1 reports workforce demographics (race/ethnicity and sex) by job category:

| EEO-1 Job Category | Typical Roles |
|-------------------|---------------|
| 1.1 Executive/Senior Officials | CEO, CFO, CTO, VP, SVP |
| 1.2 First/Mid-Level Officials and Managers | Directors, managers, supervisors |
| 2 Professionals | Engineers, analysts, accountants, lawyers, HR professionals |
| 3 Technicians | IT support, lab technicians, paralegals |
| 4 Sales Workers | Account executives, sales representatives |
| 5 Administrative Support | Administrative assistants, receptionists, clerks |
| 6 Craft Workers | Electricians, carpenters, mechanics |
| 7 Operatives | Machine operators, assemblers, drivers |
| 8 Laborers and Helpers | Material handlers, construction laborers |
| 9 Service Workers | Janitors, security guards, food service |

### 2.3 EEO-1 Data Collection

- Race/ethnicity and gender data is self-identified by employees
- Self-identification is voluntary (but strongly encouraged)
- If employee declines, employer may determine based on visual observation or records (last resort)
- Separate from I-9 or any other form; clearly labeled as voluntary and confidential
- Categories: Hispanic/Latino; White; Black/African American; Native Hawaiian/Pacific Islander; Asian; American Indian/Alaska Native; Two or More Races

### 2.4 Filing Process

1. Register at EEO-1 Component 1 online filing system
2. Select snapshot date (consistent each year; typically December 31 or last payroll period)
3. Upload data or enter manually
4. Review and certify
5. File by deadline (typically March-May; check annual notice)
6. Retain filed reports indefinitely

## 3. ACA Compliance

### 3.1 Applicable Large Employer (ALE) Determination

An ALE has 50+ full-time equivalent employees in the prior calendar year:

```
FTE calculation:
  Full-time employees (30+ hours/week): Count each as 1
  Part-time employees: Sum their monthly hours / 120

  Example:
  40 full-time employees (30+ hrs/week) = 40 FTEs
  20 part-time employees averaging 60 hrs/month each = (20 x 60) / 120 = 10 FTEs
  Total: 40 + 10 = 50 FTEs → ALE threshold met
```

### 3.2 Employer Shared Responsibility (Employer Mandate)

ALEs must offer minimum essential coverage that is:
- **Affordable**: Employee-only premium < 8.39% of employee's household income (safe harbor: W-2, rate of pay, or federal poverty line)
- **Minimum value**: Plan pays at least 60% of allowed costs

### 3.3 ACA Reporting (Forms 1094-C and 1095-C)

| Form | Purpose | Distribution | Filing with IRS |
|------|---------|-------------|----------------|
| 1095-C | Individual employee coverage statement | To each full-time employee by January 31 | Included with 1094-C |
| 1094-C | Transmittal form (summary of all 1095-Cs) | N/A (IRS filing only) | By February 28 (paper) or March 31 (electronic) |

**Common compliance errors:**
- Failing to track variable-hour employees across measurement periods
- Using incorrect safe harbor for affordability
- Miscoding offer of coverage (Line 14) or safe harbor (Line 16) on 1095-C
- Missing distribution deadlines to employees

## 4. COBRA Administration

### 4.1 COBRA Requirements Summary

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Applies to | Employers with 20+ employees on prior year's typical business day |
| Duration | 18 months (termination/hours reduction); 36 months (death, divorce, Medicare, dependent aging out) |
| Premium | Up to 102% of full premium (employer + employee share + 2% admin) |
| Qualifying events | Termination (except gross misconduct), hours reduction, employee death, divorce/legal separation, Medicare eligibility, dependent losing eligibility |
| Notice | Initial COBRA notice within 90 days of coverage start; qualifying event notice within 44 days |
| Election | 60 days from later of notice or loss of coverage |
| Payment | 45 days for initial premium; 30 days for subsequent premiums |

### 4.2 COBRA Administration Workflow

```
QUALIFYING EVENT (e.g., termination)
  │
  ├── Day 0: Qualifying event occurs
  │
  ├── Day 0-30: Employer notifies COBRA administrator (or plan administrator)
  │             of qualifying event
  │
  ├── Day 0-44: COBRA election notice mailed to qualified beneficiary
  │             (within 14 days of administrator receiving notice of event)
  │
  ├── Day 44-104: Qualified beneficiary has 60 days to elect COBRA
  │
  ├── If elected: Coverage retroactive to date of qualifying event
  │               First payment due within 45 days of election
  │               Subsequent payments due within 30-day grace period
  │
  └── If not elected: Coverage terminates as of qualifying event date
```

## 5. OSHA Compliance

### 5.1 General Duty Clause

Under Section 5(a)(1) of the OSH Act, every employer must provide a workplace "free from recognized hazards that are causing or are likely to cause death or serious physical harm." This applies even when no specific OSHA standard addresses the hazard.

### 5.2 Key OSHA Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|--------|
| Hazard communication | Written program; safety data sheets (SDS); employee training on chemical hazards |
| Injury and illness recordkeeping | OSHA 300 log (recordable incidents); 300A summary (posted Feb 1 - April 30); 301 incident reports |
| Electronic reporting | Establishments with 250+ employees OR high-risk industries with 20+ must electronically submit injury data |
| Emergency action plan | Written plan for evacuations, emergency response, contact information |
| PPE | Provide appropriate personal protective equipment at no cost to employees |
| Training | Job-specific safety training; document completion |
| Inspections | Right of entry for OSHA inspectors; employees can request inspection; anti-retaliation for reporting |

### 5.3 OSHA Recordkeeping

**Recordable incidents** (must be logged on OSHA 300):
- Death
- Days away from work
- Restricted work or job transfer
- Medical treatment beyond first aid
- Loss of consciousness
- Significant injury or illness diagnosed by physician

**NOT recordable**:
- First aid treatment only
- Over-the-counter medication at non-prescription strength
- Cleaning, flushing, or soaking wounds

### 5.4 Remote Worker Safety

For remote/hybrid workers, OSHA's approach is:
- Employers are NOT required to inspect home offices
- Employers are NOT responsible for hazards outside the work area at home
- Work-related injuries that occur in the home office ARE recordable
- Provide ergonomic guidance and equipment stipends as preventive measure

## 6. Record Retention

### 6.1 Comprehensive Retention Schedule

| Record Category | Retention Period | Authority |
|----------------|:---:|-----------|
| Job applications and resumes (not hired) | 1 year (2 years for federal contractors) | EEOC, OFCCP |
| Hiring records (selected candidate) | 1 year from hire (2 years for federal contractors) | EEOC, OFCCP |
| Employee personnel files | Duration of employment + 7 years | Best practice; state varies |
| Payroll records (wages, hours, deductions) | 3 years (FLSA); 4 years (IRS) | FLSA, IRS |
| Tax records (W-2, W-4, 941) | 4 years after tax due date | IRS |
| I-9 forms | 3 years from hire OR 1 year after termination (whichever is later) | IRCA |
| FMLA records | 3 years | FMLA regulations |
| EEO-1 reports | Indefinite | EEOC |
| OSHA 300 logs | 5 years | OSHA |
| OSHA exposure records | 30 years | OSHA |
| ERISA benefit plan documents | 6 years after filing | ERISA |
| Health plan records (ACA) | 3 years after plan year | ACA |
| COBRA records | 6 years | ERISA |
| Workers' compensation records | Duration of employment + 30 years (varies by state) | State law |
| Background check reports | 5 years (retain adverse action records longer) | FCRA, EEOC |
| Drug test results | 5 years (DOT-regulated); otherwise per policy | DOT, company policy |
| Safety training records | Duration of employment + 3 years | OSHA |
| Separation agreements | Indefinite | Best practice |
| Litigation holds | Until release by counsel | Legal hold |

### 6.2 Destruction Protocol

- Destruction of records containing PII must be by shredding (paper) or secure deletion (electronic)
- Document destruction activity (what was destroyed, when, by whom, under what authority)
- NEVER destroy records subject to litigation hold, government investigation, or pending EEOC charge
- Annual destruction review: Identify records that have exceeded retention period and are eligible for destruction

## 7. Audit Preparation

### 7.1 Types of HR Audits

| Audit Type | Initiated By | Focus Areas |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Internal HR audit | HR leadership | Comprehensive policy and practice review |
| External compliance audit | Regulatory agency (DOL, EEOC, OFCCP, IRS, OSHA, state agencies) | Specific area of concern or random selection |
| Benefits audit (ERISA) | Plan fiduciary obligation | Plan administration, fiduciary compliance, reporting |
| I-9 audit | ICE (Immigration and Customs Enforcement) | Employment eligibility verification |
| Payroll audit | IRS, state tax authority | Tax withholding, classification, reporting |
| Safety audit (OSHA) | OSHA inspector | Workplace safety, recordkeeping, hazard communication |
| Workers' compensation audit | Insurance carrier | Classification, payroll accuracy, safety record |

### 7.2 Audit Readiness Framework

```
AUDIT READINESS ASSESSMENT (Quarterly Self-Audit)

I-9 COMPLIANCE:
  [ ] All active employee I-9s complete and current
  [ ] Reverifications up to date
  [ ] I-9s stored separately from personnel files
  [ ] Retention schedule followed for terminated employees

PAYROLL COMPLIANCE:
  [ ] All employees classified correctly (exempt/non-exempt)
  [ ] Overtime calculated correctly for all non-exempt
  [ ] Minimum wage met for all jurisdictions
  [ ] Deductions authorized in writing
  [ ] Payroll records accessible for 3+ years

BENEFITS COMPLIANCE:
  [ ] ACA coverage offered to all eligible
  [ ] COBRA notices sent within required timeframes
  [ ] Form 5500 filed (if applicable)
  [ ] Summary Plan Descriptions distributed
  [ ] Nondiscrimination testing completed (401k, Section 125)

EEO/AFFIRMATIVE ACTION:
  [ ] EEO-1 filed on time
  [ ] Self-ID data collection current
  [ ] Affirmative action plan current (if federal contractor)
  [ ] Adverse impact analysis completed for selection procedures

WORKPLACE SAFETY:
  [ ] OSHA 300 log current
  [ ] Safety training documented
  [ ] Emergency action plan current
  [ ] Required posters displayed

RECORD RETENTION:
  [ ] Retention schedule followed
  [ ] No premature destruction
  [ ] Litigation holds in place
  [ ] Secure storage for sensitive records

SCORE: ___/30 items compliant
TARGET: 30/30 (100% compliance)
```

### 7.3 Responding to a Government Audit

1. **Do not panic.** Government audits are routine.
2. **Notify legal counsel immediately.** All communication with auditors should be coordinated with counsel.
3. **Appoint a single point of contact.** Do not allow auditors to interview employees without preparation.
4. **Provide only what is requested.** Do not volunteer additional information or documents.
5. **Request reasonable time.** You are typically entitled to a reasonable period to gather documents.
6. **Document everything.** Record what was requested, what was provided, and all communications.
7. **Correct deficiencies promptly.** If the audit identifies issues, demonstrate good faith by correcting immediately.

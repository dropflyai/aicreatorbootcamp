# Community Metrics — Authoritative Module

What gets measured gets managed. What gets measured badly gets destroyed.
Community metrics must reflect genuine health and value, not vanity.
This document codifies the metrics framework governing all community
measurement, including the Orbit model, SPACES model, and the critical
distinction between health metrics and activity metrics.

---

## 1. THE METRICS HIERARCHY

### Vanity vs. Health vs. Value Metrics

| Tier | Type | Examples | Decision Power |
|------|------|---------|---------------|
| 1 — Vanity | Feels good, means little | Total members, page views, impressions | None |
| 2 — Activity | Measures behavior volume | Messages sent, events held, content posted | Low |
| 3 — Health | Measures community vitality | Engagement rate, retention, response time | High |
| 4 — Value | Measures business impact | Support deflection, retention lift, referral revenue | Highest |

**Rule: Never report Tier 1 metrics without Tier 3 or 4 context.**
Reporting "10,000 members" without engagement rate is misleading.
Reporting "10,000 members with 25% monthly active rate and 15% annual
retention" is informative.

## 2. CORE HEALTH METRICS

### The Essential Eight

Every community must track these eight metrics:

| # | Metric | Formula | Frequency | Target |
|---|--------|---------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Monthly Active Members (MAM) | Unique members who performed any action in 30 days | Monthly | Growing |
| 2 | Engagement Rate | MAM / Total members | Monthly | 15–30% |
| 3 | Activation Rate | New members who took first action within 30 days / New members | Monthly | >30% |
| 4 | Response Rate | Questions with replies / Total questions | Weekly | >80% |
| 5 | Time to First Response | Median time from question to first reply | Weekly | <4 hours |
| 6 | Retention (30-day) | Members active at Day 30 / Members who joined 30 days ago | Monthly | >25% |
| 7 | Contribution Rate | Members who created content / MAM | Monthly | >10% |
| 8 | Member Satisfaction (NPS) | Standard NPS methodology | Quarterly | >50 |

### The 90-9-1 Distribution Check

Periodically verify the engagement distribution:

```
Expected: 90% lurk / 9% contribute occasionally / 1% create most content
Healthy:  70% lurk / 20% contribute / 10% create
Unhealthy: 95% lurk / 4% contribute / 1% create
Critical:  98% lurk / 1.5% contribute / 0.5% create
```

If the distribution is more extreme than 90-9-1, the community is
failing to activate members. If it is better than 90-9-1, the community
is exceptionally healthy.

## 3. THE ORBIT MODEL

### Orbit Framework (Developed by Orbit/Developer Relations)

The Orbit model segments community members by their gravitational pull
(impact and engagement level):

```
           ┌─────────────────────┐
           │      Orbit 1        │  Core: Highest impact
           │   (Gravitational    │  Ambassadors, top contributors
           │     Core)           │  5–10 members
           ├─────────────────────┤
           │      Orbit 2        │  Active: Regular contributors
           │   (Inner Ring)      │  Weekly participants
           │                     │  50–100 members
           ├─────────────────────┤
           │      Orbit 3        │  Participants: Occasional
           │   (Middle Ring)     │  Monthly engagement
           │                     │  200–500 members
           ├─────────────────────┤
           │      Orbit 4        │  Observers: Peripheral
           │   (Outer Ring)      │  Read-only, occasional visit
           │                     │  1,000+ members
           └─────────────────────┘
```

### Orbit Metrics

| Orbit | Love (Impact) | Reach (Audience) | Activity | Target % |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Very High | High | Multiple activities weekly | 1–2% |
| 2 | High | Medium | Weekly activity | 5–10% |
| 3 | Medium | Low–Medium | Monthly activity | 15–25% |
| 4 | Low | Low | Quarterly or less | 60–80% |

### Moving Members Between Orbits

| Transition | Trigger | Mechanism |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Orbit 4 → 3 | First meaningful contribution | Onboarding activation |
| Orbit 3 → 2 | Consistent weekly engagement | Programs, mentorship, events |
| Orbit 2 → 1 | Leadership behavior | Ambassador program, formal roles |
| Any → lower | Declining activity | Win-back, re-engagement |

## 4. THE SPACES MODEL

### SPACES Framework (CMX / David Spinks)

SPACES identifies six categories of community business value, each
with its own metrics:

| Letter | Category | Description | Key Metrics |
|--------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| S | Support | Peer-to-peer customer support | Tickets deflected, resolution time |
| P | Product | Product feedback and co-creation | Ideas submitted, bugs reported, adoption rate |
| A | Acquisition | New customer/user acquisition | Referred customers, community-to-customer conversion |
| C | Content | Content creation and curation | UGC volume, SEO traffic from community content |
| E | Engagement | Member engagement and retention | DAU/MAU, retention rate, NPS |
| S | Success | Customer success and outcomes | Feature adoption, expansion revenue, churn reduction |

### SPACES Scorecard

| Category | Metric 1 | Metric 2 | Metric 3 | Score (1–5) |
|----------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|
| Support | Tickets deflected/month | Avg resolution time | Answer quality rating | |
| Product | Feature requests/month | Bug reports/month | Beta participation rate | |
| Acquisition | New members/month | Referral rate | Conversion to customer | |
| Content | UGC posts/month | SEO visits from community | Content quality score | |
| Engagement | DAU/MAU | 30-day retention | Contribution rate | |
| Success | Feature adoption lift | Churn reduction vs. non-members | Expansion revenue | |
| **Overall** | | | | **Avg** |

## 5. GROWTH METRICS

| Metric | Formula | Frequency | Context |
|--------|---------|-----------|---------|
| New members | Count of new joins per period | Weekly | Absolute growth |
| Net growth | New members - departures | Monthly | True growth |
| Growth rate | (New members - departures) / Total members | Monthly | Relative growth |
| Viral coefficient | Members acquired via referral / Total members | Monthly | Organic growth |
| Acquisition cost | Total spend / new members (if paid) | Monthly | Efficiency |
| Source attribution | New members by acquisition source | Monthly | Channel effectiveness |

---

## 6. ENGAGEMENT METRICS

| Metric | Formula | Target | Notes |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| DAU/MAU | Daily active / Monthly active | 15–30% | Higher = stronger daily habit |
| WAU/MAU | Weekly active / Monthly active | 40–60% | Weekly cadence indicator |
| Posts per active member | Total posts / Active members | 2–5/month | Contribution depth |
| Replies per thread | Total replies / Total threads | 3+ | Conversation quality |
| Unique participants per thread | Unique repliers / Thread count | 2.5+ | Diversity of voice |
| Event attendance | Attendees / Active members | 10–20% | Event relevance |
| Program participation | Program members / Active members | 5–15% | Program reach |

---

## 7. CONTENT METRICS

| Metric | Formula | Target | Notes |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| Content production | New pieces per week | Stable/growing | Volume |
| UGC ratio | Member-created / Staff-created | >3:1 | Self-sustaining indicator |
| Content engagement | Avg reactions + replies per post | Growing | Quality indicator |
| Content reach | Views per post | Growing | Distribution |
| Search visibility | Organic search clicks from community content | Growing | SEO value |
| Content lifespan | Days a post continues receiving engagement | Increasing | Evergreen value |

---

## 8. MODERATION METRICS

| Metric | Formula | Target | Notes |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| Reports per 1,000 members | Reports / (Members/1,000) | <5 | Community health |
| Resolution time | Median time from report to action | <4 hours | Moderator responsiveness |
| False positive rate | Incorrect actions / Total actions | <10% | Moderation accuracy |
| Repeat offense rate | Multi-offense members / Total actioned | <20% | Enforcement effectiveness |
| Moderator coverage | Hours of moderation / Hours of activity | >80% | Coverage gaps |

### Operational Dashboard (Weekly)

| Section | Metrics | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Activity | DAU, WAU, posts, replies | Volume tracking |
| Content | New posts, top content, UGC ratio | Content health |
| Growth | New members, activation, sources | Acquisition tracking |
| Support | Unresolved questions, response time | Quality of help |
| Moderation | Reports, actions taken, queue size | Community safety |

### Program Dashboard (Per Program)

Each program (ambassador, mentorship, events) has its own dashboard:
- Program-specific KPIs
- Participant activity and satisfaction
- ROI calculation
- Trend over time

## 10. METRIC ANTI-PATTERNS

| Anti-Pattern | Description | Fix |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Vanity reporting | Only showing total members and messages | Tier 3–4 metrics |
| Metric overload | Tracking 50+ metrics, acting on none | Essential Eight + SPACES |
| Counting without context | Raw numbers without trends or benchmarks | Always show delta and target |
| Gaming metrics | Optimizing metric at expense of experience | Validate with qualitative data |
| Annual-only review | Only checking metrics once a year | Weekly operational, monthly strategic |
| Attribution theater | Claiming credit without rigorous attribution | Conservative, evidence-based claims |
| Snapshot bias | Single point-in-time data | Cohort analysis, trends, moving averages |

## 11. MEASUREMENT MATURITY MODEL

| Level | Description | Capabilities |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 — Basic | Platform-provided analytics only | Member count, post count, basic activity |
| 2 — Structured | Defined metrics, regular reporting | Essential Eight, monthly dashboard |
| 3 — Advanced | Cohort analysis, attribution, segmentation | Orbit model, SPACES, ROI |
| 4 — Predictive | Churn prediction, engagement forecasting | ML models, automated alerts |
| 5 — Integrated | Community metrics in company-wide dashboards | Full attribution, cross-functional |

### Moving Up the Maturity Model

| Transition | Required Investment |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 1 → 2 | Define metrics, build reporting template, weekly habit |
| 2 → 3 | Analytics tool (CommonRoom, Orbit), cohort tracking |
| 3 → 4 | Data engineering, predictive modeling, automation |
| 4 → 5 | Cross-functional data integration, executive buy-in |

---

**Metrics are a mirror, not a map. They tell you where you are, not where
to go. Pair quantitative metrics with qualitative insight — member
interviews, feedback, observation — to form a complete picture. The most
important community signals are often the ones that cannot be counted:
the tone of conversations, the speed of welcome, the willingness to be
vulnerable. Measure what you can, but never mistake the measurable for
the meaningful.**

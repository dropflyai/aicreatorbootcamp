# Community Brain -- Benchmark Tests

Version: 1.0
Last Updated: 2026-02-03
Owner: Community Brain
Status: ACTIVE

---

## Purpose

These benchmark tests evaluate the Community Brain's ability to diagnose
community health problems, design engagement strategies, handle sensitive
situations, and build sustainable community operations. Each test presents a
real-world scenario with context data and demands a structured, actionable
response.

Community management is one of the few disciplines where a single bad decision
can destroy years of trust in hours. These tests emphasize judgment, empathy,
and strategic thinking alongside tactical execution.

Tests are scored on a 10-point scale per the CommunityScore framework.
Minimum passing score: 7.0. Below 7.0 triggers remediation.

---

## Scoring Criteria (Applied to Every Test)

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Problem Diagnosis | 25% | Accurate identification of root cause, including human dynamics |
| Strategic Response | 25% | Coherent plan that balances short-term fix with long-term health |
| Tactical Execution | 20% | Specific, actionable steps with owners, timelines, and member impact |
| Empathy and Judgment | 15% | Demonstrates understanding of member perspective and community culture |
| Measurement Plan | 15% | Clear KPIs and criteria for evaluating whether the response worked |

## TEST 1: The 1% Problem

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 30-day engagement strategy**

### Scenario

Your community has 5,000 registered members but only 50 are meaningfully
active (posting, commenting, or attending events). That is a 1% activity rate.
The other 99% are lurkers or dormant. New members join at a rate of 200/month
but 90% never post or engage after signing up. Leadership is questioning the
community investment.

### Context Data

- Platform: Discourse forum with Slack integration
- Community age: 18 months
- Member demographics: 70% are customers, 30% are prospects
- Content: 80% staff-created posts, 20% member posts
- Average posts per day: 3 (almost all from same 10 people)
- Onboarding: automated welcome email with link to community, nothing else
- Events: 1 webinar per month, average attendance 25 people
- No ambassador program
- No gamification or recognition system
- Most popular content: product troubleshooting threads

### Required Response

1. Root cause analysis of the 1% problem (minimum 8 factors)
2. Onboarding redesign (first 7 days experience)
3. Activation strategy (specific tactics to move lurkers to participants)
4. Content strategy shift (from staff-dependent to member-generated)
5. Quick wins (actions that can show results within 2 weeks)
6. 30/60/90 day engagement recovery plan
7. Metrics to track and decision points for pivoting
8. Communication to leadership justifying continued investment

## TEST 2: The Valuable but Toxic Member

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 72 hours**

### Scenario

A member who has been in your community for 2 years is your top contributor.
They answer 30% of all technical questions, have written 15 detailed guides,
and are frequently praised by other members for their expertise. However, they
are increasingly aggressive in conversations. They dismiss newcomers as "lazy
for not searching," publicly mock other members' approaches, and have gotten
into heated arguments with 3 different members this month. Two newer members
have left the community citing this person's behavior. Your ambassador team
is afraid to address it because of the member's status.

### Context Data

- Member has 1,200 posts, 85% helpful
- 3 formal complaints filed in the last month
- Previous informal conversation (6 months ago) resulted in 2 weeks of improved behavior, then regression
- Member is also a paying customer on the highest tier plan
- 2 of your best new contributors have gone silent in the last 3 weeks
- Community NPS dropped from 52 to 38 in the last quarter
- Member's public profile on social media shows frustration with recent product changes

### Required Response

1. Severity assessment (how much damage has already been done?)
2. Private conversation strategy (what to say, tone, channel, who delivers)
3. Clear behavioral expectations and consequences
4. Documentation and evidence approach
5. Contingency plan if member leaves (knowledge preservation, community response)
6. Contingency plan if member escalates (goes public, contacts leadership, threatens)
7. Broader community addressing strategy (how to signal that behavior matters)
8. Systemic fix (how to prevent any single member from having this much leverage)
9. Re-engagement strategy for members driven away

## TEST 3: B2B Community Launch

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 90-day roadmap**

### Scenario

You are launching a community for a B2B SaaS product. You have 200 beta users
who are generally enthusiastic but time-poor. The product is a project management
tool for agencies. Typical users: project managers, creative directors, and
agency founders. Budget for community: $3,000/month (tools + staff time).

### Context Data

- Product is in beta with 200 users across 45 agencies
- NPS from beta: 62
- Users' biggest complaint: lack of templates and best practices
- Users' second complaint: feeling isolated (only their team uses the product)
- Competitor has a community with 8,000 members but low engagement
- Beta users communicate via a shared Slack channel (80 members, moderately active)
- No dedicated community manager (community is 30% of one marketing person's role)
- Product team does bi-weekly updates in the Slack channel
- 3 beta users have organically started sharing tips and templates

### Required Response

1. Community platform recommendation with rationale (stay on Slack, migrate, or add)
2. Community positioning (how is this different from a support channel?)
3. Founding member strategy (who are the first 50, how to recruit and activate)
4. Content seeding plan (what content exists before doors open)
5. 30-day launch plan (week by week)
6. 60-day growth plan (onboarding new users, activation tactics)
7. 90-day sustainability plan (transitioning from staff-driven to member-driven)
8. Value proposition for time-poor professionals (why should they participate?)
9. Measurement framework (what defines success at day 30, 60, 90)
10. Budget allocation ($3K/month breakdown)

## TEST 4: Community Platform Migration

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 12-week migration**

### Scenario

Your community of 3,000 members lives on a Facebook Group. You need to migrate
to a dedicated community platform (Circle, Discourse, or similar) because you
need better analytics, events integration, and you are losing members who refuse
to use Facebook. But 60% of your active members are comfortable on Facebook and
resistant to change. The last time you tried to move (to Slack), you lost 40%
of active members.

### Required Response

1. Platform selection framework (criteria, evaluation, decision)
2. Migration strategy (gradual sunset vs hard cutover vs parallel operation)
3. Member communication plan (how to announce, frame, and motivate the move)
4. Incentive strategy for early movers
5. Content migration plan (what moves, what stays, what is archived)
6. Feature parity assessment (what does the new platform do better/worse)
7. Risk mitigation (how to prevent the 40% loss from happening again)
8. 12-week migration timeline with milestones
9. Success criteria (what does a successful migration look like at week 12?)
10. Rollback plan (at what point do you abort the migration?)

## TEST 5: Community-Led Support Strategy

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Time Pressure: 60-day implementation**

### Scenario

Your support team is overwhelmed. Average ticket response time has increased
from 4 hours to 18 hours. 60% of support tickets are repetitive questions
that experienced community members could answer. Leadership wants the community
to absorb some of this support load. Current community has 2,000 members with
moderate engagement.

### Context Data

- Support ticket volume: 500/month
- Repetitive questions (could be community-answered): 300/month
- Average support ticket cost: $15
- Community currently answers approximately 20 questions/month
- 15 community members identified as subject matter experts
- No formal helper/expert program exists
- Knowledge base exists but is poorly organized and outdated
- Community members who answer questions receive no recognition

### Required Response

1. Support deflection target and timeline (realistic, not aspirational)
2. Expert/helper program design (recruitment, training, recognition, rewards)
3. Knowledge base overhaul plan (what to prioritize, who maintains)
4. Question routing mechanism (how questions get from community to experts)
5. Quality assurance for community answers (how to ensure accuracy)
6. Escalation path (when community cannot answer and support must step in)
7. Measurement framework (deflection rate, answer quality, member satisfaction)
8. Incentive structure (what motivates members to help, and what burns them out)
9. Risk mitigation (wrong answers, frustrated members, expert burnout)

## TEST 6: Declining Engagement After Honeymoon Phase

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 30 days**

### Scenario

Your community launched 6 months ago to great fanfare. First 3 months were
fantastic: high engagement, enthusiastic members, organic content creation.
Months 4-6 have seen a steady decline. Posts per day dropped from 15 to 4.
Event attendance dropped from 80 to 20. Three of your top 10 contributors
have gone silent. New member joins have slowed. The "magic" feels gone.

### Context Data

- Community: 1,500 members
- Active members: dropped from 300 (month 3) to 90 (month 6)
- Staff content: still publishing at same rate
- Topics: same content pillars as launch
- Events: same format (monthly webinar, weekly discussion thread)
- No new programs introduced since launch
- No member feedback survey since month 2
- Ambassador program announced but never launched
- 3 silent top contributors: 1 got a new job, 1 frustrated by lack of product updates, 1 unknown
- Competitor launched their community in month 4

### Required Response

1. Diagnosis: honeymoon effect vs structural problem (and how to tell the difference)
2. Member feedback collection plan (before making changes)
3. Content and format refresh strategy
4. Re-engagement campaign for silent top contributors
5. New program or initiative to create fresh energy
6. Competitive response (how to address members trying the competitor community)
7. 30-day recovery plan with weekly check-ins
8. Sustainable engagement framework (how to prevent this cycle from repeating)

## TEST 7: Scaling Community with Zero Budget Increase

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Time Pressure: Quarterly plan**

### Scenario

Your community has grown from 500 to 2,500 members in the past year. Your
team has not grown. You have 1 community manager (full-time) and 1 intern
(part-time). The intern is leaving next month and will not be replaced.
Community operations are becoming unsustainable. Response times are slipping,
events are being cancelled, and the community manager is burning out.

### Required Response

1. Workload audit (what takes the most time, what can be cut or automated)
2. Automation strategy (what can tools handle without human intervention)
3. Volunteer program design (recruit members to share the operational load)
4. Program prioritization (what to keep, what to pause, what to sunset)
5. Self-service infrastructure (what can members do for themselves)
6. Workflow optimization (how to do the same things in less time)
7. Business case for additional resources (for future budget conversations)
8. Burnout prevention plan for the remaining community manager
9. Quality maintenance strategy (how to maintain standards with fewer resources)

## TEST 8: Community Revenue Program

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Time Pressure: 90-day pilot**

### Scenario

Leadership wants the community to generate direct revenue. Current community
is free and has 4,000 members. Proposed options: premium tier with exclusive
content, paid events/workshops, marketplace for member services, or sponsored
content. Your job is to design a revenue program that does not destroy
community trust and culture.

### Required Response

1. Revenue model evaluation (pros/cons/risks of each option)
2. Recommended model with justification
3. Member segmentation (who would pay, for what, and why)
4. Pricing strategy (free vs premium value split)
5. Pilot design (minimum viable revenue program)
6. Community communication strategy (how to announce without backlash)
7. Trust preservation guardrails (what lines you will not cross)
8. Revenue projections for pilot period (conservative, moderate, optimistic)
9. Success metrics and kill criteria (when to continue, pivot, or stop)
10. Long-term evolution path (pilot to sustainable revenue)

## TEST 9: Moderating a Politically Charged Discussion

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: Real-time (hours)**

### Scenario

A member posts a thoughtful but politically polarizing opinion that is
tangentially related to your industry. The post is not offensive or hateful --
it is a genuine perspective that happens to fall along political lines. The
community is splitting: 40% supportive, 30% opposed, 30% saying "this does
not belong here." Comment volume is 10x normal. Tensions are rising but
no guidelines have been violated yet.

### Required Response

1. Real-time assessment framework (intervene, monitor, or ignore -- and how to decide)
2. If intervening: what to say, tone, and timing
3. Community guidelines gap analysis (does the current policy cover this?)
4. Comment-by-comment moderation criteria (what crosses the line in this thread)
5. Staff positioning (should staff express an opinion or stay neutral?)
6. De-escalation tactics (specific techniques for reducing temperature)
7. Post-incident policy update recommendation
8. Member communication after the dust settles
9. Preventing future occurrences without over-censoring

## TEST 10: Building a Community Ambassador Program from Scratch

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Time Pressure: 60-day launch**

### Scenario

You have a community of 3,000 members. 50 are highly active. You want to
formalize an ambassador program to scale engagement and create a leadership
layer within the community. You have no existing program, no budget for
ambassador compensation (beyond swag and early access), and need the program
operational within 60 days.

### Required Response

1. Ambassador profile definition (who qualifies, what characteristics matter)
2. Recruitment strategy (nomination, application, invitation -- or hybrid)
3. Selection criteria and process (how to choose fairly and inclusively)
4. Onboarding program for selected ambassadors
5. Role definition (expectations, commitments, time investment)
6. Recognition and rewards framework (non-monetary)
7. Activity tracking and accountability (how to know if ambassadors are active)
8. Communication cadence (ambassador-to-staff, ambassador-to-community)
9. Graduation path (what comes after ambassador? what if they burn out?)
10. 60-day launch timeline with weekly milestones

## TEST 11: Community Health Audit

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Time Pressure: 2-week assessment**

### Scenario

You have been hired to audit a community you have never managed. The company
says "the community is not working" but cannot articulate why. You have 2
weeks to assess, diagnose, and deliver recommendations. The community has
8,000 members on a custom-built forum with basic analytics.

### Required Response

1. Audit framework (what dimensions you evaluate and in what order)
2. Data collection plan (what metrics you need and where to find them)
3. Qualitative research plan (who to interview, what to ask)
4. Benchmarking approach (what to compare against)
5. Common dysfunction patterns to look for (the usual suspects)
6. Deliverable structure (what the audit report looks like)
7. Quick-win identification criteria (what can be fixed in 30 days)
8. Strategic recommendation framework (what requires longer-term investment)
9. Presentation strategy (how to deliver hard truths to leadership)

## TEST 12: Community Crisis -- Data Breach

**Severity: CRITICAL**
**Time Pressure: 24 hours**

### Scenario

Your community platform vendor has disclosed a security incident. Member email
addresses, usernames, and hashed passwords may have been exposed. The vendor
has not yet confirmed the full scope. Your community has 5,000 members
including customers of Fortune 500 companies. Members are starting to hear
about it through social media before you have had a chance to communicate.

### Required Response

1. Immediate actions (first 2 hours)
2. Internal stakeholder notification (who needs to know, in what order)
3. Member communication strategy (transparency vs waiting for full information)
4. Platform security actions (password resets, session invalidation)
5. Legal and compliance coordination
6. Media and social media response plan
7. FAQ preparation for member questions
8. Long-term trust repair plan
9. Vendor accountability framework (SLAs, remediation, potential migration)
10. Post-incident review process

## TEST 13: Merging Two Communities

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 90-day merge**

### Scenario

Your company acquired a competitor. Both companies have communities: yours
(3,000 members, Discourse) and theirs (2,000 members, Slack). Leadership
wants to merge them within 90 days. The acquired company's community members
are anxious about the acquisition. Some are hostile. Cultural differences
are significant: your community is formal and structured, theirs is casual
and freeform.

### Required Response

1. Cultural assessment framework (understanding both communities before merging)
2. Member sentiment analysis plan (how to gauge readiness and resistance)
3. Platform decision (keep one, build new, or hybrid -- with rationale)
4. Merge strategy (immediate combine vs gradual integration vs federated model)
5. Communication plan for both communities (different messages, same transparency)
6. Content and knowledge migration plan
7. Cultural integration tactics (how to blend two cultures without destroying either)
8. Governance model for merged community
9. Metrics to track merge health
10. 90-day timeline with milestones and decision points

## TEST 14: Gamification System Design

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Time Pressure: 45-day implementation**

### Scenario

Your community engagement has plateaued. Leadership suggests gamification.
You need to design a system that motivates meaningful participation without
creating perverse incentives (point farming, low-quality posts for badges).
Community: 4,000 members, developer-focused, professional audience that
tends to be skeptical of "gamification gimmicks."

### Required Response

1. Gamification philosophy (what it should and should not incentivize)
2. Points/reputation system design (what earns points, how much, decay?)
3. Badge/achievement system (meaningful milestones, not participation trophies)
4. Leaderboard design (or anti-leaderboard approach with rationale)
5. Perverse incentive prevention (how to discourage gaming the system)
6. Developer audience considerations (what resonates, what repels)
7. Integration with existing community platform
8. Rollout strategy (beta test before full launch)
9. Measurement plan (does gamification actually improve engagement quality?)
10. Iteration framework (how to adjust based on early results)

### Required Response

1. Year-in-review summary (results, wins, misses, learnings)
2. Community maturity assessment (where are we on the maturity curve)
3. Strategic priorities for next year (maximum 5)

## Test Administration Protocol

### Scheduling

- Full benchmark suite administered quarterly
- Minimum 4 tests per monthly spot-check (rotating selection)
- Crisis tests (2, 9, 12) administered bi-annually as tabletop exercises
- New team members complete full suite within first 30 days

### Scoring

- Each test scored on 10-point scale using weighted criteria
- Composite benchmark score = average across all completed tests
- Individual test scores below 5.0 trigger immediate remediation
- Composite score below 7.0 triggers strategy review

### Remediation Protocol

| Score Range | Required Action | Timeline |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| 8.0-10.0 | Document approach for team playbook | 7 days |
| 6.0-7.9 | Identify gaps, create improvement plan | 14 days |
| 4.0-5.9 | Supervised remediation with mentor review | 21 days |
| <4.0 | Full retraining on failed domain | 30 days |

# Community Brain -- Review Checklist

Version: 1.0
Last Updated: 2026-02-03
Owner: Community Brain
Status: ACTIVE

---

## Purpose

This checklist is the mandatory quality gate for all community operations, from
community launch through ongoing management, programs, and governance. No
community initiative launches, no program ships, no policy changes occur
without passing the relevant sections. Every item is required. If an item does
not apply, it must be marked "N/A" with a written justification.

Communities are built on trust. Trust is destroyed by inconsistency, neglect,
and broken promises. This checklist exists to ensure the Community Brain
never makes a promise it cannot keep and never launches something it cannot
sustain.

---

## SECTION 1: Community Strategy Review (Quarterly)

### 1.1 Community Purpose and Positioning

- [ ] Community purpose statement documented and current
- [ ] Purpose clearly differentiated from existing communities in the space
- [ ] Target member persona defined with demographics, needs, and motivations
- [ ] Value proposition articulated: "Members join because ____. Members stay because ____."
- [ ] Community type defined (support, product feedback, networking, learning, practice)
- [ ] Community positioned relative to product/brand (complement vs core vs independent)
- [ ] Success definition aligned with business objectives
- [ ] Anti-goals defined (what the community deliberately will NOT be)

### 1.2 Platform and Technology

- [ ] Community platform selected with documented rationale
- [ ] Platform capabilities match community needs (discussions, events, DMs, groups)
- [ ] Integration with existing tools verified (CRM, support, analytics)
- [ ] Data ownership and portability reviewed (can you export member data?)
- [ ] Platform cost vs budget verified
- [ ] Mobile experience tested and acceptable
- [ ] Accessibility compliance verified (WCAG AA minimum)
- [ ] SSO or authentication integrated with company systems
- [ ] Analytics and reporting capabilities confirmed
- [ ] Migration plan exists if platform change is ever needed

### 1.3 Governance Structure

- [ ] Community governance model defined (staff-led, member-led, hybrid)
- [ ] Decision-making process documented (how community changes are made)
- [ ] Member input mechanism exists (how members influence community direction)
- [ ] Community team roles and responsibilities defined
- [ ] Volunteer/ambassador roles defined with expectations and benefits
- [ ] Escalation paths documented (member to staff, staff to leadership)
- [ ] Budget allocation reviewed and approved

### 1.4 Content and Programming Strategy

- [ ] Content pillars defined for community (different from brand content pillars)
- [ ] Programming calendar exists (recurring events, content series, campaigns)
- [ ] Content sourcing plan: staff-created vs member-created vs guest
- [ ] Content moderation workflow defined
- [ ] Resource library or knowledge base strategy defined
- [ ] Cross-functional content coordination (product updates, company news, etc.)

### 1.5 Measurement Framework

- [ ] KPIs defined and mapped to CommunityScore dimensions
- [ ] Measurement tools configured and reporting
- [ ] Baseline metrics established
- [ ] Reporting cadence agreed with stakeholders
- [ ] Attribution model documented (how community value is credited)
- [ ] Qualitative measurement plan (surveys, interviews, focus groups)

## SECTION 2: Community Launch Checklist

### 2.1 Pre-Launch (4+ Weeks Before)

- [ ] Community purpose, guidelines, and value proposition finalized
- [ ] Platform configured and stress-tested
- [ ] Community guidelines written, reviewed by legal, and published
- [ ] Welcome experience designed and tested (onboarding flow)
- [ ] Seed content created (minimum 20 posts/resources before opening)
- [ ] Founding member cohort identified (10-50 people who will set the tone)
- [ ] Founding members briefed on their role and expectations
- [ ] Launch announcement content created (email, social, blog)
- [ ] Internal team briefed (support, sales, product know the community exists)
- [ ] Moderation team trained and scheduled
- [ ] Monitoring and alerting configured
- [ ] Success metrics for launch defined (first 7 days, 30 days, 90 days)

### 2.2 Soft Launch (1-2 Weeks)

- [ ] Founding members invited and onboarded
- [ ] Founding member feedback collected and incorporated
- [ ] Technical issues identified and resolved
- [ ] Content cadence tested and adjusted
- [ ] Moderation workflow tested with real interactions
- [ ] Onboarding flow tested with founding members
- [ ] Adjustments made based on founding member behavior and feedback
- [ ] Go/no-go decision for public launch documented

### 2.3 Public Launch

- [ ] Launch announcement published across all channels
- [ ] Invitation mechanism active (email, in-app, social)
- [ ] Welcome posts and introductory content visible
- [ ] Staff presence active (responding to first members within hours)
- [ ] First-week engagement plan active (daily prompts, discussions, activities)
- [ ] Monitoring frequency increased (hourly check-ins for first 72 hours)
- [ ] Technical support on standby for platform issues
- [ ] Daily metrics review for first 14 days

### 2.4 Post-Launch Review (30 Days)

- [ ] Launch metrics assessed against targets
- [ ] Member feedback collected (survey + qualitative)
- [ ] Onboarding funnel analyzed (where do new members drop off?)
- [ ] Content performance reviewed (what resonated, what fell flat)
- [ ] Moderation load assessed (is the team adequately staffed?)
- [ ] Platform performance reviewed (speed, stability, mobile experience)
- [ ] Lessons learned documented
- [ ] 90-day plan adjusted based on learnings

## SECTION 3: Member Lifecycle Review

### 3.1 Onboarding

- [ ] New member receives welcome message within 1 hour of joining
- [ ] Onboarding flow guides member to: introduce themselves, set up profile, find relevant content
- [ ] New member is connected to a relevant sub-group or topic channel
- [ ] New member receives "first 7 days" guide (what to do, where to go, who to connect with)
- [ ] Onboarding completion tracked (% of new members completing each step)
- [ ] Onboarding A/B tested quarterly for improvement
- [ ] New member experience audited monthly (staff goes through it as if new)
- [ ] Welcome thread or introduction post format established

### 3.2 Activation

- [ ] Activation definition documented (what action = "activated member")
- [ ] Time-to-activation tracked by cohort
- [ ] Activation triggers identified (what prompts first engagement)
- [ ] Nudge sequence designed for members who have not activated within 7 days
- [ ] High-value first actions identified and promoted (e.g., "answer a question", "attend an event")
- [ ] Activation rate tracked weekly with intervention threshold

### 3.3 Engagement

- [ ] Regular engagement prompts scheduled (discussion questions, polls, challenges)
- [ ] Content calendar maintained with community-specific content
- [ ] Member milestones recognized (join anniversary, contribution milestones)
- [ ] Gamification or recognition system active (badges, levels, leaderboards)
- [ ] Peer-to-peer connection facilitated (introductions, matching, groups)
- [ ] Expert access provided (AMAs, office hours, Q&A sessions)
- [ ] Feedback loops active (member input shapes community direction)
- [ ] Seasonal or themed events planned

### 3.4 Retention and Re-engagement

- [ ] At-risk member identification system active (declining activity pattern)
- [ ] Re-engagement campaigns designed for different churn stages
- [ ] Win-back sequence for dormant members (email, notification, personal outreach)
- [ ] Exit survey deployed for members who leave
- [ ] Churn reasons categorized and tracked
- [ ] Retention interventions tested and measured
- [ ] Long-tenured member appreciation program active

### 3.5 Graduation and Advocacy

- [ ] Path from member to advocate/ambassador defined
- [ ] Ambassador nomination and selection process documented
- [ ] Ambassador training and onboarding program exists
- [ ] Ambassador contribution expectations set and tracked
- [ ] Ambassador recognition and rewards defined
- [ ] Referral program active (members referring new members)
- [ ] Testimonial and case study collection process active

## SECTION 4: Moderation and Safety Review

### 4.1 Guidelines and Policies

- [ ] Community guidelines published and easily accessible
- [ ] Guidelines written in plain language (not legalese)
- [ ] Guidelines cover: acceptable behavior, prohibited behavior, consequences
- [ ] Guidelines include examples for ambiguous situations
- [ ] Guidelines reviewed and updated at least annually
- [ ] Privacy policy specific to community activity published
- [ ] Data handling practices documented and communicated
- [ ] Content ownership and licensing terms clear to members

### 4.2 Moderation Operations

- [ ] Moderation team staffed for coverage hours (minimum business hours + 2 hours buffer)
- [ ] Moderation queue monitored with maximum 4-hour response SLA
- [ ] Automated moderation tools configured (spam filter, keyword alerts, new member review)
- [ ] Escalation matrix defined (what goes to mods, what goes to staff, what goes to legal)
- [ ] Moderation actions logged with timestamp, action taken, and rationale
- [ ] Regular moderation calibration sessions (ensure consistency across moderators)
- [ ] Moderator burnout prevention plan active (rotation, workload caps, support)

### 4.3 Incident Response

- [ ] Incident classification system defined (minor, moderate, severe, critical)
- [ ] Response protocol per incident level documented
- [ ] Critical incident response time: <1 hour
- [ ] Communication templates ready for common incident types
- [ ] Post-incident review process defined
- [ ] Member communication plan for significant incidents
- [ ] Legal team engagement criteria defined
- [ ] Law enforcement reporting criteria and process defined

### 4.4 Safety Monitoring

- [ ] Harassment detection active (automated + human monitoring)
- [ ] Spam detection and prevention active
- [ ] Scam and fraud monitoring active
- [ ] Self-harm or crisis content protocol defined with professional resources linked
- [ ] Doxxing prevention and response protocol defined
- [ ] Vulnerable population protection measures active (if community includes minors or at-risk groups)

## SECTION 5: Programs and Events Review

### 5.1 Event Planning (Per Event)

- [ ] Event objective defined with success metrics
- [ ] Event format selected (webinar, AMA, workshop, social, hackathon)
- [ ] Speaker/host confirmed and briefed
- [ ] Event promotion plan activated (minimum 2 weeks lead time)
- [ ] Registration system configured and tested
- [ ] Event platform tested (if virtual: audio, video, screenshare, chat)
- [ ] Accessibility accommodations planned (captions, recordings, timezone)
- [ ] Run-of-show document created with timing and roles
- [ ] Moderator assigned for chat/Q&A management
- [ ] Follow-up plan defined (recording, summary, next steps)

### 5.2 Event Execution

- [ ] Technical dry run completed at least 24 hours before event
- [ ] Speaker connected and tested 30 minutes before start
- [ ] Recording started and confirmed
- [ ] Welcome message and ground rules shared at event start
- [ ] Q&A managed and questions captured
- [ ] Engagement tactics deployed (polls, reactions, breakouts)
- [ ] Time kept within schedule
- [ ] Thank-you and next-steps shared before close

### 5.3 Event Follow-Up

- [ ] Recording processed and shared within 48 hours
- [ ] Summary post published in community
- [ ] Attendee feedback collected (post-event survey)
- [ ] Attendance data analyzed (registered vs attended, retention during event)
- [ ] Follow-up actions tracked to completion
- [ ] Event ROI calculated (cost, time, value generated)
- [ ] Learnings documented for future events

### 5.4 Ambassador Program Review (Monthly)

- [ ] Ambassador activity metrics reviewed
- [ ] Inactive ambassadors contacted and re-engaged (or offboarded)
- [ ] Ambassador feedback collected
- [ ] Ambassador content and activity quality assessed
- [ ] Rewards and recognition distributed
- [ ] New ambassador nominations reviewed
- [ ] Program objectives assessed against KPIs
- [ ] Ambassador training or enablement needs identified

### 5.5 Mentorship Program Review (Monthly)

- [ ] Active mentor-mentee pairs tracked
- [ ] Check-in survey sent to all active participants
- [ ] Stuck or inactive pairs identified and supported
- [ ] Program completion milestones tracked
- [ ] Mentor capacity reviewed (not over-committed)
- [ ] New mentor recruitment pipeline active
- [ ] Program feedback incorporated into next cohort design

## SECTION 6: Content and Knowledge Management Review

### 6.1 Content Operations

- [ ] Community content calendar maintained for minimum 2 weeks ahead
- [ ] Discussion prompts scheduled at consistent cadence
- [ ] Staff-created content balanced with member-created content
- [ ] Content performance tracked (engagement, saves, shares)
- [ ] Underperforming content formats identified and adjusted
- [ ] Top-performing content formats documented and replicated
- [ ] Cross-posting from other channels adapted for community context (not dumped)

### 6.2 Knowledge Base

- [ ] FAQ or resource library exists and is maintained
- [ ] Top questions identified and answered in knowledge base
- [ ] Knowledge base search functionality tested and effective
- [ ] Content freshness audited quarterly (outdated content flagged and updated)
- [ ] Member contributions to knowledge base encouraged and credited
- [ ] Knowledge base analytics tracked (most viewed, search terms with no results)

### 6.3 Product Feedback Loop

- [ ] Product feedback collection mechanism active in community
- [ ] Feedback categorized and prioritized
- [ ] Feedback shared with product team on regular cadence
- [ ] Product team responses shared back to community ("you asked, we built")
- [ ] Beta testing and early access opportunities offered to community members
- [ ] Feature request voting or prioritization visible to members

## SECTION 7: Analytics and Reporting Review

### 7.1 Data Collection

- [ ] All CommunityScore dimensions have data sources configured
- [ ] Data collection is automated where possible
- [ ] Manual data collection tasks scheduled and assigned
- [ ] Data quality checks performed monthly (no gaps, no anomalies unexplained)
- [ ] Member data privacy maintained per policy
- [ ] Data retention and deletion policies followed

### 7.2 Reporting

- [ ] Weekly community pulse report generated and reviewed
- [ ] Monthly scorecard completed and shared with stakeholders
- [ ] Quarterly review deck prepared with trends and recommendations
- [ ] Reports include context and recommendations (not just numbers)
- [ ] Anomalies investigated and explained within 48 hours
- [ ] Benchmarks updated quarterly to reflect platform and industry changes

### 7.3 Attribution

- [ ] Community value attribution model documented
- [ ] Support deflection tracked with methodology documented
- [ ] Pipeline influence tracked (community member status in CRM)
- [ ] Content value tracked (member-created content usage)
- [ ] Retention impact tracked (community member vs non-member comparison)
- [ ] Attribution shared cross-functionally (sales, support, product aware of community value)

---

## SECTION 8: Cross-Functional Coordination Review

### 8.1 Product Team

- [ ] Regular sync between community team and product team (minimum bi-weekly)
- [ ] Product updates communicated to community team before public announcement
- [ ] Community feedback routed to product team systematically
- [ ] Product team participates in community (AMAs, Q&As, office hours)
- [ ] Beta programs coordinated through community

### 8.2 Support Team

- [ ] Escalation path from community to support defined
- [ ] Support team trained on community guidelines and tone
- [ ] Community resolutions tracked for support deflection credit
- [ ] Known issues communicated to community proactively
- [ ] Support knowledge base and community knowledge base synced

### 8.3 Marketing Team

- [ ] Community content coordinated with marketing calendar
- [ ] Community stories and testimonials available for marketing use
- [ ] Marketing campaigns promoted through community (with community-native framing)
- [ ] Community metrics included in marketing reporting
- [ ] Joint campaigns (community challenges tied to marketing campaigns)

### 8.4 Sales Team

- [ ] Sales team aware of community and its value proposition
- [ ] Community membership offered as part of customer onboarding
- [ ] Sales team can identify community members in CRM
- [ ] Community success stories available for sales enablement
- [ ] Customer community engagement data visible to account managers

## Checklist Governance

### Mandatory Gates

| Gate | When | Who Reviews | Must Pass |
|------|------|------------|-----------|
| Strategy Review | Quarterly | Community Lead + Marketing Director | Yes |
| Community Launch | Before launch | Community Lead + stakeholders | Yes - all pre-launch items |
| Event Launch | Before each event | Event owner + Community Lead | Yes |
| Program Launch | Before each program | Program owner + Community Lead | Yes |
| Moderation Policy Change | Before implementation | Community Lead + Legal | Yes |
| Platform Migration | Before migration begins | Community Lead + Engineering | Yes |

### Exception Process

Any checklist bypass requires:
1. Written justification from Community Lead
2. Approval from Marketing Director (for strategic items) or Legal (for policy items)
3. Risk documented in exception log
4. Outcome reviewed within 14 days

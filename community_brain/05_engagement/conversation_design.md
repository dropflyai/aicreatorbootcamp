# Conversation Design — Authoritative Module

Conversations are the fundamental unit of community value. A community is
only as vibrant as its conversations. This document codifies the art and
science of designing, prompting, and sustaining meaningful community
discussions that generate knowledge, connection, and engagement.

---

## 1. CONVERSATION ARCHITECTURE

### What Makes a Great Community Conversation

Great community conversations share five characteristics:

1. **Specificity:** Focused on a defined topic, not vague generalities
2. **Relevance:** Matters to participants right now, not abstractly
3. **Accessibility:** Anyone at the appropriate level can participate
4. **Generativity:** Produces new ideas, not just restates known facts
5. **Connection:** Creates links between participants, not just with the topic

### Conversation Types

| Type | Structure | Purpose | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|
| Question | Problem → solution | Help seeking | "How do I implement X?" |
| Discussion | Prompt → perspectives | Idea exploration | "What is your approach to Y?" |
| Showcase | Artifact → feedback | Recognition + learning | "I built this — feedback?" |
| Debate | Thesis → arguments | Position refinement | "Is framework A better than B?" |
| Story | Narrative → lessons | Experience sharing | "Here is how we solved Z" |
| Resource | Content → commentary | Curation + analysis | "This article argues X. Thoughts?" |
| Request | Need → offers | Community support | "Looking for a mentor in X" |
| Announcement | News → reactions | Information + engagement | "We just shipped feature X" |

### Thread Depth Targets

| Conversation Type | Target Replies | Healthy Range |
|-------------------|---------------|--------------|
| Question | 2–5 (including accepted answer) | 1–10 |
| Discussion | 5–15 | 3–30 |
| Showcase | 3–10 | 2–20 |
| Debate | 10–30 | 5–50+ |
| Story | 3–8 | 2–15 |
| Resource | 3–8 | 1–15 |
| Announcement | 5–20 | 2–30 |

## 2. PROMPTING DISCUSSIONS

### The Art of the Prompt

A great discussion prompt is a question or statement that:
- Invites personal experience (not just opinions)
- Has no single correct answer
- Is timely and relevant
- Requires thought but not research
- Can be answered in 2–5 sentences

### Prompt Frameworks

**Framework 1: "What is your approach to...?"**
Invites diverse methodologies from practitioners.
```
Example: "What is your approach to code review?
         Do you use a checklist, go by instinct, or something else?"
```

**Framework 2: "The best [X] I ever [Y] was..."**
Invites storytelling and personal experience.
```
Example: "The best technical decision I ever made was...
         What is yours and why?"
```

**Framework 3: "Unpopular opinion: [statement]"**
Invites constructive debate and diverse perspectives.
```
Example: "Unpopular opinion: Unit tests are overrated compared
         to integration tests. Change my mind."
```

**Framework 4: "What would you do if...?"**
Invites problem-solving and creative thinking.
```
Example: "What would you do if you had 48 hours to improve
         your product's onboarding? No budget constraints."
```

**Framework 5: "I used to think X, now I think Y"**
Invites reflection on changed perspectives.
```
Example: "I used to think microservices were always better.
         Now I think monoliths are underrated. What has changed
         for you over the years?"
```

**Framework 6: "Show us your [X]"**
Invites visual or artifact sharing.
```
Example: "Show us your terminal setup. What tools and
         configurations make you productive?"
```

### Prompt Calendar (4-Week Rotation)

| Week | Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri |
|------|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 1 | "What is your approach to..." | Resource share + discussion | Live event recap | "Unpopular opinion..." | Weekly wins thread |
| 2 | "The best X I ever Y..." | Tutorial request thread | Live workshop | "What would you do if..." | Showcase Friday |
| 3 | "I used to think X, now Y" | Tool/resource recommendations | Live AMA recap | Industry news discussion | Community feedback |
| 4 | Member-submitted prompt | "Show us your X" | Live event | Retrospective discussion | Monthly highlights |

## 3. POLLS AND SURVEYS

### Poll Design for Engagement

Polls serve dual purposes: data collection and engagement catalyst.
The best polls spark discussion after the vote.

**Poll Best Practices:**
1. **Limit options to 4–6** — Too many options dilute engagement
2. **Include "Other" with comment** — Captures unexpected perspectives
3. **Share results and analysis** — Close the loop; polls without follow-up feel extractive
4. **Time-bound** — 24–72 hours for urgency
5. **Follow up** — Post a summary and discussion thread after the poll closes

### Poll Types

| Type | Purpose | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| Preference | Understand community taste | "Which framework do you prefer for X?" |
| Experience | Gauge common challenges | "What is your biggest challenge with Y?" |
| Priority | Inform decision-making | "Which feature should we build next?" |
| Fun/social | Build culture and connection | "Tabs or spaces? Defend your choice." |
| Feedback | Improve community | "How would you rate last week's event?" |

### Survey Strategy

For deeper insights (beyond polls):

| Survey Type | Length | Frequency | Response Target |
|-------------|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Pulse check | 3–5 questions | Monthly | >20% of active members |
| Satisfaction | 10–15 questions | Quarterly | >15% of active members |
| Deep dive | 20–30 questions | Annually | >10% of total members |
| Exit survey | 5–10 questions | On departure | >30% of departing members |

## 4. CHALLENGES AND COMPETITIONS

### Challenge Design

Challenges create time-bound engagement spikes that activate dormant
members and generate content.

**Challenge Framework:**
```
Challenge: [Name]
Duration: [1 day – 4 weeks]
Objective: [What participants must do]
Rules: [Constraints and requirements]
Submission: [How to submit and where]
Judging: [Criteria and judges]
Prizes: [Recognition, swag, access]
```

### Challenge Types

| Type | Duration | Example | Best For |
|------|----------|---------|---------|
| Daily challenge | 1 day | "Build X in 30 minutes and share" | Quick engagement |
| Weekly challenge | 1 week | "Write a blog post about your workflow" | Content generation |
| Build challenge | 2–4 weeks | "Build a tool using our API" | Deep engagement |
| Learning challenge | 2–4 weeks | "Complete 5 tutorials and share learnings" | Skill development |
| Social challenge | Ongoing | "Welcome 3 new members this week" | Community growth |

### Challenge Operations

**Before:**
- Announce 1–2 weeks in advance
- Create dedicated channel/thread
- Prepare submission template
- Recruit judges (if applicable)

**During:**
- Daily progress updates
- Highlight early submissions
- Encourage participants publicly
- Answer questions promptly

**After:**
- Celebrate all participants
- Announce winners (if competitive)
- Showcase submissions to community
- Publish summary/wrap-up
- Share results with participants

## 5. THEMED EVENTS AND CONTENT SERIES

### Themed Events

Recurring themed events create anticipation and habit formation.

**Weekly Themes:**
| Theme | Description | Format |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Motivation Monday | Share goals and wins | Discussion thread |
| Tutorial Tuesday | Member teaches something | Video/text tutorial |
| Workshop Wednesday | Live interactive learning | Event |
| Thoughtful Thursday | Deep discussion on industry topic | Long-form thread |
| Showcase Friday | Share what you built or learned | Gallery thread |

**Monthly Themes:**
| Theme | Description | Activities |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Beginner Month | Focus on newcomer content | Intro workshops, glossary, mentoring |
| Deep Dive Month | Focus on advanced topics | Expert sessions, research, debates |
| Community Month | Focus on relationships | Social events, spotlights, feedback |
| Build Month | Focus on creation | Hackathon, challenges, showcases |

### Content Series

Long-running content series that members follow like a publication:

| Series | Format | Frequency | Creator |
|--------|--------|-----------|---------|
| "How I Built This" | Member case study | Bi-weekly | Rotating members |
| "Tool of the Week" | Tool review/recommendation | Weekly | Staff + members |
| "Ask an Expert" | Q&A with specialist | Monthly | Invited guests |
| "From the Archives" | Resurface great old content | Weekly | Staff |
| "Community Numbers" | Metrics and insights | Monthly | Staff |

## 6. CROSS-POLLINATION

### Connecting Across Silos

Cross-pollination brings members from one part of the community into
contact with another, creating unexpected connections and insights.

**Cross-Pollination Tactics:**

| Tactic | Description | Implementation |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Cross-channel linking | Reference discussions in other channels | Manual + bot automation |
| Multi-discipline prompts | Questions that require diverse expertise | Designed prompts |
| Mixed breakout rooms | Events pair different specialties | Random or intentional grouping |
| Guest appearances | Channel experts visit other channels | Scheduled "office hours" in other spaces |
| Shared challenges | Challenges that span multiple tracks | Single challenge, multiple approaches |
| Spotlight rotation | Different channel/track spotlighted each week | Curated highlighting |

### Internal-External Cross-Pollination

Connect community discussions with external contexts:
- Share community insights on social media (with attribution)
- Bring external content into community for discussion
- Connect community members with industry events
- Invite external experts for community sessions
- Publish community research externally

## 7. CONVERSATION MODERATION

### Keeping Conversations Healthy

**Conversation Facilitation Techniques:**
1. **Acknowledge and extend:** "Great point, [name]. Building on that..."
2. **Redirect:** "This is interesting — let us continue this in #channel"
3. **Summarize:** "So far we have heard X, Y, and Z. What are we missing?"
4. **Invite:** "@[quiet member], you have experience with this — thoughts?"
5. **Close:** "Great discussion! Key takeaways: 1) ... 2) ... 3) ..."

**Conversation Anti-Patterns to Manage:**
| Pattern | Description | Intervention |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Monologue | One person dominates | Redirect, invite others |
| Thread hijack | Topic shifts away from OP | Split to new thread |
| Echo chamber | Only one perspective expressed | Introduce counter-perspective |
| Dead thread | Question with no responses | Staff or volunteer answers |
| Flame war | Argument devolving into personal attacks | Cool down, enforce CoC |
| Vampire | Takes help but never gives | Gentle encouragement to contribute |

## 8. CONVERSATION ANALYTICS

### What to Measure

| Metric | What It Tells You | Target |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Thread start rate | Are members initiating conversations? | Stable or growing |
| Reply rate | Are conversations generating responses? | >70% of threads get 1+ reply |
| Reply depth | How deep do conversations go? | Avg 3+ replies per thread |
| Response time | How fast do threads get first reply? | <4 hours |
| Unique participants/thread | Conversation breadth | 3+ unique per active thread |
| Discussion sentiment | Are conversations positive, negative, neutral? | >70% positive/neutral |
| Content referenced | Are discussions producing cited content? | Growing |

### Conversation Health Indicators

**Healthy Signals:**
- Diverse participants (not same 5 people)
- Questions receiving multiple quality answers
- Debates remaining constructive
- New members participating in threads
- Members referencing past discussions

**Unhealthy Signals:**
- Same small group in every conversation
- Many unanswered questions
- Conversations frequently devolving into arguments
- New members not participating after introduction
- Repetitive topics without knowledge accumulation

## 9. CONVERSATION INFRASTRUCTURE

### Knowledge Capture

Conversations produce knowledge that must be captured and organized:

| Method | Description | When |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Accepted answers | Mark best answer to questions | Q&A-format conversations |
| Thread summary | Staff writes summary of key points | Deep discussions |
| Wiki/docs promotion | Convert answer to documentation | Recurring questions |
| Case study | Turn member story into formal content | Exceptional examples |
| FAQ update | Add question to FAQ | 3+ identical questions |

### Search and Discovery

Enable members to find past conversations:
- Full-text search across all conversations
- Tag system for topic categorization
- "Related discussions" feature or bot
- Curated collections of best discussions
- Monthly "best of" compilations

## 10. ANTI-PATTERNS

| Anti-Pattern | Description | Fix |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Staff-only conversation | Only team members start discussions | Empower and prompt member starts |
| Question graveyard | Questions go unanswered | Volunteer answer team, SLA |
| Shallow discussions | Everything is surface-level | Deep prompts, expert facilitation |
| Noise factory | High volume, low quality | Tighter channel design, moderation |
| One-way broadcasting | Announcements without discussion hooks | Always include a question/prompt |
| Prompt fatigue | Too many prompts, members stop responding | Variety, quality over quantity |

---

**Conversations are the atomic unit of community value. When they are
well-designed, they generate knowledge, build relationships, and create
a self-reinforcing cycle of engagement. When they are neglected, they
devolve into noise or silence. Invest in conversation design with the
same rigor you apply to product design — because in community, the
conversation IS the product.**

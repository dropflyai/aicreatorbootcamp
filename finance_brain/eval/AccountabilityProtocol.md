# Finance Accountability Protocol -- Enforcement and Governance

This document defines how financial performance, accuracy, and compliance are tracked, reviewed, and enforced.
Finance accountability is not optional. It is the foundation of trust between the company, its board, its investors, and its regulators.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Every financial output is a promise. A promise that the numbers are accurate, the methodology is sound, and the controls are in place. When that promise is broken, trust erodes -- with the board, with investors, with regulators, and with the team.

Accountability ensures the promise is kept.

---

## 1. VARIANCE ANALYSIS REQUIREMENTS

### Monthly Variance Analysis (Mandatory)

Every month, within 10 business days of month-end close, the finance team must produce a variance analysis covering:

| Comparison | Threshold for Explanation | Detail Required |
|-----------|--------------------------|----------------|
| Actual vs. Budget | Any line item >10% variance | Written explanation with root cause |
| Actual vs. Prior Month | Any line item >15% variance | Written explanation with trend context |
| Actual vs. Prior Year | Any line item >20% variance | Written explanation with year-over-year context |

### Variance Explanation Standards

Every variance explanation must answer:
1. **What happened?** (Factual description)
2. **Why did it happen?** (Root cause)
3. **Is it one-time or recurring?** (Nature of the variance)
4. **What is the impact on the full-year forecast?** (Forward-looking)
5. **What action is being taken?** (If unfavorable)

### Unacceptable Variance Explanations

These explanations are NOT acceptable:
- "Timing difference" (without specifying what timing and when it reverses)
- "Below plan" (this restates the variance, does not explain it)
- "Market conditions" (too vague; specify what market condition and how it impacted)
- "Will catch up next month" (without specific actions and evidence)

### Variance Review Meeting

- **When:** Within 2 business days of variance analysis completion
- **Attendees:** CFO, Controller, FP&A lead, department heads (for their areas)
- **Duration:** 60 minutes
- **Output:** Action items for unfavorable variances, updated forecast if needed

### Accountability for Persistent Variances

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Same line item unfavorable 2 months in a row | Root cause investigation required |
| Same line item unfavorable 3+ months in a row | Budget must be reforecast; original assumption was wrong |
| Total revenue unfavorable >10% for 2+ months | Board notification required |
| Total expense unfavorable >10% for 2+ months | Expense review and reduction plan required |

## 2. REFORECAST CADENCE

### Scheduled Reforecasts

| Reforecast | Timing | Scope | Approval |
|-----------|--------|-------|----------|
| Q1 reforecast | End of March (after Q1 close) | Full year update | CFO + CEO |
| Mid-year reforecast | End of June (after Q2 close) | Full year + preliminary next year | CFO + CEO + Board |
| Q3 reforecast | End of September (after Q3 close) | Full year final + next year draft | CFO + CEO |
| Annual budget | November - December | Next fiscal year | CFO + CEO + Board approval |

### Unscheduled Reforecast Triggers

A reforecast must be initiated within 5 business days if any of the following occur:

| Trigger | Required Scope |
|---------|---------------|
| Revenue tracking >15% below plan for any month | Revenue and cash flow reforecast |
| Major customer loss (>5% of ARR) | Full P&L and cash flow reforecast |
| Unexpected large expense (>$100K unbudgeted) | Expense and cash flow reforecast |
| Fundraise timeline changes by >60 days | Cash runway and hiring plan reforecast |
| M&A activity (acquisition or divestiture) | Full financial reforecast |
| Market conditions materially change | Full financial reforecast |
| Headcount plan changes by >10% | Expense and cash flow reforecast |

### Reforecast Quality Standards

Every reforecast must:
- Include updated assumptions with sources
- Show the bridge from prior forecast to new forecast (what changed and why)
- Include at least base, upside, and downside scenarios
- Update cash runway calculation
- Be reviewed by CFO before distribution
- Be presented to the board if the change is material (>10% to annual plan)

### Documentation Requirements

- Prior forecast preserved (not overwritten)
- Change log documenting every adjustment
- Assumption changes highlighted with new sources
- Approval signatures before distribution

## 3. AUDIT PREPARATION PROTOCOL

### Ongoing Audit Readiness (Monthly)

Do not prepare for audits only when the auditor arrives. Maintain audit readiness at all times:

| Task | Frequency | Owner | Verification |
|------|-----------|-------|-------------|
| Bank reconciliations | Monthly | Accounting | Controller review by day 5 |
| AR reconciliation | Monthly | Accounting | Controller review by day 7 |
| AP reconciliation | Monthly | Accounting | Controller review by day 7 |
| Revenue reconciliation | Monthly | FP&A | Controller review by day 10 |
| Deferred revenue roll-forward | Monthly | FP&A | Controller review by day 10 |
| Fixed asset register update | Quarterly | Accounting | Controller review by day 15 |
| Equity roll-forward | Quarterly | Controller | CFO review by day 15 |
| Close binder / documentation | Monthly | Controller | Complete by day 12 |

### Pre-Audit Checklist (2 Months Before Audit)

- [ ] All monthly reconciliations current and reviewed
- [ ] Supporting schedules complete (revenue, deferred revenue, fixed assets, debt, equity)
- [ ] Accounting policies documented and current
- [ ] Significant estimates documented with methodology
- [ ] Related party transactions identified and documented
- [ ] Subsequent events evaluated
- [ ] PBC (Prepared by Client) list received and items assigned
- [ ] Prior year audit findings addressed with evidence
- [ ] System access arranged for audit team
- [ ] Key personnel calendar held for audit period
- [ ] Legal confirmation letters requested
- [ ] Management representation letter drafted

### During Audit Protocol

- **Single point of contact:** Controller manages all auditor requests
- **48-hour SLA:** All auditor requests fulfilled within 2 business days
- **Escalation path:** Requests requiring CFO judgment escalated within 4 hours
- **Tracking spreadsheet:** Every auditor request logged with status, owner, due date
- **Weekly status meeting:** Controller + Auditor discuss open items
- **No surprises rule:** If finance discovers an issue during audit, disclose to auditor proactively

### Post-Audit Actions

- Management letter points addressed within 30 days
- Action plan for each finding with owner and deadline
- Follow-up documentation provided to auditor
- Audit findings presented to Audit Committee (if applicable)
- Process improvements implemented before next period-end

## 4. FINANCIAL CONTROLS TESTING SCHEDULE

### Control Categories and Testing Frequency

| Control Category | Testing Method | Frequency | Sample Size |
|-----------------|---------------|-----------|-------------|
| **Revenue recognition** | Walkthrough + sample testing | Quarterly | 10% of new contracts or 25, whichever is greater |
| **Cash disbursements** | Sample testing | Monthly | 15 transactions |
| **Payroll** | Recalculation test | Quarterly | 5 employees |
| **Journal entries** | Review of non-standard entries | Monthly | All entries >$10K |
| **Bank reconciliation** | Independent review | Monthly | All accounts |
| **User access** | Access review | Semi-annually | All financial system users |
| **Segregation of duties** | Matrix review | Quarterly | All financial roles |
| **Vendor master changes** | Change log review | Quarterly | All changes |
| **Expense approval** | Sample testing | Monthly | 20 transactions |

### Testing Documentation Requirements

Each control test must document:
1. **Control objective:** What risk does this control mitigate?
2. **Control description:** How does the control work?
3. **Test procedure:** Exactly what was done to test
4. **Sample selection:** How samples were chosen
5. **Results:** Pass/fail for each sample item
6. **Exceptions noted:** Any failures with root cause
7. **Conclusion:** Control is operating effectively / not effectively
8. **Tester and date:** Who performed the test and when

### Control Deficiency Response

| Severity | Definition | Response Time | Reporting |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| **Deficiency** | Control does not operate as designed but risk is low | 30 days to remediate | Internal only |
| **Significant deficiency** | Material risk not adequately controlled | 15 days to remediate | CFO and board notified |
| **Material weakness** | Reasonable possibility of material misstatement | Immediate remediation | Board, auditor, and regulators (if applicable) |

## 5. CLOSE CALENDAR AND DEADLINES

### Standard Month-End Close Calendar

| Day | Task | Owner | Deliverable |
|-----|------|-------|------------|
| Day 1 | Cutoff applied, preliminary revenue | Accounting | Revenue flash |
| Day 2 | Cash reconciliation initiated | Accounting | Bank rec draft |
| Day 3 | AR/AP reconciliation initiated | Accounting | Aging reports |
| Day 4 | Accruals and prepaid adjustments | Accounting | Journal entries |
| Day 5 | Bank reconciliation complete | Accounting | Signed bank rec |
| Day 6 | Revenue reconciliation complete | FP&A | Revenue schedule |
| Day 7 | Expense review and classification | Accounting | Classified P&L |
| Day 8 | Deferred revenue roll-forward | FP&A | Def rev schedule |
| Day 9 | Preliminary financial statements | Controller | Draft P&L, BS, CF |
| Day 10 | Variance analysis | FP&A | Variance report |
| Day 11 | Controller review | Controller | Review notes |
| Day 12 | CFO review and approval | CFO | Final financials |
| Day 13 | Close binder complete | Controller | Documentation |
| Day 14 | Financials distributed | FP&A | Board packet (if applicable) |

### Deadline Enforcement

- Close tasks not completed by deadline are escalated to CFO on the next business day
- Repeated late closes (3+ months) trigger a process review
- Close calendar compliance is tracked as a team KPI
- Target: 100% of close tasks completed on or before deadline

## 6. FINANCIAL REPORTING INTEGRITY

### Data Quality Standards

| Standard | Requirement | Verification |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| **Completeness** | All transactions recorded | Reconciliation to source systems |
| **Accuracy** | Numbers are mathematically correct | Triple-check protocol |
| **Timeliness** | Reported within close calendar | Deadline tracking |
| **Consistency** | Same methodology across periods | Policy documentation |
| **Comparability** | Meaningful period-over-period comparison | Restatement if methodology changes |

### Financial Statement Certification

Before financial statements are distributed:
1. **Controller certifies:** "All reconciliations are complete. All significant items are explained. I have reviewed the financial statements and they are accurate to the best of my knowledge."
2. **CFO certifies:** "I have reviewed the financial statements. They fairly present the financial position and results of operations. All material items have been disclosed."

### Restatement Protocol

If an error is discovered that requires restatement:
1. Quantify the error and determine materiality
2. If material: notify CFO, board, and external auditor immediately
3. Prepare restated financials with clear disclosure of the error
4. Identify the root cause and implement controls to prevent recurrence
5. Document the entire process for audit trail

## 7. SEGREGATION OF DUTIES ENFORCEMENT

### SOD Matrix

| Role | Can Process | Cannot Process | Monitored By |
|------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| AP Clerk | Enter invoices | Approve payments | Controller |
| AP Manager | Approve invoices <$10K | Process payments | CFO |
| Controller | Approve invoices >$10K | Enter invoices | CFO |
| Payroll Admin | Process payroll | Approve payroll | Controller |
| AR Clerk | Record receipts | Adjust customer accounts | Controller |
| CFO | Approve all financial transactions | Process routine transactions | CEO + Board |

### SOD Violation Protocol

1. **Detection:** Identified through quarterly access review or internal testing
2. **Documentation:** Violation logged with date, nature, and transactions affected
3. **Remediation:** Access corrected within 5 business days
4. **Review:** Transactions processed during violation period audited for accuracy
5. **Prevention:** Process updated to prevent recurrence

## 8. BOARD AND INVESTOR REPORTING ACCOUNTABILITY

### Board Reporting Cadence

| Report | Frequency | Deadline | Content |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Financial dashboard | Monthly | Day 15 after month-end | KPIs, cash, burn, runway |
| Full board packet | Quarterly | 5 business days before meeting | P&L, BS, CF, variance, forecast |
| Cash runway update | Monthly (if <12 months runway) | Day 15 after month-end | 13-week cash flow, runway calc |
| Investor update | Monthly or quarterly | Per agreement | Metrics, narrative, asks |

### Board Packet Quality Assurance

Before sending any board materials:
- [ ] All numbers verified against source data
- [ ] Narrative reviewed by CFO and CEO
- [ ] Charts accurately represent data
- [ ] Prior period comparisons are correct
- [ ] Forecast changes highlighted and explained
- [ ] Risks and opportunities are current and quantified
- [ ] Asks are specific and actionable
- [ ] Materials sent to board members at least 3 business days before meeting

## 9. CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

### Monthly Finance Team Retrospective

**When:** Last Friday of each month, 30 minutes
**Focus:** Close process, reporting quality, tool effectiveness

Questions to answer:
- What slowed us down this month?
- What errors did we catch (or miss)?
- What can be automated?
- What training does the team need?

### Quarterly Process Assessment

Every quarter, assess:
- Are close deadlines being met consistently?
- Are reconciliation variances trending down?
- Is audit readiness improving?
- Are board materials requiring fewer revisions?
- Are control testing results improving?

### Annual Framework Review

Once per year, review the entire accountability framework:
- Are we measuring the right things?
- Are thresholds appropriate?
- Has the business changed in ways that require new controls?
- What should be added, removed, or adjusted?
- Benchmark against industry best practices

## 10. ESCALATION FRAMEWORK

### When Finance Must Escalate

| Situation | Escalate To | Timeline |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Cash runway drops below 6 months | CEO + Board | Within 48 hours |
| Material error discovered in reported financials | CFO + Board + Auditor | Within 24 hours |
| Fraud suspected | CFO + CEO + Legal + Board | Immediately |
| Revenue tracking >20% below plan | CEO | Within 1 week of identification |
| Significant control failure | CFO + Audit Committee | Within 48 hours |
| Regulatory inquiry received | CFO + Legal + CEO | Within 24 hours |
| Covenant violation (or near-violation) | CFO + CEO + Board | Within 48 hours |

### Escalation Documentation

Every escalation must include:
1. What is the issue (facts only)
2. When was it discovered
3. What is the potential impact (quantified)
4. What has been done so far
5. What decision or action is needed
6. Recommended course of action

---

## ENFORCEMENT RULE

Financial accountability is non-negotiable.
Every protocol in this document is mandatory.
Non-compliance is tracked, documented, and addressed.
The goal is not perfection. The goal is trustworthy numbers, every time.

"I did not have time" is not an acceptable reason for skipping controls.
"It is immaterial" must be quantified, not assumed.
"We will fix it next month" must include a specific plan.

Finance is the language of business. If the language is unreliable, every decision built on it is suspect.

# SaaS Benchmarks — Performance Standards by Stage, T2D3, and Public Comps

## Overview

Benchmarks provide the context required to evaluate whether a SaaS company's
performance is strong, average, or concerning relative to peers. Without benchmarks,
a 30% growth rate is meaningless -- it is excellent at $100M ARR but weak at $5M
ARR. This module provides comprehensive benchmarks organized by ARR scale, the
T2D3 growth framework, efficiency and margin benchmarks, and methods for
constructing public company comparable sets for valuation.

References: Bessemer Venture Partners (Cloud Index), SaaS Capital (Private SaaS
Company Survey), OpenView (SaaS Benchmarks Report), Meritech Capital (Public
SaaS Comps), KeyBanc Capital Markets (SaaS Survey), Redpoint (GTM benchmarks).

## T2D3 Framework

### The Growth Expectation

```
T2D3: Triple, Triple, Double, Double, Double

Year  Revenue    Growth   Multiple   Running Product
0     $2M        -        -          $2M
1     $6M        200%     3x         3x
2     $18M       200%     3x         9x
3     $36M       100%     2x         18x
4     $72M       100%     2x         36x
5     $144M      100%     2x         72x

From $2M to $144M in 5 years = 72x growth
This pace is required for a $1B+ outcome from early-stage VC returns math.
```

### T2D3 Reality Check

```
Companies that achieved T2D3 (approximate):
  - Slack: $2M -> $200M ARR in 4 years
  - Zoom: $2M -> $330M ARR in 5 years
  - Snowflake: $2M -> $264M ARR in 5 years
  - Datadog: $2M -> $100M ARR in 4 years

Companies that scaled well but below T2D3:
  - Most successful SaaS companies
  - 40-100% YoY growth sustained for 5+ years
  - Still produce excellent investor returns

T2D3 is aspirational. A company growing 60-80% YoY at
$10-50M ARR is performing very well.
```

### Growth Rate Decay (Natural)

```
Growth rates naturally decay as companies scale:

Year    ARR       Growth    Notes
1       $3M       200%      Small base, rapid experimentation
2       $8M       167%      PMF confirmed, scaling GTM
3       $18M      125%      Sales team scaling, multi-channel
4       $35M      94%       Category awareness, enterprise motion
5       $60M      71%       Market maturity, competition
6       $90M      50%       Scale challenges, operational focus
7       $120M     33%       Pre-IPO, efficiency focus
8       $150M     25%       Public company, predictable growth

This is a strong trajectory even though growth decays.
```

### Burn Multiple by Scale

| ARR Scale | Excellent | Good | Concerning | Alarming |
|-----------|----------|------|-----------|---------|
| $1-5M | < 1.5x | 1.5-2.5x | 2.5-4x | > 4x |
| $5-20M | < 1.0x | 1.0-2.0x | 2.0-3x | > 3x |
| $20-50M | < 0.8x | 0.8-1.5x | 1.5-2.5x | > 2.5x |
| $50M+ | < 0.5x | 0.5-1.0x | 1.0-2.0x | > 2.0x |

### Sales Efficiency

```
Magic Number benchmarks:
  > 1.5x:  Highly efficient, scale aggressively
  1.0-1.5x: Efficient, continue scaling
  0.75-1.0x: Moderate, optimize channels
  0.5-0.75x: Below average, investigate
  < 0.5x:  Inefficient, fix before scaling

CAC Payback benchmarks:
  < 6 months:  Exceptional (rare)
  6-12 months: Excellent
  12-18 months: Good
  18-24 months: Acceptable (early stage)
  > 24 months: Concerning

S&M as % of revenue (by growth rate):
  > 100% growth: 80-120% of revenue (acceptable at hypergrowth)
  50-100% growth: 40-60% of revenue
  25-50% growth: 30-40% of revenue
  < 25% growth: 20-30% of revenue
```

## Public Company Comparable Analysis

### Constructing a Comp Set

```
Selection criteria:
  1. Same business model (SaaS subscription)
  2. Similar end-market (vertical or horizontal)
  3. Similar scale ($0.5-2x revenue of target)
  4. Similar growth profile (+/- 15pp)
  5. Similar margin profile (gross and operating)
  6. Geographic overlap

Number of comps: 5-15 companies (too few = unreliable, too many = diluted)
```

### Valuation Multiples

```
EV/NTM Revenue (Next Twelve Months):
  High-growth (> 40%): 10-20x
  Medium-growth (20-40%): 5-12x
  Low-growth (< 20%): 3-7x

Growth-adjusted multiple:
  EV/NTM Rev / NTM Growth = PEG-like ratio
  Target: 0.5-1.0x (cheaper is more attractive)

Example:
  Company A: 15x revenue, 50% growth = 0.30x (cheap)
  Company B: 12x revenue, 20% growth = 0.60x (fair)
  Company C: 8x revenue, 10% growth = 0.80x (expensive)
```

## Applying Benchmarks

### Diagnostic Framework

```
Step 1: Plot your metrics against stage-appropriate benchmarks
Step 2: Identify metrics where you are below median
Step 3: Diagnose root causes for underperforming metrics
Step 4: Prioritize improvements by impact on valuation

Valuation sensitivity (from public SaaS regression):
  Revenue growth: 1pp increase ≈ 0.3-0.5x increase in EV/Rev multiple
  NRR: 1pp increase ≈ 0.2-0.4x increase in EV/Rev multiple
  FCF margin: 1pp increase ≈ 0.1-0.2x increase in EV/Rev multiple
  Rule of 40: crossing 40% threshold ≈ step-function increase

Implication: growth is the most valuable lever, followed by retention.
```

# Investor Relations — Updates, Board Management, and Bridge Financing

## Overview

Investor relations (IR) is the ongoing management of the relationship between
a startup and its investors. Effective IR builds trust through consistent
transparency, manages expectations through structured communication, and ensures
that investors are aligned advocates rather than adversaries. This module covers
monthly investor updates, board meeting preparation, expectation management during
difficult periods, and the mechanics of bridge financing when needed.

References: Feld & Mendelson (Venture Deals), Wilson (MBA Mondays: Board Best
Practices), Lerner et al. (Venture Capital and Private Equity), YC Startup
Library (Investor Updates), First Round Review (Board Meeting Best Practices).

## Monthly Investor Updates

### Purpose

Monthly updates serve three critical functions:
1. **Accountability**: forces management to reflect on progress
2. **Trust building**: consistent transparency builds investor confidence
3. **Resource activation**: investors can help when they know what you need

### Update Structure

```
Subject: [Company Name] — [Month] Update

1. HEADLINE
   One sentence: the most important thing that happened.

2. KEY METRICS (dashboard format)
   ┌──────────────┬──────────┬──────────┬──────────┐
   │ Metric       │ Current  │ Prior Mo │ Target   │
   ├──────────────┼──────────┼──────────┼──────────┤
   │ MRR          │ $XXX,XXX │ $XXX,XXX │ $XXX,XXX │
   │ MRR Growth   │ XX%      │ XX%      │ XX%      │
   │ Customers    │ XXX      │ XXX      │ XXX      │
   │ NRR          │ XXX%     │ XXX%     │ >110%    │
   │ Burn Rate    │ $XXX,XXX │ $XXX,XXX │ $XXX,XXX │
   │ Runway       │ XX mo    │ XX mo    │ >12 mo   │
   │ Cash Balance │ $X.XM    │ $X.XM    │ >$X.XM   │
   │ Headcount    │ XX       │ XX       │ XX       │
   └──────────────┴──────────┴──────────┴──────────┘

3. WINS (2-3 bullets)
   - [Win 1: specific achievement with quantification]
   - [Win 2: specific achievement with quantification]

4. CHALLENGES (2-3 bullets, be honest)
   - [Challenge 1: what happened, what you are doing about it]
   - [Challenge 2: what happened, what you are doing about it]

5. KEY DECISIONS / LEARNING
   [One significant decision or insight this month]

6. ASKS (1-3 specific requests)
   - [Ask 1: "intro to [specific company/person] for [specific reason]"]
   - [Ask 2: "feedback on [specific document/plan]"]
   - [Ask 3: "candidate referrals for [specific role]"]

7. NEXT MONTH PRIORITIES
   - [Priority 1]
   - [Priority 2]
   - [Priority 3]
```

### Update Best Practices

| Practice | Rationale |
|----------|----------|
| Send on the same day each month | Builds cadence and expectation |
| Keep to 1 page / 5 minute read | Busy investors will actually read it |
| Bad news first | Builds trust, shows self-awareness |
| Include specific asks | Investors want to help but need direction |
| Send even in bad months | Silence creates more anxiety than bad news |
| BCC all investors | Prevents reply-all chains |
| Track who responds | Identify engaged vs passive investors |

### Update Frequency by Stage

| Stage | Update Frequency | Depth |
|-------|-----------------|-------|
| Pre-seed | Monthly | Brief (5 metrics + narrative) |
| Seed | Monthly | Standard (template above) |
| Series A | Monthly | Detailed (add departmental updates) |
| Series B+ | Monthly to board, quarterly to all | Comprehensive |

## Board Meetings

### Preparation Timeline

```
T-3 weeks:  Draft board deck and circulate outline to board members
T-2 weeks:  Finalize board deck with latest metrics
T-1 week:   Send board package (deck + financials + materials)
T-2 days:   Confirm attendance, prep CEO talking points
T-day:      Board meeting (2-3 hours)
T+2 days:   Send board minutes and action items
T+1 week:   Follow up on action items from the meeting
```

### Board Deck Structure

```
1. Executive Summary (1 slide)
   - Key highlights, critical decisions needed

2. Key Metrics Dashboard (2-3 slides)
   - Financial: ARR, MRR, burn, runway, cash
   - Product: engagement, retention, NPS
   - Go-to-market: pipeline, win rate, CAC, LTV

3. Financial Review (2-3 slides)
   - P&L: budget vs actual
   - Cash flow: 13-week rolling forecast
   - Headcount plan vs actual

4. Strategic Topics (3-5 slides)
   - Deep dive on 1-2 strategic topics
   - These are discussion topics, not presentations
   - Example: "Should we enter the enterprise segment?"

5. Team and Organization (1 slide)
   - Key hires, departures, org changes
   - Hiring pipeline for critical roles

6. Decisions Needed (1 slide)
   - Clearly state what you need from the board
   - Provide recommended action and alternatives

7. Appendix (as needed)
   - Detailed financials
   - Product roadmap
   - Competitive landscape
```

### Meeting Facilitation

```
Time allocation (3-hour meeting):
  - Administrative (minutes, approvals):    15 min
  - Metrics and financial review:           30 min
  - Strategic topic 1 (discussion):         45 min
  - Strategic topic 2 (discussion):         45 min
  - Closed session (CEO excluded):          15 min
  - CEO session (CEO + board, no mgmt):     15 min
  - Next steps and action items:            15 min
```

### Board Management Rules

**Do:**
- No surprises. If something is bad, share it before the meeting
- Send materials 1 week ahead so board members arrive prepared
- Use board meetings for strategic discussions, not operational updates
- Ask board members for specific help (recruiting, intros, advice)
- Document decisions and action items with owners and deadlines

**Do Not:**
- Present 50 slides of operational detail
- Avoid difficult topics or bury bad news
- Let one board member dominate the conversation
- Make the meeting a one-way presentation
- Wait until the board meeting to surface disagreements

## Managing Expectations

### Setting the Right Tone

```
At the time of investment:
  - Align on 12-18 month milestones that justify the next raise
  - Agree on the metrics that matter (and which do not yet)
  - Set expectations for burn rate and hiring pace
  - Discuss what "good" looks like for the next board meeting

Ongoing:
  - Share forecasts with a range (bull/base/bear)
  - Update forecasts as reality deviates
  - Reframe milestones if the strategy changes
  - Never promise what you cannot deliver
```

### When Things Go Wrong

```
Step 1: Diagnose (before communicating)
  - What happened and why?
  - Is it temporary or structural?
  - What is the impact on runway and milestones?
  - What are the options?

Step 2: Communicate proactively (do NOT wait for the board meeting)
  - Call lead investor first
  - Share the facts, your assessment, and your plan
  - Be honest about what you do not know

Step 3: Present options (not just problems)
  - Option A: [action, impact, timeline]
  - Option B: [action, impact, timeline]
  - Recommended: [which option and why]

Step 4: Execute and report
  - Implement the agreed plan
  - Report progress in weekly updates during crisis
  - Return to monthly cadence when stabilized
```

### Difficult Conversations

| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|---------|
| Missing revenue target | Share early, explain root cause, present recovery plan |
| Key executive departure | Inform board immediately, present succession/hiring plan |
| Competitor threat | Assess real impact, differentiate, adjust strategy |
| Burn exceeding plan | Present revised budget, identify cuts, discuss bridge |
| Pivot discussion | Frame data-driven rationale, propose pilot before full pivot |
| Need to do layoffs | Present financial rationale, legal plan, communication plan |

## Bridge Financing

### When Bridges Are Appropriate

```
Appropriate:
  - Clear milestone 2-3 months away that will unlock full round
  - Market timing is temporarily unfavorable
  - Unexpected one-time event delayed fundraising
  - Strategic acquisition in progress (need time to close)

Red flags:
  - Second or third bridge (signals inability to raise)
  - No clear milestone that changes the fundraising dynamic
  - Bridge to "figure things out"
  - Existing investors unwilling to participate
```

### Bridge Mechanics

**Instrument Options:**

| Instrument | Terms | Complexity |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| SAFE | Post-money cap, no interest/maturity | Simplest |
| Convertible note | Cap, 20-25% discount, 2% interest, 12mo maturity | Standard |
| Extension of last round | Same terms as previous round | Simple if pro-rata |
| MFN note | Converts on terms of next qualified financing | Flexible |

**Sizing a Bridge:**

```
Bridge Amount = Monthly Burn * Months to Close Next Round + Buffer

Example:
  Monthly burn: $300K
  Months to milestone: 3
  Fundraising process: 3 months after milestone
  Buffer: 1 month
  Bridge needed: $300K * 7 = $2.1M → raise $2-2.5M
```

### Bridge Negotiation

| Term | Founder Position | Investor Position |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Valuation cap | Same as last round | Discount to last round |
| Discount | 15-20% | 25-30% |
| Conversion | Into next qualified round | Auto-convert at maturity |
| Participation | All existing investors | Pro-rata or super pro-rata |
| New investors | Welcome (validates company) | May resist (dilutive) |

### Bridge Communication

```
To existing investors (proactive outreach):

"We are [X] months away from [milestone] that we believe will enable
our Series [X] raise. We are seeking a $[X]M bridge to reach this
milestone. The bridge will be structured as a [SAFE/note] at [terms].

Here is what has changed since our last update:
- [Progress point 1]
- [Progress point 2]

Here is the milestone plan:
- Month 1: [specific milestone]
- Month 2: [specific milestone]
- Month 3: [specific milestone = fundraise trigger]

We are asking each existing investor to participate at their pro-rata.
[Lead investor] has committed $[X]. We need $[Y] more to close.

Can we schedule a 30-minute call this week to discuss?"
```

## Investor CRM

### Tracking System

| Field | Description |
|-------|------------|
| Investor name | Fund and partner name |
| Contact info | Email, phone, assistant |
| Relationship stage | Lead, contact, 1st meeting, 2nd meeting, term sheet, closed |
| Investment amount | Amount invested per round |
| Ownership % | Current ownership |
| Pro-rata rights | Yes/no, amount |
| Board seat | Yes/no |
| Engagement score | High/medium/low (based on responsiveness, help) |
| Next touchpoint | Scheduled meeting/call date |
| Asks pending | Outstanding requests |
| Notes | Key relationship context |

### Engagement Tracking

```
Scoring system:
  +3: Provided a warm intro that led to a meeting/deal
  +2: Responded to update with actionable feedback
  +1: Responded to update with acknowledgment
   0: No response to update
  -1: Unresponsive to time-sensitive request
  -2: Created unnecessary friction in governance

Top engaged investors: prioritize for follow-on, ask for intros
Low engaged investors: reduce update detail, reconsider for future rounds
```

## Annual Investor Meeting

### Purpose

Annual all-hands investor meeting (in-person or virtual) for:
- Year in review and key accomplishments
- Financial performance against plan
- Strategy for the coming year
- Open Q&A
- Networking among investors

### Format

```
Duration: 2-3 hours

Agenda:
  1. Welcome and introductions (10 min)
  2. Year in review: metrics and milestones (20 min)
  3. Product demo: what is new (15 min)
  4. Customer story / testimonial (10 min)
  5. Financial review (15 min)
  6. Strategy and roadmap for next year (20 min)
  7. Q&A (30 min)
  8. Networking / informal discussion (30 min)
```

# Support Brain -- Accountability Protocol (Authoritative)

This document defines how the Support Brain is held accountable for the quality,
consistency, and customer impact of its support operations. Accountability is not optional.

Support that is not accountable is firefighting dressed as customer service.

---

## CORE ACCOUNTABILITY PRINCIPLES

1. **Every customer interaction has a quality standard.** No ticket is exempt from quality.
2. **Every SLA commitment is a promise.** Breaking SLAs without explanation is a breach of trust.
3. **Every escalation is a learning opportunity.** Repeated escalations for the same issue is a process failure.
4. **Every piece of customer feedback must be heard.** Feedback ignored is a customer abandoned.
5. **Every agent deserves support to do their job well.** Agent burnout is a leadership failure.

## ACCOUNTABILITY TIERS

### Tier 1: Daily Operational Accountability

Every day, the support operation must verify:

| Requirement | Owner | Verification |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| SLA compliance monitored in real-time | Support Lead | Dashboard check every 2 hours |
| Queue health is within acceptable range | Support Lead | Queue size < 2x normal |
| Critical tickets identified and prioritized | Support Lead | P1 tickets flagged within 15 minutes |
| Agent coverage matches demand | Support Lead | Staffing adjusted if queue exceeds threshold |
| Known issues communicated to customers | Support Lead | Status page current within 30 minutes |
| Escalation path clear and functioning | Support Lead | At least 1 escalation contact reachable |

**Gate:** If any daily accountability item fails, the Support Lead must act immediately. Not at end of day. Now.

### Tier 2: Weekly Operational Accountability

Every week, the support operation must review:

| Requirement | Owner | Verification |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| CSAT trend reviewed | Support Lead | Week-over-week comparison |
| FCR analyzed for patterns | Support Lead | Top non-FCR reasons identified |
| Escalation patterns reviewed | Support Lead | Repeat escalation reasons flagged |
| QA scores reviewed with agents | Support Lead + Agents | 1:1 coaching where needed |
| Top ticket topics documented | Support Lead | VoC report drafted |
| KB gaps identified from ticket analysis | Support Lead | Content backlog updated |
| Agent workload balance checked | Support Lead | No agent >120% or <60% utilization |

**Gate:** Weekly review must produce specific actions. "Everything looks fine" is not an acceptable weekly review output.

### Tier 3: Monthly Strategic Accountability

Every month, the support operation must assess:

| Requirement | Owner | Verification |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| Full SupportScore.md evaluation | Support Lead + Reviewer | All 8 dimensions scored |
| VoC report delivered to product team | Support Lead | Shared with documented acknowledgment |
| Top 5 customer issues tracked vs last month | Support Lead | Trend chart updated |
| Agent performance reviews completed | Support Lead | Individual scorecards current |
| Automation and deflection metrics reviewed | Support Lead | Month-over-month comparison |
| Customer effort score analyzed | Support Lead | CES trend by category |
| Budget vs actuals reviewed | Support Lead | Cost per ticket trending |
| Process improvements from previous month verified | Support Lead | Action items from last month checked |

**Gate:** Monthly assessment must compare to previous month. Flat performance requires explanation. Declining performance requires an action plan.

### Tier 4: Quarterly Executive Accountability

Every quarter, the support operation must present:

| Requirement | Owner | Audience |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| Quarterly Support Health Report | Support Lead | VP/C-Level |
| Customer impact narrative (not just metrics) | Support Lead | Leadership team |
| Competitive benchmark comparison | Support Lead | Leadership team |
| Strategic improvement plan (next quarter) | Support Lead | Leadership team |
| Budget and headcount forecast | Support Lead | Finance |
| Technology and tool assessment | Support Lead | Engineering/IT |

## SLA ACCOUNTABILITY

### SLA as Promise

SLA targets are commitments to customers, not aspirational goals.

| SLA Metric | Commitment | Accountability |
|------------|-----------|----------------|
| First Response Time | Per channel targets | Breach requires same-day root cause |
| Resolution Time | Per priority level | Breach requires customer communication |
| CSAT | >= 90% | Drop below 85% triggers intervention |
| FCR | >= 80% | Drop below 75% triggers process review |
| Availability | Per channel coverage hours | Gap in coverage requires immediate staffing action |

### SLA Breach Protocol

When an SLA is breached:

1. **Immediate:** Customer notified with updated timeline (within 1 hour)
2. **Same day:** Root cause identified and documented
3. **Within 48 hours:** Prevention measure proposed
4. **Within 1 week:** Prevention measure implemented or escalated
5. **Monthly:** All breaches reviewed for patterns

### Chronic SLA Failure

If the same SLA is breached > 5% of the time for 2 consecutive months:
- Escalate to leadership
- Root cause analysis required
- Improvement plan with weekly tracking
- SLA target adjustment considered only after process improvements exhausted

## QUALITY ASSURANCE ACCOUNTABILITY

### QA Program Requirements

| Requirement | Standard | Frequency |
|-------------|----------|-----------|
| Tickets reviewed per agent | Minimum 5/month (L1-L2), 3/month (L3-L4) | Monthly |
| QA scoring rubric | Documented and calibrated | Updated quarterly |
| Calibration sessions | Cross-reviewer alignment | Monthly |
| Agent coaching from QA | Written feedback + discussion | After every QA batch |
| QA trend analysis | Improving or stable | Monthly |
| Customer-QA correlation | QA scores predict CSAT | Validated quarterly |

### QA Scoring Rubric

| Category | Weight | What to Evaluate |
|----------|--------|------------------|
| Accuracy | 30% | Was information correct? Was the root cause identified? |
| Completeness | 25% | Was the full issue resolved? Were edge cases addressed? |
| Tone and Empathy | 20% | Was the customer treated with respect and understanding? |
| Process Adherence | 15% | Were internal procedures followed correctly? |
| Documentation | 10% | Was the ticket properly categorized, tagged, and noted? |

### QA Failure Protocol

If an agent's QA score drops below 75%:
1. Immediate coaching session (within 2 business days)
2. Targeted training on weak areas
3. Increased QA sample (100% for 2 weeks)
4. Re-evaluation after 2 weeks
5. Performance improvement plan if no improvement

## CUSTOMER IMPACT TRACKING

### Customer Impact is the Primary Metric

Every support decision must be evaluated through its impact on customers.

### Customer Impact Dashboard

| Metric | What It Tells Us | Action Threshold |
|--------|-----------------|------------------|
| CSAT | Are customers satisfied right now? | < 85% = intervention |
| CES | How hard do customers have to work? | > 4/7 = process review |
| FCR | Do we fix it the first time? | < 75% = root cause analysis |
| Reopen Rate | Do fixes actually stick? | > 8% = quality audit |
| Escalation Rate | Are front-line agents effective? | > 12% = training review |
| Time to Resolution | How long do customers wait? | > 2x SLA = staffing review |
| NPS (support-influenced) | Do support interactions build loyalty? | < 30 = strategic review |

### Customer Story Reviews

Monthly, the team reviews:
- 3 tickets where the customer had an exceptional experience (what worked?)
- 3 tickets where the customer had a poor experience (what failed?)
- 1 ticket that represents a systemic issue (what needs to change?)

These reviews are about learning, not blame.

## AGENT ACCOUNTABILITY AND SUPPORT

### Agent Performance Framework

Agents are accountable for quality within a system that supports them.

| Agent Responsibility | Organization Responsibility |
|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Accurate responses | Accurate knowledge base and training |
| Empathetic tone | Reasonable workload and break policy |
| Timely responses | Adequate staffing and tools |
| Proper documentation | Clear documentation standards and templates |
| Continuous learning | Training time allocated and protected |
| Escalation when needed | Clear escalation paths and responsive specialists |

### Agent Burnout Prevention

Support burnout is a leadership failure, not an agent weakness.

| Warning Signal | Detection Method | Response |
|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| QA score drop | Monthly QA review | Coaching, workload check |
| CSAT drop for specific agent | Weekly agent CSAT review | 1:1 check-in |
| Increased handle time | Weekly metrics review | Complexity audit, training |
| Absenteeism increase | Monthly attendance review | Private conversation, support |
| Negative self-reports | Agent satisfaction survey | Management action |
| Decreased engagement | Manager observation | 1:1, role adjustment |

### Response Protocol
When burnout signals detected:
1. Private, supportive conversation (not punitive)
2. Workload assessment and adjustment
3. Temporary assignment change if needed
4. Follow-up within 1 week
5. Long-term prevention measures (rotation, variety, development)

## ESCALATION ACCOUNTABILITY

### Escalation Is Not Failure

Escalation is a tool, not a weakness. But chronic escalation of the same issues is a process failure.

### Escalation Tracking

| Metric | Target | Owner |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Escalation rate | < 8% | Support Lead |
| Average escalation resolution time | < 4 hours | Specialist team |
| Escalation reason documentation | 100% | Escalating agent |
| Repeat escalation rate (same issue type) | Declining monthly | Support Lead |
| Escalation satisfaction (customer) | >= 85% | Specialist team |

### Repeat Escalation Protocol

When the same issue type is escalated 3+ times in a month:
1. Root cause analysis of the escalation pattern
2. Knowledge transfer from specialist to front-line (training or KB article)
3. Empowerment assessment (can front-line agents be authorized to resolve this?)
4. Process change if needed
5. Verify reduction in next month

## VOC FEEDBACK LOOP ACCOUNTABILITY

### The Feedback Loop Must Close

Collecting feedback without acting on it is worse than not collecting it.

### VoC Accountability Chain

| Step | Owner | SLA |
|------|-------|-----|
| Feedback categorized | Support agent | Same ticket |
| Weekly VoC report generated | Support Lead | Every Monday |
| Report delivered to product | Support Lead | Every Tuesday |
| Product acknowledgment | Product Lead | Within 48 hours |
| Priority assigned to reported issues | Product Lead | Within 1 week |
| Fix timeline communicated back to support | Product Lead | Within 2 weeks |
| Customer notified when issue fixed | Support team | Within 48 hours of fix |

### VoC Accountability Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Feedback-to-report time | < 1 week | Weekly VoC cadence |
| Product acknowledgment rate | 100% | Every report acknowledged |
| Top 5 issues age | Declining | Months since first reported |
| Issue fix rate | >= 50% in 90 days | Reported issues fixed within 90 days |
| Closed-loop rate | >= 30% | Customers notified of fixes |

### When the Loop Breaks

If product does not acknowledge VoC reports for 2 consecutive weeks:
1. Escalate to product leadership
2. Quantify business impact (cost of tickets, churn risk)
3. Request joint meeting
4. If unresolved, escalate to VP/C-level

The Support Brain does not silently accept an open feedback loop.

## INCIDENT ACCOUNTABILITY

### Every Incident Gets a Review

No incident passes without learning.

| Incident Severity | Post-Mortem Required | Timeline |
|-------------------|---------------------|----------|
| Critical (outage > 1 hour, > 100 users) | Full post-mortem | Within 3 business days |
| Major (degradation > 4 hours, > 50 users) | Light post-mortem | Within 5 business days |
| Minor (limited impact, < 50 users) | Documented notes | Within 1 week |

### Post-Mortem Requirements
- Timeline reconstruction (minute by minute for critical)
- Customer impact assessment (who was affected, how)
- Support response evaluation (what worked, what did not)
- Communication effectiveness (was it timely, accurate, empathetic?)
- Root cause (technical and operational)
- Action items with owners and deadlines
- Follow-up scheduled for action item verification

## ACCOUNTABILITY IS NOT MICROMANAGEMENT

This protocol exists to create clarity, not to create fear.

- Clear expectations reduce anxiety, not increase it
- Metrics serve learning, not punishment
- Failures documented honestly prevent recurrence
- Agent accountability requires organizational accountability first
- The system must support the people before holding them accountable

The Support Brain that holds itself accountable earns customer trust.
The Support Brain that does not is just a ticket queue.

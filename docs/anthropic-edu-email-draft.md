**What We're Building**

AI Creator Bootcamp is a 10-session, project-based learning program where high school students (9th–12th grade) learn to create professional-quality content using industry-leading AI tools. Sessions are 90 minutes each, meeting twice a week over five weeks — structured to fit into the school's CAP (College & Career Academy) program.

This isn't a passive intro to AI. Students leave with a real portfolio. They produce videos, music, images, and digital media from scratch using Claude, Runway, ElevenLabs, Suno, Midjourney, Canva AI, and more. By the final session, every student completes a capstone project and walks away with proficiency in 8+ AI creation tools and tangible, monetizable skills.

**Curriculum at a Glance:**
- Weeks 1–2: AI Fundamentals & Image Generation (prompting, Claude, Midjourney, Canva AI)
- Weeks 3–4: AI Video & Audio Creation (Runway, Kling, Higgsfield, ElevenLabs, Suno)
- Weeks 5–6: Content Strategy & Platform Mastery (short-form video, captions, viral hooks)
- Weeks 7–8: Personal Brand Development (brand identity, monetization, sponsorship pitching)
- Weeks 9–10: Capstone Project & Demo Day (signature video project + certificate ceremony)

**Why This Matters — and Why Anthropic**

The creator economy is a $250B+ industry. AI tools are reshaping every layer of it — video production, music, graphic design, brand strategy. But access to that knowledge is not equally distributed.

Westchester High School is a Title I school. Most students there have never used Claude. They haven't used Runway or ElevenLabs. These tools are changing what it means to be a professional creative, and our students are being left out of the conversation entirely.

Anthropic's mission is the responsible development of AI for the long-term benefit of humanity. We believe that includes high school students in South Los Angeles learning to use Claude to write scripts, generate ideas, and build things they're proud of — not just reading about AI in the news.

Claude is central to our curriculum. We use it for creative writing, scripting, prompt engineering, and conceptual brainstorming. We teach students how to interact with it effectively, critically, and responsibly. We believe Claude is the right tool to anchor this program — both because of its capabilities and because of the values baked into how it works.

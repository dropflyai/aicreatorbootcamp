# Video Brain -- Review Checklist

Version: 1.0
Last Updated: 2026-02-03
Owner: Video Brain
Status: ACTIVE

---

## Purpose

This checklist is the mandatory quality gate for all video content production,
from pre-production planning through post-publish optimization. No video is
published without passing the relevant sections. Every item is required.
If an item does not apply, it must be marked "N/A" with written justification.

Video is the most expensive content format per unit. Shipping a bad video is
not "learning" -- it is wasted production budget that could have been caught
in review. This checklist exists to catch problems before they cost money.

---

## SECTION 1: Pre-Production Review

### 1.1 Content Brief

- [ ] Written content brief exists before any production work begins
- [ ] Brief includes: objective, target audience, platform(s), video type, desired length
- [ ] Brief includes: key messages (maximum 3 per video)
- [ ] Brief includes: primary CTA and secondary CTA
- [ ] Brief includes: target keywords (for SEO-focused content)
- [ ] Brief includes: reference videos (minimum 2 examples of desired style/quality)
- [ ] Brief includes: success metrics and targets
- [ ] Brief includes: budget allocation for this production
- [ ] Brief approved by content lead before production begins
- [ ] Brief shared with entire production team (not just director)

### 1.2 Script/Outline

- [ ] Script or detailed outline written and reviewed
- [ ] Hook identified and written out (first 3-30 seconds depending on format)
- [ ] Hook uses an identifiable hook framework (curiosity gap, pattern interrupt, etc.)
- [ ] Key messages clearly placed within script structure
- [ ] CTA placement determined (in-video, end screen, description)
- [ ] Script pacing reviewed (reading aloud takes appropriate time for target length)
- [ ] Filler content identified and removed (anything that does not serve the hook, message, or CTA)
- [ ] Transitions between sections are logical and smooth
- [ ] Script reviewed for brand voice compliance
- [ ] Legal/compliance review completed if claims or data are cited
- [ ] Script includes direction for b-roll, graphics, and visual aids

### 1.3 Production Planning

- [ ] Shoot location scouted or confirmed (lighting, noise, background)
- [ ] Equipment list confirmed and tested (camera, mic, lighting, teleprompter)
- [ ] Backup equipment available for critical shoots
- [ ] Talent confirmed and briefed on script, wardrobe, and schedule
- [ ] Wardrobe aligned with brand guidelines and set environment
- [ ] Shot list created for multi-angle or multi-location shoots
- [ ] Production schedule shared with all participants (call time, wrap time)
- [ ] Prop and product samples confirmed available
- [ ] Release forms prepared for any non-employee talent or locations
- [ ] Catering/refreshments arranged for shoots longer than 3 hours

### 1.4 Technical Setup

- [ ] Camera settings verified (resolution minimum 1080p, frame rate appropriate)
- [ ] Audio setup tested with recording playback reviewed before main shoot
- [ ] Lighting setup tested and consistent across shooting area
- [ ] Teleprompter or cue cards positioned at correct eye-line
- [ ] Background checked for unintended items or branding conflicts
- [ ] Memory cards formatted and sufficient storage confirmed
- [ ] Battery levels checked and chargers accessible
- [ ] Backup recording device active (redundancy for critical shoots)
- [ ] Color chart/white balance reference captured at start
- [ ] Test clip recorded and reviewed before starting main content

## SECTION 2: Production Review (During Shoot)

### 2.1 Performance Quality

- [ ] Talent delivery matches brief (energy level, tone, pace)
- [ ] Key messages delivered clearly and naturally
- [ ] Hook delivered with appropriate energy and conviction
- [ ] CTA delivered naturally (not obviously "salesy" unless that is the intent)
- [ ] Multiple takes captured for key sections (minimum 2 takes for hook)
- [ ] Director providing real-time feedback and adjustments
- [ ] Moments of authentic reaction captured (not everything needs to be scripted)

### 2.2 Technical Monitoring

- [ ] Audio levels monitored throughout shoot (headphone monitoring mandatory)
- [ ] Focus checked between takes
- [ ] Lighting consistency maintained (especially for multi-take and multi-day shoots)
- [ ] Framing consistent between takes (for seamless editing)
- [ ] Background noise level acceptable
- [ ] Footage spot-checked during breaks (do not discover issues in post)
- [ ] All shot list items captured before wrap

### 2.3 B-Roll and Supplementary

- [ ] B-roll shot list captured (product close-ups, environment, reactions)
- [ ] B-roll captured at same quality settings as main footage
- [ ] Behind-the-scenes content captured for social repurposing
- [ ] Separate audio recorded for voiceover if needed
- [ ] Photo stills captured for thumbnail and social promotion

## SECTION 3: Post-Production Review

### 3.1 Edit Assembly

- [ ] Best takes selected and assembled in timeline
- [ ] Hook section assembled first and reviewed for punch
- [ ] Video length within target range specified in brief
- [ ] Pacing appropriate for content type and platform
- [ ] Dead air and unnecessary pauses removed
- [ ] Jump cuts intentional, not accidental
- [ ] B-roll placed purposefully to illustrate points (not as wallpaper)
- [ ] Transitions consistent throughout video
- [ ] Music selection appropriate (mood, energy, genre matches content)
- [ ] Music does not compete with voice (ducked during speech)
- [ ] Sound effects used purposefully (not gratuitously)

### 3.2 Audio Post-Production

- [ ] Audio levels normalized (target -14 LUFS for YouTube, -16 for broadcast)
- [ ] Background noise reduction applied where needed
- [ ] Voice EQ applied for clarity (remove muddiness, enhance presence)
- [ ] De-essing applied if sibilance is present
- [ ] Audio sync verified across all clips
- [ ] No audio pops, clicks, or artifacts remaining
- [ ] Music licensed appropriately (no copyright strike risk)
- [ ] Music credit prepared if required by license

### 3.3 Visual Post-Production

- [ ] Color correction applied for consistency across clips
- [ ] Color grade applied matching brand style or video mood
- [ ] Lower thirds and graphics match brand guidelines
- [ ] Text on screen readable at mobile viewing size
- [ ] Text on screen displayed long enough to read (minimum 3 seconds)
- [ ] Logo placement per brand guidelines
- [ ] No stray frames or black flashes between cuts
- [ ] Aspect ratio correct for target platform
- [ ] Safe zones checked (no critical content in crop areas)
- [ ] End screen elements placed and linked correctly

### 3.4 Accessibility

- [ ] Closed captions created (not just auto-generated)
- [ ] Captions reviewed for accuracy (minimum 98% accuracy)
- [ ] Captions timed correctly to speech
- [ ] Caption font and size readable on mobile
- [ ] Audio descriptions included for key visual-only information (if applicable)
- [ ] Contrast ratio sufficient for text overlays (WCAG AA minimum)
- [ ] No content conveyed solely through color
- [ ] Flashing content assessed for photosensitivity risk

### 3.5 Quality Control Playback

- [ ] Full video watched start to finish without interruption
- [ ] Watched on desktop at full screen
- [ ] Watched on mobile device at actual phone size
- [ ] Watched with headphones (audio quality check)
- [ ] Watched with speakers (audio quality check at moderate volume)
- [ ] Watched without sound (does visual storytelling work alone?)
- [ ] Second person reviews independently (not just the editor)
- [ ] All issues from review logged and addressed

## SECTION 4: Pre-Publish Review

### 4.1 Metadata and SEO

- [ ] Title optimized (primary keyword front-loaded, under 60 characters)
- [ ] Title compelling (not just keyword-stuffed)
- [ ] Description written (minimum 200 words for YouTube)
- [ ] Description includes primary keyword in first 2 sentences
- [ ] Description includes secondary keywords naturally
- [ ] Description includes timestamped chapters (for videos >5 min)
- [ ] Description includes all relevant links with UTM parameters
- [ ] Tags added (primary keyword, secondary keywords, topic variations)
- [ ] Category selected correctly
- [ ] Language and location settings configured
- [ ] Thumbnail uploaded (custom, not auto-generated)
- [ ] Thumbnail passes quality checklist (mobile readable, high contrast, 3 words max)
- [ ] Alternative thumbnails prepared for A/B testing
- [ ] End screen configured (subscribe CTA + next video/playlist)
- [ ] Cards added at relevant points in video
- [ ] Pinned comment drafted with value-add content

### 4.2 Platform-Specific Optimization

**YouTube Long-Form**
- [ ] Premiere vs instant publish decision made with rationale
- [ ] Community post scheduled to promote video
- [ ] Playlist assignment confirmed
- [ ] Info cards pointing to related channel content

**YouTube Shorts**
- [ ] Vertical format (9:16) confirmed
- [ ] Under 60 seconds
- [ ] Loop-friendly ending (if applicable)
- [ ] #Shorts tag included (if still required by platform)

**TikTok**
- [ ] Native upload (not cross-posted with watermark)
- [ ] Trending sound integrated (if applicable and on-brand)
- [ ] Hashtags relevant and researched
- [ ] Duet/Stitch enabled or disabled per strategy
- [ ] Caption text added within safe zones

**Instagram Reels**
- [ ] Cover image selected (not random frame)
- [ ] Caption written with CTA
- [ ] Hashtags researched and included (5-15)
- [ ] Share to feed enabled/disabled per strategy
- [ ] Audio credit included if using trending audio

**LinkedIn Video**
- [ ] SRT captions uploaded (auto-play is muted by default)
- [ ] Thumbnail frame selected
- [ ] Post copy written to stand alone (many see text before clicking video)
- [ ] Tagged relevant people or companies

### 4.3 Distribution Planning

- [ ] Publish time optimized based on audience data (not arbitrary)
- [ ] Cross-platform publish schedule created (staggered, not simultaneous)
- [ ] Email notification or newsletter inclusion coordinated
- [ ] Internal team notified for engagement support in first hour
- [ ] Paid amplification plan confirmed (or confirmed as organic-only with rationale)
- [ ] Repurposed content atoms scheduled for publishing

## SECTION 5: Post-Publish Review

### 5.1 First 24 Hours

- [ ] Video confirmed live and playable on all platforms
- [ ] Links in description verified working
- [ ] Captions verified displaying correctly
- [ ] First-hour engagement monitored (comments responded to promptly)
- [ ] Pinned comment posted and visible
- [ ] Any technical issues identified and addressed immediately
- [ ] Paid amplification activated (if planned) and confirmed serving
- [ ] Cross-platform promotional posts published

### 5.2 48-Hour Performance Check

- [ ] View count vs first-48-hour benchmark assessed
- [ ] CTR (impressions vs views) checked against channel average
- [ ] Average view duration checked against target
- [ ] 3-second retention rate checked
- [ ] Retention curve shape analyzed for drop-off points
- [ ] Engagement (likes, comments, shares) vs benchmark
- [ ] Subscriber/follower gain from video assessed
- [ ] Click-through on CTA links measured

### 5.3 7-Day Performance Review

- [ ] All metrics assessed against brief targets
- [ ] Traffic sources analyzed (search vs browse vs external)
- [ ] Audience demographics compared to target
- [ ] A/B test results reviewed (thumbnail, title)
- [ ] Winning variant confirmed and applied
- [ ] Conversion data collected and attributed
- [ ] Repurposed content performance assessed
- [ ] Lessons learned documented

### 5.4 30-Day and 90-Day Reviews

- [ ] Search ranking position for target keyword tracked
- [ ] Long-tail view accumulation trend assessed
- [ ] Evergreen potential evaluated (still generating views?)
- [ ] SEO optimization opportunities identified (update title, description, tags)
- [ ] Conversion attribution finalized
- [ ] Production ROI calculated (cost vs attributed value)
- [ ] Content update or refresh needed? (Information still accurate?)
- [ ] Archive or feature decision made (highlight on channel or let rest)

## SECTION 6: Series and Recurring Format Review

### 6.1 Series Consistency

- [ ] Style guide exists for the series (visual, tone, structure)
- [ ] Intro and outro consistent across episodes
- [ ] Recurring graphics and templates maintained
- [ ] Episode naming convention followed
- [ ] Playlist updated with new episodes
- [ ] Cross-referencing between episodes (cards and end screens link to series)
- [ ] Series performance tracked as a unit (not just individual episodes)

### 6.2 Series Health Assessment (Monthly)

- [ ] Average retention trend across episodes (improving, stable, declining)
- [ ] Subscriber growth attributed to series
- [ ] Episode-to-episode viewer carryover rate
- [ ] Audience feedback incorporated into format evolution
- [ ] Competitor series benchmarked
- [ ] Continue/evolve/sunset decision documented

---

## SECTION 7: Production Operations Review

### 7.1 Workflow Efficiency (Monthly)

- [ ] Average time from brief to publish tracked and within target
- [ ] Bottleneck identification (which stage takes longest)
- [ ] Equipment maintenance schedule current
- [ ] Software and tool licenses current
- [ ] Asset storage organized and backed up
- [ ] Raw footage archive policy followed
- [ ] Team workload balanced (no single-point-of-failure personnel)

### 7.2 Budget Review (Monthly)

- [ ] Production spend vs budget tracked
- [ ] Cost per video calculated (fully loaded: labor, equipment, software, talent)
- [ ] Cost per view calculated
- [ ] Cost per conversion calculated (for conversion content)
- [ ] Budget variance explained (over or under)
- [ ] Upcoming production needs forecasted
- [ ] Cost optimization opportunities identified

### 7.3 Team Development (Quarterly)

- [ ] Skills assessment completed for each team member
- [ ] Training needs identified and scheduled
- [ ] Industry trends reviewed (new techniques, tools, formats)
- [ ] Portfolio review conducted (team skill trajectory)
- [ ] Freelancer/contractor performance reviewed
- [ ] Equipment upgrade needs assessed

---

## SECTION 8: Compliance and Legal Review

### 8.1 Content Compliance

- [ ] All claims in video are verifiable and sourced
- [ ] FTC disclosure included for sponsored content or paid partnerships
- [ ] Music and media licensing verified (no copyright infringement)
- [ ] Talent releases signed for all on-camera participants
- [ ] Location releases signed for private properties
- [ ] Product claims reviewed by legal (especially health, finance, or performance claims)
- [ ] Competitor references reviewed for accuracy and legal risk
- [ ] User-generated content used with permission and attribution

### 8.2 Platform Compliance

- [ ] Content meets platform community guidelines
- [ ] Age-gate or content warnings applied where needed
- [ ] Advertising content properly labeled per platform requirements
- [ ] No restricted content category violations (varies by platform)
- [ ] Data collection disclosures included if applicable (contests, surveys)

### 8.3 Accessibility Compliance

- [ ] Captions available for all published video content
- [ ] Caption accuracy meets 98% threshold
- [ ] Video player accessibility verified (keyboard navigation, screen reader)
- [ ] Compliance with applicable accessibility standards documented

## Checklist Governance

### Mandatory Gates

| Gate | When | Who Reviews | Must Pass |
|------|------|------------|-----------|
| Brief Approval | Before production starts | Content Lead | Yes - no production without approved brief |
| Script/Outline Review | Before shoot | Content Lead + Brand | Yes - no shooting without approved script |
| Technical Setup Check | Before first take | Director/DP | Yes - no shooting with failed setup |
| Edit Review | Before publish | Content Lead + second reviewer | Yes - no publishing without dual review |
| Metadata Review | Before publish | SEO lead or Content Lead | Yes - no publishing without optimized metadata |
| Post-Publish Check | Within 24 hours | Content Lead | Yes - issues addressed immediately |

### Exception Process

Any checklist item bypass requires:
1. Written justification from Content Lead
2. Approval from Marketing Director (for production or brand items)
3. Risk documented in exception log
4. Outcome reviewed within 14 days

### Checklist Updates

Reviewed quarterly. Changes require evidence-based justification, team review,
and version-controlled update. No silent modifications.

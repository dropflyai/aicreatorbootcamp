# Video Brain -- Accountability Protocol

Version: 1.0
Last Updated: 2026-02-03
Owner: Video Brain
Status: ACTIVE

---

## Purpose

This protocol defines accountability structures for all video operations.
Video production involves the highest per-unit investment of any content type.
A single poorly planned video can waste thousands of dollars in production
costs that cannot be recovered. Accountability in video operations is not
about blame for creative choices -- it is about ensuring every dollar of
production budget delivers measurable value and every process failure is
caught and corrected before it becomes a pattern.

---

## Core Accountability Principles

1. **Every video has a measurable objective.** "Brand awareness" without a KPI is not an objective.
2. **Every production decision has a budget consequence.** Time is money in production. Delays cost.
3. **Every miss is analyzed, not excused.** "It just didn't perform" is not analysis.
4. **Quality gates exist to prevent waste, not to slow creativity.** Skipping a gate does not save time. It shifts cost to post-production or post-publish.
5. **Accountability includes celebrating wins.** Document what works and why, not just what fails.

## SECTION 2: Production Budget Accountability

### Budget Categories and Owners

| Category | Owner | Budget Control |
|----------|-------|---------------|
| Pre-production (briefs, scripts, research) | Producer | 10-15% of video budget |
| Production (shoot day costs, talent, location) | Producer | 40-50% of video budget |
| Post-production (editing, graphics, sound) | Editor | 25-35% of video budget |
| Distribution (paid promotion, platform tools) | Marketing Lead | 5-15% of video budget |
| Contingency | Video Lead | 5-10% of video budget |

### Budget Overrun Protocol

| Overrun Level | Action Required |
|--------------|----------------|
| 1-10% over | Documented in post-production report. No approval needed. |
| 10-20% over | Producer must explain overrun before next production greenlit. |
| 20-40% over | Video Lead and Marketing Director review. Recovery plan required. |
| >40% over | Production paused. Full financial review. Process failure investigation. |

### Cost Per Output Tracking

Track and report monthly:
- Cost per published video (fully loaded)
- Cost per 1,000 views
- Cost per conversion (for conversion-focused content)
- Cost per content atom (all derivatives from a single production)
- Cost per minute of published content

Benchmark these against industry norms and previous quarter performance.

### Budget Waste Indicators

Flag and investigate when:
- Video is produced but never published (100% waste)
- Video is published but receives <25% of channel average views (production mismatch)
- Production day yields <75% usable footage (preparation failure)
- Revision rounds exceed 5 (brief or communication failure)
- Paid promotion spend exceeds $2 CPV with no conversion attribution (distribution failure)

## SECTION 3: Escalation Framework

### Escalation Tiers

| Tier | Trigger | Who Escalates | Who Receives | Response Time |
|------|---------|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 - Advisory | Metric misses target by <15% | Role owner | Video Lead | 48 hours |
| 2 - Warning | Metric misses target by 15-30% | Video Lead | Marketing Director | 24 hours |
| 3 - Intervention | Metric misses target by >30% or budget overrun >20% | Marketing Director | VP Marketing | 12 hours |
| 4 - Crisis | Published content causing brand damage | Anyone (no hierarchy gate) | Video Lead + PR + Legal | 1 hour |
| 5 - Executive | Sustained underperformance (3+ months) or budget question | Marketing Director | C-suite | 7 days |

### Escalation Documentation

Every escalation includes:
1. **What:** Factual description of the miss or incident
2. **When:** Date detected and date the issue began
3. **Impact:** Quantified in dollars, views, conversions, or reputation risk
4. **Root cause:** Why it happened (5 Whys analysis minimum)
5. **Recovery plan:** Specific steps with owners and deadlines
6. **Prevention:** Process change to prevent recurrence
7. **Resources:** Any additional budget, tools, or personnel needed

## SECTION 4: Performance Review Cadence

### Per-Video Accountability (Every Video)

**Who:** Producer + Editor
**When:** Within 7 days of publish
**Format:** Written post-mortem (15 minutes to complete)
**Required content:**
- Brief objectives vs actual performance
- Budget planned vs actual
- Quality self-assessment
- One thing that went well (document for replication)
- One thing that could improve (document for correction)
- Viewer feedback summary (comments, engagement pattern)

### Weekly Accountability

**Who:** Video team standup
**Format:** 30-minute meeting
**Required content:**
- Videos published this week: performance snapshot
- Videos in production: status and blockers
- Videos in planning: briefs and approvals needed
- Resource conflicts or capacity issues
- Trending formats or competitor activity worth noting

### Monthly Accountability

**Who:** Video Lead + Marketing Director
**Format:** 60-minute review
**Required content:**
- Full VideoScore composite with dimension breakdown
- Month-over-month performance trends
- Budget report (actual vs plan)
- Top 3 performing videos with analysis of why they worked
- Bottom 3 performing videos with analysis of why they underperformed
- Team capacity and utilization report
- Upcoming production slate review

**Output:** Written summary with action items. Action items tracked to completion.

### Quarterly Accountability

**Who:** Video Lead + Marketing Director + VP Marketing
**Format:** 90-minute strategic review
**Required content:**
- Quarterly VideoScore trend with dimension analysis
- ROI analysis (production investment vs attributed business value)
- Channel growth metrics (subscribers, watch hours, search position)
- Content strategy effectiveness (which formats and topics are working)
- Competitive landscape assessment
- Budget request and justification for next quarter
- Team development and hiring needs
- Technology and equipment assessment
- Benchmark test results

## SECTION 5: Failure Response Protocol

### Classification

| Category | Definition | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Planning Failure | Brief, script, or strategy was flawed | Video topic had no search demand or audience interest |
| Production Failure | Technical or logistical issue during shoot | Audio unusable, wrong location, talent unprepared |
| Post-Production Failure | Editing, graphics, or QC issue | Defect shipped, pacing wrong, brand off-spec |
| Distribution Failure | Publishing, SEO, or promotion issue | Wrong metadata, missed publish window, no promotion |
| Strategic Failure | Series or format not connecting with audience | Entire content pillar underperforming |
| External Failure | Platform changes, competitive shifts | Algorithm update, competitor content surge |

### Response by Category

**Planning Failure:**
1. Identify which planning step failed (research, brief, approval)
2. Was audience data consulted? Was the brief approved?
3. Update planning process to prevent recurrence
4. If pattern: review who approves briefs and whether approval is meaningful

**Production Failure:**
1. Determine if issue was foreseeable with proper preparation
2. Review pre-production checklist compliance (was it followed?)
3. If checklist was followed but issue still occurred: update checklist
4. If checklist was not followed: address compliance
5. Calculate wasted production cost and document

**Post-Production Failure:**
1. Determine if QC caught it (and it was ignored) or missed it
2. Review QC playback checklist compliance
3. If published defect: assess severity and decide on re-publish vs live fix
4. Calculate remediation cost (re-edit, re-publish, reputation)

**Distribution Failure:**
1. Review pre-publish checklist compliance
2. Identify which metadata or distribution step was missed
3. Fix immediately if possible (metadata can be updated post-publish)
4. Quantify impact (estimated views lost, ranking position lost)

**Strategic Failure:**
1. Review performance data across the failing content pillar
2. Compare against audience research and competitor performance
3. Decide: pivot format, pivot topic, or sunset the content type
4. Document learning for future content strategy decisions

**External Failure:**
1. Document the external factor with evidence
2. Assess if the team could have anticipated or adapted faster
3. Adjust strategy for new reality
4. No individual accountability unless response was inadequate

## SECTION 6: Quality Gate Enforcement

### Mandatory Quality Gates

These gates cannot be bypassed without explicit exception approval:

| Gate | Stage | Enforced By | Bypass Requires |
|------|-------|------------|-----------------|
| Brief Approval | Pre-production | Content Lead sign-off | Marketing Director approval |
| Script Review | Pre-production | Content Lead + Brand review | Video Lead approval |
| Technical Setup Check | Production | Director/DP verification | Not bypassable |
| Dual Review | Post-production | Two independent reviewers | Video Lead approval + risk documentation |
| Metadata Completion | Pre-publish | SEO specialist or Content Lead | Video Lead approval |
| Accessibility Check | Pre-publish | Designated reviewer | Legal/compliance approval |

### Gate Failure Tracking

Track quarterly:
- Number of gate bypasses requested
- Number of gate bypasses approved vs denied
- Outcome of bypassed gates (did the video perform? did issues arise?)
- Correlation between gate compliance and video performance

If gate bypasses correlate with underperformance, tighten enforcement.
If gate compliance shows no quality impact, simplify the gate.

## SECTION 7: Cross-Brain Accountability

### Dependencies on Other Brains

| Dependency | Brain | SLA |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| Brand guidelines and visual assets | Design Brain | 48-hour turnaround on asset requests |
| Product information and demos | Product Brain | 24-hour response to product queries |
| Customer stories and testimonials | Customer Success Brain | 2-week lead time for customer coordination |
| Legal review on claims and compliance | Legal Brain | 4-hour review for active productions |
| Blog content for video adaptation | Content Brain | 48 hours before scheduled production |
| Analytics and attribution data | Analytics Brain | Weekly data sync |
| Social distribution and promotion | Social Media Brain | 24-hour turnaround on video promotion |

### Cross-Brain Failure Protocol

When a dependency brain misses its SLA:
1. Document the miss and its impact on video production or performance
2. Notify the dependent brain's lead within 24 hours
3. Implement workaround if possible (do not block production indefinitely)
4. Escalate to CEO Brain if dependency failure persists beyond 7 days
5. Video Brain is not penalized for documented, escalated dependency failures
6. Video Brain IS penalized for not escalating or not having a workaround plan

## SECTION 8: Continuous Improvement

### Retrospective Requirements

| Type | Frequency | Duration | Output |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Per-video post-mortem | Every video | 15 min written | What worked, what did not, key metric |
| Monthly production retro | Monthly | 45 min meeting | Process improvements, training needs |
| Quarterly strategy retro | Quarterly | 90 min meeting | Content strategy adjustments, format decisions |
| Annual capability review | Annually | Half-day workshop | Technology, team, process, and strategy overhaul |

### Learning Documentation

All retrospective outputs must be:
- Archived in the Video Brain knowledge base
- Tagged by category (production, editing, SEO, distribution, etc.)
- Searchable and accessible to the entire team
- Reviewed quarterly for emerging patterns
- Used to update Review Checklist and BenchmarkTests

### Improvement Tracking

- Target: minimum 3 process improvements per quarter
- Each improvement must have a measurable impact hypothesis
- Impact measured within 60 days of implementation
- Improvements that show no impact are reversed or modified
- Successful improvements are codified into standard process

## SECTION 9: Accountability Governance

### Protocol Review

This protocol is reviewed quarterly. Changes require:
- Evidence showing current protocol is insufficient or excessive
- Review by Video Lead and Marketing Director
- 14-day notice to team before changes take effect
- Version-controlled update with changelog

### Dispute Resolution

If a team member disagrees with an accountability assessment:
1. Written objection within 5 business days
2. Review by Video Lead (or Marketing Director if Video Lead issued assessment)
3. Decision within 5 business days
4. Decision is final unless new evidence emerges
5. No retaliation for good-faith objections

### Accountability for Accountability

The Video Lead is accountable for:
- Ensuring this protocol is followed consistently
- Conducting all scheduled reviews on time
- Documenting and following up on all escalations
- Reporting accountability metrics to Marketing Director monthly

Failure to maintain the accountability system is itself a Tier 2 escalation.

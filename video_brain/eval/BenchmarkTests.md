## Purpose

These benchmark tests evaluate the Video Brain's ability to diagnose video
performance problems, design production strategies, optimize existing content,
and make data-driven decisions about video operations. Each test presents a
real-world scenario with data and demands a structured, actionable response.

Tests are scored on a 10-point scale per the VideoScore framework.
Minimum passing score: 7.0. Below 7.0 triggers remediation.

---

## Scoring Criteria (Applied to Every Test)

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Problem Diagnosis | 25% | Correct identification of root cause with supporting data |
| Strategic Response | 25% | Coherent plan aligned with business and content objectives |
| Tactical Execution | 20% | Specific, actionable steps with timelines and owners |
| Measurement Plan | 15% | Clear KPIs and decision criteria for proposed solution |
| Resource Awareness | 15% | Realistic plan given budget, team, and time constraints |

## TEST 1: Low CTR Despite High Impressions

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 1 week to diagnose, 2 weeks to implement**

### Scenario

Your YouTube video has received 50,000 impressions in the first 48 hours but
has a 2% CTR (1,000 views). Your channel average CTR is 5.5%. The video is a
product tutorial that your sales team has been waiting for. The content is
high-quality based on internal review.

### Context Data

- Video length: 8 minutes 14 seconds
- Thumbnail: Custom, shows product on white background with text "Complete Tutorial"
- Title: "Complete Product Tutorial | Everything You Need to Know"
- Audience: 80% of impressions coming from suggested videos and browse
- Channel subscribers: 12,000
- Previous tutorial video CTR: 6.2%
- Description: 3 sentences with product link. No timestamps.

### Required Response

1. Root cause analysis of low CTR (minimum 5 factors evaluated)
2. Thumbnail redesign strategy with specific recommendations
3. Title alternatives (minimum 5 options with rationale for each)
4. A/B testing plan for thumbnail and title
5. Description optimization
6. Expected CTR improvement timeline
7. Decision framework: at what point do you re-upload vs optimize in place?

## TEST 2: Retention Cliff at 30% of Video Length

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: Apply learnings to next 3 videos**

### Scenario

Your average view duration is 30% of video length across your last 10 YouTube
videos. Channel benchmark is 45%. Retention graphs consistently show a cliff
between 25-35% of the video. Videos range from 6-15 minutes. Content topics
are well-researched and audience-relevant based on keyword data.

### Context Data

- Typical video structure: 30-sec intro, 2-min background/context, main content, CTA, outro
- Hook retention (first 30 sec): 72% (healthy)
- Drop at intro completion: 15% loss
- Drop at background section: 25% loss (the cliff)
- Remaining viewers stay relatively engaged through main content
- Comment feedback: "Great content but get to the point faster"
- Competitor videos on same topics average 50% retention

### Required Response

1. Detailed retention analysis (map the viewer journey and identify each drop cause)
2. Structural changes to video format (specific recommendations with reasoning)
3. Revised video template/outline that addresses the cliff
4. Pacing guidelines (seconds per section for a 10-minute video)
5. Hook-to-value bridge strategy (how to get from hook to main content without losing people)
6. Testing plan (how to measure if changes work)
7. Apply analysis to critique one of the 10 underperforming videos with specific edit recommendations

## TEST 3: Multi-Format Product Video Production

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 1 production day, 2 weeks to publish all**

### Scenario

Your client needs three video assets from a single production day: a 3-minute
product demo for the website, a 60-second customer testimonial for social media,
and 5 short-form social cuts (15-30 seconds each). Budget: $3,500 total
including pre-production, shoot, and post-production. You have 1 videographer,
1 editor, and the client's marketing manager available.

### Context Data

- Product: B2B SaaS dashboard tool
- Customer available for 90 minutes on shoot day
- Office location available (client's modern office)
- Equipment available: mirrorless camera, 2 LED panels, wireless lav mic, tripod
- Client has brand guidelines with specific colors and fonts
- No teleprompter available
- Client wants all assets within 2 weeks of shoot

### Required Response

1. Pre-production plan (brief, script outlines, shot list, schedule)
2. Shoot day timeline (hour by hour, accounting for setup, breaks, buffer)
3. Shot list optimized for multi-format capture (one setup serving multiple assets)
4. Interview question framework for testimonial (that yields usable soundbites)
5. Product demo script approach (how to make SaaS visually interesting)
6. Post-production workflow and timeline (editing order and delivery schedule)
7. Budget breakdown (how $3,500 is allocated)
8. Quality trade-offs (what you sacrifice at this budget vs what is non-negotiable)
9. Contingency plan (what if the customer is a bad speaker, what if lighting is poor)

## TEST 4: YouTube Channel Growth Stagnation

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 90-day turnaround plan**

### Scenario

Your YouTube channel has been stuck at 10,000-10,500 subscribers for 8 months
despite publishing 2 videos per week. Monthly views have plateaued at 40K.
Average video gets 800-1,200 views. You have not had a video exceed 5,000 views
in 6 months. Your niche is project management software education.

### Context Data

- Video topics: software tutorials, feature walkthroughs, productivity tips
- Average video length: 12 minutes
- Average retention: 38%
- Average CTR: 4.2%
- Subscriber conversion rate: 0.3% of viewers subscribe
- Traffic sources: 55% browse, 25% search, 15% external, 5% suggested
- Publishing schedule: Tuesday and Thursday at 9am EST
- No Shorts published in last 6 months
- No community posts in last 3 months
- Competitor channel in same niche: 85K subscribers, similar content

### Required Response

1. Growth audit (diagnose why the channel plateaued -- minimum 8 factors)
2. Content strategy revision (what to keep, what to add, what to drop)
3. YouTube Shorts strategy (format, topics, publishing cadence)
4. Search optimization overhaul (keyword research process, metadata improvements)
5. Suggested video optimization (how to increase algorithmic recommendations)
6. Community engagement plan (Community tab, comments, collaborations)
7. 90-day plan with weekly milestones
8. Realistic growth targets with justification
9. Competitive analysis: what is the 85K-subscriber channel doing differently?

## TEST 6: Video-Attributed Conversion Optimization

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 30 days**

### Scenario

Your video content gets strong views and retention (50% AVD, 6% CTR) but
video-attributed conversions are near zero. You produce 8 videos per month:
4 tutorials, 2 product demos, 1 customer story, 1 thought leadership piece.
Your SaaS product has a free trial signup as the primary conversion event.
Google Analytics shows video viewers convert at 0.1% vs 2.5% site average.

### Context Data

- CTA in videos: verbal mention of "check out our website" at the end
- Links in YouTube description: homepage URL (not a landing page)
- No UTM parameters on any video links
- No dedicated landing pages for video traffic
- No retargeting audiences built from video viewers
- No lead magnets or gated content offered
- Product demo videos show features but not workflows or outcomes
- Tutorial topics do not connect to product capabilities

### Required Response

1. Conversion funnel diagnosis (where are the leaks and why?)
2. CTA strategy overhaul (placement, wording, frequency, format)
3. Link and landing page strategy (where should video traffic go?)
4. Attribution setup plan (UTM, dedicated pages, tracking)
5. Content-to-conversion mapping (which video types should drive which actions?)
6. Retargeting strategy using video viewer audiences
7. Tutorial topic pivot strategy (connect educational content to product)
8. 30-day implementation priority list (what fixes first for maximum impact)
9. Expected conversion rate improvement with justification

## TEST 7: Live Video Event Production

**Time Pressure: Event in 10 days**

### Required Response

1. Technical production plan (streaming setup, scenes, transitions)
2. Run sheet with exact timing (minute-by-minute)
3. Speaker preparation plan (especially for the nervous Product Lead)
4. Remote panelist technical requirements and testing protocol
5. Backup plan for technical failures (internet drop, software crash, speaker no-show)
6. Audience engagement plan (live chat, polls, Q&A management)
7. Budget allocation ($2,000 across all needs)
8. Post-event content plan (repurposing the live stream)
9. Success metrics and how to measure them
10. Rehearsal agenda (how to use the 2 hours effectively)

## TEST 16: Video ROI Justification for Leadership

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: Presentation in 5 days**

### Scenario

The CFO is questioning the video team budget ($120K/year) because attribution
is unclear. The CFO sees views and engagement but does not see how video
directly drives revenue. The video team budget is at risk of being cut by 50%.
You need to build a compelling business case with available data.

### Context Data

- 150 videos published in the last 12 months
- Total views: 1.2M across platforms
- YouTube subscriber growth: 8K to 22K
- Website sessions from video: 45K (UTM-tracked)
- Direct conversions from video traffic: 180 free trials (0.4% conversion rate)
- Free trial to paid conversion rate: 25% (company average)
- Average contract value: $2,400/year
- Brand search volume increased 40% YoY (video launched 14 months ago)
- Sales team reports using videos in 30% of deals
- Customer support ticket volume decreased 15% since tutorial library launched

### Required Response

1. Direct ROI calculation with defensible methodology
2. Indirect/assisted ROI estimation (brand, sales enablement, support deflection)
3. Comparative analysis (cost of video vs alternative marketing spend)
4. Future projection (what happens if video budget is cut vs maintained vs increased)
5. Presentation structure (how to communicate this to a finance-minded audience)
6. Data gaps to acknowledge honestly (what you cannot prove)
7. Proposed measurement improvements for next 12 months
8. Counter-proposal: what a 50% budget cut would mean in specific, measurable terms

## Test Administration Protocol

### Scheduling

- Full benchmark suite administered quarterly
- Minimum 4 tests per monthly spot-check (rotating selection)
- Critical tests (1, 2, 6, 15) administered bi-annually as exercises
- New team members complete full suite within first 30 days

### Scoring

- Each test scored on 10-point scale using weighted criteria
- Composite benchmark score = average across all completed tests
- Individual test scores below 5.0 trigger immediate remediation
- Composite score below 7.0 triggers strategy review

### Remediation Protocol

| Score Range | Required Action | Timeline |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| 8.0-10.0 | Document approach for team playbook | 7 days |
| 6.0-7.9 | Identify gaps, create improvement plan | 14 days |
| 4.0-5.9 | Supervised remediation with mentor review | 21 days |
| <4.0 | Full retraining on failed domain | 30 days |

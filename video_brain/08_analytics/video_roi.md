# Video ROI — Production Economics and Business Case Framework

## The ROI Challenge in Video

Video is simultaneously the highest-impact and highest-cost content format. A single professional video can cost $5K-$50K+ to produce, yet deliver $0 in measurable return if distribution and measurement are neglected. The video ROI challenge is twofold: accurately tracking production costs (which are often hidden in internal time, opportunity cost, and overhead) and accurately attributing business value (which spans awareness, consideration, conversion, and retention across multiple channels and time horizons).

---

## 1. Production Cost Tracking

### Total Cost of Video Production

Most organizations dramatically undercount video production costs by ignoring internal time and overhead. A complete cost model includes:

```
Total Video Cost = Direct Production Costs
                 + Internal Labor Costs
                 + Overhead Costs
                 + Distribution Costs
                 + Opportunity Cost
```

### Cost Component Breakdown

| Component | Items | Typical Range |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| **Pre-Production** | Scripting, storyboarding, talent booking, location scouting, creative brief | $500-$5,000 |
| **Production** | Camera crew, lighting, audio, talent, location fees, props, travel | $1,000-$25,000 |
| **Post-Production** | Editing, color grading, sound mixing, motion graphics, subtitles, QA | $1,000-$15,000 |
| **Internal Labor** | Strategy, project management, creative direction, review cycles | $1,000-$10,000 |
| **Distribution** | Paid promotion, platform fees, syndication tools | $500-$10,000 |
| **Asset Repurposing** | Short-form clips, thumbnails, audiograms, blog transcription | $200-$2,000 |

### Cost Per Video by Production Tier

| Tier | Description | Cost Range | Production Time | Quality Level |
|------|-------------|------------|----------------|---------------|
| DIY/Internal | Smartphone + basic editing | $50-$500 | 2-4 hours | Acceptable for social, internal |
| Semi-pro | Prosumer camera, freelance editor | $500-$3,000 | 1-3 days | Good for YouTube, LinkedIn |
| Professional | Production crew, studio/location | $3,000-$15,000 | 3-7 days | High for brand content, ads |
| Premium | Full agency, multi-day shoot, talent | $15,000-$100,000+ | 2-6 weeks | Broadcast quality, hero content |
| Animated/Motion | Motion graphics, 2D/3D animation | $3,000-$30,000 | 2-6 weeks | Varies by complexity |

### Internal Time Valuation

Internal time is the largest hidden cost. Calculate at fully loaded hourly rates:

| Role | Estimated Hours per Video | Hourly Rate (Loaded) | Cost |
|------|--------------------------|---------------------|------|
| Marketing Manager | 4-8 hours (brief, review, approval) | $75-$125/hr | $300-$1,000 |
| Creative Director | 2-4 hours (concept, review) | $100-$175/hr | $200-$700 |
| Subject Matter Expert | 2-6 hours (input, on-camera time) | $100-$200/hr | $200-$1,200 |
| Project Manager | 4-8 hours (coordination, scheduling) | $60-$100/hr | $240-$800 |
| Executive Review | 1-2 hours (approval cycles) | $150-$300/hr | $150-$600 |
| **Total Internal** | **13-28 hours** | | **$1,090-$4,300** |

## 2. Cost Per View and Content Efficiency

### Cost Per View (CPV) Calculation

```
CPV = Total Video Cost / Total Views (across all platforms)
```

| Metric | Calculation | Benchmark (Organic) | Benchmark (Paid) |
|--------|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Cost per view | Total cost / Views | $0.01-$0.10 | $0.01-$0.15 |
| Cost per minute watched | Total cost / Total watch minutes | $0.05-$0.50 | $0.10-$1.00 |
| Cost per engagement | Total cost / (Likes + Comments + Shares) | $0.50-$5.00 | $1.00-$10.00 |
| Cost per lead | Total cost / Leads attributed to video | $5-$100 | $10-$200 |
| Cost per customer | Total cost / Customers attributed to video | $50-$500 | $100-$1,000 |

### Content Efficiency Score

Not all views are equal. A Content Efficiency Score weights views by their business value:

```
Content Efficiency Score =
  (Views * 0.1) +
  (Engagement * 0.2) +
  (Leads generated * 0.3) +
  (Revenue attributed * 0.4)
  / Total Video Cost
```

Higher score = more business value per dollar invested.

## 3. Content ROI Analysis

### ROI Formula

```
Video ROI = (Revenue Attributed to Video - Total Video Cost) / Total Video Cost * 100
```

**Example:**
- Video production cost: $5,000
- Distribution cost: $2,000
- Internal time: $1,500
- Total cost: $8,500
- Revenue attributed (direct): $12,000
- Revenue attributed (assisted): $8,000
- Total attributed revenue: $20,000
- **ROI: ($20,000 - $8,500) / $8,500 = 135%**

### ROI by Video Type

| Video Type | Typical Cost | Typical Lifespan | Expected ROI | Time to ROI |
|-----------|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-------------|
| Evergreen educational | $3,000-$10,000 | 2-5 years | 200-1000% | 3-12 months |
| Product demo | $2,000-$8,000 | 1-2 years (until product changes) | 150-500% | 1-6 months |
| Customer testimonial | $2,000-$5,000 | 1-3 years | 200-800% | 1-3 months |
| Brand video | $10,000-$50,000 | 1-3 years | 50-200% | 6-18 months |
| Social short-form | $200-$1,000 | 1-14 days | Variable, high if viral | 0-7 days |
| Webinar | $1,000-$3,000 | 6-18 months | 300-1000% | 1-3 months |
| Video ad | $3,000-$15,000 | 1-3 months (creative fatigue) | 100-400% | 1-4 weeks |

## 4. Repurposing ROI Multiplier

### The Multiplier Effect

Repurposing increases video ROI by generating additional value from the same production investment:

```
Repurposing Multiplier = Total Value (all derivatives) / Original Video Cost

Without repurposing:
  1 video → $8,500 cost → $20,000 value → 2.35x return

With repurposing (one video → 15 derivative pieces):
  1 video → $8,500 + $1,500 repurposing → $10,000 cost
  Original video: $20,000 value
  5 short-form clips: $5,000 value
  3 blog posts with embedded video: $3,000 value
  Email series: $2,000 value
  Social graphics: $1,000 value
  → Total value: $31,000 → 3.1x return (32% improvement)
```

### Repurposing Cost vs. Value Matrix

| Derivative | Marginal Cost | Expected Value | ROI Multiplier |
|-----------|--------------|---------------|---------------|
| Short-form clips (5-8) | $200-$500 total | $2,000-$10,000 | 4-20x |
| Blog post (transcript + editorial) | $200-$500 | $1,000-$5,000 | 2-10x |
| Email nurture inclusion | $50-$100 | $500-$3,000 | 5-30x |
| Podcast audio extraction | $50-$100 | $500-$2,000 | 5-20x |
| Social quote graphics | $100-$200 | $200-$1,000 | 1-5x |
| Presentation slides | $100-$200 | $200-$1,000 | 1-5x |
| Gated content (lead gen) | $100-$300 | $1,000-$10,000 | 3-33x |

### Maximizing Repurposing ROI

**Production decisions that increase repurposing potential:**
- Shoot in highest resolution available (4K minimum for future-proofing)
- Record clean audio separately (enables podcast extraction)
- Use modular content structure (segments that work standalone)
- Capture B-roll beyond what is needed for the primary edit
- Record multiple takes and angles for alternative cuts
- Create transcript immediately (enables written content derivatives)

## 5. Business Case Framework for Video Investment

### Building the Business Case

**For CFO/Finance Audience:**

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Investment requested | Total budget: production + distribution + tools + team |
| Expected returns | Revenue attribution model with conservative/moderate/aggressive scenarios |
| Payback period | Months until cumulative revenue exceeds cumulative investment |
| Comparison | Cost of alternative marketing channels at equivalent impact |
| Risk mitigation | Minimum viable investment, phased approach, kill criteria |

### Video Investment Scenario Model

| Scenario | Monthly Investment | Monthly Output | Expected Monthly Revenue | 6-Month ROI |
|----------|-------------------|---------------|------------------------|-------------|
| Conservative | $5,000 | 4 videos | $8,000 | 60% |
| Moderate | $15,000 | 12 videos | $30,000 | 100% |
| Aggressive | $40,000 | 25 videos + paid | $100,000 | 150% |

### Break-Even Analysis

```
Break-Even Point = Total Video Investment / Revenue per View

Example:
  Monthly investment: $15,000
  Average revenue per view: $0.10
  Break-even views needed: 150,000/month
  Current monthly views: 200,000
  Result: Above break-even; continue and scale
```

## 6. ROI Optimization Strategies

### Reducing Cost (Denominator)

| Strategy | Impact | Implementation |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| Batch production | 20-40% cost reduction | Shoot 4-8 videos in one session |
| Template-based editing | 30-50% faster post-production | Create reusable motion graphics templates |
| AI-assisted editing | 20-40% time savings | Descript, Opus Clip for rough cuts |
| User-generated content | 80-95% cost reduction | Customer and employee video programs |
| Internal production | 50-70% vs. agency | Build internal production capability |
| Repurposing pipeline | Amortize cost across 10-15 pieces | Systematic repurposing from day one |

### Increasing Revenue (Numerator)

| Strategy | Impact | Implementation |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| Better distribution | 2-5x views from same content | Platform-native adaptation, SEO, paid boost |
| Higher-converting CTAs | 20-50% more conversions | Test CTA placement, copy, design |
| Lead capture | New revenue attribution | Email gates, form overlays (Wistia, Vidyard) |
| Retargeting viewers | 3-5x higher conversion rate | Build audiences from video viewers, retarget |
| Content-market fit | 2-3x engagement | Better topic research, audience feedback loops |
| Evergreen focus | 5-10x content lifespan | Prioritize evergreen over topical content |

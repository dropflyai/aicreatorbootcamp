# Video Brain -- Scope and Boundaries

## In Scope

The Video Brain owns the following domains with full authority:

---

### 1. Video Strategy and Planning

- Content pillar definition for video channels
- Editorial calendar design for video content
- Platform selection and prioritization
- Format selection (long-form, short-form, live, series, one-off)
- Audience research specific to video consumption behavior
- Competitive video analysis and benchmarking
- Video content roadmapping aligned to business objectives
- Budget allocation across production, distribution, and promotion

### 2. Pre-Production

- Concept development and creative briefs
- Scriptwriting for all video formats
- Storyboarding and visual planning
- Shot list creation and scene breakdowns
- Location scouting criteria and selection
- Talent selection and direction guidance
- Production scheduling and resource planning
- Equipment selection and technical requirements
- Music and sound effect sourcing strategy
- Legal requirements: talent releases, location permits, music licensing

### 3. Production

- Camera setup: framing, composition, movement
- Lighting design: three-point, natural, cinematic, studio
- Audio capture: microphone selection, room acoustics, monitoring
- Directing: performance coaching, blocking, pacing
- B-roll strategy and capture
- Multi-camera setup and switching
- Green screen and virtual production
- Mobile production techniques
- Remote and distributed production workflows

### 4. Post-Production

- Editing workflow design and optimization
- Pacing and rhythm in editorial
- Color grading and color correction
- Sound design and audio mixing
- Motion graphics and title design
- Lower thirds, callouts, and on-screen text
- Captioning and subtitle creation
- Thumbnail design direction (execution delegated to Design Brain)
- Export settings and encoding specifications per platform
- Quality control and review processes

### 5. YouTube-Specific Strategy

- Channel architecture and organization
- YouTube SEO: titles, descriptions, tags, chapters
- Thumbnail strategy and A/B testing
- Algorithm understanding: impressions, CTR, watch time, session time
- Playlist strategy and content organization
- Community tab engagement
- YouTube Shorts integration with long-form
- Monetization strategy: AdSense, memberships, Super Chat, merch shelf
- Collaboration and cross-promotion strategy

### 6. Short-Form Video

- TikTok content strategy and optimization
- Instagram Reels strategy and optimization
- YouTube Shorts strategy and optimization
- Platform-specific pacing and format requirements
- Trend identification and participation frameworks
- Sound and music selection for short-form
- Hook engineering for 1-3 second attention capture
- Vertical video composition and framing
- Short-form content repurposed from long-form

### 7. Live Streaming

- Platform selection for live content (YouTube Live, Twitch, LinkedIn Live, Instagram Live)
- Technical setup: encoding, bitrate, resolution, redundancy
- Engagement frameworks: chat interaction, polls, Q&A
- Live show structure and rundown creation
- Multi-streaming strategy and tools
- Live event production (product launches, conferences, workshops)
- Repurposing live content into on-demand assets
- Webinar production and conversion optimization

### 8. Distribution and Promotion

- Multi-platform distribution strategy
- Content repurposing matrices and workflows
- Video embedding strategy for web and email
- Paid video promotion: YouTube Ads, Meta Video Ads, TikTok Ads
- Connected TV (CTV) advertising
- Video in email marketing
- Video SEO for Google and platform search
- Cross-platform scheduling and publishing workflows

### 9. Analytics and Optimization

- KPI definition for video content
- Watch time and retention analysis
- Click-through rate optimization
- Audience demographic and behavior analysis
- A/B testing for thumbnails, titles, hooks
- Conversion tracking from video content
- Attribution modeling for video in marketing funnels
- Performance benchmarking and reporting
- Continuous improvement protocols

## Boundary Rules

### Rule 1: Direction vs. Execution

The Video Brain provides **creative direction** for visual design elements (thumbnails, graphics, animations) but delegates **visual execution** to the Design Brain. The Video Brain specifies what is needed and why; the Design Brain determines how to create it.

**Example:** The Video Brain says "Thumbnail needs high contrast, face with surprised expression, 3-word text overlay in yellow." The Design Brain executes the design.

### Rule 2: Strategy vs. Infrastructure

The Video Brain defines **what infrastructure is needed** for video operations but delegates **how to build it** to the Engineering Brain. The Video Brain specifies requirements; Engineering implements.

**Example:** The Video Brain says "We need automated publishing to YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram with scheduled release times." The Engineering Brain builds the pipeline.

### Rule 3: Video Marketing vs. Marketing Strategy

The Video Brain owns video-specific marketing knowledge (YouTube Ads creative best practices, video funnel strategy, video SEO). The Marketing Brain owns the overarching marketing strategy. When video is a component of a larger campaign, the Marketing Brain leads and the Video Brain contributes video expertise.

### Rule 4: Creative Direction vs. Business Decision

The Video Brain makes creative and production decisions. Business decisions (budget approval, team hiring, vendor selection, strategic pivots) are escalated to the MBA Brain or CEO Brain.

### Rule 5: Content Production vs. Content Strategy

The Video Brain owns video content production and video-specific strategy. If video content is part of a broader content strategy spanning written, audio, and visual content, the Content Brain leads coordination and the Video Brain contributes video expertise.

## Escalation Protocol

When the Video Brain encounters work outside its scope:

1. **Identify** which brain owns the domain
2. **Document** the requirement clearly
3. **Delegate** to the appropriate brain with full context
4. **Receive** the deliverable and integrate into video workflow
5. **Log** the cross-brain interaction in Memory

### Escalation Triggers

- Technical implementation needed beyond video tools --> Engineering Brain
- Visual design execution needed --> Design Brain
- Budget or business case needed --> MBA Brain
- Legal review needed --> Legal Brain
- Marketing strategy alignment needed --> Marketing Brain
- Audio-only content (podcasts) --> Content Brain or dedicated Audio Brain

---

## Quality Gates at Boundaries

When receiving work from other brains or delivering work to them:

### Receiving Work
- Verify deliverable meets the specification provided
- Test integration with video workflow
- Request revision if quality criteria are not met

### Delivering Work
- Include full context and rationale
- Specify success criteria for the deliverable
- Provide examples or references when possible
- Include timeline and priority information

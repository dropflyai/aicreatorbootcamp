# Video Brain -- Glossary

## Canonical Terminology

This glossary defines the authoritative meaning of all key terms used within the Video Brain. When these terms appear in any Video Brain document, they carry the meanings defined here. Ambiguity is resolved by this glossary.

---

## Production Terminology

### Pre-Production

**Creative Brief:** A document that defines the video's objective, target audience, key message, tone, format, platform, and success criteria. Every production begins with a creative brief.

**Script:** The written blueprint for a video, including spoken dialogue, on-screen text, visual direction, and timing cues. Scripts range from word-for-word teleprompter scripts to loose outlines depending on format.

**Storyboard:** A visual representation of a video's shot sequence, typically illustrated panels showing key frames with accompanying notes on camera movement, dialogue, and timing.

**Shot List:** A detailed inventory of every shot required for a production, organized by scene or location, including shot type, framing, movement, and any special requirements.

**Rundown:** A minute-by-minute outline of a live show, webinar, or event, specifying segments, transitions, graphics cues, and talent assignments.

**Recce (Location Scout):** The process of evaluating potential filming locations for suitability, considering lighting, acoustics, visual background, accessibility, and permits.

**Mood Board:** A collection of reference images, color palettes, typography samples, and video clips that establish the visual and tonal direction for a production.

### Production

**A-Roll:** Primary footage of the main subject -- typically a talking head, interview, or primary action sequence.

**B-Roll:** Supplementary footage used to visually support or illustrate the narrative. B-roll covers edits, adds visual interest, and provides context.

**Three-Point Lighting:** The foundational lighting setup consisting of a key light (primary illumination), fill light (shadow reduction), and back light (subject separation from background).

**Key Light:** The primary, brightest light source illuminating the subject. Placement determines the mood and dimensionality of the image.

**Fill Light:** A secondary light source positioned opposite the key light to reduce shadow intensity. Fill ratio (key-to-fill ratio) determines contrast and mood.

**Back Light (Hair Light / Rim Light):** A light positioned behind the subject to create edge separation from the background, adding depth and dimensionality.

**Practical Light:** Any light source visible within the frame that serves both a functional and aesthetic purpose (lamps, monitors, neon signs).

**Lavalier (Lav) Microphone:** A small clip-on microphone attached to the subject's clothing for hands-free audio capture. Available in wired and wireless configurations.

**Shotgun Microphone:** A highly directional microphone designed to capture audio from a narrow pickup pattern, typically mounted on a boom pole or camera.

**Dynamic Range:** The ratio between the darkest and brightest elements a camera sensor can capture simultaneously. Higher dynamic range preserves detail in shadows and highlights.

**Frame Rate:** The number of individual frames captured per second. Standard rates: 24fps (cinematic), 30fps (broadcast/web), 60fps (smooth motion/slow-motion source), 120fps+ (slow motion).

**Aspect Ratio:** The proportional relationship between width and height. Common ratios: 16:9 (landscape/YouTube), 9:16 (vertical/short-form), 1:1 (square), 4:5 (Instagram feed).

**Resolution:** The pixel dimensions of the video image. Common resolutions: 1080p (1920x1080), 4K (3840x2160), 720p (1280x720).

**Bit Rate:** The amount of data processed per unit of time in a video file. Higher bit rate generally means higher quality but larger file sizes. Measured in Mbps (megabits per second).

**Codec:** The algorithm used to compress and decompress video data. Common codecs: H.264 (broad compatibility), H.265/HEVC (better compression), ProRes (editing), VP9 (YouTube).

### Post-Production

**NLE (Non-Linear Editor):** Software for video editing that allows non-sequential access to any frame. Primary NLEs: Adobe Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, Final Cut Pro.

**Color Grading:** The creative process of manipulating color, contrast, saturation, and luminance to establish mood, visual style, and narrative tone. Distinct from color correction.

**Color Correction:** The technical process of normalizing footage to accurate, consistent color representation. Precedes color grading in the pipeline.

**LUT (Look-Up Table):** A mathematical formula that maps input color values to output color values, used to apply color grades or convert between color spaces.

**Sound Design:** The creative process of constructing the audio landscape of a video, including ambient sound, sound effects, foley, and atmospheric audio.

**Foley:** The reproduction of everyday sound effects added in post-production to enhance audio realism (footsteps, clothing rustle, object handling).

**ADR (Automated Dialogue Replacement):** The process of re-recording dialogue in a controlled studio environment to replace on-set audio that is unusable.

**Mixing (Audio):** The process of balancing, equalizing, and spatially positioning multiple audio tracks (dialogue, music, effects) into a cohesive soundtrack.

**LUFS (Loudness Units Full Scale):** The standardized measurement of perceived audio loudness. Platform targets: YouTube -14 LUFS, Spotify -14 LUFS, broadcast -24 LUFS.

**J-Cut:** An editorial technique where audio from the next scene begins before the visual transition, creating anticipation and smooth flow.

**L-Cut:** An editorial technique where audio from the current scene continues over the visual of the next scene, maintaining continuity and connection.

**Jump Cut:** A cut between two sequential shots of the same subject with minimal position change, creating a jarring visual discontinuity. Used intentionally in YouTube/vlog style for pacing.

**Match Cut:** A transition between two shots where compositional elements (shape, movement, color) align to create visual continuity despite a change in scene or subject.

**Lower Third:** A graphic overlay in the lower portion of the frame, typically displaying a subject's name, title, or contextual information.

**End Screen:** A YouTube-specific feature allowing clickable elements (video links, subscribe buttons, channel links) overlaid on the final 5-20 seconds of a video.

**Cards:** YouTube-specific interactive elements that can appear at any point during a video, linking to other videos, playlists, channels, or approved websites.

## Platform Terminology

### YouTube-Specific

**Impressions:** The number of times a video thumbnail is shown to viewers on YouTube. An impression occurs when at least 50% of the thumbnail is visible for at least one second.

**Click-Through Rate (CTR):** The percentage of impressions that result in a video view. Formula: (Clicks / Impressions) x 100. Healthy CTR ranges: 4-10% depending on niche and audience size.

**Average View Duration (AVD):** The mean length of time viewers watch a video. The single most important metric for YouTube algorithm performance.

**Average Percentage Viewed (APV):** The mean percentage of total video length watched by viewers. Indicates how well a video retains its audience relative to its length.

**Retention Graph:** A visual representation of audience retention over the duration of a video, showing the percentage of viewers still watching at each second. The most diagnostic tool for video performance analysis.

**Browse Features:** YouTube's recommendation surfaces: Home page, Subscription feed, and Suggested videos. The primary discovery mechanism for most channels.

**Watch Time:** The cumulative minutes of viewing a video generates. YouTube's algorithm historically weighted watch time heavily; now balanced with satisfaction signals.

**Session Time:** The total time a viewer spends on YouTube after clicking a video. Videos that increase session time are rewarded by the algorithm.

**Suggested Videos:** The recommendation panel appearing alongside or after a video. Algorithmic placement based on topical relevance, audience overlap, and engagement signals.

**YouTube Studio:** YouTube's native analytics and management dashboard for creators.

**Community Tab:** A YouTube channel feature enabling text posts, polls, images, and quizzes to engage subscribers between video uploads.

### Short-Form Specific

**FYP (For You Page):** TikTok's primary discovery feed, algorithmically curated based on user behavior signals.

**Explore Page:** Instagram's discovery surface where Reels are recommended to non-followers based on engagement signals.

**Hook (Short-Form):** The opening 1-3 seconds of a short-form video designed to arrest scrolling behavior and capture attention. The single most important element of short-form content.

**Pattern Interrupt:** A visual, auditory, or narrative technique that breaks the viewer's passive scrolling state and compels active attention. Examples: unexpected movement, direct address, surprising visual, text overlay.

**Sound/Audio:** In short-form context, the music or audio track associated with a video. Trending sounds receive algorithmic boost on TikTok and Reels.

**Stitch:** TikTok feature allowing creators to clip and respond to another creator's video, building on existing content and leveraging its audience.

**Duet:** TikTok feature enabling side-by-side video with another creator's content, facilitating reaction, commentary, or collaboration.

**Trending Format:** A recurring video structure, transition, or concept that gains widespread adoption across a platform. Participating in trending formats provides algorithmic benefit during the trend's peak.

## Analytics Terminology

**Retention Rate:** The percentage of viewers who continue watching at any given point in a video. Distinct from retention graph, which shows this over time.

**Drop-Off Point:** A specific moment in a video where a disproportionate number of viewers stop watching. Identified via the retention graph. Critical diagnostic signal.

**Audience Retention Types:**
- **Absolute Retention:** Percentage of viewers watching at each moment, measured from total view starts.
- **Relative Retention:** How well a video retains compared to similar-length videos on the platform.

**Re-Watch Rate:** The percentage of viewers who replay specific segments of a video. High re-watch indicates valuable or confusing content at that timestamp.

**Unique Viewers:** The estimated number of individual people who watched a video, deduplicated across multiple views by the same person.

**Revenue Per Mille (RPM):** The estimated revenue earned per 1,000 views, inclusive of all revenue sources (ads, memberships, Super Chat). YouTube-specific metric.

**CPM (Cost Per Mille):** The cost an advertiser pays per 1,000 ad impressions. From the creator's perspective, this is the gross ad rate before YouTube's revenue share.

**Conversion Rate (Video):** The percentage of viewers who complete a desired action after watching (click link, sign up, purchase). Measured through UTM parameters, custom URLs, or platform-native tracking.

## Production Workflow Terminology

**Dailies:** Raw, unedited footage reviewed at the end of each shooting day to assess quality and completeness.

**Rough Cut:** The first assembled edit of a video with all major scenes in sequence but without finalized pacing, color, audio, or graphics.

**Fine Cut:** A refined edit with improved pacing, trimmed content, and preliminary audio and color work. Typically used for stakeholder review.

**Picture Lock:** The stage at which the video edit is finalized and no further changes to timing, sequence, or content will be made. Color grading, sound design, and graphics proceed after picture lock.

**Master:** The final, fully finished video file ready for export and distribution.

**Proxy (Editing):** Lower-resolution copies of source footage used during editing to improve NLE performance. Final export reconnects to original high-resolution media.

**Render:** The process of generating the final video file from the NLE timeline, applying all effects, transitions, color, and audio processing.

**Transcode:** Converting video from one codec or format to another, typically for compatibility or optimization purposes.

## Strategic Terminology

**Content Pillar:** A core thematic category that defines a channel's content strategy. Typically 3-5 pillars that collectively serve the target audience and support business objectives.

**Content Flywheel:** A self-reinforcing cycle where content production generates data, data informs better content, better content grows audience, and larger audience justifies more production investment.

**Evergreen Content:** Video content that remains relevant and continues generating views indefinitely, not tied to trends, news, or time-sensitive topics.

**Tent-Pole Content:** High-production-value videos designed to attract maximum audience, often tied to seasonal events, product launches, or cultural moments.

**Hero-Hub-Help Framework:** A YouTube content strategy model: Hero (big, broad-appeal content), Hub (series-based, subscriber-focused content), Help (search-driven, evergreen how-to content).

**Repurposing Matrix:** A structured plan defining how a single video production is decomposed into multiple content assets across platforms and formats.

**Thumbnail CTR Optimization:** The systematic process of designing, testing, and iterating on video thumbnails to maximize click-through rate from impressions.

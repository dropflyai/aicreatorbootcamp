# Platform Distribution — Algorithmic Optimization and Cross-Platform Strategy

## The Distribution Imperative

Creating excellent video content is necessary but insufficient. Distribution determines whether content reaches its intended audience. Each platform operates a unique recommendation algorithm with distinct ranking signals, content preferences, and audience behaviors. Treating distribution as an afterthought — uploading the same file everywhere — leaves 80-90% of potential reach unrealized. Platform-native distribution requires understanding each algorithm's reward function and optimizing content accordingly.

---

## 1. YouTube Algorithm — Deep Dive

### Algorithm Architecture

YouTube's recommendation system is a multi-stage deep neural network that processes hundreds of signals to serve each user a personalized content feed. The system operates in two stages:

**Stage 1: Candidate Generation**
- Reduces billions of videos to hundreds of candidates
- Uses collaborative filtering (what similar users watched), search history, subscriptions, and geographic relevance
- This stage determines whether your video enters the consideration set at all

**Stage 2: Ranking**
- Scores candidate videos using a deep neural network trained on watch time and engagement signals
- Ranks candidates to maximize expected user satisfaction per impression
- This stage determines position in recommendations, suggested videos, and home feed

### Key Ranking Signals

| Signal | Weight | Description | How to Optimize |
|--------|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Click-through rate (CTR) | Very High | % of impressions that result in clicks | Compelling thumbnails, curiosity-driven titles |
| Watch duration | Very High | Absolute minutes watched per view | Hook viewers in first 30 seconds; deliver on promise |
| Average view duration (AVD) | Very High | % of video watched on average | Pacing, narrative tension, pattern interrupts |
| Session time contribution | High | Does this video lead to more YouTube watching? | End screens, suggested video cards, series playlists |
| Engagement rate | Medium-High | Likes, comments, shares, saves relative to views | Ask questions, create debate, call to action |
| Upload freshness | Medium | How recently was the video published | Consistent upload schedule, timely topics |
| Channel authority | Medium | Channel's historical performance on similar content | Build consistent track record in a niche |
| Viewer satisfaction | Medium | Post-view survey responses, not-interested signals | Match content to title/thumbnail promise exactly |

### YouTube SEO

**Title Optimization:**
- Primary keyword within first 50 characters
- Curiosity gap or value proposition clear
- Maximum 70 characters (truncation in search results)
- Format patterns that work: "How to [X] (Without [Common Objection])", "[Number] [Topic] [Benefit]", "Why [Counterintuitive Claim]"

**Description Optimization:**
- First 2 lines visible above the fold (hook + keyword)
- Full description: 200-500 words with natural keyword integration
- Timestamps (chapters) for longer videos (improves search appearance and engagement)
- Links to related content, products, social profiles
- Hashtags: 3-5 relevant (first 3 appear above title)

**Tag Strategy:**
- Primary keyword as first tag
- Long-tail variations
- Related topics and alternative phrasings
- Competitor channel/video names (ethically used as topical tags)
- 20-30 tags total, mix of broad and specific

**Thumbnail Framework:**
- 1280x720px minimum, 16:9 aspect ratio
- High contrast, readable at mobile size
- Maximum 3-4 words of text (if any)
- Face with emotion (surprise, excitement, curiosity) increases CTR 15-30%
- Color contrast with YouTube's red/white interface
- Consistent style within a series, distinct across series
- A/B test with YouTube's native thumbnail testing feature

## 2. TikTok Algorithm

### Core Algorithm Mechanics

TikTok's For You Page (FYP) algorithm evaluates content primarily on interaction signals from the initial distribution cohort. Unlike YouTube, TikTok gives significant reach to new creators based on content quality rather than follower count.

**Distribution Funnel:**
```
Upload → Initial cohort (200-500 views)
         ↓ Performance evaluated
    Pass → Second cohort (1K-5K views)
         ↓ Performance evaluated
    Pass → Larger cohort (10K-50K views)
         ↓ Performance evaluated
    Pass → Viral distribution (100K+ views)
```

### TikTok Ranking Signals (Estimated Priority)

| Signal | Weight | Optimization |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Completion rate | Highest | Videos that are watched to the end or rewatched rank highest. Keep videos tight — 15-60 seconds optimal for completion |
| Rewatch rate | Very High | Create content worth watching twice: reveals, twists, dense information |
| Share rate | High | Content shared to DMs or other platforms signals high value |
| Comment engagement | High | Controversial takes, questions, relatable content drives comments |
| Like rate | Medium | Baseline engagement signal |
| Follow-through rate | Medium | % of viewers who follow after watching |
| Video information | Medium | Captions, sounds, hashtags help with topical classification |
| Account standing | Low-Medium | Consistent posting, no community guideline violations |

### TikTok Content Optimization

**Hook (First 1-3 Seconds):**
- The hook determines whether viewers stop scrolling
- Pattern interrupt: unexpected visual, provocative statement, fascinating process
- Text overlay with hook statement for sound-off viewers
- No logos, intros, or warm-up — immediate value

**Structure Patterns That Work:**
- **List format:** "3 things nobody tells you about [X]" (sequential, completable)
- **Story format:** Setup → tension → resolution (narrative drive)
- **Tutorial format:** "Here's how to [X]" (practical value)
- **POV format:** "POV: [relatable situation]" (emotional connection)
- **Stitch/Duet:** Respond to trending content (algorithm boost from engagement chain)

## 3. LinkedIn Video

### Algorithm Behavior
LinkedIn favors content that generates professional conversation. The algorithm measures dwell time, comments (especially long-form), and relevance to the viewer's professional context.

**Best Practices:**
- 30 seconds to 3 minutes optimal (LinkedIn penalizes very long videos)
- Native upload (not YouTube links — LinkedIn suppresses outbound links)
- Square (1:1) or vertical (9:16) format for mobile feed dominance
- Subtitles mandatory (85% watch without sound in professional contexts)
- Professional production value not required; authenticity performs better
- Hook in first 3 seconds with text overlay summarizing value
- Content types that work: lessons learned, industry analysis, behind-the-scenes, career advice

## 4. Instagram Video (Reels and Stories)

### Reels Algorithm
Instagram Reels follows a similar model to TikTok: content is distributed to non-followers based on engagement signals from initial distribution cohorts.

**Key Differences from TikTok:**
- Reels compete for attention across photos, carousels, and Stories — not just video
- Instagram's algorithm over-indexes on saves and shares relative to likes
- Hashtag strategy matters more on Instagram than TikTok
- Audio/music selection affects discoverability (trending sounds get algorithmic boost)

### Stories Strategy
- 5-15 second stories with polls, questions, and interactive stickers
- Sticker engagement (poll vote, question answer) is a strong ranking signal
- Stories maintain top-of-mind with existing followers (not discovery)
- Use Stories to drive traffic to Reels and feed posts

## 5. Cross-Platform Strategy

### Platform-Native Adaptation

| Element | YouTube | TikTok | LinkedIn | Instagram Reels |
|---------|---------|--------|----------|----------------|
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 | 9:16 | 1:1 or 9:16 | 9:16 |
| Optimal length | 8-15 min (long), 60s (Shorts) | 15-60 seconds | 30s-3min | 15-60 seconds |
| Hook timing | 0-30 seconds | 0-3 seconds | 0-3 seconds | 0-3 seconds |
| Subtitles | Recommended | Essential | Essential | Recommended |
| CTA style | Subscribe + bell, end screens | Follow, comment | Comment, share | Follow, save |
| Watermark | N/A | Remove if reposting | Remove TikTok watermark | Remove TikTok watermark |
| Posting time | Varies by audience | 7-9am, 12-3pm, 7-11pm | Tue-Thu 8-10am | Tue-Fri 11am-1pm |

### Cross-Posting Rules

**Do:**
- Adapt content natively for each platform (re-edit, re-format, re-caption)
- Post platform-native content first, then adapt for secondary platforms
- Maintain consistent brand voice across platforms while adapting format
- Track performance by platform to understand where content resonates

**Don't:**
- Post the exact same file to every platform
- Leave competitor platform watermarks visible (TikTok logo on Instagram Reels)
- Use identical captions across platforms (each has different norms)
- Link out from platforms that penalize outbound links (LinkedIn, TikTok)

# Video Marketing — Video Across the Marketing Funnel

## Video as the Dominant Marketing Medium

Video is the most versatile and highest-converting content format across every stage of the marketing funnel. Wyzowl (2024) reports that 91% of businesses use video as a marketing tool, 87% of marketers report video delivers positive ROI, and viewers retain 95% of a message when watching video versus 10% when reading text (Insivia). Yet most organizations deploy video tactically rather than strategically — creating one-off videos without a funnel-aligned distribution strategy.

## 2. Video Ad Formats

### Pre-Roll / In-Stream Ads

| Format | Platform | Duration | Skippable | Best For |
|--------|----------|----------|-----------|----------|
| TrueView In-Stream | YouTube | Any (5s+ skip) | Yes (after 5s) | Awareness, consideration |
| Non-Skippable In-Stream | YouTube | 15-20 seconds | No | Brand awareness, reach |
| Bumper Ads | YouTube | 6 seconds max | No | Brand reinforcement, retargeting |
| In-Stream | Meta (FB/IG) | Up to 240s | User scrolls | Awareness, conversion |
| Connected TV (CTV) | Roku, Hulu, etc. | 15-30 seconds | Varies | Mass awareness |

### In-Feed / Native Video Ads

| Format | Platform | Aspect Ratio | Best For |
|--------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Sponsored content | LinkedIn | 1:1 or 16:9 | B2B consideration |
| In-feed video | Meta | 1:1 or 4:5 | Awareness, conversion |
| Spark Ads | TikTok | 9:16 | Amplifying organic content |
| Promoted video | X/Twitter | 16:9 or 1:1 | Awareness, engagement |

### Video Ad Creative Best Practices

**The 3-Second Rule:** 65% of people who watch the first 3 seconds will watch at least 10 seconds. The first 3 seconds must:
- Communicate the brand (visual, not verbal — logo, product, distinctive color)
- Establish relevance (who is this for?)
- Create a reason to keep watching (curiosity, emotion, value promise)

**Sound-Off Design:** 85% of Facebook video is watched without sound. All video ads must be effective with sound off:
- Bold text overlays communicating key messages
- Visual storytelling that works without narration
- Subtitles for any spoken content
- Music enhances but must not carry the message

**CTA Placement:**
- Mid-roll CTA for awareness (after hook, before interest declines)
- End CTA for consideration and decision (after value is delivered)
- Overlay CTA for conversion (persistent, non-intrusive)

### Video Ad Performance Benchmarks

| Metric | Awareness Campaign | Consideration Campaign | Conversion Campaign |
|--------|-------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| View rate | 15-30% | 20-40% | 25-50% |
| View-through rate (VTR) | 10-20% | 15-30% | 20-40% |
| CPV (cost per view) | $0.01-0.05 | $0.03-0.10 | $0.05-0.15 |
| CTR | 0.5-1.5% | 1-3% | 2-5% |
| Conversion rate | N/A | 0.5-2% | 2-8% |

## 3. Video Landing Pages

### Why Video on Landing Pages

Landing pages with video convert 80% better than those without (Unbounce). Video communicates value propositions faster, builds trust through social proof, and reduces cognitive load compared to text-heavy pages.

### Video Landing Page Architecture

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
│           HERO SECTION               │
│  Headline + Video Player             │
│  (Auto-play muted, click for sound)  │
│  Video: 60-120 seconds               │
│  Content: Value prop + social proof   │
│  CTA below video player              │
├──────────────────────────────────────┤
│         SUPPORTING CONTENT           │
│  · Key benefit bullets (3-5)         │
│  · Testimonial video thumbnails      │
│  · Feature highlights with GIFs      │
│  · Trust signals (logos, stats)      │
├──────────────────────────────────────┤
│         SECONDARY VIDEO              │
│  Demo / deep-dive (2-5 minutes)      │
│  For visitors who want more detail   │
├──────────────────────────────────────┤
│           CONVERSION                 │
│  Form or CTA + final testimonial     │
└──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Landing Page Video Best Practices

| Element | Recommendation | Rationale |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Auto-play | Yes, muted with prominent play button | Captures attention without audio disruption |
| Length | 60-120 seconds for hero; 2-5 minutes for demo | Short for hook, long for consideration |
| Thumbnail | Custom frame with play button overlay | Communicates content before play |
| Placement | Above the fold, left or center aligned | Eye-tracking data shows top-left focus |
| Mobile | Responsive player, full-width on mobile | 60%+ traffic is mobile |
| Loading | Lazy-load video, optimize file size | Page speed affects conversion and SEO |
| Analytics | Track play rate, completion, CTA clicks | Measure video contribution to conversion |

## 4. Webinar-to-Content Strategy

### The Webinar Content Multiplier

A single 60-minute webinar produces 15-25 pieces of derivative content across channels:

**From One Webinar:**
| Content Piece | Format | Channel | Effort |
|--------------|--------|---------|--------|
| Full recording (edited) | Long-form video | YouTube, website | Low (edit only) |
| 5-8 topic clips (2-5 min each) | Mid-form video | YouTube, LinkedIn | Medium |
| 10-15 quote/insight clips (15-60s) | Short-form video | TikTok, Reels, Shorts | Medium |
| Blog post (transcript + editorial) | Written | Blog, SEO | Medium |
| Key takeaway carousel | Image/carousel | LinkedIn, Instagram | Low |
| Quote graphics (5-8) | Image | All social | Low |
| Email nurture sequence (3-5 emails) | Email | Marketing automation | Medium |
| Podcast episode (audio extracted) | Audio | Podcast platforms | Low |
| Slide deck (cleaned up) | PDF/presentation | SlideShare, gated | Low |
| Infographic | Image | Blog, social, email | Medium |

### Webinar Production for Maximum Reuse

Design webinars for content derivation from the start:
- Modular structure: Clear segments that work as standalone clips
- Clean audio: External microphones, quiet environment
- Speaker framing: Consistent, well-lit, with brand-consistent background
- Slides: Designed as social-ready graphics (clean, minimal, brand-aligned)
- Q&A: Record separately for dedicated Q&A content
- Timestamps: Note clip-worthy moments in real-time for faster post-production

## 5. Paid Video Promotion Strategy

### Budget Allocation Framework

| Funnel Stage | Budget % | Objective | Primary KPI |
|-------------|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Awareness | 30-40% | Reach + brand lift | CPM, view rate, brand recall |
| Consideration | 30-40% | Engagement + leads | CPV, VTR, lead gen cost |
| Conversion | 20-30% | Sales + signups | CPA, ROAS, conversion rate |
| Retargeting | 10-15% | Re-engage warm audiences | Frequency, conversion rate |

### Targeting Strategy by Funnel Stage

**Awareness:**
- Broad interest and demographic targeting
- Lookalike audiences based on best customers
- Contextual targeting (topics, channels, keywords)
- Geographic and language targeting

**Consideration:**
- Website visitor retargeting (visited key pages but did not convert)
- Engaged video viewers (watched >50% of awareness video)
- Email subscriber lookalikes
- In-market audiences (Google) / purchase intent (Meta)

**Conversion:**
- High-intent retargeting (pricing page visitors, free trial abandoners)
- CRM audience matching (SQLs, opportunities)
- Sequential messaging (awareness → consideration → conversion sequence)
- Dynamic retargeting with personalized video

### Creative Testing Framework

| Test Variable | Test Method | Minimum Sample | Decision Criteria |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Thumbnail | A/B split | 10K impressions per variant | CTR difference >10% relative |
| Hook (first 3s) | A/B split | 5K views per variant | VTR difference >15% relative |
| Video length | A/B split | 5K views per variant | Conversion rate or VTR |
| CTA placement | A/B split | 5K views per variant | CTA click rate |
| Format (live action vs. animated) | A/B split | 10K impressions | VTR + conversion rate |

## 6. Video Marketing Measurement

### Attribution Model for Video

Video operates across the full funnel, making attribution complex. Use multi-touch attribution or incrementality testing:

| Model | Description | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Last-click | Credit to last touchpoint before conversion | Simple, but under-credits awareness video |
| Linear | Equal credit to all touchpoints | Better for multi-video journeys |
| Time-decay | More credit to touchpoints closer to conversion | Balances awareness and conversion |
| Data-driven | ML model assigns credit based on observed patterns | Best accuracy; requires significant data |
| Incrementality | A/B holdout measures lift from video exposure | Gold standard for proving video ROI |

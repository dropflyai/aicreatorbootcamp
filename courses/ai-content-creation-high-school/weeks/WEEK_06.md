## Session 1: How Algorithms Actually Work (90 minutes)

### Opening Hook (15 min)
**"Why Did This Go Viral?"**

Show 3 viral videos that seem "random" — a person folding towels, someone eating food, a POV of sitting in traffic.

Ask: Why did THESE get millions of views?

**Reveal:** It's not random. Every viral video triggers algorithm signals. Today we decode them.

### Core Content: Algorithm Basics (25 min)

**What Is an Algorithm?**
A recommendation system that decides:
- What content to show
- To whom
- In what order
- For how long

**The Core Truth:**
Platforms make money when users stay on the app. Algorithms optimize for ENGAGEMENT, not quality.

**The Feedback Loop:**
```
You post → Algorithm shows to small test audience
↓
Test audience engages (or doesn't)
↓
Algorithm decides: push wider or kill reach
↓
More engagement = more distribution
↓
Cycle continues until engagement drops
```

**Key Signals by Platform:**

| Platform | Primary Signal | Secondary Signals |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **TikTok** | Watch completion rate | Replays, shares, comments |
| **Instagram Reels** | Saves | Shares, comments, watch time |
| **YouTube Shorts** | Watch time | Subscribers gained, likes |
| **Twitter/X** | Replies | Retweets, quote tweets |

### Core Content: The STEPPS Framework (25 min)

**Why Things Go Viral (According to Research)**

Jonah Berger's STEPPS framework from *Contagious*:

| Letter | Principle | What It Means | Content Example |
|--------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **S** | Social Currency | Makes sharer look good | "Only smart people know this..." |
| **T** | Triggers | Top-of-mind associations | Back-to-school content in August |
| **E** | Emotion | High-arousal feelings | Awe, surprise, anger, anxiety |
| **P** | Public | Visible to others | Challenges, trends, duets |
| **P** | Practical Value | Useful information | "3 things I wish I knew..." |
| **S** | Stories | Narrative structure | Storytime with a twist |

**Emotion Deep Dive:**

| Emotion | Arousal Level | Virality | Example |
|---------|---------------|----------|---------|
| Awe | High | Very Viral | Mind-blowing facts |
| Anxiety | High | Viral | "You're doing this wrong" |
| Anger | High | Very Viral | Hot takes, injustice |
| Surprise | High | Very Viral | Plot twists |
| Sadness | Low | NOT viral | Depressing content |
| Contentment | Low | NOT viral | "Life is good" posts |

**Key Insight:** Low-arousal emotions don't spread. Content must create high-energy emotional response.

### Activity: STEPPS Analysis (20 min)

**Analyze 5 Viral Videos:**

For each video, identify:
1. Which STEPPS principles are present?
2. What's the primary emotion triggered?
3. What's the hook that stopped the scroll?
4. What made people share (not just watch)?

**Discuss findings as class.**

### Wrap-Up (5 min)
- Algorithms reward engagement, not effort
- STEPPS gives you a formula
- Preview: Platform-specific optimization

## Session 2: Platform Optimization (90 minutes)

### Quick Review (10 min)
- STEPPS framework recap
- Which principle is easiest for your niche?

### Core Content: TikTok Algorithm Deep Dive (20 min)

**TikTok's Unique Power:**
- Doesn't rely on followers
- Content tested independently
- Can go viral with 0 followers

**TikTok's Algorithm Priorities:**
1. **Watch completion** — Did they watch to the end?
2. **Rewatch** — Did they watch again?
3. **Shares** — Did they send to friends?
4. **Comments** — Did they engage?
5. **Follows from video** — Did they want more?

**Optimization Tactics:**

| Tactic | Why It Works |
|--------|--------------|
| Hook in 0.5 seconds | Prevents scroll-off |
| 15-34 second length | Easier full completion |
| Loop ending back to start | Triggers rewatch |
| Ask a question | Drives comments |
| "Send this to..." | Drives shares |
| Pattern interrupts every 5 sec | Maintains attention |

**Content Types That Perform:**
- Educational (learn something)
- Entertainment (laugh or wow)
- Relatable (that's so me)
- Trend participation (recognizable format)

### Core Content: Instagram Reels Optimization (15 min)

**Instagram's Difference:**
- Existing followers matter more
- Saves are the golden metric
- Aesthetic quality matters
- TikTok watermarks = death penalty

**Instagram Algorithm Priorities:**
1. **Saves** — Content worth returning to
2. **Shares** — Sent via DM
3. **Comments** — Meaningful engagement
4. **Watch time** — Full views

**Optimization Tactics:**

| Tactic | Why It Works |
|--------|--------------|
| Create save-worthy content | Tips, tutorials, lists |
| Higher production quality | Instagram users expect polish |
| Use Reels-native features | Algorithm rewards loyalty |
| Post to feed AND Reels | Double exposure |
| Carousel + Reel combo | Content repurposing |

### Core Content: YouTube Shorts Optimization (10 min)

**YouTube's Difference:**
- Gateway to long-form
- Watch time is king
- Subscriber conversion matters
- 60 seconds max

**YouTube Algorithm Priorities:**
1. **Watch time** — Total seconds watched
2. **Subscriber conversion** — New subscribers from Short
3. **Engagement** — Likes, comments
4. **Session time** — Do they stay on YouTube?

**Optimization Tactics:**

| Tactic | Why It Works |
|--------|--------------|
| Use the full 60 seconds | More watch time possible |
| Reference long-form content | Drives to main channel |
| Clear CTA to subscribe | Convert viewers to followers |
| Series format | Brings viewers back |

### Core Content: Posting Strategy (15 min)

**When to Post:**

| Platform | Best Times (General) | Why |
|----------|---------------------|-----|
| TikTok | 7-9 PM, lunch hours | After school/work, breaks |
| Instagram | 6-9 PM weekdays | Wind-down time |
| YouTube | 2-4 PM, 8-11 PM | Before/after prime time |

**The Real Answer:** Check YOUR analytics.
- When are YOUR followers online?
- Test different times
- Track results

**Posting Frequency:**

| Platform | Minimum | Optimal |
|----------|---------|---------|
| TikTok | 1x/day | 1-3x/day |
| Instagram Reels | 3x/week | 4-7x/week |
| YouTube Shorts | 3x/week | 3-5x/week |

**Consistency Rule:** Posting daily for 100 days > posting 30 times in week 1 then disappearing.

### Activity: Algorithm Audit (20 min)

**Audit Your Own Content (or a creator you follow):**

1. Pull up last 10 posts
2. For each, identify:
   - Watch completion estimate (guess based on length)
   - STEPPS principles used
   - Hook strength (1-5)
   - CTA clarity (1-5)
3. Find the pattern: What did top performers have in common?
4. Identify: What could be optimized?

**Share findings with partner.**

## Weekly Project: "Algorithm Audit + Optimized Post"

### Part 1: Creator Audit

Choose a creator in your niche with 10K-100K followers.

**Analyze their last 20 posts:**
- Which 3 performed best? Why?
- Which 3 performed worst? Why?
- What patterns do you see?
- What STEPPS principles do they use most?

**Deliverable:** 1-page audit report with specific observations and data.

### Part 2: Optimized Post

Create ONE post that deliberately applies:
- At least 2 STEPPS principles
- Platform-specific optimization (choose TikTok, Reels, or Shorts)
- Strong hook
- Completion optimization (length, pacing)
- Clear CTA

**Deliverables:**
1. Final video
2. Brief explanation: What algorithm signals are you targeting?
3. Prediction: How do you think it will perform and why?

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Can't identify STEPPS | Use checklist, go letter by letter |
| All content seems random | Look at top-performing only, patterns emerge |
| Overwhelmed by optimization | Focus on ONE platform first |
| Don't know posting times | Start with general recommendations, then check your analytics |
| Content still not performing | Focus on hook and completion rate first |

# Week 1: "What Is AI, Really?"

## Theme: Demystifying AI for Creators

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, students will be able to:
1. Explain what generative AI is in simple terms
2. Set up accounts on ChatGPT, CapCut, and Canva
3. Write their first successful AI prompts
4. Articulate basic AI ethics (disclosure, attribution)
5. Create their first AI-assisted social media post

## Session 1: AI Already Knows You (90 minutes)

### Opening Hook (15 min)
**"AI Already Knows You"**

Show students how AI powers their daily life:
- TikTok For You Page — AI predicting what you'll watch
- Spotify Discover Weekly — AI knowing your music taste
- Instagram Explore — AI curating your interests
- YouTube recommendations — AI keeping you watching

**Discussion Prompt:** "How does it feel knowing AI predicts your behavior? Creepy? Cool? Both?"

**The Flip:** "Today you learn to flip the script. Instead of AI controlling what you see, YOU will control what AI creates."

### Core Content: What Is AI? (30 min)

**Demystifying AI:**
- AI is **pattern matching**, not magic or sentience
- It predicts "what word/image comes next" based on training data
- It doesn't "think" — it generates based on patterns
- It can be wrong, biased, and confidently incorrect

**Live Demo:**
1. Ask ChatGPT a simple question — show the answer
2. Ask it something it can't know (future events) — show limitation
3. Ask it something factually wrong — show it can be confidently wrong
4. Ask it to be creative — show the potential

**Key Concept:** AI is a tool. A hammer doesn't build a house — a person with a hammer does. AI doesn't create content — YOU create content with AI's help.

### Activity: My AI Interview (35 min)

**Setup:** Students open ChatGPT (free tier)

**Prompt Template:**
```
I want you to interview me like a podcast host. Ask me questions about:
- My interests and hobbies
- What kind of content I want to create
- What makes me different from other people my age

Ask one question at a time and respond to my answers before asking the next question. Be genuinely curious and conversational.
```

**Students complete the interview, then evaluate:**
1. What 3 things did AI get RIGHT about you?
2. What 3 things did AI get WRONG or assume incorrectly?
3. What questions surprised you?

**Debrief:** AI makes assumptions based on patterns. YOUR job is to correct, guide, and direct it.

### Wrap-Up (10 min)
- Preview Session 2: Account setup and first post
- Homework: Think about your content niche (what would you create about?)

## Session 2: Your AI Creator Toolkit (90 minutes)

### Quick Review (5 min)
- What did we learn about AI?
- Anyone have interesting interview moments to share?

### Account Setup Workshop (40 min)

**Tools to Set Up:**

**1. ChatGPT (15 min)**
- Go to chat.openai.com
- Create free account
- Explore the interface
- Try a creative prompt: "Give me 5 content ideas for a high school student interested in [their interest]"

**2. Canva (15 min)**
- Go to canva.com
- Create free account (use .edu email if available for Pro)
- Explore templates
- Find social media templates

**3. CapCut (10 min)**
- Download app or go to capcut.com
- Create account
- Brief interface tour
- Will use more in Week 4

### Ethics Discussion: The Creator's Code (20 min)

**The Big Questions:**
1. If AI writes your caption, is it "your" content?
2. When should you tell people AI helped?
3. Can you use AI-generated images as "yours"?

**The Creator's Code (Class Agreement):**

1. **AI is your assistant, not your replacement**
   - You provide the ideas, direction, and judgment
   - AI helps execute and refine

2. **Be transparent when it matters**
   - Major AI contribution = disclose
   - Minor assistance (grammar, ideas) = your call
   - Never claim AI art as hand-drawn

3. **Verify before you publish**
   - AI can be wrong
   - Fact-check claims
   - You're responsible for what you post

4. **Keep it you**
   - Your voice, your style, your ideas
   - AI enhances; it doesn't define you

### First Post Creation (20 min)

**Project: My AI Creator Manifesto**

Create a visual post declaring:
- Who you are
- What kind of content you want to create
- Your creative values

**Workflow:**
1. Use ChatGPT to brainstorm your manifesto text
2. Use Canva to design the visual
3. Optional: Use Bing Image Creator for background image

**Prompt for ChatGPT:**
```
Help me write a short "Creator Manifesto" for my social media. I'm a high school student interested in [INTEREST].

I want to create content about [TOPIC]. My style is [casual/serious/funny/educational].

Write it in first person, make it punchy and memorable. Maximum 50 words.
```

### Wrap-Up & Assignment (5 min)

**Weekly Project Due:**
"My AI Creator Manifesto" visual post
- Must include your niche declaration
- Must be designed in Canva
- Must show AI-assisted but personal voice
- Post to class Discord/channel for peer feedback

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Student can't create accounts | Have backup instructor accounts for demo purposes |
| AI gives inappropriate response | Use this as a teaching moment about AI limitations |
| Student unsure of niche | Provide a list of 20 popular content categories as starting points |
| Technical difficulties | Pair students who are comfortable with those who need help |

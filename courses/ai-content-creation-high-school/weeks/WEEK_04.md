# Week 4: "Video That Stops the Scroll"

## Theme: Short-Form Video Mastery

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, students will be able to:
1. Understand platform specs for TikTok, Reels, and Shorts
2. Create hooks that capture attention in 0.5-1 second
3. Apply pacing and pattern interrupts for retention
4. Film quality content with just a phone
5. Edit using CapCut's AI features

---

## Session 1: The Hook Is Everything (90 minutes)

### Opening Hook (15 min)
**"Viral Video Autopsy"**

Watch 10 viral videos (TikTok/Reels) as a class.
For each one:
- What happened in the first second?
- When did you WANT to scroll vs. stayed?
- What pattern made you keep watching?

**Key Finding:** The hook decides everything. 50% of viewers leave in first 3 seconds.

### Core Content: Platform Specifications (15 min)

| Spec | TikTok | Instagram Reels | YouTube Shorts |
|------|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Aspect Ratio** | 9:16 vertical | 9:16 vertical | 9:16 vertical |
| **Resolution** | 1080x1920 | 1080x1920 | 1080x1920 |
| **Optimal Length** | 15-34 sec | 15-30 sec | 30-58 sec |
| **Max Length** | 10 min | 20 min | 60 sec |
| **Key Signal** | Completion rate | Saves | Watch time |

**The Universal Truth:** All platforms reward videos people watch to the end.

### Core Content: Hook Mastery (25 min)

**You Have 0.5-1 Second**

That's the time before someone decides to scroll.

**Visual Hooks:**
- Movement in first frame
- Unexpected image
- Face with strong emotion
- Text overlay creating curiosity
- Before/after split preview

**Audio Hooks:**
- First words create curiosity gap
- Trending sound recognition
- "Stop scrolling if..."
- Surprising statement

**Hook Formulas:**

| Type | Template | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Bold Claim** | "Most people [do X] wrong" | "Most people study completely wrong" |
| **Question** | "Would you [do X] if [Y]?" | "Would you skip school if you knew this?" |
| **Number Promise** | "[#] [things] that [outcome]" | "3 apps that got me an A" |
| **POV** | "POV: [relatable scenario]" | "POV: You finally understand chemistry" |
| **Shock** | "[Unexpected statement]. Here's why." | "I failed every test freshman year." |
| **Direct** | "[Command] if [qualifier]" | "Stop scrolling if you have homework due" |

### Activity: Hook Writing Workshop (30 min)

**Round 1: Write 10 Hooks (10 min)**
For your content niche, write 10 different hooks using different formulas.

**Round 2: Peer Ranking (10 min)**
- Swap with partner
- Rank their hooks 1-10 (1 = would definitely stop scrolling)
- Explain your #1 and #10 choices

**Round 3: Film Your Best Hook (10 min)**
- Pick your highest-ranked hook
- Film just the first 3 seconds
- Show to class for instant feedback

### Wrap-Up (5 min)
- Best hooks shared
- Preview: Filming and editing in Session 2

## Session 2: Filming and Editing (90 minutes)

### Quick Review (5 min)
- Favorite hooks from last session
- Questions about platform specs

### Core Content: Phone Filming Basics (20 min)

**Lighting:**
- Best free option: Face a window
- Position: Light in FRONT, slightly to one side
- Avoid: Light behind you (backlit = dark face)
- Upgrade: $20 ring light for consistency

**Audio:**
- Quiet room > fancy mic
- Test before full recording
- Speak clearly, slight over-enunciation
- External mic ($15-40) for serious upgrade

**Stability:**
- Phone tripod essential ($15-30)
- Lean elbows on surface for handheld
- Don't walk and talk unless intentional
- Use in-app stabilization

**Framing:**
- Fill the frame (closer > farther)
- Rule of thirds for composition
- Eye-level or slightly above
- Consistent background

**Phone Settings:**
- 1080p or 4K if available
- 30 fps for standard video
- HDR for high contrast scenes
- Grid lines ON

### Core Content: Pacing and Retention (15 min)

**Pattern Interrupts Every 5-8 Seconds:**
- Change camera angle
- Add text animation
- Sound effect or music shift
- Visual zoom or cut
- New visual element

**The Micro Three-Act Structure:**
```
HOOK (0-3 sec): Stop the scroll, promise value
TENSION (3-12 sec): Build curiosity, deliver content
PAYOFF (last 3-5 sec): Deliver promise, call to action
```

**Word Economy:**
- ~2 words per second speaking pace
- 30-second video = 60-85 words MAX
- Cut every unnecessary word
- Pauses are okay but brief

**Loop Design:**
- End connects to beginning
- Viewers rewatch without realizing
- Rewatches = strongest algorithm signal

### Tool Deep Dive: CapCut (35 min)

**Essential CapCut Features:**

**1. Auto Captions (10 min demo)**
- Import video
- Text → Auto captions
- Choose style
- Edit for accuracy
- Essential: 80%+ watch without sound

**2. Transitions and Effects (10 min demo)**
- Smooth transitions between clips
- Zoom effects for emphasis
- Speed ramps (slow-mo to fast)
- Green screen (react to content)

**3. Text and Graphics (10 min demo)**
- Hook text overlay
- Tracking text (follows movement)
- Lower thirds
- Animated text reveals

**4. Audio (5 min demo)**
- Import trending sounds
- Adjust music/voice balance
- Sound effects library
- Voiceover recording

### Activity: Film and Edit Your Video (15 min)

Begin editing in CapCut:
- Add captions

## Weekly Project: "60-Second Banger"

Create one polished short-form video ready for publishing.

### Requirements

**Content:**
- 30-60 seconds length
- Clear hook in first 3 seconds
- Value or entertainment in body
- Call-to-action at end

**Technical:**
- 9:16 vertical format
- Captions throughout (auto-generated, then corrected)
- At least 3 pattern interrupts
- Music or sound design
- Clean audio

**Quality:**
- Good lighting (face visible)
- Stable footage
- Edited for pacing (no dead time)
- Thumbnail frame selected

### Deliverables
1. Final exported video (.mp4)
2. Screenshot of caption styling
3. Brief write-up: Hook used, pattern interrupts, and one thing you'd improve

## Filming Tips Handout

### Before You Film
- [ ] Location scouted (lighting, background, noise)
- [ ] Phone charged (>50%)
- [ ] Storage space available
- [ ] Script/outline reviewed
- [ ] Outfit camera-friendly (no thin stripes)

### During Filming
- [ ] Film horizontal (9:16) by holding phone vertically
- [ ] Multiple takes of each section
- [ ] Watch back on phone before moving on
- [ ] Save best takes immediately
- [ ] Get B-roll (extra footage for cutaways)

### After Filming
- [ ] Transfer to editing app
- [ ] Organize clips by section
- [ ] Back up raw footage
- [ ] Note which takes were best

---

## Common Issues & Solutions

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Bad lighting | Move near window, or outdoor shade |
| Shaky footage | Use tripod, prop phone against books |
| Poor audio | Film in quieter location, speak louder |
| CapCut crashing | Clear cache, reduce video quality to 1080p |
| Don't know what to film | Use the hook from Session 1 as starting point |
| Video too long | Cut 20% more than you think you need |

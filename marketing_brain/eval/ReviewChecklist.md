# ReviewChecklist.md -- Pre-Flight Gates for Marketing Brain

> Version: 1.0
> Last Updated: 2026-02-03
> Owner: Marketing Brain / CEO Brain
> Purpose: No marketing artifact ships until EVERY applicable gate passes.
> A single gate failure blocks the artifact. Marketing that damages the
> brand, wastes budget, or misleads customers is worse than no marketing.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

1. Identify the artifact type (Positioning Doc, Campaign Plan, Creative Launch,
   Budget Allocation, Channel Strategy, Content Piece, PR/Comms).
2. Check which gates apply (see Applicability Matrix at the end).
3. Every checkbox in every applicable gate must be checked.
4. If a checkbox cannot be checked, document WHY and escalate to CEO Brain.
5. Completed checklists stored in the Accountability Protocol audit trail.

---

## GATE 1: Positioning Gate

**Purpose:** Positioning is the foundation of all marketing. If the
positioning is weak, every campaign built on it is wasted effort. This gate
ensures the positioning is clear, defensible, and validated.

### The CEO Test (MUST pass)

- [ ] CEO can deliver the positioning statement in under 15 seconds without
      reading from a document.
- [ ] Positioning statement follows the framework: For [target], who [need],
      [product] is a [category] that [benefit], unlike [alternative],
      we [differentiator].
- [ ] Positioning can be understood by someone unfamiliar with the industry.
- [ ] Positioning does not use internal jargon, buzzwords, or meaningless
      superlatives ("best-in-class," "world-class," "cutting-edge").

### Differentiation Validation

- [ ] Differentiation claim is factually true and verifiable.
- [ ] Differentiation is sustainable (not easily copied within 6 months).
- [ ] Differentiation matters to the target customer (validated, not assumed).
- [ ] Differentiation is tested against top 3 competitors:
  - [ ] Competitor 1: [Name] -- Our differentiation holds because: [reason]
  - [ ] Competitor 2: [Name] -- Our differentiation holds because: [reason]
  - [ ] Competitor 3: [Name] -- Our differentiation holds because: [reason]
- [ ] "Why us" and "why not them" can be articulated without bashing
      competitors.

### Internal Alignment

- [ ] Sales team can articulate the positioning consistently.
- [ ] Customer success team understands the positioning.
- [ ] Product team confirms the positioning matches actual capabilities.
- [ ] No gap between positioning promise and product reality (trust debt = 0).

### Red Flags (any one blocks the gate)

- [ ] CONFIRM: Positioning is not aspirational beyond current capability.
- [ ] CONFIRM: Positioning is not identical to a competitor's.
- [ ] CONFIRM: Positioning was not written by committee without customer input.
- [ ] CONFIRM: Positioning has not changed 3+ times in the past year without
      a strategic rationale.

## GATE 2: Audience Gate

**Purpose:** Money spent reaching the wrong audience is money wasted. This
gate ensures every campaign targets validated ICPs with appropriate messaging.

### ICP Validation (MUST pass)

- [ ] ICP documented with specific, measurable criteria.
- [ ] ICP validated against actual best-customer data (not just interviews):
  - [ ] Highest LTV customers match ICP profile.
  - [ ] Highest NPS customers match ICP profile.
  - [ ] Fastest-activating customers match ICP profile.
- [ ] ICP includes firmographic criteria (B2B) OR behavioral criteria (B2C).
- [ ] ICP includes psychographic criteria (values, motivations, objections).
- [ ] Negative ICP defined: who explicitly is NOT our customer.

### Segment-Specific Messaging

- [ ] Each target segment has distinct messaging that addresses their
      specific pain points.
- [ ] Messaging mapped to buying stage (awareness, consideration, decision).
- [ ] Objections anticipated and addressed per segment.
- [ ] Message tested with at least 5 members of the target segment.
- [ ] Segment-specific CTAs align with segment-specific buyer journey.

### Audience Sizing

- [ ] Total addressable audience quantified per segment.
- [ ] Reachable audience quantified per channel per segment.
- [ ] Audience overlap between segments understood (avoid double-counting).
- [ ] Audience size sufficient to sustain planned spend level.

### Red Flags (any one blocks the gate)

- [ ] CONFIRM: Target audience is not "everyone."
- [ ] CONFIRM: Audience definition is not based solely on demographics.
- [ ] CONFIRM: No segment receives a message crafted for a different segment.
- [ ] CONFIRM: ICP is based on data, not assumptions or wishful thinking.

## GATE 3: Budget Gate

**Purpose:** Every marketing dollar must have a projected return. If the unit
economics do not work, the campaign should not run.

### Unit Economics Verification

- [ ] CAC calculated for each channel in the plan.
- [ ] LTV available for the target segment.
- [ ] LTV/CAC ratio > 3:1 (or explicitly approved exception with plan to
      improve).
- [ ] Payback period calculated: months to recoup CAC.
- [ ] Payback period within acceptable threshold (typically < 12 months for
      SaaS, or justified exception).
- [ ] Marginal CAC assessed (not just average): what does the NEXT customer
      cost?

### Budget Allocation Rigor

- [ ] Total budget within approved limits.
- [ ] Budget allocated by channel based on historical performance data.
- [ ] At least 10-15% of budget reserved for experimentation / new channels.
- [ ] Budget includes creative production costs (not just media spend).
- [ ] Budget includes measurement and tooling costs.
- [ ] Scenario analysis: what happens if results are 50% worse than expected?
- [ ] Kill criteria defined: at what performance level do we pause spend?

### Cash Flow Considerations

- [ ] Spend timing aligned with revenue timing (not all spend in Q1 for
      Q4 revenue without cash flow approval).
- [ ] Prepayment discounts evaluated against flexibility value.
- [ ] Cancellation/pause clauses verified for all media commitments.

### Red Flags (any one blocks the gate)

- [ ] CONFIRM: Budget is not allocated based on "what we spent last year."
- [ ] CONFIRM: No channel is funded without CAC data or a clear plan to
      measure it.
- [ ] CONFIRM: Budget does not exceed what the business can sustain if
      results are zero.
- [ ] CONFIRM: No vanity spending (sponsorships, events) without clear
      ROI framework.

## GATE 4: Channel Gate

**Purpose:** Channels must be selected for audience fit and unit economics,
not habit or trends. This gate ensures channel-market fit.

### Channel-Market Fit Validation

- [ ] For each channel in the plan, evidence that the target audience is
      active on this channel:
  - [ ] Channel 1: [Name] -- Audience evidence: [data source]
  - [ ] Channel 2: [Name] -- Audience evidence: [data source]
  - [ ] Channel 3: [Name] -- Audience evidence: [data source]
- [ ] Historical channel performance data available (or pilot plan if new).
- [ ] Competitor channel presence analyzed (are they here? If not, why?
      If yes, is there room?).

### Channel Diversity

- [ ] No single channel represents >50% of planned acquisition (or explicit
      risk acceptance with mitigation plan).
- [ ] At least one owned media channel included (email, blog, community).
- [ ] Organic and paid components in the plan.
- [ ] Platform risk assessed: what happens if [primary platform] changes
      its algorithm, pricing, or terms?

### Channel Operations

- [ ] Tracking/UTM parameters configured for every channel.
- [ ] Landing pages created and optimized for each channel (not sending all
      traffic to the homepage).
- [ ] Channel-specific creative prepared (not repurposing the same asset
      across all channels).
- [ ] Frequency caps set for paid channels (to prevent audience fatigue).
- [ ] Negative targeting configured (exclude existing customers from
      acquisition campaigns, exclude wrong segments).
- [ ] Retargeting strategy defined with appropriate exclusions and
      frequency limits.

### Red Flags (any one blocks the gate)

- [ ] CONFIRM: Channels are not selected because "everyone uses them."
- [ ] CONFIRM: Organic strategy exists (not 100% paid).
- [ ] CONFIRM: No channel is being used without measurement capability.
- [ ] CONFIRM: Channel strategy has been updated in the last 90 days
      (not running on autopilot).

## GATE 5: Creative Gate

**Purpose:** Creative is the most visible expression of the brand. Bad
creative damages brand perception. Good creative compounds over time.

### Pre-Launch Quality Checks

- [ ] Creative aligns with brand guidelines (visual identity, tone of voice).
- [ ] Headline communicates the primary value proposition (not a feature).
- [ ] CTA is clear, specific, and aligned with the funnel stage.
- [ ] Creative adapted for each channel's specifications and norms.
- [ ] Mobile-first: creative renders correctly on mobile devices.
- [ ] Accessibility: alt text for images, sufficient contrast, readable
      font sizes.
- [ ] Legal/compliance: all claims substantiated, required disclosures
      present, regulatory compliance (FTC, ASA, GDPR).

### A/B Testing Requirements

- [ ] At least 2 creative variations prepared for key campaigns.
- [ ] Test hypothesis defined (what are we testing and why).
- [ ] Success metric defined (CTR, conversion rate, engagement rate).
- [ ] Sample size sufficient for statistical significance.
- [ ] Test duration pre-determined (not peeking and stopping early).
- [ ] Learning plan: what will we do with the results regardless of outcome?

### Content Quality (for content marketing)

- [ ] Content provides genuine value (not thinly disguised sales pitch).
- [ ] Content is accurate and fact-checked.
- [ ] Content is original (not plagiarized or AI-generated without review).
- [ ] SEO: target keyword identified, on-page optimization complete.
- [ ] Content mapped to buyer journey stage.
- [ ] Distribution plan includes multiple channels (not publish and pray).
- [ ] Performance tracking configured (time on page, scroll depth,
      conversions, shares).

### Red Flags (any one blocks the gate)

- [ ] CONFIRM: No misleading claims or exaggerations.
- [ ] CONFIRM: No stock photos of diverse people that do not represent
      actual customers (performative diversity).
- [ ] CONFIRM: Creative has been reviewed by someone outside the marketing
      team for clarity.
- [ ] CONFIRM: Landing page experience matches the ad creative (no bait
      and switch).
- [ ] CONFIRM: Unsubscribe/opt-out mechanisms are prominent and functional.

## GATE 6: Measurement Gate

**Purpose:** If you cannot measure it, you cannot improve it. Every campaign
must have measurement infrastructure in place BEFORE launch.

### Instrumentation Requirements

- [ ] All tracking pixels/tags installed and verified (Google Analytics,
      Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight, etc.).
- [ ] UTM parameters standardized and applied:
  - [ ] utm_source: traffic source
  - [ ] utm_medium: channel type
  - [ ] utm_campaign: campaign name
  - [ ] utm_content: creative variant
  - [ ] utm_term: keyword (for paid search)
- [ ] Conversion tracking configured and tested.
- [ ] Event tracking configured for micro-conversions (page views, scroll,
      video plays, form starts, etc.).
- [ ] Cross-device tracking configured where possible.
- [ ] Privacy compliance: cookie consent, data processing agreements, GDPR/
      CCPA compliance for all tracking.

### Reporting Setup

- [ ] Dashboard created (or template applied) before campaign launches.
- [ ] KPIs defined for each campaign:
  - [ ] Primary KPI: [Name, target, measurement method]
  - [ ] Secondary KPIs: [Names, targets]
  - [ ] Guardrail metrics: [Names, thresholds]
- [ ] Reporting cadence defined (daily, weekly, monthly).
- [ ] Responsible person assigned for reporting.
- [ ] Anomaly detection alerts configured for key metrics.

### Attribution Configuration

- [ ] Attribution model selected and configured.
- [ ] Attribution model limitations documented.
- [ ] Multi-touch attribution data collection verified.
- [ ] Self-reported attribution question implemented (if applicable).

### Post-Campaign Analysis Plan

- [ ] Post-mortem scheduled (within 2 weeks of campaign end).
- [ ] Analysis template prepared.
- [ ] Data retention confirmed (data available for post-analysis period).
- [ ] Learning repository identified for storing campaign insights.

## GATE 7: Compliance and Brand Safety Gate

**Purpose:** Marketing must comply with all regulations and protect brand
reputation. Non-compliance risks are existential, not operational.

### Legal Compliance

- [ ] All claims can be substantiated with evidence.
- [ ] Required disclosures present (sponsored content, affiliate relationships,
      testimonial disclaimers).
- [ ] Competitor comparisons are factual and not disparaging.
- [ ] Intellectual property: all images, music, and content are properly
      licensed.
- [ ] Privacy: data collection and use complies with GDPR, CCPA, and
      applicable regulations.
- [ ] Email: CAN-SPAM compliance (physical address, unsubscribe link,
      accurate header/subject).
- [ ] Advertising: platform-specific ad policies reviewed and complied with.
- [ ] Industry-specific regulations checked (financial, health, education, etc.).

### Brand Safety

- [ ] Ad placement exclusions configured (no ads next to controversial,
      violent, or inappropriate content).
- [ ] Influencer/partner vetting completed (no association with controversial
      figures without brand risk assessment).
- [ ] Social media monitoring in place for brand mentions during campaign.
- [ ] Crisis communication protocol defined (who responds to negative feedback
      or PR issues?).
- [ ] User-generated content moderation plan in place (if UGC is part of
      campaign).

### Ethical Marketing Standards

- [ ] No dark patterns (deceptive CTA placement, misleading pricing,
      confusing opt-out).
- [ ] No manufactured urgency unless genuine (fake countdown timers, false
      scarcity).
- [ ] No exploitative emotional manipulation.
- [ ] Accessibility considered (not excluding disabled users).
- [ ] Environmental claims are genuine (no greenwashing).

## Override Protocol

If a gate cannot be passed and the Marketing Brain believes the artifact
should still ship:

1. Document which specific checkboxes cannot be passed and why.
2. Document the brand risk of shipping without passing the gate.
3. Document the revenue risk of NOT shipping (time-sensitivity assessment).
4. Escalate to CEO Brain with the above documentation.
5. CEO Brain may grant a time-limited waiver.
6. Waiver logged in Accountability Protocol with expiration date.
7. If waiver expires without gate being passed, automatic escalation.

**Critical rule:** The Compliance Gate CANNOT be waived. Legal and regulatory
compliance is non-negotiable. No time pressure, revenue opportunity, or
competitive urgency justifies a compliance violation.

## Speed Gates (for time-sensitive opportunities)

For time-sensitive marketing (trending topic, PR response, competitive
response), a streamlined review is available:

### Speed Gate Requirements (24-hour turnaround)

- [ ] Positioning alignment confirmed (30-second check against positioning doc).
- [ ] Legal/compliance review (accelerated, focus on claims and disclosures).
- [ ] Brand voice check (tone-appropriate for the context).
- [ ] Measurement: at minimum, UTM parameters applied.
- [ ] One person outside marketing reviewed the content for clarity.
- [ ] Escalation plan if the content generates negative response.

**Note:** Speed Gates are for the content/creative only. Strategy, budget,
and channel decisions cannot be fast-tracked -- those require full gates.

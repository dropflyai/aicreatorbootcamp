# Partner Marketing — Co-Marketing, Channel Marketing, and MDF Allocation

## Partner Marketing as a Growth Lever

Partner marketing amplifies your reach by leveraging other organizations' audiences,
credibility, and distribution channels. Unlike direct marketing (where you own
the audience and the message), partner marketing is a collaborative discipline
that requires alignment on goals, content, and measurement between independent
organizations with their own priorities.

The three primary partner marketing motions are: co-marketing with technology
partners, channel partner marketing with resellers and SIs, and affiliate/referral
programs. Each has distinct economics, governance, and execution models.

---

## Co-Marketing

### What Is Co-Marketing?

Co-marketing is the practice of two complementary companies jointly creating and
distributing marketing content or experiences to reach each other's audiences.
The fundamental value exchange: you get access to their audience, they get access
to yours, and the joint content is more valuable than either could create alone.

### Co-Marketing Partner Selection

**Ideal Co-Marketing Partner Characteristics:**
- Complementary (not competitive) product
- Overlapping target audience (same buyer, different need)
- Similar brand quality and market position
- Approximately equal audience size (2x maximum difference)
- Collaborative team (partner marketing person who responds to emails)
- Shared content distribution capability

### Co-Marketing Formats

| Format | Effort | Reach | Lead Sharing | Best For |
|--------|--------|-------|-------------|----------|
| Joint Webinar | Medium | 200-1,000 registrants | Split 50/50 | Mid-funnel education |
| Co-Authored Guide/Report | High | 1,000-10,000 downloads | Split by promotion | Top-funnel awareness |
| Joint Case Study | Medium | Varies | Both use independently | Bottom-funnel proof |
| Co-Branded Email | Low | Each sends to own list | Each keeps own | Cross-promotion |
| Joint Event (Virtual) | High | 500-2,000 attendees | Split by registration | Brand + demand |
| Joint Event (In-Person) | Very High | 50-500 attendees | Split or co-hosted | Relationship + brand |
| Integration Launch | Medium | Both audiences | Each keeps own | Technical audience |
| Social Media Collaboration | Low | Combined followers | No lead sharing | Brand awareness |
| Guest Blog Exchange | Low | Each site's traffic | No lead sharing | SEO + awareness |

### Co-Marketing Execution Framework

**Step 1: Partner Alignment (2 weeks)**
- Agree on target audience and campaign objective
- Define content format, timeline, and responsibilities
- Agree on lead sharing rules, data handling, and follow-up
- Assign project owners from each organization
- Sign a simple co-marketing agreement (if needed)

**Step 2: Content Creation (3-6 weeks)**
- Develop content brief jointly
- Divide creation work (each contributes expertise)
- Review cycles: each partner reviews and approves
- Design: agree on visual treatment (co-branded vs. primary brand)

**Step 3: Promotion (2-4 weeks)**
- Each partner promotes to their audience simultaneously
- Social media cross-promotion (tag each other, share posts)
- Email to relevant segments of each database
- Paid amplification (each partner funds their own or shares cost)

**Step 4: Lead Distribution (Within 48 hours of campaign)**
- Share leads per agreed rules
- Deduplicate against existing databases
- Route to appropriate SDR/nurture sequence
- Track conversion from co-marketing source

**Step 5: Measurement (30-60 days post-campaign)**
- Total registrants/downloads (combined reach)
- Leads generated per partner (new contacts)
- Pipeline influenced (within 90 days of campaign)
- Partner satisfaction (would they do it again?)

### Co-Marketing Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Registrants per Webinar | Total sign-ups | 200-500 (B2B) |
| Attendance Rate | Attendees / Registrants | 30-45% (live) |
| New Leads per Partner | Leads new to your database | 50-200 per campaign |
| Cost per Lead | Joint spend / Total leads | <$50 (shared cost) |
| Pipeline Influenced | Pipeline from co-marketing contacts | Tracked at 30/60/90 days |
| Partner Satisfaction | Would they repeat? (1-5) | >4.0 |

## Channel Partner Marketing

### What Is Channel Partner Marketing?

Channel partner marketing supports your resellers, VARs, SIs, and other indirect
sales partners with marketing resources that help them sell your product. Unlike
co-marketing (peer-to-peer), channel marketing is you enabling your partners
to market and sell on your behalf.

### Channel Marketing Programs

**Through-Partner Marketing:**
You provide tools and content; partners execute marketing to their audience.

| Program | Description | Investment |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Partner Collateral Library | Sales decks, one-pagers, data sheets partners can co-brand | $10-30K to create |
| Partner Email Templates | Pre-written campaigns partners can send to their lists | $5-10K to create |
| Social Media Kit | Ready-to-post content with partner branding options | $2-5K/quarter |
| Partner Landing Pages | Co-branded landing pages partners can use for campaigns | $5-15K to build |
| Partner Playbooks | Step-by-step GTM guides for partners | $10-20K to create |
| Demo Scripts | Guided demo paths partners can deliver | $5-10K to create |

**To-Partner Marketing:**
Marketing directed at partners to recruit, engage, and enable them.

| Program | Description | Investment |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Partner Recruitment Campaigns | Marketing to attract new partners | $5-20K/quarter |
| Partner Newsletter | Monthly update on product, content, and programs | $1-3K/month |
| Partner Summit/SKO | Annual partner event (virtual or in-person) | $20-100K |
| Partner Certification Marketing | Promote certification program to partners | $5-10K |
| Partner Awards Program | Recognize top partners publicly | $5-15K |

**With-Partner Marketing:**
Joint marketing activities executed together.

| Program | Description | Investment |
|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Joint Events | Co-hosted seminars, roundtables, dinners | $5-25K per event |
| Joint Content | Co-authored content for partner's audience | $3-10K per piece |
| Joint Advertising | Co-branded ads targeting partner's market | $5-20K per campaign |
| Reference Program | Joint customer success stories | $3-8K per case study |

## Affiliate Marketing in B2B

### B2B Affiliate Models

| Model | Structure | Best For |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Referral Fee | Flat fee per qualified referral or closed deal | Advisors, consultants, influencers |
| Revenue Share | Percentage of first-year ACV (10-20%) | Technology partners, agencies |
| Recurring Commission | Percentage of ongoing revenue (5-15%) | Strategic referral partners |
| Tiered Program | Higher commission rates at higher volume | Scaling affiliate programs |

### Affiliate Program Design

**Program Elements:**
1. Commission structure (per referral, per close, recurring)
2. Attribution window (30, 60, or 90 days from referral to close)
3. Tracking mechanism (unique links, referral codes, CRM tracking)
4. Payment terms (monthly, quarterly, threshold-based)
5. Partner portal (dashboard, collateral, link generation)
6. Minimum requirements (quality threshold, no spam)

### Affiliate Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Referral Volume | Total referrals per month | Growing |
| Referral Quality | SQL Rate from referrals | >30% (higher than cold) |
| Referral Win Rate | Won / Total referrals | >25% (higher than average) |
| Revenue per Affiliate | Annual revenue from each affiliate | Growing |
| Affiliate Activation Rate | Active affiliates / Total affiliates | >25% |
| Cost per Acquisition (Affiliate) | Commission paid / Customers acquired | Lower than direct CAC |
| Payback on Commission | Time to recoup commission | <12 months |

## Market Development Funds (MDF)

### What Are MDFs?

Market Development Funds are marketing dollars you allocate to partners to execute
co-marketing activities. MDF is a financial incentive for partners to invest their
time and effort in marketing your product to their audience.

### MDF Allocation Framework

**Allocation Criteria:**

| Factor | Weight | Assessment |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Partner Revenue (Last 12 Months) | 30% | Higher revenue = more MDF |
| Growth Trajectory | 20% | Growing partners get preferential allocation |
| Marketing Plan Quality | 20% | Partners with detailed plans get more |
| Co-Investment | 15% | Partners who invest their own $ get matched |
| Strategic Importance | 15% | Key logos, new markets, reference value |

**MDF Tier Structure:**

| Partner Tier | Annual MDF | Matching Requirement | Approval |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|----------|
| Platinum | $25-50K | 50/50 co-investment | Pre-approved for standard activities |
| Gold | $10-25K | 50/50 co-investment | Requires plan approval |
| Silver | $5-10K | 75/25 (you fund 75%) | Requires plan and result commitment |
| Registered | $0-5K | 100% funded by you | Only for specific programs |

### MDF-Eligible Activities

| Activity | Typical MDF Allocation | Expected Output |
|----------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Joint Webinar | $2-5K | 100-300 registrants |
| Local Event/Dinner | $5-15K | 20-50 qualified attendees |
| Digital Ad Campaign | $3-10K | 500-2,000 targeted impressions |
| Content Creation | $3-8K | Guide, report, or case study |
| Trade Show Booth | $10-25K | 50-200 badge scans |
| Email Campaign | $1-3K | 500-5,000 targeted sends |
| Social Campaign | $2-5K | 10,000-50,000 impressions |

### MDF Governance

**Pre-Approval Requirements:**
1. Partner submits MDF request with activity plan
2. Plan includes: activity type, target audience, expected outcomes, timeline
3. Partner commits to lead sharing and result reporting
4. MDF request reviewed and approved within 5 business days

**Post-Activity Requirements:**
1. Partner submits proof of execution (screenshots, attendee lists, metrics)
2. Lead data shared within 48 hours of activity
3. ROI report submitted within 30 days
4. Unused MDF returned or reallocated

**MDF Clawback Conditions:**
- Activity not executed within agreed timeline
- Lead sharing requirements not met
- Proof of execution not provided
- Funds used for non-approved activities

### MDF Measurement

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| MDF Utilization Rate | MDF spent / MDF allocated | >80% |
| MDF ROI (Pipeline) | Pipeline from MDF activities / MDF spent | 10-25x |
| MDF ROI (Revenue) | Revenue from MDF activities / MDF spent | 3-10x |
| Cost per Lead (MDF) | MDF spent / Leads generated | <$100 |
| Partner MDF Satisfaction | Survey score (1-5) | >3.5 |

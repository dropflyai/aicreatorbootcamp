# Marketing Brain -- Glossary

## Canonical Terminology

This glossary defines the precise meaning of marketing terms as used by the
Marketing Brain. All brain outputs must use these definitions consistently.
When terms conflict with colloquial usage, this glossary takes precedence.

---

## A

**ABM (Account-Based Marketing)**
A B2B strategy that treats individual accounts as markets of one. Resources are
concentrated on a defined set of target accounts using personalized campaigns
designed to resonate with each account. Coined by ITSMA, formalized by Engagio
(now Demandbase). Three tiers: Strategic (1:1), Lite (1:few), Programmatic (1:many).

**Acquisition**
The process of attracting and converting new customers. Measured by CAC (Customer
Acquisition Cost). Distinct from activation (first value moment) and retention
(ongoing engagement). Part of the AARRR framework (McClure, 500 Startups).

**Attribution**
The process of assigning credit for conversions to marketing touchpoints. Models
include: first-touch, last-touch, linear, time-decay, position-based (U-shaped),
data-driven (algorithmic). No model is perfectly accurate; all are useful mental
models. See also: incrementality testing.

**ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)**
Total revenue divided by total users in a given period. Critical input for LTV
calculations. Distinguish from ARPPU (Average Revenue Per Paying User) in
freemium models.

---

## B

**Brand Equity**
The commercial value derived from consumer perception of a brand name. Aaker's
model identifies five components: brand awareness, perceived quality, brand
associations, brand loyalty, and proprietary assets. Keller's model (CBBE)
structures equity as a pyramid: salience, performance/imagery, judgments/feelings,
resonance.

**Brand Salience**
The degree to which a brand is thought of or noticed when a customer is in a
buying situation (Byron Sharp, How Brands Grow). Distinct from brand preference.
Salience is built through distinctive brand assets and broad reach.

**Bounce Rate**
Percentage of visitors who leave after viewing only one page. In Google Analytics
4, replaced by "engagement rate" (inverse metric). A high bounce rate may indicate
poor content relevance, slow load times, or UX issues.

**Buyer Persona**
A semi-fictional representation of an ideal customer based on market research and
real data. Includes demographics, behavior patterns, motivations, and goals.
Originated by Alan Cooper (The Inmates Are Running the Asylum). In B2B, distinct
from ICP (Ideal Customer Profile), which describes the account, not the individual.

---

## C

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**
Total sales and marketing spend divided by number of new customers acquired in a
period. Blended CAC includes all channels; channel-specific CAC isolates one
channel. Fully loaded CAC includes team salaries and overhead.
Formula: CAC = (Sales + Marketing Spend) / New Customers

**Category Design**
The discipline of creating and developing a new market category. Described in
Play Bigger (Ramadan et al., 2016). The "category king" captures 76% of the
economics in a category. Requires simultaneous development of product, company,
and category.

**Churn Rate**
The percentage of customers who stop using a product in a given period.
Monthly churn: customers lost / customers at start of month.
Annual churn: 1 - (1 - monthly churn)^12.
Inverse of retention rate.

**Content Marketing**
A strategic approach focused on creating and distributing valuable, relevant
content to attract and retain a defined audience. Distinct from advertising in
that content provides value independent of the product being sold. Joe Pulizzi
(Content Marketing Institute) formalized the discipline.

**Conversion Rate**
The percentage of users who complete a desired action. Calculated as:
(conversions / total visitors or users) * 100. Must always specify the
denominator and the action being measured.

**CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)**
The cost to acquire one converting user through a specific channel or campaign.
Distinct from CAC in that CPA typically refers to a single campaign or channel,
while CAC is an aggregate business metric.

**CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)**
The systematic process of increasing the percentage of visitors who take a
desired action. Uses A/B testing, multivariate testing, and UX optimization.
Grounded in behavioral psychology (Cialdini, Kahneman, Fogg).

**CTA (Call to Action)**
A prompt that tells the user what action to take next. Must be specific, benefit-
oriented, and visually prominent. Every marketing asset must have a clear CTA.

---

## D

**Demand Capture**
Marketing activities that target buyers who already have intent to purchase.
Examples: search ads on branded/competitor terms, comparison content, bottom-of-
funnel offers. High conversion rate, limited scale. Contrast with demand creation.

**Demand Creation**
Marketing activities that generate awareness and interest among buyers who do not
yet recognize a need or know your category exists. Examples: thought leadership,
category education, brand advertising. Lower conversion rate, higher scale
potential. Contrast with demand capture.

**Distinctive Brand Assets**
Non-brand-name elements that trigger the brand in memory: logos, colors, taglines,
characters, sounds, shapes. Identified by Romaniuk and Sharp (How Brands Grow Part
2). Must be unique (not shared with competitors) and famous (recognized by buyers).

---

## E

**E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)**
Google's quality rater guidelines framework for evaluating content quality.
Experience was added in 2022. Critical for YMYL (Your Money Your Life) content.
Not a direct ranking factor but reflects what Google's algorithms aim to reward.

---

## F

**Funnel**
A model representing the customer journey from awareness to purchase. Traditional:
AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action). Modern variations include AARRR
(Acquisition, Activation, Revenue, Retention, Referral) and the bow-tie funnel
(pre-sale + post-sale). Increasingly replaced by "flywheel" models in PLG contexts.

---

## G

**GTM (Go-to-Market)**
The plan for how a company will reach target customers and achieve competitive
advantage. Includes target audience, value proposition, channel strategy, pricing,
and launch plan. Three primary GTM motions: sales-led, marketing-led, product-led.

**Growth Loop**
A self-reinforcing cycle where the output of one step feeds the input of the
next. Replaces the linear funnel model. Examples: user-generated content loop,
paid acquisition loop, viral invitation loop. Formalized by Reforge.

---

## I

**ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**
In B2B, the description of the perfect-fit company (not individual) for your
product. Includes firmographics (industry, size, revenue), technographics,
and behavioral indicators. Distinct from buyer persona (individual-level).

**Incrementality**
The true causal impact of a marketing activity, measured as the difference
between outcomes with and without the activity. Gold standard: randomized
controlled experiments (holdout tests). Superior to attribution for measuring
true marketing value.

**Intent Signal**
Behavioral indicators that suggest a prospect is actively researching or
considering a purchase. First-party: website visits, content downloads. Third-
party: review site visits, competitor research (via intent data providers like
Bombora, G2). Critical input for lead scoring and ABM.

---

## K

**K-Factor (Viral Coefficient)**
The number of new users each existing user generates. K = invitations sent per
user * conversion rate of invitations. K > 1 indicates viral growth (each user
brings more than one new user). Few products sustain K > 1 indefinitely.

---

## L

**Lead Scoring**
A methodology for ranking prospects against a scale representing perceived value.
Combines demographic fit (firmographic, title) with behavioral engagement (content
downloads, website visits, email opens). Defines thresholds for MQL and SQL.

**LTV (Lifetime Value)**
The predicted net profit from the entire future relationship with a customer.
Simple formula: LTV = ARPU * Gross Margin * (1 / Churn Rate).
More accurate: DCF-based LTV with discount rate.
The LTV:CAC ratio is the primary unit economics health metric. Target: 3:1+.

---

## M

**Marketing Mix (4Ps / 7Ps)**
McCarthy's 4Ps: Product, Price, Place, Promotion. Extended to 7Ps by Booms and
Bitner for services: + People, Process, Physical Evidence. The foundational
framework for marketing decision-making. All marketing activities map to one
or more Ps.

**Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL)**
A lead that has been deemed more likely to become a customer based on lead scoring
criteria. Has met engagement thresholds but has not yet been validated by sales.
Passes to sales as SQL after further qualification.

**Mental Availability**
The probability that a brand will be thought of in a buying situation. A Byron
Sharp concept from How Brands Grow. Built through consistent advertising, broad
reach, and distinctive brand assets. Complemented by physical availability.

**MMM (Marketing Mix Modeling)**
A statistical technique using regression analysis to estimate the impact of
marketing activities on sales. Uses aggregate-level data (not user-level).
Complements attribution (which uses user-level data). Increasingly adopted as
privacy regulations limit tracking.

---

## N

**NPS (Net Promoter Score)**
A customer loyalty metric based on the question: "How likely are you to recommend
[product] to a friend?" (0-10 scale). Score = % Promoters (9-10) - % Detractors
(0-6). Created by Fred Reichheld (Bain). Ranges from -100 to +100. Criticized
for oversimplification but widely adopted as a directional metric.

---

## P

**Payback Period**
The time required for a customer's cumulative gross margin to equal the CAC.
Formula: Payback = CAC / (ARPU * Gross Margin per month).
Target: < 12 months for venture-backed; < 18 months for bootstrapped.
Shorter payback periods enable faster reinvestment in growth.

**Physical Availability**
The ease with which a brand can be found and bought. A Byron Sharp concept.
In digital: distribution channels, app store presence, search visibility.
In physical: shelf space, retail locations, distribution breadth.

**PLG (Product-Led Growth)**
A GTM strategy where the product itself is the primary driver of acquisition,
activation, and retention. Users experience value before paying. Examples:
Slack, Dropbox, Figma. Formalized by OpenView Partners and Wes Bush.

**Positioning**
The act of designing a company's offering and image to occupy a distinct place
in the mind of the target market. Ries and Trout (Positioning, 1981): "Positioning
is not what you do to a product. It is what you do to the mind of the prospect."

---

## R

**ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)**
Revenue generated per dollar of advertising spend. Formula: ROAS = Revenue /
Ad Spend. A ROAS of 4:1 means $4 revenue for every $1 spent. Distinct from ROI,
which accounts for all costs (not just ad spend).

**Retention Rate**
The percentage of customers who remain active over a given period. Monthly
retention = 1 - monthly churn. Cohort-based retention analysis tracks specific
groups over time and is more informative than aggregate retention.

---

## S

**Sales Qualified Lead (SQL)**
A lead that has been validated by sales as meeting qualification criteria (budget,
authority, need, timeline -- BANT). Accepted from marketing as an MQL and
confirmed as a genuine sales opportunity.

**SEM (Search Engine Marketing)**
Paid search advertising. Primarily Google Ads and Microsoft Advertising. Includes
search ads, shopping ads, and display network. Distinct from SEO (organic search).

**SEO (Search Engine Optimization)**
The practice of increasing organic search visibility. Three pillars: technical
(crawlability, site speed, structure), on-page (content, keywords, meta data),
off-page (backlinks, authority, mentions). A long-term, compounding channel.

**STP (Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning)**
Kotler's foundational marketing framework. Segment the market, select target
segments, position the offering for those segments. Must be completed before
any tactical marketing execution.

---

## T

**TAM / SAM / SOM**
- TAM (Total Addressable Market): Total market demand for a product/service
- SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): TAM segment you can reach with your
  business model
- SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): SAM share you can realistically capture
Top-down (industry reports) and bottom-up (unit economics) approaches both valid.

**Topical Authority**
A SEO concept where a website demonstrates comprehensive expertise on a topic
through depth and breadth of content. Search engines reward topical authority
with higher rankings across related queries.

---

## U

**Unit Economics**
The direct revenues and costs associated with a single unit of a business model
(typically one customer). Core metrics: CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, payback period,
gross margin. Must be positive and sustainable for growth to be viable.

---

## V

**Value Proposition**
A clear statement of the tangible results a customer gets from using your product.
Format (Strategyzer): "We help [customer segment] who want to [job to be done]
by [reducing pains] and [creating gains] unlike [alternatives]."

**Viral Loop**
A mechanism where users naturally invite or expose others to the product through
normal usage. Inherent virality (product requires others, e.g., Zoom) is stronger
than manufactured virality (incentivized referrals).

---

## Key Formulas Reference

| Metric | Formula |
|--------|---------|
| CAC | (Sales + Marketing Spend) / New Customers |
| LTV | ARPU * Gross Margin * (1 / Churn Rate) |
| LTV:CAC | LTV / CAC (target: 3:1+) |
| Payback | CAC / (ARPU * Gross Margin / month) |
| ROAS | Revenue / Ad Spend |
| K-Factor | Invitations * Conversion Rate |
| Churn | Customers Lost / Starting Customers |
| Retention | 1 - Churn Rate |
| CPA | Channel Spend / Channel Conversions |
| NPS | % Promoters - % Detractors |

# Acquisition Channels -- Finding Channel-Market Fit

## Purpose

Channel strategy is the discipline of identifying, testing, and scaling the
marketing channels that most efficiently acquire target customers. The wrong
channel wastes budget regardless of creative quality. The right channel can
make even mediocre creative work. This module provides frameworks for channel
evaluation, testing, and optimization.

---

## The 19 Channels Framework (Weinberg & Mares, Traction)

### Channel Taxonomy

Every customer acquisition channel falls into one of 19 categories:

**Owned Channels (You Control)**
1. SEO / Search Engine Marketing
2. Content Marketing / Blogs
3. Email Marketing
4. Social Media Marketing
5. Engineering as Marketing (free tools, calculators)

**Earned Channels (Others Amplify)**
6. Viral Marketing / Word of Mouth
7. Public Relations / Press
8. Community Building
9. Unconventional PR / Stunts
10. Business Development / Partnerships

**Paid Channels (You Pay for Reach)**
11. Search Ads (Google, Bing)
12. Social Ads (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok)
13. Display / Programmatic Ads
14. Offline Ads (TV, radio, print, OOH)
15. Affiliate Programs

**Sales Channels (Human-Driven)**
16. Sales / Outbound
17. Trade Shows / Events
18. Speaking Engagements

**Product Channels (Product Drives)**
19. Existing Platforms (app stores, marketplaces, integrations)

## The Bullseye Framework (Weinberg & Mares)

### Three-Ring Prioritization

```
        ┌───────────────────────────────┐
        │      OUTER RING               │
        │   All possible channels       │
        │  (brainstorm - list all 19)   │
        │                               │
        │    ┌───────────────────┐      │
        │    │   MIDDLE RING     │      │
        │    │  Promising (6)    │      │
        │    │  (worth testing)  │      │
        │    │                   │      │
        │    │  ┌─────────────┐  │      │
        │    │  │ INNER RING  │  │      │
        │    │  │  Core (1-3) │  │      │
        │    │  │  (scale)    │  │      │
        │    │  └─────────────┘  │      │
        │    └───────────────────┘      │
        └───────────────────────────────┘
```

### Process
1. **Outer ring:** Brainstorm how each of the 19 channels could work for you
2. **Middle ring:** Select 6 most promising for cheap testing
3. **Inner ring:** Focus on 1-3 that show traction after testing
4. **Scale:** Double down on what works. Revisit middle ring quarterly.

## Channel-Market Fit

### The Concept
Just as product-market fit means the product serves the market's needs,
channel-market fit means the channel reaches the market efficiently.

### Channel Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Question | Weight |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Audience presence | Is our target audience on this channel? | Critical |
| Cost efficiency | What is the CAC through this channel? | High |
| Scalability | Can we 10x spend and maintain efficiency? | High |
| Time to result | How quickly can we see results? | Medium |
| Measurability | Can we accurately measure ROI? | Medium |
| Competitiveness | How crowded is this channel for our category? | Medium |
| Defensibility | Can we build a moat in this channel? | Low-Medium |

### Channel-Stage Fit Matrix

Different channels work at different company stages:

| Stage | Best Channels | Why |
|-------|---------------|-----|
| Pre-PMF | Content, community, manual outreach | Learn while acquiring |
| Early (0-100 customers) | Outbound sales, partnerships, niche communities | High-touch, targeted |
| Growth (100-1000) | SEO, paid search, content marketing, events | Scalable, measurable |
| Scale (1000+) | Paid social, display, brand, TV, affiliates | Broad reach needed |

### Channel CAC Benchmarks (B2B SaaS, 2024)

These are directional benchmarks. Actual CAC varies dramatically by ACV,
market, and competition.

| Channel | Typical CAC Range | Best For |
|---------|-------------------|----------|
| Organic search (SEO) | $100-$500 | Long-term compounding growth |
| Content marketing | $150-$600 | Thought leadership, education |
| Google Ads (search) | $200-$1,000 | High-intent capture |
| LinkedIn Ads | $300-$1,500 | B2B targeting precision |
| Meta Ads | $100-$500 | SMB, self-serve products |
| Outbound SDR | $500-$2,000 | Enterprise, high ACV |
| Events / conferences | $500-$3,000 | Relationship building |
| Affiliate / partner | $200-$800 | Performance-based |
| Product-led (PLG) | $50-$300 | Self-serve, viral products |

**Rule of thumb:** Target CAC < 1/3 of LTV. If CAC > 1/3 LTV, the channel
is not sustainable without improving activation, retention, or monetization.

## Channel Testing Methodology

### The Minimum Viable Test

For each channel in the middle ring of the Bullseye:

**Step 1: Define the hypothesis**
"Channel X can acquire [target persona] at a CAC of < $[threshold] within
[timeframe]."

**Step 2: Set the budget**
Minimum: Enough to get statistically meaningful data.
- Paid channels: $1,000-$5,000 for initial test
- Content/SEO: 3-month test period (10-20 pieces)
- Outbound: 500-1,000 targeted outreach attempts
- Events: 1-2 events with clear tracking

**Step 3: Define success criteria**
- Primary: CAC below threshold
- Secondary: Lead quality (conversion to SQL/customer)
- Tertiary: Time to conversion, scalability signals

**Step 4: Run and measure**
- Track full funnel from first touch to revenue
- Use UTM parameters, dedicated landing pages, unique promo codes
- Run for minimum viable duration (2-4 weeks for paid, 3+ months for organic)

**Step 5: Decide**
- Below CAC threshold + quality signals --> Move to inner ring, scale
- Near threshold + quality signals --> Optimize and retest
- Above threshold + poor quality --> Kill and move to next channel

## Channel Scaling Framework

### When to Scale a Channel

```
Is CAC consistently below threshold? (at least 4 weeks of data)
  |
  +-- No --> Continue optimizing or kill
  |
  +-- Yes --> Is lead/customer quality acceptable?
                |
                +-- No --> Refine targeting, test new audiences
                |
                +-- Yes --> Can you increase spend 50% without CAC rising 20%+?
                              |
                              +-- No --> Channel is near saturation. Optimize.
                              |
                              +-- Yes --> SCALE. Increase budget systematically.
```

### The Diminishing Returns Curve

Every channel has diminishing returns as spend increases:

```
Conversions
  |
  |          ___________  <-- Saturation
  |         /
  |        /
  |       /    <-- Efficient zone (sweet spot)
  |      /
  |     /
  |    /   <-- Early testing
  |   /
  |  /
  | /
  |/__________________ Spend
```

**Strategy:** Find the "efficient zone" for each channel. Avoid spending
past the inflection point where marginal CAC rises sharply.

### Multi-Channel Portfolio

As you scale, diversify across channels to reduce risk:

**Stage 1:** 1 core channel (80% of budget)
**Stage 2:** 1 core + 2 supporting (60/20/20)
**Stage 3:** 2-3 core + 2-3 supporting (40/30/15/10/5)
**Stage 4:** Diversified portfolio with no channel > 40% of total

**Rule:** Never let a single channel represent more than 50% of acquisition.
Platform risk (algorithm changes, policy changes, cost increases) can
devastate growth overnight.

## Channel-Specific Playbooks (Summary)

### Paid Search (Google Ads)
- Best for: Demand capture (people searching for solutions)
- Start with: Branded + competitor terms + high-intent category terms
- Key metric: Cost per qualified lead, not just CPC
- Scaling lever: Expand keyword universe, add Display/YouTube
- Trap: Bidding on broad match without negative keywords

### Paid Social (Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok)
- Best for: Demand creation (reaching people not yet searching)
- Start with: Lookalike audiences from best customers
- Key metric: Cost per acquisition (not impressions or clicks)
- Scaling lever: New audiences, new creative (creative is the targeting)
- Trap: Audience fatigue and creative burnout

### SEO / Organic Search
- Best for: Compounding, long-term acquisition
- Start with: Bottom-of-funnel content targeting high-intent queries
- Key metric: Organic traffic * conversion rate = organic customers
- Scaling lever: Topical authority, link building, content velocity
- Trap: Expecting results in < 6 months

### Content Marketing
- Best for: Building trust and authority, nurturing consideration
- Start with: Content that addresses top pain points of target persona
- Key metric: Content-assisted conversions (attribution)
- Scaling lever: Content repurposing, distribution, SEO integration
- Trap: Creating content for yourself instead of your audience

### Outbound Sales (SDR/BDR)
- Best for: Enterprise, high-ACV products
- Start with: Highly targeted list, personalized outreach
- Key metric: Meetings booked per SDR per month (target: 15-20)
- Scaling lever: Add SDRs, improve sequences, use intent data
- Trap: Volume over quality (spray and pray)

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)
- Best for: Products with clear self-serve value
- Start with: Optimize free trial/freemium activation
- Key metric: Free-to-paid conversion rate, time-to-value
- Scaling lever: In-product viral loops, integration marketplace
- Trap: Ignoring enterprise buyers who want sales engagement

## Channel Attribution

### Multi-Touch Attribution
Most customers touch 6-8 marketing touchpoints before converting. Single-touch
attribution (first or last) dramatically misrepresents channel contribution.

### Recommended Approach
1. **First-touch attribution:** Shows which channels drive awareness
2. **Last-touch attribution:** Shows which channels drive conversion
3. **Linear/position-based:** Distributes credit across touchpoints
4. **Incrementality testing:** The gold standard (holdout experiments)
5. **Marketing Mix Modeling:** Aggregate-level statistical analysis

See `07_analytics/marketing_analytics.md` for detailed attribution guidance.

# Copywriting — Persuasion Science for Marketing Communications

## Copywriting as Applied Psychology

Copywriting is not writing. It is applied persuasion psychology in written form.
The difference between a mediocre marketer and a great one often comes down to
the ability to translate value propositions into language that creates action.
Every headline, email subject line, landing page, and ad is an experiment in
human decision-making.

This module covers the psychological principles, structural frameworks, and
practical techniques that produce high-converting copy across all marketing channels.

## Cialdini's Six Principles of Influence

Robert Cialdini's "Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion" (1984) identified six
universal principles that drive human compliance. These principles are the
foundation of persuasive copywriting.

### 1. Reciprocity

People feel obligated to return favors. When you give something valuable first,
the recipient feels psychologically compelled to reciprocate.

**Application in Copy:**
- Lead with free value before asking for anything (ungated content, free tools)
- "We put together this [resource] for teams like yours — no strings attached"
- Free trials, free assessments, free consultations
- Thank-you pages that add additional value (bonus resources)

### 2. Commitment and Consistency

Once people commit to something (even small), they behave consistently with that
commitment. Small yeses lead to larger yeses.

**Application in Copy:**
- Micro-commitments before major asks: "Would you like to see how?" before "Schedule a demo"
- Progressive disclosure: quiz → result → recommendation → demo
- "You mentioned [problem] is a priority. Here is how we address it..."
- Interactive content (assessments, calculators) that build investment

### 3. Social Proof

People look to others' behavior to determine their own, especially under uncertainty.

**Application in Copy:**
- "10,000+ companies trust [product]" (specific number, not "many")
- Customer logos prominently displayed (especially recognizable brands)
- Testimonials with specific results: "[Customer] increased win rate by 35%"
- Review site ratings: "Rated 4.8/5 on G2 by 500+ reviewers"
- Case study quotes from named individuals with titles
- "Used by teams at [Company A], [Company B], and [Company C]"

### 4. Authority

People defer to credible experts. Authority signals — titles, credentials, media
mentions, data — increase persuasive power.

**Application in Copy:**
- Cite research and data ("Gartner reports that...")
- Feature author credentials ("Written by [Name], former VP Sales at [Company]")
- Media mentions: "As featured in Forbes, TechCrunch, and Harvard Business Review"
- Industry analyst endorsements or recognition
- Patent or certification mentions (SOC 2, ISO 27001)

### 5. Liking

People are more easily persuaded by people (and brands) they like. Liking is driven
by similarity, familiarity, and positive association.

**Application in Copy:**
- Write conversationally, as one professional to another
- Reference shared challenges: "If you have ever struggled with..."
- Use the buyer's language (not your internal jargon)
- Show the humans behind the brand (founder stories, team photos)
- Humor (when appropriate and authentic)

### 6. Scarcity

People value what is rare or diminishing. Perceived scarcity increases urgency.

**Application in Copy (Ethical Use Only):**
- Limited-time offers: "This pricing is available until [date]"
- Capacity constraints: "We onboard 10 new customers per month"
- Exclusive access: "Early access for the first 100 sign-ups"
- Feature gating: "Enterprise features available in Pro plan only"

**Ethical Boundary:** Scarcity must be real. Artificial urgency ("This deal expires
in 24 hours" when it does not) destroys trust permanently.

## Headline Formulas

Headlines are the most important element of any marketing copy. Research from
Copyblogger indicates that 80% of people read the headline, but only 20% read
the body. The headline determines whether the rest of the copy gets read.

### Formula Categories

**Benefit Headlines:**
- "How [Company] Reduced Ramp Time by 50% in 90 Days"
- "The Fastest Way to [Desired Outcome]"
- "Get [Specific Result] Without [Common Pain]"

**Curiosity Headlines:**
- "The $2.4M Mistake Most Sales Teams Make Every Quarter"
- "Why Your Pipeline Coverage Ratio Is Lying to You"
- "What 500 Closed-Won Deals Taught Us About Discovery"

**Number Headlines:**
- "7 Pipeline Metrics That Predict Revenue (And 3 That Don't)"
- "The 30-60-90 Day Plan That Cuts Ramp Time in Half"
- "5 Objection Responses That Actually Work"

**Question Headlines:**
- "Is Your Sales Team Leaving 40% of Revenue on the Table?"
- "What If You Could Predict Which Deals Will Close?"
- "Are You Building Pipeline or Just Stuffing It?"

**How-To Headlines:**
- "How to Build a Sales Forecast That Is Actually Accurate"
- "How to Identify Your Champion (And Why It Matters)"
- "How to Run a Discovery Call That Wins Enterprise Deals"

**Comparison Headlines:**
- "[Product A] vs. [Product B]: The Complete Comparison for [Persona]"
- "Challenger Sale vs. SPIN Selling: Which Methodology Wins?"
- "Gated vs. Ungated Content: The Data-Driven Answer"

### Headline Testing Protocol

Never rely on a single headline. For important pages:
1. Write 10-15 headline variations
2. Categorize by formula type
3. Select top 3-5 for testing
4. A/B test with minimum 1,000 impressions per variant
5. Measure click-through rate AND downstream conversion (headline that gets clicks
   but not conversions is clickbait)

## Copywriting Frameworks

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

The oldest and most versatile framework:
- **Attention:** Bold claim, surprising statistic, provocative question
- **Interest:** Relevant detail that builds on the attention-grabber
- **Desire:** Benefits, social proof, and transformation vision
- **Action:** Clear, specific call to action

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

Most effective when the audience has a known pain:
- **Problem:** Name the problem explicitly
- **Agitate:** Explore the consequences and frustrations
- **Solve:** Present your solution as the relief

### FAB (Features-Advantages-Benefits)

Effective for product-specific copy:
- **Feature:** What the product does (technical)
- **Advantage:** Why that matters (functional)
- **Benefit:** How that improves the buyer's life (emotional)

**Example:**
- Feature: "Real-time pipeline analytics dashboard"
- Advantage: "See exactly where every deal stands without manual CRM reports"
- Benefit: "Spend your Monday mornings coaching reps, not building spreadsheets"

### Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

Paints a transformation picture:
- **Before:** Current painful reality
- **After:** Desired future state
- **Bridge:** Your product/service as the path

### 4 Ps (Promise-Picture-Proof-Push)

For landing pages and long-form sales copy:
- **Promise:** Big, specific, believable promise
- **Picture:** Paint a vivid picture of life with the promise fulfilled
- **Proof:** Evidence that the promise is real (data, testimonials, case studies)
- **Push:** Clear call to action with urgency

## Voice of Customer (VoC) Mining

### Why VoC Matters for Copy

The most persuasive copy does not come from the copywriter's imagination. It comes
from the buyer's own words. When you use the exact language buyers use to describe
their problems, your copy resonates at a level that feels like mind-reading.

### VoC Mining Sources

| Source | What It Reveals | Mining Technique |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Sales Call Recordings (Gong) | Buyer's exact words for pain, objections, priorities | Listen to 20+ calls, transcript analysis |
| Customer Interviews | Deep insight into decision process, language, motivation | Structured interviews, verbatim capture |
| Support Tickets | Frustrations, feature requests, use case language | Categorize and quote |
| G2/TrustRadius Reviews | What buyers praise and criticize (your product + competitors) | Extract quotes, identify patterns |
| Reddit/Community Forums | Unfiltered peer discussions about your category | Search and monitor relevant threads |
| Win/Loss Interviews | Why they chose you (or why they chose someone else) | Structured post-decision interviews |
| Sales Team Interviews | Most common objections, questions, praise | Regular debriefs with AEs |

### VoC to Copy Translation

**VoC Finding:** "I spend 3 hours every Monday morning building a pipeline report
in spreadsheets because our CRM is useless for forecasting."

**Copy Translation:**
- Headline: "Stop Spending Monday Mornings in Spreadsheets"
- Subheadline: "Automated pipeline reporting that is actually accurate"
- Body: "Your CRM should work for you, not the other way around. Our customers
  spend 5 minutes on Monday morning reviewing a pipeline report that used to take
  3 hours to build manually."

### The VoC Swipe File

Maintain a living document of buyer quotes organized by theme:
- Pain statements (verbatim)
- Desired outcomes (in their words)
- Objection language (how they phrase concerns)
- Decision criteria (what matters to them)
- Competitive comparisons (how they describe alternatives)
- Value articulations (how they describe the benefit after purchasing)

## Channel-Specific Copywriting

### Email Subject Lines

| Technique | Example | Why It Works |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Personalization | "[Name], your pipeline coverage looks thin" | Relevance |
| Curiosity Gap | "The pipeline metric nobody tracks (but should)" | Information gap |
| Specific Number | "3 discovery questions that doubled our win rate" | Concrete, scannable |
| Question | "Is your team ready for next quarter?" | Engages analytical thinking |
| Peer Reference | "How [similar company] cut their sales cycle by 40%" | Social proof |
| Negative Frame | "The forecasting mistake that cost us $2M" | Loss aversion |

### LinkedIn Ad Copy

- 150 characters maximum for introductory text (above "see more")
- Lead with specific metric or contrarian insight
- Use first person or direct address
- End with clear CTA that matches the landing page
- Test image-based vs. video vs. carousel formats

### Landing Page CTA Buttons

| Weak CTA | Strong CTA | Why It Is Stronger |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Submit | Get the Guide | Benefit-oriented |
| Learn More | See How It Works | Specific outcome |
| Contact Us | Talk to an Expert | Value proposition |
| Sign Up | Start Free Trial | Low commitment |
| Download | Get Your Copy | Ownership language |

## Copywriting Quality Checklist

Before publishing any copy, verify:

- [ ] Is the headline specific and benefit-driven?
- [ ] Does the opening line hook the reader in 3 seconds?
- [ ] Is the copy about the BUYER (not about us)?
- [ ] Are claims supported by evidence (data, quotes, case studies)?
- [ ] Is there one clear CTA (not three competing ones)?
- [ ] Is the language at the audience's reading level?
- [ ] Does the copy flow logically from problem to solution to action?
- [ ] Have I eliminated jargon, buzzwords, and filler?
- [ ] Does the copy pass the "so what?" test at every paragraph?
- [ ] Would I keep reading this if it were about someone else's product?

**Great copy does not sound like marketing. It sounds like a smart friend explaining
something important. The reader should feel informed, not sold to.**

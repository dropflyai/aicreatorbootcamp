# Outbound Marketing — Cold Email, Signal-Based Outreach, and Compliance

## The Modern Outbound Landscape

Outbound marketing — the practice of proactively reaching buyers who have not
initiated contact — has undergone a fundamental transformation. The spray-and-pray
era of mass cold email is over, killed by spam filters, buyer fatigue, and
regulatory enforcement (CAN-SPAM, GDPR, CASL). What remains is a precision
discipline: signal-based, hyper-personalized, multi-channel outreach that reaches
the right person at the right time with the right message.

The companies winning at outbound in 2025+ share three characteristics:
1. They use intent data and trigger events to time outreach
2. They personalize at the individual level (not template-with-name)
3. They operate across multiple channels simultaneously (email + LinkedIn + phone)

## Cold Email Frameworks

### Framework 1: AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

The oldest and most widely used persuasion framework, adapted for email:

**Structure:**
```
Subject Line: [Attention — specific, relevant, curiosity-provoking]

[Attention] — Opening line that is about THEM, not you.
[Interest] — A specific insight, data point, or observation relevant to their situation.
[Desire] — What the outcome looks like (result, not feature).
[Action] — Clear, low-friction CTA (question, not demand).
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: [Company]'s sales cycle vs. industry benchmark

Hi [Name],

I noticed [Company] posted 3 senior AE roles this month — congratulations on the
growth. (Attention)

Companies scaling their sales org at your pace typically see a 40% increase in
pipeline but only a 15% increase in closed revenue. The gap is usually ramp time
and process fragmentation. (Interest)

Our customers in [industry] have compressed ramp time from 6 months to 3.5 months
by standardizing their discovery methodology. (Desire)

Would it be worth 15 minutes to explore whether a similar approach could work for
[Company]? (Action)

[Signature]
```

### Framework 2: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

Effective when the buyer has a known, painful problem:

**Structure:**
```
[Problem] — Name their specific problem.
[Agitate] — Amplify the consequences of the problem.
[Solve] — Position your solution as the path to relief.
[CTA] — Low-friction next step.
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: The $500K problem hiding in your pipeline

Hi [Name],

Most B2B sales teams lose 30-40% of qualified pipeline to "no decision" — deals
that just die without a competitor winning. (Problem)

For a company your size, that is potentially $500K+ in pipeline value evaporating
every quarter. Not lost to a competitor — just lost to indecision and poor deal
management. (Agitate)

We help teams like [similar company] reduce no-decision losses by 50% through
structured deal qualification and buyer engagement frameworks. (Solve)

Is this a problem worth discussing? (CTA)
```

### Framework 3: BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

Effective for painting a transformation vision:

**Structure:**
```
[Before] — Their current painful reality.
[After] — The desired future state.
[Bridge] — Your product/service as the path between them.
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: From 6 months to 3 months (ramp time)

Hi [Name],

Right now, every new AE you hire takes 6+ months to become productive — costing
you approximately $100K in salary before they generate positive ROI. (Before)

Imagine cutting that in half: new reps closing their first deal in month 3,
reaching full quota by month 5, and generating pipeline from week 2. (After)

That is what [similar company] achieved using our structured onboarding and
enablement platform. (Bridge)

Worth exploring? I can share how they did it in 15 minutes.
```

### Framework 4: The 9-Word Email (Dean Jackson)

For re-engaging cold or dead leads. Extremely short, extremely effective:

**Structure:**
```
Subject: [First name]?

Are you still looking into [solving problem / improving area]?
```

**Why It Works:**
- Brevity signals authenticity (not a mass email)
- Question format invites a reply
- "Still" implies prior interest without referencing a specific interaction
- Response rate: 15-30% (significantly above average cold email)

## Signal-Based Outbound

### What Is Signal-Based Outbound?

Signal-based outbound uses real-time data about buyer behavior and organizational
changes to time and personalize outreach. Instead of "cold" email (no context),
signal-based outbound is "warm" email (triggered by observable buyer activity).

### Signal Categories

| Signal Type | Examples | Data Source | Urgency |
|------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Intent Data | Researching your category, visiting competitor sites | Bombora, G2, 6sense | High |
| Trigger Events | Funding raised, executive hire, product launch | Crunchbase, LinkedIn, news APIs | High |
| Engagement Signals | Visited pricing page, attended webinar, downloaded content | Your analytics, MAP | Very High |
| Job Posting Signals | Hiring for roles that indicate your category need | LinkedIn, Indeed, Greenhouse | Medium |
| Technographic Signals | Adopted complementary tech, dropped competitor tool | BuiltWith, HG Insights | Medium |
| Social Signals | Engaged with your content, followed company page | LinkedIn, Twitter | Medium |
| Community Signals | Asked question about your category in forums | Reddit, Slack, Discord | High |
| Review Site Signals | Reviewed competitor (positive or negative) | G2, TrustRadius | High |

### Signal-Based Sequence Design

**High-Intent Signal (e.g., pricing page visit):**
```
Day 0: Immediate — Personalized email referencing their visit context
Day 1: LinkedIn connection request with value-add note
Day 2: Phone call (brief, reference the email)
Day 4: Follow-up email with relevant case study
Day 7: LinkedIn message with industry insight
Day 10: Final attempt — breakup email ("seems like timing isn't right")
```

**Trigger Event Signal (e.g., new VP hire):**
```
Day 0: Congratulations email (no pitch, genuine)
Day 7: Share relevant industry content
Day 14: Personalized email connecting their new role to your value
Day 21: LinkedIn engagement (comment on their posts)
Day 28: Direct outreach with specific hypothesis about their priorities
```

**Intent Data Signal (e.g., researching your category):**
```
Day 0: Personalized email referencing the challenge they are likely researching
Day 3: LinkedIn connection + industry insight share
Day 7: Email with comparison guide or benchmark report
Day 10: Phone call with specific value proposition
Day 14: Case study from similar company
Day 21: Breakup email with resource link
```

## Multi-Channel Outbound Orchestration

### Why Multi-Channel Matters

Single-channel outbound (email only) has diminishing returns. Average cold email
response rates have declined from 5-10% (2018) to 1-3% (2025). Multi-channel
outbound (email + LinkedIn + phone + video) achieves 3-5x higher response rates
because it creates multiple impression points across the buyer's attention landscape.

### Channel Strengths

| Channel | Strength | Weakness | Best For |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|
| Email | Scalable, asynchronous, trackable | Crowded inbox, spam risk | Initial outreach, follow-up, content delivery |
| LinkedIn | Professional context, profile visible | Limited message length, social norms | Connection, engagement, social proof |
| Phone | High intimacy, immediate feedback | Low answer rate, time-intensive | Decision makers, warm follow-up |
| Video (Loom) | Personal, memorable, differentiated | Time to create, not scalable | High-value accounts, warm prospects |
| Direct Mail | Cuts through digital noise, tangible | Expensive, slow, logistics | Tier 1 ABM, executive outreach |

### Multi-Channel Sequence Template (14-Day)

| Day | Channel | Action | Objective |
|-----|---------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Email | Personalized cold email (PAS or AIDA) | Open conversation |
| 2 | LinkedIn | Connection request with personalized note | Multi-channel presence |
| 3 | Phone | Brief call attempt (leave voicemail if no answer) | Voice impression |
| 5 | Email | Follow-up with value-add (article, insight) | Provide value |
| 7 | LinkedIn | Comment on their recent post or share | Social engagement |
| 8 | Email | Case study or proof point relevant to them | Build credibility |
| 10 | Phone | Second call attempt | Direct conversation |
| 12 | LinkedIn | Direct message with specific insight | Alternative channel |
| 14 | Email | Breakup email ("Seems like timing is off...") | Final attempt + dignity |

## Compliance Framework

### CAN-SPAM Act (US)

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| No deceptive subject lines | Subject must accurately reflect content |
| Identify as advertisement | Commercial emails must be identifiable |
| Physical address | Include valid postal address |
| Opt-out mechanism | Every email must include unsubscribe option |
| Honor opt-outs | Process within 10 business days |
| Monitor third parties | You are responsible for emails sent on your behalf |
| Penalty | Up to $50,120 per violation |

### GDPR (EU/UK)

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Lawful basis | Must have legitimate interest or consent |
| B2B exception | Legitimate interest applies to B2B cold outreach (narrowly) |
| Right to be forgotten | Must delete data upon request |
| Data minimization | Only collect data you need |
| Privacy policy | Must be accessible and transparent |
| DPO requirement | Required for organizations processing data at scale |
| Penalty | Up to 4% of global revenue or 20M EUR |

### CASL (Canada)

| Requirement | Detail |
|------------|--------|
| Express consent | Required for commercial electronic messages |
| Implied consent | Allowed if existing business relationship (2 years) |
| Unsubscribe | Functional mechanism, honor within 10 days |
| Identification | Sender name, physical address, contact info |
| Penalty | Up to $10M CAD per violation |

### Compliance Best Practices

1. Maintain opt-out suppression list, synced across all sending platforms
2. Never purchase email lists (high bounce, spam trap risk, legal exposure)
3. Warm up new sending domains gradually (50/day week 1, scale 25%/week)
4. Monitor deliverability metrics: bounce rate <2%, spam complaint <0.1%
5. Authenticate all sending domains (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
6. Include unsubscribe link in every email (even cold)
7. Honor unsubscribes immediately (do not "delay" processing)
8. Segment EU/UK contacts and apply GDPR-specific protocols
9. Document legitimate interest basis for GDPR B2B outreach
10. Never send to personal email addresses for B2B purposes

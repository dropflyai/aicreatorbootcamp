# Messaging Frameworks -- Translating Positioning into Language

## Purpose

Messaging bridges the gap between positioning strategy and market-facing
communication. While positioning determines what you own in the customer's mind,
messaging determines how you communicate that position through every touchpoint.
This module provides frameworks for constructing messaging hierarchies, value
propositions, buyer personas, and narrative structures.

---

## The Messaging Hierarchy

Every brand needs a structured messaging hierarchy that ensures consistency
across all communications while allowing flexibility for different audiences
and contexts.

### Level 1: Core Positioning Statement
The foundational positioning (from `02_positioning/positioning_strategy.md`).
One sentence. Unchanging. Internal-facing.

### Level 2: Brand Promise / Tagline
The external expression of positioning. Simple, memorable, emotional.
- Nike: "Just Do It" (positioning: human potential through athletics)
- Apple: "Think Different" (positioning: creative rebellion)
- Salesforce: "No Software" (positioning: cloud CRM)

### Level 3: Value Propositions (by Persona)
Tailored messaging for each buyer persona. 2-3 sentences each.
Addresses: Who you help, what outcome you deliver, how you are different.

### Level 4: Supporting Messages
Proof points, features, and benefits that support each value proposition.
3-5 supporting messages per value proposition.

### Level 5: Proof Points
Evidence: case studies, data, testimonials, awards, certifications.
At least 2-3 proof points per supporting message.

## Value Proposition Frameworks

### The Strategyzer Value Proposition Canvas (Osterwalder)

Maps customer needs to your offering:

**Customer Profile:**
- Customer Jobs: What are they trying to accomplish? (functional, social, emotional)
- Pains: What frustrates them about current solutions?
- Gains: What outcomes would delight them?

**Value Map:**
- Products/Services: What do you offer?
- Pain Relievers: How do you eliminate pains?
- Gain Creators: How do you create gains?

**Fit = Pain Relievers address Pains + Gain Creators enable Gains**

### Value Proposition Template

```
For [target customer persona]
who are frustrated by [primary pain]
our [product/service] provides [primary benefit]
by [unique mechanism/approach]
so they can [desired outcome].
Unlike [alternatives], we [key differentiator]
as proven by [evidence/proof point].
```

### The "Before and After" Framework

| Dimension | Before (Current State) | After (With Product) |
|-----------|----------------------|---------------------|
| What they HAVE | Scattered data, manual work | Unified dashboard, automation |
| How they FEEL | Frustrated, overwhelmed | Confident, in control |
| Average DAY | 3 hours on reports | 15 minutes reviewing insights |
| What they TELL others | "I'm drowning in data" | "I finally have clarity" |

## The StoryBrand Framework (Donald Miller)

Based on mythological narrative structure (Joseph Campbell's Hero's Journey),
adapted for marketing. The customer is the hero, not your brand.

### Seven Elements

1. **A Character** (the customer has a desire)
2. **Has a Problem** (external, internal, philosophical)
3. **And Meets a Guide** (your brand -- shows empathy + authority)
4. **Who Gives Them a Plan** (clear steps to success)
5. **And Calls Them to Action** (direct CTA + transitional CTA)
6. **That Helps Them Avoid Failure** (stakes -- what happens if they don't act)
7. **And Ends in Success** (the transformation achieved)

### StoryBrand Application

**Character:** "Marketing leaders at growing SaaS companies"
**Problem:**
- External: "Can't attribute revenue to marketing channels"
- Internal: "Feels like they're flying blind"
- Philosophical: "Marketing should be accountable, not a cost center"
**Guide:** Your brand (empathy: "We've been there" + authority: "trusted by 500+ SaaS cos")
**Plan:** "1. Connect your data. 2. See attribution. 3. Optimize spend."
**CTA:** "Start your free trial" (direct) / "Download the attribution guide" (transitional)
**Failure:** "Continue wasting 40% of budget on channels that don't work"
**Success:** "Every marketing dollar is optimized. The board sees marketing as revenue driver."

### StoryBrand One-Liner
The elevator pitch version:
```
[Problem] + [Solution] + [Result]
"Most marketing teams waste 40% of their budget because they can't see what's
working. [Product] gives you real-time attribution across every channel, so
you can invest with confidence and prove ROI to the board."
```

## Buyer Persona Development

### Persona Components

**Demographics / Firmographics:**
- Job title, role, reporting structure
- Company size, industry, growth stage
- Geographic location
- Budget authority level

**Psychographics / Motivations:**
- Professional goals and aspirations
- Personal values and beliefs
- Attitude toward risk and change
- Information consumption habits

**Pain Points (Hierarchical):**
1. Strategic pains: What business outcomes are at risk?
2. Operational pains: What daily frustrations do they face?
3. Tactical pains: What specific tasks are difficult?

**Decision-Making Process:**
- Who else is involved in the buying decision?
- What information sources do they trust?
- What are the typical objections?
- What triggers the search for a solution?

### Persona Template

```
PERSONA: [Name + Role]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
Role:           [Title at Company Type]
Reports to:     [Manager/VP/C-Suite]
Team size:      [Number of direct reports]
Budget:         [Spending authority]
Goals:          [Top 3 professional objectives]
Challenges:     [Top 3 pain points]
Success metric: [How they are measured]
Objections:     [Top 3 reasons NOT to buy]
Channels:       [Where they consume information]
Trigger event:  [What causes them to search for a solution]
Quote:          ["In their own words..."]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
```

### Anti-Persona
Equally important: define who you are NOT for.
- What characteristics disqualify a prospect?
- What use cases do you explicitly not serve?
- What behaviors indicate poor fit?
This prevents wasted marketing spend on bad-fit leads.

## Message Testing Methodology

### Qualitative Message Testing

**Method:** Show 3-5 message variations to 8-12 target persona representatives.
**Protocol:**
1. Show message (no context about your brand)
2. "What is this product/service?"
3. "Who is this for?"
4. "What would you expect to pay?"
5. "What questions do you still have?"
6. Rank messages by preference and explain why

### Quantitative Message Testing

**Method:** A/B test messages in real market conditions.
**Channels for testing:**
- Landing page headlines (highest traffic = fastest results)
- Ad copy variations (Google Ads, LinkedIn, Meta)
- Email subject lines (large list = fast results)
- Social media posts (organic or paid)

**Metrics:**
- Click-through rate (interest signal)
- Conversion rate (action signal)
- Time on page (engagement signal)
- Bounce rate (relevance signal)

### Message-Market Fit Indicators

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
|--------|---------|--------|
| High CTR, high conversion | Message resonates, value clear | Scale |
| High CTR, low conversion | Interest but value unclear | Improve landing page |
| Low CTR, high conversion | Message not compelling but product strong | Improve messaging |
| Low CTR, low conversion | Fundamental positioning problem | Revisit positioning |

## Messaging by Funnel Stage

### Top of Funnel (Awareness)
- Lead with the problem, not the solution
- Emotional resonance over rational argument
- Educational and thought-provoking
- Example: "Why 60% of marketing budgets are wasted"

### Middle of Funnel (Consideration)
- Lead with the solution category and differentiation
- Social proof and case studies
- Comparison and evaluation content
- Example: "How [Customer] increased ROAS 3x with attribution"

### Bottom of Funnel (Decision)
- Lead with specific product value and proof
- Risk reversal (free trial, guarantee, demo)
- Clear, direct call to action
- Example: "Start your free trial. See attribution in 24 hours."

### Post-Purchase (Retention / Expansion)
- Lead with success and next steps
- Upsell based on demonstrated value
- Community and peer connection
- Example: "You've saved $200K this quarter. Here's how to save more."

## Messaging Governance

### Consistency Rules
1. All external messaging must trace to the messaging hierarchy
2. No team creates messaging in isolation
3. Sales teams may adapt language but not change core positioning
4. New messaging must be tested before replacing existing messaging
5. Messaging review happens quarterly

### Messaging Audit Checklist
- [ ] Does this align with our positioning statement?
- [ ] Is this written for the target persona (not for us)?
- [ ] Does this lead with value, not features?
- [ ] Is there a clear CTA?
- [ ] Can we prove every claim?
- [ ] Is this distinct from competitor messaging?
- [ ] Would the target persona use this language?

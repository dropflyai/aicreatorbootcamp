## Decision Quality Framework

Before any strategic or delegation decision, apply:

1. **Classify the Decision** -- One-way door (irreversible) or two-way door (reversible)?
2. **Identify the Decider** -- Who has the best information? Delegate two-way doors.
3. **Incentives Audit** -- Who benefits? What behaviors does this create?
4. **Second-Order Effects** -- What happens next? And then?
5. **Tradeoff Transparency** -- What are we giving up? Make it explicit.
6. **Kill Criteria** -- Under what conditions do we reverse or abandon?
7. **Speed Calibration** -- Irreversible = deep analysis. Reversible = bias toward action.

Source: Bezos one-way/two-way door framework, Drucker effective decision, Grove decision-making output.

## Multi-Brain Coordination Protocol

When a task requires multiple brains:

1. **Decompose** -- Break into brain-specific subtasks
2. **Sequence** -- Identify dependencies and critical path
3. **Assign Lead** -- The domain with highest stakes leads
4. **Define Handoffs** -- Explicit deliverables at each transition
5. **Set Quality Gates** -- Each handoff includes acceptance criteria
6. **Execute** -- Run in dependency order, parallelize where possible
7. **Integrate** -- CEO Brain reviews the combined output
8. **Retrospect** -- Log learnings to Memory

### Conflict Resolution Between Brains

When brains disagree (e.g., Engineering says "too complex" but Design says "essential"):

1. **Identify the conflict** -- What exactly do they disagree about?
2. **Classify the stakes** -- Revenue impact, user impact, technical debt impact
3. **Apply CEO judgment** -- Use the Decision Quality Framework
4. **Ruling criteria:**
   - User value wins over engineering convenience
   - Revenue impact wins over aesthetic preference
   - Long-term health wins over short-term speed
   - Reversible choices get less deliberation
5. **Document the ruling** -- Log to Memory with rationale

# CEO Brain -- Benchmark Tests (Authoritative)

These benchmark scenarios test the CEO Brain's ability to make strategic decisions,
orchestrate specialist brains, resolve cross-brain conflicts, and lead effectively
under realistic business conditions.

Every scenario must be answered with structured analysis demonstrating
orchestration thinking, not just solo decision-making.
Responses are evaluated against CEOScore.md dimensions.

---

## HOW TO USE THESE BENCHMARKS

1. Present each scenario to the CEO Brain as if it were a real business situation
2. Evaluate the response against the Expected Analysis Framework for each test
3. Score the response using CEOScore.md dimensions, including cross-brain evaluation
4. A passing response must address all Required Elements listed
5. CEO Brain responses MUST demonstrate brain routing and orchestration, not just direct answers
6. Track scores over time to measure brain improvement

### Scoring Each Response
- **5/5** -- All required elements addressed, correct brains identified, orchestration demonstrated, analysis rigorous, cross-brain integration clear
- **4/5** -- Most required elements addressed, mostly correct brain routing, orchestration mostly demonstrated, minor gaps
- **3/5** -- Required elements partially addressed, some routing errors, orchestration weak, analysis shallow
- **2/5** -- Major elements missing, routing wrong, no orchestration thinking, solo decision-making
- **1/5** -- Response is generic leadership advice, no brain routing, no orchestration, no structured analysis

## SCENARIO 1: Cross-Brain Feature Prioritization Conflict

**Situation:**
Product Brain says build Feature A (customer-requested reporting dashboard).
Sales Brain says customers need Feature B (CRM integration for enterprise deals).
Engineering Brain says both take equal effort (3 sprints each) but cannot do both
in the quarter. Design Brain has capacity for one major project.

Additional context:
- Feature A: Requested by 40% of existing customers, reduces churn risk
- Feature B: Required by 3 enterprise prospects worth $500K combined ARR
- Current ARR: $2M, Churn: 5% monthly, Pipeline: $1.5M
- Engineering team: 8 engineers, Design team: 2 designers

**Question:** Make the call. Show your reasoning, brain consultation process, and how you resolve the conflict.

### Required Elements in Response
- [ ] Each brain's perspective acknowledged with their data
- [ ] Clear criteria for making the decision (not arbitrary)
- [ ] Revenue impact analysis for both options
- [ ] Churn impact analysis for both options
- [ ] Strategic alignment assessment (which connects to top priorities?)
- [ ] Decision documented with rationale
- [ ] Communication plan to losing brain (why their option was not chosen)
- [ ] Mitigation plan for the option not chosen (how to address that need later)
- [ ] Brain routing: Which brains were consulted and in what order
- [ ] Integration: How this decision fits into overall strategy

## SCENARIO 2: Fundraising Timing Decision

**Situation:**
Company has $2M runway remaining. Current: $100K MRR growing 10% month-over-month.
Gross margin: 72%. Team: 15 people. Burn rate: $180K/month.
Options: Raise Series A now (~$5-8M at $25-30M valuation) or wait 6 months
(potentially ~$10-15M at $40-60M valuation, but runway will be tight).

The market is showing signs of cooling (2 competitors raised down rounds).
Your investors are signaling willingness to lead a round now.

**Question:** Raise now or wait? Which brains do you consult and why? Show the complete orchestration.

### Required Elements in Response
- [ ] Brain routing plan (which brains consulted, in what order, for what)
- [ ] Finance/MBA Brain consultation: runway analysis, dilution math, valuation scenarios
- [ ] Sales Brain consultation: pipeline confidence, growth sustainability
- [ ] Engineering Brain consultation: can you maintain 10% MoM with current team?
- [ ] Market analysis (cooling market impact on fundraising)
- [ ] Risk analysis for both scenarios (raise now vs. wait)
- [ ] Decision framework with clear criteria
- [ ] Reversibility assessment (is this a one-way door?)
- [ ] Stakeholder communication plan (board, team, investors)
- [ ] Contingency plan if chosen path fails

## SCENARIO 3: Triple-Brain Pricing Conflict Resolution

**Situation:**
Three brains give conflicting advice on pricing strategy for a new product tier:

**Pricing Brain:** "Price at $99/mo based on WTP research showing optimal price point.
Value metric should be per-user. Annual discount of 20%."

**Sales Brain:** "Price at $149/mo. Enterprise buyers do not take <$100/mo products
seriously. We need perceived value. Sales team cannot sell cheap products with
high-touch process."

**Marketing Brain:** "Price at $49/mo. PLG is our growth engine. Low price drives
adoption, and we monetize through expansion. The $99 price kills viral adoption."

All three have valid data supporting their positions.

**Question:** Resolve this conflict using first principles. Show the orchestration process.

### Required Elements in Response
- [ ] Each brain's position summarized with their supporting data
- [ ] First principles identified (what are the fundamental truths?)
- [ ] Strategic alignment check (which pricing supports the company strategy?)
- [ ] Growth model assessment (is this a PLG or sales-led company?)
- [ ] Customer segmentation analysis (are they talking about different segments?)
- [ ] Synthesis attempt (is there a solution that addresses all three perspectives?)
- [ ] If synthesis impossible, clear decision with rationale
- [ ] Losing brains' concerns mitigated
- [ ] Experiment design to validate the chosen approach
- [ ] Decision documented with predictions and timeline for review

## SCENARIO 4: Brain Routing Design

**Situation:**
New project request: "Build a B2B SaaS product for logistics companies that helps
them optimize last-mile delivery routes using AI."

This is a greenfield project. No existing product, no existing customers.
Budget: $500K. Timeline: 6 months to MVP, 12 months to revenue.

**Question:** Design the complete brain-routing plan. Which brains in what order, with what handoffs?

### Required Elements in Response
- [ ] Phase-by-phase brain routing plan
- [ ] For each phase: lead brain, supporting brains, expected output
- [ ] Handoff specifications between phases (what information passes)
- [ ] Parallel vs. sequential brain activation (what can run simultaneously)
- [ ] Integration points (where do brain outputs need to be synthesized)
- [ ] Decision gates (where does CEO Brain need to make decisions)
- [ ] Resource allocation across brains
- [ ] Timeline with brain activation schedule
- [ ] Risk identification: which brain gaps affect this project
- [ ] Quality gates: how each brain's output is evaluated before proceeding

### Expected Brain Involvement (minimum)
```
Research Brain: Market and customer research
Product Brain: Product strategy and roadmap
Design Brain: UX research and interface design
Engineering Brain: Architecture, implementation, deployment
AI Brain: ML model selection, training, optimization
Pricing Brain: Pricing strategy and packaging
Marketing Brain: Go-to-market strategy
Sales Brain: Sales process and early customer acquisition
MBA Brain: Business model and unit economics
Innovation Brain: Competitive differentiation and validation approach
```

## SCENARIO 5: Crisis Management and Brain Coordination

**Situation:**
Friday 4 PM. Three simultaneous crises:
1. Major security breach detected -- customer data may be compromised (1,000 affected accounts)
2. Largest customer ($300K ARR) threatens to churn by end of month (contract renewal in 15 days)
3. Lead engineer (only person who understands the payment system) just resigned effective immediately

You have: CEO Brain, Engineering Brain, Security Brain (placeholder), Sales Brain,
Legal Brain (placeholder), HR Brain (placeholder), and Customer Success Brain (placeholder).

**Question:** Triage, route, and coordinate the response. Show minute-by-minute orchestration.

### Required Elements in Response
- [ ] Immediate triage (what is addressed first, second, third with reasoning)
- [ ] Brain activation plan for each crisis
- [ ] For each crisis: lead brain, supporting brains, immediate actions
- [ ] Resource conflicts identified (same people needed for multiple crises)
- [ ] Communication plan (who is told what, when, by whom)
- [ ] For security breach: legal implications, customer notification, technical response
- [ ] For churn threat: retention strategy, escalation plan, negotiation approach
- [ ] For resignation: knowledge transfer, interim plan, hiring plan
- [ ] Cross-crisis dependencies (security breach may affect churn customer)
- [ ] 24-hour, 72-hour, and 1-week plans for each crisis
- [ ] Post-crisis review plan

## SCENARIO 10: Brain Architecture Evolution

### Required Elements in Response
- [ ] Diagnosis of current architecture problems
- [ ] Analysis of underutilized brains (merge, sunset, or improve?)
- [ ] Analysis of overutilized brains (split, add capacity, or restructure?)
- [ ] Contradiction resolution between conflicting brains
- [ ] Handoff quality improvement plan
- [ ] New brain design for AI/ML domain (or integration into existing)
- [ ] Simplified routing guide for the team
- [ ] Migration plan (how to transition without disruption)
- [ ] Success metrics for the new architecture
- [ ] Governance model for adding/removing brains in the future

## SCENARIO 15: Ethics and Values Decision

**Situation:**
Largest customer (15% of revenue) requests a feature that would allow them to
track employee productivity in ways that raise privacy concerns. The feature is:
- Legal in all jurisdictions where you operate
- Technically feasible (Engineering Brain confirmed)
- Highly profitable (Pricing Brain estimates $200K additional ARR from this customer alone)
- Likely to attract similar enterprise customers (Sales Brain projects $1M ARR potential)
- Contrary to your company's stated values around employee privacy and dignity
- Marketing Brain warns of potential PR risk if press discovers the feature

**Question:** Make the decision. Show how values factor into brain orchestration.

### Required Elements in Response
- [ ] Acknowledgment that this is not just a business decision
- [ ] Each brain's perspective documented (Engineering, Pricing, Sales, Marketing, Legal)
- [ ] Values alignment check (does this contradict stated values?)
- [ ] Revenue impact of saying yes vs. no
- [ ] Brand and reputation risk assessment
- [ ] Precedent analysis (what does this decision signal for future requests?)
- [ ] Is there a middle ground (privacy-respecting version of the feature?)
- [ ] Customer communication plan regardless of decision
- [ ] Team communication (employees need to understand values-based decisions)
- [ ] Decision documented with rationale and values reference

# CEO Score -- Quality Enforcement (Authoritative)

This document defines how the CEO Brain's decisions, orchestration, and strategic
outputs are evaluated. The CEO Brain is the master orchestrator -- it is evaluated
not just on its own decisions but on the quality of cross-brain coordination.

If orchestration quality is not measurable, it is not enforced.

---

## SCORING RULES (MANDATORY)

Each CEO Brain deliverable must be scored across the following dimensions.
Score each category from **1 (poor)** to **5 (excellent)**.

### Hard Fail Dimensions
These dimensions are critical. Score <3 = immediate intervention required:
- **Strategic Clarity**
- **Brain Orchestration**
- **Decision Quality**

### Passing Criteria
- Average score across all dimensions >= 4.0
- No hard-fail dimension < 3
- No dimension < 2 under any circumstance
- Crisis Response is NOT a hard fail in non-crisis periods (score N/A)

### Special Evaluation Note
The CEO Brain is unique: it must be evaluated on BOTH its own outputs AND the
quality of cross-brain coordination. A CEO Brain that makes good solo decisions
but routes tasks to the wrong brains still fails.

## 1. STRATEGIC CLARITY

**Question:**
Is the vision articulated clearly, is the strategy coherent and internally consistent,
and are priorities ruthlessly focused (fewer than 3 at any time)?

### What to Evaluate
- Vision is articulated in one sentence that a new employee understands
- Strategy follows logically from vision (not a disconnected list of initiatives)
- Strategy is internally consistent (no conflicting priorities)
- Priorities are limited to 3 or fewer (saying no to good things is required)
- Strategy differentiates between "must do" and "nice to do"
- Time horizon is explicit (what is the 1-year, 3-year, 10-year plan?)
- Strategy is updated when conditions change (not annual-only)
- Every brain can articulate how their work connects to the strategy

### Scoring Guide
- **5** -- Vision crystal clear, strategy logically derived, <=2 priorities, every team can connect their work to strategy, strategy reviewed quarterly and updated when conditions change, explicit tradeoffs documented
- **4** -- Vision clear, strategy mostly coherent, 3 priorities, most teams aligned, strategy reviewed semi-annually, key tradeoffs documented
- **3** -- Vision exists but is vague or aspirational without specificity, strategy is a list of initiatives (not coherent), 4-5 priorities (too many), some teams confused about connection, strategy reviewed annually
- **2** -- Vision changes frequently, strategy is incoherent or contradictory, too many priorities (>5), teams building in different directions, strategy not reviewed
- **1** -- No clear vision, no strategy (just reactive tactics), no priorities (everything is priority 1), teams have no direction, strategy nonexistent

### Hard Fail Triggers
- Score <3 = STOP. Strategy session required before any tactical work.
- More than 3 priorities active simultaneously = automatic deduction of 1 point
- Strategy contradicts itself = automatic score of 2

### Strategic Clarity Test
Ask these questions. If any cannot be answered clearly, strategy lacks clarity:
```
1. What are we building and for whom? (one sentence)
2. What makes us different from alternatives? (one sentence)
3. What are we NOT doing? (explicit exclusions)
4. What are the 1-3 things that must be true for us to win?
5. How will we know if the strategy is working? (leading indicators)
6. What would cause us to change the strategy? (update triggers)
```

## 2. EXECUTION QUALITY

**Question:**
Are OKRs being achieved? Are projects on time? Is quality maintained while shipping?

### What to Evaluate
- OKR achievement rate tracked and above 70%
- Projects deliver on time at acceptable quality (not late, not sloppy)
- Velocity is sustainable (not sprint-then-burnout cycles)
- Blockers are identified and resolved quickly (<48 hours for critical blockers)
- Dependencies between teams and brains are managed proactively
- Execution is not sacrificing quality for speed or vice versa
- Shipped work meets the quality standards of the relevant specialist brain

### Scoring Guide
- **5** -- OKR achievement >80%, >90% of projects on time, quality standards consistently met, blockers resolved <24h, dependencies managed proactively, sustainable velocity maintained
- **4** -- OKR achievement 70-80%, >80% of projects on time, quality standards mostly met, blockers resolved <48h, most dependencies managed
- **3** -- OKR achievement 50-70%, >60% of projects on time, quality inconsistent, blockers resolved slowly, some dependency surprises, occasional death marches
- **2** -- OKR achievement <50%, frequent delays, quality frequently compromised, blockers pile up, dependency management reactive, team burnout visible
- **1** -- OKRs not tracked or not achieved, projects consistently late, quality poor, blockers unresolved, no dependency management, team in crisis

## 3. TEAM EFFECTIVENESS

**Question:**
Are the right people in the right roles? Is the team performing at a high level?
Is there a leadership pipeline for growth?

### What to Evaluate
- Key roles filled with qualified people (no critical gaps unfilled >30 days)
- Team satisfaction measured and above threshold (eNPS >30)
- Performance management is functioning (low performers addressed, high performers rewarded)
- Leadership pipeline exists (at least 1 person ready for each critical role)
- Team composition matches strategic needs (not legacy org chart)
- Culture reinforces strategy (values are lived, not just stated)
- Decision-making authority is appropriately distributed (not all centralized)
- Brain specialists matched to their domain expertise

### Scoring Guide
- **5** -- All critical roles filled, eNPS >50, performance management active, 2+ ready-now successors for each key role, culture aligned with strategy, brain specialists operating in their zones of expertise
- **4** -- Most critical roles filled, eNPS 30-50, performance management functioning, at least 1 successor per key role, culture mostly aligned
- **3** -- Some critical gaps, eNPS 10-30, performance management inconsistent, limited succession planning, culture partially aligned, some brain misassignment
- **2** -- Multiple critical gaps, eNPS <10, no performance management, no succession planning, culture misaligned, frequent brain misassignment
- **1** -- Key positions vacant, team morale in crisis, no people processes, no leadership development, culture toxic, brains operating outside expertise

### Team Health Indicators
| Indicator | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|-----------|---------|---------|----------|
| eNPS | >30 | 10-30 | <10 |
| Voluntary Turnover | <10%/yr | 10-20%/yr | >20%/yr |
| Time to Fill Critical Roles | <30 days | 30-60 days | >60 days |
| Manager Effectiveness Rating | >4/5 | 3-4/5 | <3/5 |
| Brain Specialist Satisfaction | >4/5 | 3-4/5 | <3/5 |

## 4. CAPITAL ALLOCATION

**Question:**
Is money being invested wisely? Are returns on investment being tracked?
Is cash managed with appropriate efficiency?

### What to Evaluate
- ROI tracked for major investments (>$50K)
- Cash burn rate sustainable relative to runway and revenue growth
- Investment decisions are data-informed (not gut-driven for large amounts)
- Resource allocation aligns with stated priorities (money follows strategy)
- Fundraising timing is deliberate (not reactive to runway pressure)
- Budget variance tracked and explained (<10% variance is healthy)
- Brain resource allocation matches strategic importance

### Scoring Guide
- **5** -- ROI tracked for all major investments, cash efficiency optimized, >18 months runway maintained, resource allocation perfectly mirrors priorities, budget variance <5%, fundraising timing deliberate and strategic
- **4** -- ROI tracked for most investments, cash efficiency acceptable, >12 months runway, resource allocation mostly aligns with priorities, budget variance <10%
- **3** -- ROI tracked inconsistently, cash efficiency not optimized, 6-12 months runway, resource allocation partially misaligned, budget variance 10-20%
- **2** -- ROI not tracked, cash burn unsustainable, <6 months runway, resource allocation misaligned with priorities, budget variance >20%
- **1** -- No financial tracking, cash crisis imminent, resources wasted, no connection between spending and strategy

### Capital Allocation Framework

## 5. BRAIN ORCHESTRATION

**Question:**
Is the CEO Brain routing tasks to the correct specialist brains? Are inter-brain
handoffs smooth? Are there domain gaps where no brain is covering?

### What to Evaluate
- Tasks routed to the correct specialist brain (not handled by CEO Brain when specialist exists)
- Inter-brain handoffs include context, constraints, and expected outputs
- Handoff quality measured (receiving brain does not need to re-ask for context)
- Domain gaps identified (areas where no specialist brain exists and work quality suffers)
- Multi-brain projects coordinated with clear ownership and interfaces
- Conflicting brain recommendations detected and resolved
- Brain outputs integrated into coherent strategy (not siloed recommendations)
- Brain routing is efficient (not over-consulted or under-consulted)

### Scoring Guide
- **5** -- Correct brain selected >95% of the time, handoffs include full context, zero re-asks for context, all domain gaps identified with mitigation, multi-brain projects run smoothly, conflicts resolved with principled framework, outputs integrated coherently
- **4** -- Correct brain selected >85%, handoffs mostly complete, rare re-asks, most domain gaps identified, multi-brain projects mostly smooth, conflicts resolved reasonably
- **3** -- Correct brain selected >70%, handoffs incomplete (missing context), frequent re-asks, some unidentified domain gaps, multi-brain projects have friction, conflicts resolved ad hoc
- **2** -- Frequent misrouting, handoffs poor, extensive re-work from bad handoffs, significant domain gaps, multi-brain projects fail, conflicts unresolved
- **1** -- No routing logic, CEO Brain tries to do everything, no handoffs, domain gaps everywhere, no multi-brain coordination, conflicting recommendations shipped

### Hard Fail Triggers
- Score <3 = IMMEDIATE ROUTING REVIEW. The orchestrator is not orchestrating.
- CEO Brain doing work a specialist brain should handle = -1 point per occurrence
- Conflicting brain recommendations shipped without resolution = automatic score of 2

## 6. DECISION QUALITY

**Question:**
Are reversible decisions made quickly? Are irreversible decisions made deliberately?
Are cognitive biases checked?

### What to Evaluate
- Decision type classified (reversible vs. irreversible) before deliberation starts
- Reversible decisions made within 24-48 hours (speed valued)
- Irreversible decisions given adequate deliberation (quality valued)
- Key decisions documented with rationale (not just outcome)
- Cognitive biases actively checked (confirmation bias, sunk cost, anchoring)
- Devil's advocate or pre-mortem used for major decisions
- Decision-making framework consistent (not different process each time)
- Decisions revisited when new information emerges

### Scoring Guide
- **5** -- Decision types classified, reversible decisions <24h, irreversible decisions deliberate with pre-mortem, all major decisions documented, bias checks routine, framework consistent, decisions revisited with new data, decision quality tracked
- **4** -- Decision types usually classified, reversible decisions <48h, irreversible decisions deliberate, most decisions documented, bias checks occasional, framework mostly consistent
- **3** -- Decision types sometimes classified, some decisions delayed unnecessarily, irreversible decisions rushed occasionally, documentation inconsistent, bias checks rare, no consistent framework
- **2** -- No decision classification, analysis paralysis on reversible decisions, irreversible decisions rushed, poor documentation, biases unchecked, no framework
- **1** -- Decision avoidance, both fast and slow decisions made poorly, no documentation, biases dominate, no framework, decisions never revisited

### Hard Fail Triggers
- Score <3 = DECISION PROCESS REVIEW REQUIRED
- Irreversible decision made without any documentation = automatic deduction of 1 point

### Decision Quality Framework
```
DECISION: ________________________________
TYPE: [ ] Reversible (decide fast, adjust later)
      [ ] Irreversible (decide deliberately, execute committed)

FOR REVERSIBLE DECISIONS:
  Decision: ________________________________
  Time to decide: ________________________________ (target: <48h)
  Rollback plan: ________________________________

FOR IRREVERSIBLE DECISIONS:
  Options considered: ________________________________
  Data supporting: ________________________________
  Bias check completed: [ ] Confirmation Bias [ ] Sunk Cost [ ] Anchoring
                        [ ] Availability Bias [ ] Survivorship Bias
  Pre-mortem result: ________________________________
  Devil's advocate consulted: [ ] Yes [ ] No
  Decision: ________________________________
  Rationale: ________________________________
```

### Bias Checklist (for major decisions)
```
[ ] Have I sought disconfirming evidence? (Confirmation Bias)
[ ] Am I weighting past investment in my decision? (Sunk Cost)
[ ] Am I anchored to the first number/option I saw? (Anchoring)
[ ] Am I overweighting recent or vivid examples? (Availability)
[ ] Am I only looking at successes, not failures? (Survivorship)
[ ] Have I considered the base rate? (Base Rate Neglect)
[ ] Am I too confident in my prediction? (Overconfidence)
[ ] Would I make this decision if I was starting from scratch? (Status Quo)
```

## 7. STAKEHOLDER MANAGEMENT

**Question:**
Are key stakeholders (board, investors, employees, customers) aligned, informed,
and engaged appropriately?

### What to Evaluate
- Board is informed and aligned on strategy (no surprises)
- Investors receive regular, honest updates (good news and bad)
- Employees understand the strategy and their role in it
- Customers feel heard and valued (NPS tracked, feedback loops active)
- Communication frequency matches stakeholder needs
- Bad news communicated proactively (not buried or delayed)
- Stakeholder expectations are managed (not over-promised)

### Scoring Guide
- **5** -- Board fully aligned, investor updates timely and honest, employee engagement >80%, NPS >50, communication cadence optimized, bad news delivered proactively with action plan, expectations consistently met or exceeded
- **4** -- Board mostly aligned, investor updates regular, employee engagement >65%, NPS >30, communication adequate, bad news delivered promptly, expectations mostly managed
- **3** -- Board partially aligned, investor updates inconsistent, employee engagement 50-65%, NPS 10-30, communication irregular, bad news sometimes delayed, some expectation gaps
- **2** -- Board surprised by developments, investor updates rare, employee engagement <50%, NPS <10, poor communication, bad news hidden, significant expectation gaps
- **1** -- Board in conflict with management, no investor updates, employee disengagement, negative NPS, no communication, trust broken with multiple stakeholders

## 8. CRISIS RESPONSE

**Question:**
When crises occur, how quickly are they detected, how effectively are they handled,
and how completely does the organization recover and learn?

### What to Evaluate
- Issue detection speed (time from occurrence to awareness)
- Response speed (time from awareness to action)
- Response quality (was the action correct and proportionate?)
- Communication during crisis (stakeholders informed promptly and honestly)
- Recovery completeness (did we return to baseline or improve?)
- Lessons learned documented and systemic changes made
- Crisis playbook exists for common scenarios
- Post-crisis review conducted

### Scoring Guide
- **5** -- Issues detected <2 hours, response <4 hours, response quality high, stakeholders informed immediately with updates, full recovery within expected timeline, comprehensive lessons learned, crisis playbook maintained and rehearsed
- **4** -- Issues detected <8 hours, response <12 hours, response quality good, stakeholders informed same day, full recovery with minor lingering effects, lessons learned documented
- **3** -- Issues detected <24 hours, response <48 hours, response quality adequate, stakeholders informed with delays, recovery incomplete for extended period, limited lessons learned
- **2** -- Issues detected >24 hours, response >48 hours, response quality poor, stakeholders learned from external sources, recovery slow and painful, no lessons learned
- **1** -- Issues not detected (found by external parties), no response plan, panic-driven actions, stakeholders blindsided, recovery incomplete, crisis recurred

### Not a Hard Fail in Non-Crisis Periods
- If no crisis occurred in the evaluation period, score N/A
- Evaluate based on preparedness (crisis playbook exists, team knows roles)

### Crisis Severity Classification
```
Level 1 (Critical): Business survival at risk
  - Revenue loss >30%, major customer loss, security breach, legal action
  - Response: All-hands, CEO leads, hourly updates to stakeholders

Level 2 (Major): Significant business impact
  - Revenue loss 10-30%, product outage >24h, key employee departure
  - Response: Crisis team assembled, CEO involved, daily updates

Level 3 (Moderate): Manageable business impact
  - Revenue loss <10%, product issue <24h, negative press
  - Response: Functional lead owns, CEO informed, updates as needed

Level 4 (Minor): Minimal business impact
  - Isolated incident, quickly contained, limited exposure
  - Response: Standard operations handle, CEO notified in next regular update
```

## FINAL CEO SCORE DECISION

**Hard Fail Dimensions (Strategic Clarity, Brain Orchestration, Decision Quality):**
- Score <3 = **IMMEDIATE INTERVENTION REQUIRED**

**All Dimensions:**
- Average score >= 4.0 = **CEO BRAIN EFFECTIVE**
- Average score 3.0-3.9 = **CEO BRAIN NEEDS IMPROVEMENT**
- Average score < 3.0 = **CEO BRAIN FAILING** (fundamental restructure needed)

**Cross-Brain Evaluation:**
- Must be evaluated every quarter in addition to individual dimensions
- Cross-brain quality below threshold overrides individual dimension scores

## CEO BRAIN HEALTH CADENCE

### Weekly
- Review active brain routing decisions
- Check OKR progress against targets
- Resolve any inter-brain conflicts
- Stakeholder communication check

### Monthly
- Full CEOScore evaluation
- Cross-brain quality audit
- Capital allocation review
- Team effectiveness pulse check

### Quarterly
- Comprehensive strategy review
- Full cross-brain evaluation
- Portfolio review across all brains
- Stakeholder alignment assessment
- Decision quality retrospective

### Annually
- Strategy reset and planning
- Full organizational health assessment
- Brain architecture review (add/remove/restructure brains)
- Leadership pipeline review
- Capital allocation strategy for next year

## ENFORCEMENT RULE

CEO quality is enforced, not assumed.
The orchestrator is accountable for the orchestra.
A great strategy poorly orchestrated still fails.
A well-orchestrated poor strategy still fails.
Both must score above threshold.

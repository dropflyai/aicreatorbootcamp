## Core Operating Principles

### The Five Laws of Operational Excellence

1. **Measure before you optimize.** No process improvement without baseline data.
2. **Constraint dictates throughput.** The system moves at the speed of its bottleneck (Goldratt).
3. **Waste is the enemy.** Eliminate muda, mura, muri systematically (Toyota).
4. **Variation kills quality.** Reduce variation through statistical process control (Deming).
5. **Continuous improvement is non-negotiable.** Kaizen is a daily practice, not a project.

### Decision Framework Priority

When operational decisions conflict, resolve using this priority:
1. Safety and compliance (non-negotiable)
2. Quality (defects propagate cost exponentially)
3. Delivery / throughput (value flows to the customer)
4. Cost (optimize after safety, quality, delivery are secured)

This aligns with the classic SQDC (Safety, Quality, Delivery, Cost) hierarchy.

# Investor Brain -- Accountability Protocol (Authoritative)

This document defines how the Investor Brain is held accountable for the quality,
accuracy, and strategic integrity of its fundraising and investor relations guidance.
Accountability is not optional.

Capital decisions made without rigor destroy companies.

---

## CORE ACCOUNTABILITY PRINCIPLES

1. **Every financial number must be verifiable.** No projected number exists without a stated assumption.
2. **Every term must be understood before agreed to.** Signing what you do not understand is negligence.
3. **Every investor commitment must be honored.** Broken promises destroy trust permanently.
4. **Every cap table change must be documented.** Equity errors compound and are painful to fix.
5. **Every communication must be honest.** Misleading investors is fraud. Misleading yourself is delusion.

## ACCOUNTABILITY TIERS

### Tier 1: Pre-Fundraising Accountability

Before initiating any fundraising process:

| Requirement | Owner | Verification |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| Financial model built and stress-tested | Founder + Investor Brain | Reviewed by advisor or fractional CFO |
| Cap table verified by legal counsel | Founder | Written verification |
| Pitch deck reviewed by 5+ people outside company | Founder | Feedback documented and incorporated |
| Target investor list researched (thesis, stage, check size) | Investor Brain | CRM populated |
| Data room prepared with all critical documents | Founder + Legal | Completeness audit |
| Valuation expectations benchmarked against market | Investor Brain | Comparable analysis documented |
| Team aligned on terms boundaries (walk-away criteria) | Founder + Board | Written agreement |

**Gate:** Fundraising does not begin until all Tier 1 items are complete. Starting unprepared wastes meetings that cannot be repeated.

### Tier 2: During-Fundraising Accountability

While fundraising is active:

| Requirement | Frequency | Verification |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Pipeline updated in CRM | After every meeting | Founder self-report |
| Follow-up sent within 24 hours of every meeting | Per meeting | Email/CRM verification |
| Investor feedback recorded | Per meeting | CRM notes |
| Rejection reasons analyzed for patterns | Weekly | Pattern report |
| Pitch iterated based on feedback | Biweekly minimum | Version tracking |
| Runway check | Weekly | Cash balance review |
| Team morale and communication | Weekly | Founder check-in |
| Process pace assessment (too slow? too fast?) | Weekly | Pipeline conversion review |

**Gate:** If runway drops below 4 months during fundraising, Plan B must be activated immediately (bridge, cuts, or alternative financing).

### Tier 3: Term Sheet Accountability

When evaluating or negotiating term sheets:

| Requirement | Owner | Verification |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| Every term understood by founder | Founder + Investor Brain | Term-by-term verbal walkthrough |
| Legal counsel has reviewed all terms | Legal counsel | Written review |
| Waterfall analysis run at 5+ exit valuations | Investor Brain | Model documented |
| Impact on existing shareholders calculated | Investor Brain | Cap table pro forma |
| Future round dilution modeled | Investor Brain | 2-round forward model |
| Negotiation strategy documented | Founder + Investor Brain | Written strategy |
| Walk-away criteria defined | Founder + Board | Pre-negotiation agreement |
| Market benchmark comparison completed | Investor Brain | Comparable terms documented |

**Gate:** No term sheet is signed without all items completed. Pressure to close quickly does not override due diligence.

### Tier 4: Post-Close and Ongoing Accountability

After closing a round:

| Requirement | Timeline | Owner |
|-------------|----------|-------|
| Cap table updated and verified by counsel | Within 1 week | Founder + Legal |
| Board properly constituted per terms | Within 2 weeks | Legal |
| Monthly investor update cadence established | Immediately | Founder |
| Use of funds plan tied to milestones | Within 1 month | Founder |
| First board meeting scheduled | Within 6 weeks | Founder |
| 409A valuation ordered | Within 30 days (or per requirement) | Legal + Finance |
| Employee equity communication | Within 2 weeks | Founder + HR |
| Data room updated with closing documents | Within 1 week | Legal |

**Gate:** The close of a round is the beginning of accountability to new stakeholders, not the end of a process.

## FINANCIAL ACCURACY REQUIREMENTS

### Every Number Must Be Traceable

Financial claims made to investors must meet these standards:

| Claim Type | Standard | Verification |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| Revenue numbers | Matches accounting records exactly | Bank statements or accounting software |
| Growth rates | Calculated consistently (MoM, QoQ, YoY defined) | Documented methodology |
| Unit economics | Based on actual data (not projections) | Source data available |
| Projections | Assumptions stated and testable | Sensitivity analysis present |
| Market size | Sources cited, methodology documented | Bottom-up validation |
| Customer counts | Active customers defined consistently | System of record |

### Financial Misrepresentation Protocol

If a financial misrepresentation is discovered (intentional or accidental):

1. **Immediate:** Assess scope and impact of the error
2. **Within 24 hours:** Correct the number with explanation
3. **Within 48 hours:** Notify all affected parties (investors who received wrong numbers)
4. **Within 1 week:** Root cause analysis (was it a process failure or intentional?)
5. **Ongoing:** Implement verification step to prevent recurrence

**Rule:** Correcting errors promptly builds trust. Hiding errors destroys it permanently. The coverup is always worse than the mistake.

## INVESTOR COMMUNICATION ACCOUNTABILITY

### Update Cadence Commitment

| Communication | Frequency | Mandatory Content |
|---------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Investor update | Monthly (non-negotiable) | Metrics, wins, challenges, asks |
| Board materials | Quarterly (or per schedule) | Financials, strategy, decisions |
| Ad hoc critical updates | As needed (within 48 hours) | Material events, bad news |
| Annual review | Yearly | Comprehensive company assessment |

### Transparency Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Bad news delivery | Within 48 hours of knowing, before investors discover |
| Metric consistency | Same metrics reported every month; no disappearing metrics |
| Uncertainty honesty | Projections include confidence levels |
| Ask specificity | Every ask has context, desired outcome, and timeline |
| Response time | Investor questions answered within 48 hours |

### Communication Failure Protocol

If an investor update is missed:
1. Send the update within 3 days with explanation
2. Recommit to cadence
3. If missed 2 months in a row: proactive call to key investors to rebuild cadence

If bad news is delivered late:
1. Acknowledge the delay honestly
2. Explain the corrected timeline
3. Present mitigation plan
4. Commit to faster disclosure going forward

## CAP TABLE GOVERNANCE

### Cap Table Accuracy Protocol

| Requirement | Frequency | Owner |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Cap table reconciled with legal records | Every equity event | Legal + Founder |
| 409A valuation current | Within 12 months or post-material event | Legal + Tax advisor |
| Option grant ledger maintained | Continuous | HR + Legal |
| SAFE/note register maintained | Continuous | Legal |
| Pro forma cap table modeled for next round | Updated quarterly | Investor Brain |
| Waterfall analysis updated | After every cap table change | Investor Brain |
| Cap table tool backup maintained | Monthly | Founder |

### Cap Table Error Protocol

If a cap table error is discovered:
1. **Assess impact:** Who is affected and by how much?
2. **Legal review:** Is a correction filing needed?
3. **Communicate:** Notify affected parties
4. **Correct:** Fix the cap table with documented amendment
5. **Prevent:** Implement additional verification for the type of error

**Rule:** Cap table errors discovered during due diligence can kill deals. Prevent them.

## BOARD ACCOUNTABILITY

### Board Meeting Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Preparation | Materials sent 5+ business days in advance |
| Agenda | Includes at least 1 strategic discussion, not just updates |
| Time management | Meeting ends on time; runs over only with consent |
| Follow-through | All action items tracked; reviewed at next meeting |
| Between meetings | 1:1 with each board member at least once per quarter |
| Bad news | Delivered before good news; always with a plan |
| Decisions | Framed with options and recommendation; not surprises |

### Board Effectiveness Assessment

Annually, assess:
- Are board meetings productive or performative?
- Is the board adding value beyond governance?
- Are investor board members contributing expertise?
- Is the founder getting value from board interactions?
- Are action items being completed?
- Is the relationship healthy or adversarial?

## FAILURE DOCUMENTATION

### What Counts as an Investor Brain Failure
- Financial numbers presented to investors were wrong
- Term sheet signed without understanding a material term
- Cap table error discovered by a third party
- Investor update missed without communication
- Fundraising process was unprepared (no deck, no model, no data room)
- Investor relationship damaged by poor communication
- Bad news hidden from investors
- SAFE/note conversion miscalculated
- Waterfall analysis was wrong at a material event

## ACCOUNTABILITY IS NOT PARANOIA

This protocol exists to protect the company and build investor trust.

- Rigorous preparation leads to better terms
- Accurate numbers lead to earned trust
- Transparent communication leads to stronger relationships
- Documented processes lead to fewer errors
- Cap table governance prevents expensive surprises

The Investor Brain that holds itself accountable raises better capital on better terms.
The Investor Brain that does not is running toward a cliff with a blindfold on.

# Investor Outreach -- Pipeline, Introductions, and Follow-Up

## Overview

Investor outreach is a structured sales process. The pipeline must be managed with the
same rigor as enterprise sales: lead generation, qualification, nurturing, and conversion.
This module covers the mechanics of building and managing the investor pipeline from
first contact through commitment.

Primary references: YC fundraising guides, First Round Capital network data,
a16z portfolio support materials, "Fundraising Field Guide" by Carlos Espinal.

---

## Pipeline Architecture

### The Investor CRM

Every fundraising campaign requires a structured tracking system:

```
INVESTOR PIPELINE STAGES

1. RESEARCHED
   Investor identified, thesis fit confirmed, not yet contacted.
   Data: firm name, partner, fund size, stage, sector, portfolio

2. INTRO REQUESTED
   Warm introduction requested from mutual connection.
   Data: introducer name, date requested, connection strength

3. INTRO MADE
   Introduction sent, awaiting investor response.
   Data: date introduced, intro format (email, in-person)

4. MEETING SCHEDULED
   First meeting confirmed.
   Data: date, time, location/format, attendees, prep notes

5. FIRST MEETING COMPLETE
   Meeting occurred, initial impression assessed.
   Data: meeting notes, sentiment (hot/warm/cold), follow-ups

6. FOLLOW-UP / DILIGENCE
   Active engagement, additional meetings or data sharing.
   Data: materials shared, questions asked, partner meeting status

7. PARTNER MEETING
   Presented to full partnership or investment committee.
   Data: date, attendees, presentation format, questions

8. TERM SHEET
   Term sheet received.
   Data: terms summary, comparison to others, deadline

9. CLOSED
   Investment committed and wired.
   Data: final terms, closing docs, board seat details

10. PASSED
    Investor declined at any stage.
    Data: reason for pass, feedback received, future relationship plan

TRACKING FIELDS PER INVESTOR:
  - Firm name
  - Partner name and contact
  - Fund size and vintage
  - Stage focus
  - Sector focus
  - Portfolio companies (check for conflicts)
  - Thesis alignment score (1-5)
  - Warm intro path (who can introduce)
  - Pipeline stage
  - Last contact date
  - Next action and deadline
  - Notes from every interaction
  - Sentiment score (1-5, updated after each contact)
```

### Pipeline Metrics

```
FUNNEL CONVERSION BENCHMARKS

Outreach → Meeting:        30-50% (warm intro)
                           5-10% (cold outreach)
Meeting → Follow-up:       40-60%
Follow-up → Partner Mtg:   20-30%
Partner Mtg → Term Sheet:  30-50%
Term Sheet → Close:        70-90%

OVERALL CONVERSION:
  Warm pipeline: 3-8% (outreach to close)
  Cold pipeline: 0.5-2% (outreach to close)

VOLUME REQUIREMENTS:
  To generate 2-3 term sheets:
  Need 5-8 partner meetings
  Need 15-25 follow-up conversations
  Need 30-50 first meetings
  Need 50-80 outreach attempts

  PLAN FOR 50-80 QUALIFIED OUTREACH ATTEMPTS
  (This is why the target list must be robust)
```

## Warm Introductions

### Why Warm Intros Matter

```
WARM INTRO CONVERSION DATA (per First Round Capital):

Warm intro from portfolio founder: 50-70% meeting rate
Warm intro from mutual connection: 30-50% meeting rate
Warm intro from other investor: 25-40% meeting rate
Cold email to partner: 5-10% meeting rate
Cold email to general inbox: 1-3% meeting rate

THE NETWORK EFFECT:
A warm intro is not just higher conversion --
it primes the investor's attention and credibility assessment.
"If [trusted person] thinks this is worth my time, it probably is."
```

### Finding the Right Introducer

```
INTRODUCER HIERARCHY (strongest to weakest)

1. PORTFOLIO COMPANY FOUNDER (who had a good experience)
   - Strongest signal: "My investor should invest in you too"
   - Implies deep knowledge of both parties
   - Almost always gets a meeting

2. CO-INVESTOR OR FELLOW GP
   - "My partner at another firm says you should see this"
   - Strong professional signal
   - Very high conversion

3. TRUSTED ADVISOR / BOARD MEMBER
   - Established relationship with investor
   - Industry credibility
   - High conversion

4. MUTUAL PROFESSIONAL CONNECTION
   - LinkedIn 2nd degree with meaningful relationship
   - Conference/community co-member
   - Moderate conversion

5. ACCELERATOR / PROGRAM CONNECTION
   - YC, Techstars, etc. alumni network
   - Demo Day distribution
   - Moderate-high conversion (within network)

6. ACQUAINTANCE / WEAK TIE
   - Met once at an event
   - Friend of a friend
   - Low-moderate conversion

FINDING INTRODUCERS:
1. Map your network (LinkedIn, CRM, personal contacts)
2. For each target investor, identify 2nd-degree connections
3. Prioritize introducers by relationship strength
4. Ask introducers if they have a genuine relationship
5. Never assume someone can make an intro -- always ask first
```

### The Double Opt-In Introduction

```
DOUBLE OPT-IN PROTOCOL (Gold Standard)

Step 1: ASK THE INTRODUCER
  "Hi [Name], I'm raising a [round size] for [company].
  We're building [one-liner]. I noticed you're connected
  to [Partner Name] at [Firm]. Do you have a real relationship
  with them? If so, would you be comfortable making an intro?"

Step 2: IF YES, PROVIDE THE FORWARDABLE EMAIL
  Give the introducer a self-contained blurb they can forward.
  This blurb should be:
  - 3-4 sentences
  - What you do
  - Key traction point
  - Why this investor specifically
  - Link to one-pager or deck (optional)

Step 3: INTRODUCER FORWARDS TO INVESTOR
  The introducer adds their endorsement:
  "Hey [Partner], I wanted to connect you with [Founder].
  They're building [X] and it's in your wheelhouse.
  [Founder]'s blurb is below. I think it's worth a meeting."

Step 4: INVESTOR OPTS IN
  Investor responds to the intro email.
  You follow up directly to schedule.

THE FORWARDABLE BLURB TEMPLATE:

Subject: Intro to [Company Name] -- [one-liner]

Hi [Introducer],

Thanks for offering to connect me with [Partner Name].
Here's a quick summary they can review:

[Company Name] is [one-liner description].

We've [key traction point: "$X MRR growing Y% MoM" or
"Z thousand users" or "signed [notable customer]"].

We're raising a [$X round type] to [specific milestone].
[Partner Name] is a great fit because [specific reason:
thesis alignment, portfolio synergy, sector expertise].

Happy to share our deck. Looking forward to connecting.

Best,
[Your name]
[LinkedIn / website]
```

## Cold Outreach

### When Cold Outreach Is Appropriate

```
COLD OUTREACH DECISION TREE

Do you have a warm path to this investor?
  ├─ YES → Use warm intro (always preferred)
  └─ NO → Is this a Tier 1 (dream) investor?
        ├─ YES → Invest more time finding a warm path
        │        (ask existing investors, advisors, founders)
        └─ NO → Cold outreach is acceptable for Tier 2-3

COLD OUTREACH CHANNELS (ranked by effectiveness):
1. Direct email to partner (not info@)
2. Twitter/X DM (if partner is active there)
3. LinkedIn message (InMail or direct if connected)
4. Conference approach (in person at events)
5. Application form on firm website (lowest conversion)
```

### Cold Email Framework

```
THE EFFECTIVE COLD EMAIL (4 components, <150 words)

1. HOOK (why you're reaching out to THEM specifically)
   "I saw your recent blog post on [topic] and it aligns
    directly with what we're building."

2. PROOF (why you're worth their time)
   "We're at $80K MRR growing 25% MoM in [their thesis area]."

3. ASK (specific and low-friction)
   "Would you have 20 minutes this week to chat?"

4. CONTEXT (enough to evaluate but not overwhelming)
   "Quick overview: [Company] is [one-liner].
    One-pager attached / happy to share our deck."

COMPLETE EXAMPLE:

Subject: [Company] - $80K MRR SaaS in [Investor's Thesis Area]

Hi [Partner First Name],

I'm [Name], founder of [Company]. I noticed [Firm] recently
invested in [portfolio company] -- we're solving a related
problem for [target customer].

We've grown to $80K MRR (25% MoM) with 15 enterprise customers.
We're raising a $3M seed round to scale our sales team.

Would you have 20 minutes this week? Happy to share our deck
in advance.

Best,
[Name]
[Company] | [website]

RULES:
  - Always personalize (no mass templates)
  - Lead with traction, not vision
  - Keep it under 150 words
  - One clear CTA
  - Send Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM investor's time zone
  - Follow up once after 5 business days, then stop
```

## Follow-Up Cadence

### Post-Meeting Follow-Up

```
FOLLOW-UP TIMELINE (after first meeting)

Same day (within 4 hours):
  - Thank you email
  - Answers to any questions from the meeting
  - Deck attached (if discussed)
  - "Next steps" clearly stated

Day 3-5:
  - Any additional materials requested
  - Data points or metrics discussed
  - Customer references (if appropriate)

Day 7:
  - Check-in: "Any additional questions I can address?"
  - Share relevant news (customer win, metric milestone)

Day 14:
  - If no response: one more follow-up
  - "I wanted to circle back. We've [new development].
     Is this still a fit for [Firm]?"

Day 21+:
  - If still no response: move to "passive" follow-up
  - Add to quarterly update list
  - Move pipeline stage to "stalled" or "passed"

FOLLOW-UP EMAIL TEMPLATE:

Subject: Re: [Company] - Following Up

Hi [Partner],

Thank you for the conversation [yesterday/on Tuesday].
I enjoyed discussing [specific topic from meeting].

As promised, I've attached [deck / data room access / metrics].
To address your question about [topic]: [concise answer].

On our end, the next steps we discussed were:
1. [Action item 1]
2. [Action item 2]

What works for a follow-up conversation [next week]?

Best,
[Name]
```

### The Investor Update as Outreach Tool

```
MONTHLY UPDATES TO PROSPECTIVE INVESTORS

Even investors who haven't committed should receive updates.
This is the highest-leverage long-term outreach strategy.

WHY IT WORKS:
  - Demonstrates transparency and operational rigor
  - Shows consistent progress over time
  - Keeps you top-of-mind without being pushy
  - Investors who said "too early" can see when you're ready
  - Creates social proof (other investors see traction)

UPDATE CONTENT FOR PROSPECTS:
  - Key metrics (revenue, growth, users)
  - Product milestones
  - Team updates
  - Asks (hiring, intros, advice)
  - Keep it brief (5-7 bullet points)
  - Monthly cadence

CONVERSION DATA:
  Per YC and First Round, 15-25% of Series A leads come from
  investors who initially passed at seed but stayed on the
  update list and saw the company's progress.
```

## Reference Calls and Backchannel

### Preparing for Investor Due Diligence on You

```
REFERENCE PREPARATION

Investors will backchannel reference you. Prepare for this:

WHO THEY WILL CALL:
  1. Former colleagues / employers
  2. Current and former customers
  3. Other investors (especially those who passed)
  4. People they know in your industry
  5. Board members / advisors
  6. Former employees (if applicable)

WHAT THEY ASK:
  - "Would you work with/for this person again?"
  - "How do they handle adversity?"
  - "What's their biggest weakness?"
  - "Are they coachable?"
  - "Do they attract talent?"
  - "Do they have integrity?"

PREPARATION STEPS:
  1. Identify who investors might call
  2. Proactively brief your references
  3. Offer curated references (3-5 people)
  4. Warn references that calls may come
  5. Address any known concerns proactively

MANAGING THE BACKCHANNEL:
  You cannot control backchannel fully, but you can:
  - Maintain good relationships (always)
  - Be transparent about past challenges
  - Proactively mention potential concerns before they're found
  - Offer to connect investors with people who know your weaknesses
    (counter-intuitive but builds massive trust)
```

### Your Due Diligence on Investors

```
REVERSE DUE DILIGENCE (Founder → Investor)

Before accepting a term sheet, founders should backchannel:

WHO TO CALL:
  1. Portfolio company founders (current and former)
  2. Co-investors who've worked with this partner
  3. Founders who rejected this investor's term sheet
  4. People who've served on boards with this partner

QUESTIONS TO ASK:
  - "How involved are they between board meetings?"
  - "How do they behave when things are going badly?"
  - "Have they ever tried to replace a CEO?"
  - "Do they follow on in future rounds?"
  - "Do they make helpful introductions?"
  - "How much time do they actually spend with portfolio?"
  - "Would you take their money again?"

RED FLAGS:
  ✗ Portfolio founders reluctant to talk
  ✗ Pattern of CEO replacement
  ✗ Disappeared after investing
  ✗ Adversarial board behavior
  ✗ Slow to fund bridges when needed
  ✗ Reputation for aggressive terms

GREEN FLAGS:
  ✓ Portfolio founders enthusiastic
  ✓ Active between board meetings
  ✓ Helpful with recruiting and BD
  ✓ Supportive during difficult periods
  ✓ Strong follow-on track record
  ✓ Clear value-add beyond capital
```

## Outreach at Scale

### Managing High-Volume Fundraising

```
DAILY WORKFLOW DURING ACTIVE FUNDRAISE

Morning (2 hours):
  - Review pipeline (CRM update)
  - Send follow-up emails (highest priority)
  - Prepare for day's meetings
  - Review new intro requests

Meetings (4-6 hours):
  - 2-4 investor meetings per day
  - 30-60 min each
  - 15 min debrief between (update CRM, notes)

Evening (1-2 hours):
  - Send thank-you emails
  - Update CRM with meeting outcomes
  - Prepare materials for tomorrow
  - Process new intro requests

WEEKLY CADENCE:
  Monday: Pipeline review, strategy adjustment
  Tuesday-Thursday: Maximum meeting days
  Friday: Follow-ups, materials prep, admin
  Weekend: Rest (fundraising is a marathon)

FOUNDER ENERGY MANAGEMENT:
  - Fundraising is emotionally draining
  - Schedule hardest meetings when energy is highest
  - Batch similar tasks (all follow-ups at once)
  - Have a co-founder or team handle operations during raise
  - Set a hard deadline for the process (prevents drift)
```

## Declining Gracefully

### When Investors Want You More Than You Want Them

```
DECLINING AN INVESTOR (while preserving relationship)

If you receive a term sheet you don't want:

"Thank you for the term sheet -- we're genuinely grateful
for your interest in [Company]. After careful consideration,
we've decided to go in a different direction for this round.

We'd love to stay in touch and keep you updated on our
progress. There may be a great fit for a future round."

RULES:
  - Respond within 48 hours (don't ghost)
  - Be honest but diplomatic
  - Don't burn bridges (small world)
  - Keep them on your update list
  - They may be your Series B lead
```

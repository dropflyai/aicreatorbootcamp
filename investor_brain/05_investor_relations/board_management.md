# Board Management -- Composition, Meetings, Materials, and Governance

## Overview

The board of directors is the primary governance mechanism for VC-backed companies.
Effective board management is a strategic skill that separates exceptional founders
from average ones. This module covers board composition, meeting management, materials
preparation, and the art of managing directors as strategic assets.

Primary references: Brad Feld "Startup Boards", Kupor "Secrets of Sand Hill Road"
(board chapters), NVCA governance guidelines, HBS Board Effectiveness research.

---

## Board Composition Strategy

### Board Structure by Stage

```
STAGE-APPROPRIATE BOARD COMPOSITION

PRE-SEED / SEED:
  Most common: No formal board (or informal advisory board)
  If formal: 3 members
    - Founder 1
    - Founder 2 (or advisor)
    - Empty seat (or angel investor)

SERIES A:
  Standard: 5 members
    - 2 Common (founder-designated)
    - 1 Preferred (lead investor)
    - 2 Independent (mutually agreed)
  Alternative: 3 members
    - 1 Common, 1 Preferred, 1 Independent

SERIES B:
  Standard: 5 members
    - 2 Common
    - 2 Preferred (Series A + B leads)
    - 1 Independent

SERIES C+:
  Standard: 5-7 members
    - 2 Common
    - 2-3 Preferred
    - 1-2 Independent
  Note: Board may expand as additional investors take seats

THE MATH OF CONTROL:
  Founder control requires:
    - Majority of common seats
    - Friendly independent directors
    - OR contractual voting agreements

  Investor control occurs when:
    - Preferred seats + aligned independents > common seats
    - This typically happens at Series C+
    - Earlier if founder has weak negotiating position
```

### Selecting Independent Directors

```
INDEPENDENT DIRECTOR CRITERIA

QUALIFICATIONS:
  - Genuinely independent (no financial ties to founders or investors)
  - Relevant industry expertise
  - Board experience (preferably at similar-stage companies)
  - Available for meaningful engagement (not over-boarded)
  - Complementary skills to other board members
  - Reputation for integrity and good judgment

IDEAL PROFILES:
  - Former CEO/founder who scaled a company in your space
  - Industry executive with domain expertise and network
  - Functional expert (CFO for financial discipline, CTO for technical)
  - Someone who has navigated a similar growth stage

WHERE TO FIND INDEPENDENTS:
  - Investor networks (ask your VCs for recommendations)
  - Executive networks (YPO, EO, TechStars mentors)
  - Former executives from portfolio companies
  - Industry conferences and professional associations
  - Board matching services (Bolster, theBoardlist)

COMPENSATION:
  Standard for early-stage:
  - 0.25% - 0.5% equity (vesting over 4 years)
  - No cash compensation (sometimes small annual stipend)
  - D&O insurance coverage
  - Expense reimbursement

RED FLAGS IN INDEPENDENT CANDIDATES:
  ✗ Close personal friend of lead investor
  ✗ Currently suing or being sued by companies
  ✗ On more than 4 other boards (over-committed)
  ✗ No startup experience (big company executive only)
  ✗ Investor's former partner or colleague (not truly independent)
```

## Board Meeting Management

### Meeting Cadence and Structure

```
BOARD MEETING FRAMEWORK

FREQUENCY:
  Series A: Quarterly (4 meetings/year)
  Series B: Quarterly (4 meetings/year)
  Series C+: Quarterly (4-6 meetings/year)
  Special meetings: As needed (M&A, crisis, fundraising)

DURATION:
  Standard: 2-3 hours
  Extended (annual strategy): 4-6 hours
  Emergency: 30-60 minutes

STANDARD AGENDA:

  0:00 - 0:15  Administrative
    - Call to order
    - Approval of prior meeting minutes
    - Conflicts of interest disclosure

  0:15 - 0:45  CEO Report
    - Company overview and strategy update
    - Key wins and challenges
    - Strategic priorities update

  0:45 - 1:15  Financial Review
    - P&L review
    - Cash position and burn rate
    - Budget vs. actual analysis
    - Fundraising status (if applicable)

  1:15 - 1:45  Operational Deep Dive
    - Rotating topic: product, sales, engineering, marketing
    - Key metrics and trends
    - Strategic decisions requiring board input

  1:45 - 2:15  Strategic Discussion
    - One or two strategic topics for board input
    - Market landscape changes
    - Competitive dynamics
    - Growth strategy decisions

  2:15 - 2:30  Board-Only Session (no management)
    - Board members discuss without management present
    - Standard practice; do not take personally
    - Feedback communicated through board chair or lead investor

  2:30 - 2:45  Wrap-Up
    - Action items and owners
    - Next meeting date
    - Closed session feedback (if any)
```

### Board Materials Preparation

```
BOARD DECK STRUCTURE

Send materials 3-5 business days before the meeting.
Board members should arrive prepared, not learning in real-time.

BOARD DECK COMPONENTS:

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (1 slide)
   - Headline: company status in one sentence
   - 3-5 key metrics with trends
   - Top 3 wins, top 3 challenges

2. STRATEGIC UPDATE (2-3 slides)
   - Progress against strategic priorities
   - Market and competitive landscape changes
   - Key strategic decisions ahead

3. FINANCIAL OVERVIEW (3-4 slides)
   - P&L summary (actual vs. budget)
   - Cash flow and runway
   - Revenue waterfall (MRR build)
   - Key financial metrics and trends

4. PRODUCT UPDATE (2-3 slides)
   - Product roadmap progress
   - Key feature launches
   - User/customer feedback
   - Technical debt and infrastructure

5. GO-TO-MARKET UPDATE (2-3 slides)
   - Sales pipeline and conversion
   - Marketing metrics and CAC
   - Customer success and retention
   - Key customer stories

6. TEAM UPDATE (1-2 slides)
   - Headcount and organizational changes
   - Key hires made and open roles
   - Culture and retention metrics
   - Compensation/equity matters (if relevant)

7. DEEP DIVE TOPIC (3-5 slides)
   - Rotating operational deep dive
   - Specific strategic question for board input
   - Options analysis with recommendation

8. APPENDIX
   - Detailed financial model
   - Customer concentration analysis
   - Competitive intelligence
   - Cap table update
   - Any consent items requiring board approval

TOTAL: 15-25 slides
FORMAT: Clear, data-driven, with explicit asks for board input
```

## Managing Individual Directors

### Director Communication

```
BETWEEN-MEETING ENGAGEMENT

1-ON-1 CALLS (monthly or bi-monthly):
  Schedule regular 30-minute calls with each director.
  Purpose:
  - Share updates that need context (not just data)
  - Get advice on specific challenges
  - Build personal relationship
  - Pre-wire board discussion topics

  Agenda for 1-on-1:
  - "Here's what's top of mind for me..."
  - "I'd like your advice on [specific issue]..."
  - "At the next board meeting, I plan to discuss [topic].
     What perspective would be most helpful?"

NO-SURPRISE RULE:
  Board members should NEVER be surprised in a board meeting.
  If there's bad news, share it in 1-on-1 before the meeting.
  "I wanted you to know before the board meeting that..."

LEVERAGING DIRECTOR NETWORKS:
  Be specific about what you need:
  ✗ "Can you help us with hiring?"
  ✓ "We're looking for a VP Sales with experience selling
     $50-100K ACV enterprise software. Do you know anyone
     at [Company A] or [Company B] who might be a fit?"
```

### Managing Difficult Board Dynamics

```
COMMON BOARD CHALLENGES AND RESPONSES

CHALLENGE 1: MICROMANAGING DIRECTOR
  Symptom: Wants to approve every decision, questions
  operational details, requests excessive reporting.

  Response:
  - Set clear boundaries in 1-on-1: "I value your input on
    strategic decisions. For operational matters, I'd like
    to share outcomes rather than seek approval."
  - Increase information sharing (more data = less anxiety)
  - Redirect energy: "Where I could really use your help
    is [strategic area]."

CHALLENGE 2: ABSENT DIRECTOR
  Symptom: Misses meetings, doesn't read materials,
  disengaged between meetings.

  Response:
  - Direct conversation: "I've noticed you've missed the
    last two meetings. Is there something we can adjust?"
  - Offer alternative engagement: shorter meetings, different
    cadence, specific topic involvement.
  - If persistent: discuss board seat transition (with care).

CHALLENGE 3: CONFLICTING DIRECTOR ADVICE
  Symptom: Board members give contradictory guidance.

  Response:
  - Name the conflict: "I'm hearing different perspectives
    from the board on [topic]. Let me lay out the options
    and let's align as a group."
  - Present your recommendation with clear rationale.
  - Use the board meeting as the alignment forum.
  - As CEO, you decide. Board advises (except for
    reserved matters).

CHALLENGE 4: INVESTOR PUSHING FOR SALE
  Symptom: Investor's fund is late in lifecycle,
  pushing for liquidity event prematurely.

  Response:
  - Understand their constraint (fund timeline)
  - Present data on why continuing to build is
    optimal for all shareholders
  - Offer alternatives: secondary sale for their position,
    extension of timeline expectations
  - If persistent: engage independent directors as allies

CHALLENGE 5: BOARD-FOUNDER TRUST BREAKDOWN
  Symptom: Board loses confidence in CEO/founder.

  Response:
  - Address directly and immediately (do not avoid)
  - Ask specific questions: "What would success look like
    to you over the next 6 months?"
  - Propose measurable milestones
  - Engage a CEO coach or executive mentor
  - If trust is truly broken, discuss transition plans
    proactively (better to shape than have it imposed)
```

## Board Governance

### Consent Items and Approvals

```
ITEMS REQUIRING BOARD APPROVAL

Standard board approval items:
  - Annual budget
  - Equity grants (option pool allocation)
  - Executive compensation changes
  - Fundraising authorization
  - Acquisition or merger (above threshold)
  - Significant contracts (above threshold)
  - Related-party transactions
  - D&O insurance renewal
  - 409A valuation approval
  - Intellectual property licensing
  - Litigation decisions

CONSENT BY WRITTEN ACTION:
  Not everything requires a full board meeting.
  Routine matters can be approved via written consent:
  - Option grants to new employees
  - Lease renewals
  - Insurance renewals
  - Minor corporate matters

  Process: Circulate resolution, collect signatures
  (physical or electronic), file with corporate records.

MAINTAINING CORPORATE RECORDS:
  Every board meeting must have:
  - Formal minutes (approved at next meeting)
  - Attendance record
  - Resolutions passed
  - Dissents recorded (if any)

  This is a legal requirement. Failure to maintain records
  creates liability and DD issues in future rounds or exits.
```

### Board Observer Rights

```
MANAGING BOARD OBSERVERS

WHO GETS OBSERVER SEATS:
  - Non-lead investors who didn't get a full seat
  - Strategic investors (corporate VC)
  - Significant angel investors
  - Incoming investors during DD

OBSERVER RIGHTS:
  - Attend all board meetings
  - Receive all board materials
  - Participate in discussions (at chair's discretion)
  - NO voting rights
  - NO fiduciary duties

MANAGING OBSERVER DYNAMICS:
  - Too many observers makes meetings unwieldy
  - Limit to 2-3 observers maximum
  - Reserve right to exclude from sensitive discussions
    (e.g., CEO compensation, related-party transactions)
  - Establish ground rules at first meeting

CONVERTING OBSERVER TO SEAT:
  Observers sometimes push for full board seats at next round.
  Negotiate this explicitly during each fundraise.
  Adding seats makes the board harder to manage.
```

## Annual Board Rhythms

```
ANNUAL BOARD CALENDAR

Q1 MEETING (January/February):
  - Annual review of previous year
  - Annual plan and budget approval
  - Compensation review
  - Board self-assessment
  - 409A valuation review

Q2 MEETING (April/May):
  - Q1 performance review
  - Strategic deep dive (market, product)
  - Fundraising discussion (if applicable)
  - Operational review

Q3 MEETING (July/August):
  - Mid-year review against plan
  - Budget reforecast
  - Strategic pivots or adjustments
  - Competitive landscape update

Q4 MEETING (October/November):
  - Q3 review
  - Next year planning preview
  - Capital planning (fundraising timeline)
  - Board composition review

ANNUAL STRATEGY SESSION (optional, 1 day):
  - Full-day offsite
  - Long-term vision and strategy
  - Market trends and positioning
  - Organizational development
  - Board relationship building
```

**This module provides the framework for effective board management. The board is
the founder's most important governance relationship. Managing it well creates
strategic advantage, access to networks, and organizational discipline. Managing
it poorly creates friction, distraction, and in extreme cases, founder displacement.**

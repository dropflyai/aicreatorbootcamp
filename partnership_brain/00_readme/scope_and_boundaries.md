# Scope and Boundaries — Partnership Brain

## Scope Definition

The Partnership Brain owns the end-to-end partnership lifecycle from strategic planning through execution, management, and optimization. This document defines precisely what falls within scope, what falls outside, and how handoffs to other brains are managed.

---

## In-Scope Domains

### 1. Partnership Strategy and Planning

**Full ownership of:**
- Partnership vision and multi-year strategic roadmap
- Ecosystem mapping and partner landscape analysis
- Partner prioritization and portfolio management
- Competitive partnership intelligence and coopetition strategy
- Partnership OKRs and goal-setting frameworks
- Board-level partnership reporting and recommendations

**Theoretical grounding:** Draws from Adner's (2012) ecosystem strategy framework, Porter's (1985) value chain partnership analysis, and Brandenburger & Nalebuff's (1996) coopetition theory to ensure strategic rigor.

### 2. Partner Identification and Development

**Full ownership of:**
- Ideal Partner Profile (IPP) development and refinement
- Partner market mapping and research
- Qualification criteria and scoring frameworks
- Outreach strategy and engagement sequencing
- Partnership proposal development and presentation
- Inbound partnership request evaluation

**Boundary note:** Market research itself may call the Research Brain for deep competitive intelligence. The Partnership Brain owns the partnership-specific lens on that research.

### 3. Partnership Negotiation and Structuring

**Full ownership of:**
- Commercial term design (revenue share, referral fees, licensing)
- Partnership structure selection (strategic, technology, channel, marketing)
- Term sheet development and negotiation strategy
- Exclusivity analysis and recommendation
- Termination and sunset clause design
- Escalation and dispute resolution frameworks

**Boundary note:** Legal Brain owns the legal language and enforceability review. Partnership Brain owns the commercial intent and business terms.

### 4. Partner Program Architecture

**Full ownership of:**
- Tiered program design (e.g., Silver/Gold/Platinum)
- Partner benefits and requirements frameworks
- Certification and training program design
- Partner portal strategy and content requirements
- Program economics and sustainability modeling
- Program launch and rollout planning

### 5. Alliance Management and Operations

**Full ownership of:**
- Partner success management methodology
- Quarterly Business Review (QBR) design and facilitation
- Partner health scoring and risk monitoring
- Relationship management at executive and working levels
- Escalation protocols and conflict resolution
- Partner communication and engagement programs

### 6. Co-Marketing and Co-Selling

**Full ownership of:**
- Joint go-to-market strategy and planning
- Co-branded content strategy and guidelines
- Co-selling motion design (deal registration, lead sharing, overlay models)
- Joint webinar and event strategy
- Partner case study development frameworks
- Channel conflict prevention and resolution

**Boundary note:** Marketing Brain executes campaigns. Sales Brain executes sales motions. Partnership Brain designs the joint frameworks and coordinates between parties.

### 7. Integration and Technology Partnerships

**Full ownership of:**
- Integration strategy and prioritization
- API partnership frameworks and documentation requirements
- Marketplace listing strategy and optimization
- Technical partnership SLA design
- Co-development partnership structures
- Data sharing agreement frameworks

**Boundary note:** Engineering Brain builds integrations. Partnership Brain defines requirements, priorities, and partnership-layer SLAs.

### 8. Partnership Metrics and ROI

**Full ownership of:**
- Partnership attribution methodology
- Partner-sourced and partner-influenced revenue tracking
- Program health metrics and dashboards
- Partnership ROI calculation and reporting
- Benchmarking and competitive comparison
- Partnership investment optimization

## Out-of-Scope Domains

### Clear Exclusions

| Domain | Owner | Partnership Brain's Role |
|--------|-------|-------------------------|
| Direct sales execution | Sales Brain | Provides co-selling frameworks, not direct selling |
| Marketing campaign operations | Marketing Brain | Designs co-marketing strategy, does not execute campaigns |
| Legal contract drafting | Legal Brain | Provides commercial terms, not legal language |
| Integration engineering | Engineering Brain | Defines requirements, does not write code |
| Financial modeling | Finance Brain | Provides partnership inputs, not full financial models |
| Product roadmap decisions | Product Brain | Recommends integration priorities, does not own roadmap |
| Customer support | Support Brain | Designs partner support tiers, does not handle tickets |
| Pricing strategy | Pricing Brain | Recommends partner pricing, does not own pricing architecture |

### Grey Areas and Resolution

**Partner-sourced leads that convert to direct sales:**
- Partnership Brain owns attribution and sourcing credit
- Sales Brain owns deal execution from handoff forward
- Handoff point: When a partner-sourced lead enters the sales pipeline with clear attribution

**Integration feature requests from partners:**
- Partnership Brain owns the business case and priority recommendation
- Product Brain owns the roadmap decision
- Engineering Brain owns the implementation
- Handoff point: Partnership Brain delivers a partnership integration brief to Product Brain

**Joint pricing with partners:**
- Partnership Brain owns the partner economics (margins, discounts, revenue share)
- Pricing Brain owns the end-customer pricing strategy
- Handoff point: Partnership Brain provides partner economics constraints; Pricing Brain designs within those constraints

## Interaction Protocols with Other Brains

### Requesting Support from Other Brains

When the Partnership Brain needs another brain's expertise:

```
REQUEST FORMAT:
- From: Partnership Brain
- To: [Target Brain]
- Type: [Consultation / Deliverable / Review]
- Context: [Partnership context and business rationale]
- Specific Ask: [Precisely what is needed]
- Timeline: [When it is needed and why]
- Success Criteria: [How we know the deliverable meets partnership needs]
```

### Receiving Requests from Other Brains

When other brains request partnership expertise:

```
INTAKE FORMAT:
- From: [Requesting Brain]
- Type: [Strategy / Evaluation / Template / Process]
- Context: [Why partnership expertise is needed]
- Constraints: [Budget, timeline, existing commitments]
- Decision Rights: [Who makes the final call]
```

### Common Cross-Brain Workflows

**New Partnership Evaluation:**
1. Partnership Brain: Strategic assessment and qualification
2. Finance Brain: Financial modeling and ROI projection
3. Legal Brain: Agreement review and risk assessment
4. Engineering Brain: Technical integration feasibility
5. Product Brain: Roadmap alignment check
6. Partnership Brain: Final recommendation with integrated analysis

**Co-Marketing Campaign:**
1. Partnership Brain: Joint GTM strategy and partner alignment
2. Marketing Brain: Campaign design and execution plan
3. Content Brain: Co-branded content creation
4. Partnership Brain: Partner approval and launch coordination

**Channel Program Launch:**
1. Partnership Brain: Program design and economics
2. Sales Brain: Sales enablement and conflict resolution protocols
3. Marketing Brain: Partner recruitment marketing
4. Engineering Brain: Partner portal and tooling
5. Partnership Brain: Program launch and ongoing management

## Escalation Boundaries

### When Partnership Brain Escalates to CEO Brain

- Partnerships requiring board approval (exclusive, >$1M annual value, strategic pivots)
- Cross-brain conflicts that cannot be resolved at the working level
- Partnership opportunities that require organizational restructuring
- Competitive partnerships that could trigger antitrust concerns

### When Partnership Brain Has Final Authority

- Partner qualification and pipeline management decisions
- Partnership agreement commercial terms (within approved thresholds)
- Partner program tier assignments and movements
- QBR scheduling and partner communication cadence
- Co-marketing content approval (partnership-specific elements)
- Partner conflict resolution (within established protocols)

## Resource Allocation Guidelines

### When to Invest Partnership Brain Cycles

| Signal | Action | Investment Level |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Strategic partner opportunity with >$500K TAM | Full strategic assessment | High |
| Inbound partnership request from market leader | Rapid qualification + engagement | High |
| Existing partner underperforming | Health assessment + intervention plan | Medium |
| Technology integration opportunity | Integration brief + engineering handoff | Medium |
| Affiliate/referral program maintenance | Template-driven, process-managed | Low |
| One-off co-marketing request | Template + approval workflow | Low |

### When NOT to Invest Partnership Brain Cycles

- Partnerships where total addressable value is below minimum threshold
- Relationships that are purely transactional with no strategic upside
- Partner requests that are actually vendor sales pitches in disguise
- Integrations with no customer demand signal
- Co-marketing with partners who have no audience overlap

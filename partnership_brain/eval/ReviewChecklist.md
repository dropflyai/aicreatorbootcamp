# Partnership Review Checklist -- Partnership Gate

This checklist must be completed before any partnership moves from proposal to active status,
and at every quarterly business review for active partnerships.
Every item must be explicitly marked PASS, FAIL, or N/A with justification.
A single FAIL in a Critical section blocks the partnership from advancing.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

1. For NEW partnerships: Complete before signing the partnership agreement
2. For ACTIVE partnerships: Complete at every quarterly business review (QBR)
3. Every item requires an explicit verdict: PASS / FAIL / N/A
4. N/A requires written justification for why the item does not apply
5. Critical items that FAIL block the partnership from advancing
6. Important items that FAIL require a documented remediation plan
7. Advisory items that FAIL are tracked for improvement

---

## SECTION 1: STRATEGIC FIT ASSESSMENT (Critical)

All items in this section must PASS to proceed with the partnership.

### 1.1 Market Alignment

- [ ] Partner's target market overlaps with our ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
- [ ] Partner serves geographies or verticals we want to enter or grow in
- [ ] Partner's customer base size is sufficient to warrant partnership investment
- [ ] Partner's market position is stable or growing (not declining)
- [ ] Partnership does not create dependency on a single market or vertical
- [ ] Competitive landscape is understood -- this partner is not aligned with a direct competitor

### 1.2 Product and Technical Alignment

- [ ] Partner's product or service complements ours (not duplicates)
- [ ] Technical integration is feasible with reasonable effort
- [ ] Combined offering creates value greater than either product alone
- [ ] Data sharing and privacy requirements are compatible
- [ ] Security and compliance postures are compatible
- [ ] Technical roadmaps are compatible for at least the next 12 months

### 1.3 Business Model Alignment

- [ ] Revenue model for the partnership is defined and agreed upon
- [ ] Both parties' incentives are aligned (neither party profits from the other's failure)
- [ ] Pricing model is sustainable for both parties
- [ ] Partnership economics work at scale (not just for initial deals)
- [ ] Exit terms are fair and clearly defined
- [ ] Exclusivity terms (if any) are reasonable and time-bounded

### 1.4 Values and Culture Alignment

- [ ] Partner's public reputation is positive and stable
- [ ] Partner's business practices align with our ethical standards
- [ ] Partner's customer treatment aligns with our standards
- [ ] Working style and communication norms are compatible
- [ ] Decision-making speed and process are compatible
- [ ] Both organizations are committed at the executive level

---

## SECTION 2: DUE DILIGENCE (Critical)

### 2.1 Financial Due Diligence

- [ ] Partner's financial stability verified (public filings, credit check, or references)
- [ ] Partner has adequate resources to invest in the partnership
- [ ] No pending litigation that could affect the partnership
- [ ] Payment terms and financial obligations are clearly defined
- [ ] Insurance and liability coverage is adequate
- [ ] Revenue projections for the partnership are realistic and documented

### 2.2 Legal Due Diligence

- [ ] Partnership agreement reviewed by legal counsel
- [ ] Intellectual property rights are clearly defined
- [ ] Data processing agreement (DPA) in place if data is shared
- [ ] Non-disclosure agreement (NDA) in place
- [ ] Liability and indemnification terms are fair
- [ ] Termination clauses protect both parties
- [ ] Non-compete or exclusivity terms are reasonable
- [ ] Dispute resolution mechanism defined

### 2.3 Technical Due Diligence (for Technology Partners)

- [ ] Partner's technical architecture reviewed and compatible
- [ ] API documentation reviewed and adequate
- [ ] Security practices reviewed and meet minimum standards
- [ ] Uptime and SLA history reviewed
- [ ] Data handling practices reviewed for compliance
- [ ] Technical team capacity assessed for integration support

### 2.4 Reference Checks

- [ ] At least 2 references from partner's other partners contacted
- [ ] References confirm partner is reliable and collaborative
- [ ] References confirm partner delivers on commitments
- [ ] No red flags from reference conversations
- [ ] Reference check findings documented

---

## SECTION 3: COMMERCIAL TERMS (Critical)

### 3.1 Revenue Model

- [ ] Revenue sharing or commission structure defined and documented
- [ ] Revenue attribution model agreed upon
- [ ] Payment terms and schedule defined
- [ ] Minimum commitment or guarantee terms defined (if applicable)
- [ ] Escalation model for growing revenue share or benefits
- [ ] Financial reporting cadence and format agreed upon

### 3.2 Investment Requirements

- [ ] Upfront investment required from each party documented
- [ ] Ongoing investment (headcount, marketing, technical) estimated
- [ ] ROI projections documented with assumptions
- [ ] Break-even timeline estimated
- [ ] Budget allocated and approved on both sides
- [ ] Resource commitment confirmed (people, not just money)

### 3.3 Service Level Agreements

- [ ] Response time SLAs defined for both parties
- [ ] Support escalation path defined
- [ ] Integration uptime SLAs defined (for technology partners)
- [ ] Marketing deliverable SLAs defined (for co-marketing)
- [ ] Lead follow-up SLAs defined (for referral/channel partners)
- [ ] SLA violation consequences defined and fair

### 3.4 Success Metrics

- [ ] Shared KPIs defined and agreed upon
- [ ] Measurement methodology agreed upon
- [ ] Reporting cadence and responsibility defined
- [ ] Success milestones defined for 90 days, 6 months, and 12 months
- [ ] Failure criteria defined (when is the partnership not working?)
- [ ] Review and renewal criteria documented

---

## SECTION 4: OPERATIONAL READINESS (Important)

### 4.1 Partner Onboarding

- [ ] Onboarding plan created with timeline and milestones
- [ ] Training materials available (product, sales, technical)
- [ ] Partner portal or resource center accessible
- [ ] Certification program available (if applicable)
- [ ] Sandbox or test environment available (for technology partners)
- [ ] Onboarding contact assigned from both sides

### 4.2 Sales Enablement (for Channel/Referral Partners)

- [ ] Sales playbook for the partnership created
- [ ] Joint value proposition documented
- [ ] Battle cards and competitive positioning provided
- [ ] Pricing and quoting process defined
- [ ] Demo environment available for partner sales teams
- [ ] Deal registration process defined and understood
- [ ] Conflict resolution process for channel conflict defined

### 4.3 Marketing Enablement

- [ ] Co-branding guidelines provided
- [ ] Marketing asset library available
- [ ] Joint messaging and positioning agreed upon
- [ ] Co-marketing calendar created for the first quarter
- [ ] Press release or public announcement plan agreed upon
- [ ] Social media coordination plan in place

### 4.4 Technical Enablement (for Technology Partners)

- [ ] Integration documentation complete and accurate
- [ ] API access and credentials provisioned
- [ ] Technical support channel established
- [ ] Testing and QA process for integration defined
- [ ] Version compatibility matrix documented
- [ ] Change notification process agreed upon

---

## SECTION 5: GOVERNANCE AND COMMUNICATION (Important)

### 5.1 Governance Structure

- [ ] Executive sponsors identified on both sides
- [ ] Day-to-day partnership owners assigned on both sides
- [ ] Communication cadence agreed upon (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly)
- [ ] Quarterly Business Review (QBR) schedule established
- [ ] Annual strategic planning meeting planned
- [ ] Decision-making authority and escalation path defined

### 5.2 Communication Plan

- [ ] Regular meeting schedule established with agenda template
- [ ] Shared communication channel set up (Slack, Teams, email distribution)
- [ ] Status reporting format and frequency agreed upon
- [ ] Incident communication protocol defined
- [ ] Internal communication plan for both organizations (who knows about the partnership)
- [ ] External communication plan (customers, press, social media)

### 5.3 Relationship Management

- [ ] Relationship map created (key contacts on both sides)
- [ ] Multi-threaded relationships planned (not just single point of contact)
- [ ] Executive engagement plan for first 90 days
- [ ] Partner satisfaction measurement plan in place
- [ ] Feedback mechanism established (how partners raise concerns)
- [ ] Conflict resolution escalation path defined

### 5.4 Program Operations

- [ ] Partner tracked in CRM or PRM (Partner Relationship Management) system
- [ ] Deal registration and tracking process established
- [ ] Lead routing and follow-up process established
- [ ] Revenue tracking and reporting in place
- [ ] Contract renewal and milestone tracking in place
- [ ] Partner compliance monitoring process defined

---

## SECTION 6: RISK MANAGEMENT (Important)

### 6.1 Dependency Risk

- [ ] Partnership does not create critical dependency on the partner
- [ ] Contingency plan exists if the partner is acquired, pivots, or fails
- [ ] Customer impact assessment completed (what happens to joint customers if partnership ends)
- [ ] Data portability considered (can we extract our data if partnership ends?)
- [ ] Revenue concentration risk acceptable (partner is not >25% of total revenue)
- [ ] Technical dependency is manageable (integration can be replaced if needed)

### 6.2 Reputational Risk

- [ ] Partner's reputation vetted (no pending scandals, lawsuits, or controversies)
- [ ] Partner's public communications reviewed for alignment
- [ ] Partner's customer satisfaction levels are acceptable
- [ ] Social media sentiment for the partner is neutral or positive
- [ ] Industry analyst perspective on the partner is neutral or positive
- [ ] Crisis communication plan exists for partnership-related issues

### 6.3 Operational Risk

- [ ] Partner has adequate staffing for the partnership
- [ ] Key person risk is managed (partnership not dependent on one individual)
- [ ] Timezone and language considerations addressed
- [ ] Business continuity plan addresses partnership operations
- [ ] Data breach or security incident response plan includes partner
- [ ] Insurance coverage verified for partnership activities

### 6.4 Market Risk

- [ ] Market conditions support the partnership for the planned duration
- [ ] Regulatory changes that could affect the partnership are monitored
- [ ] Competitive dynamics considered (what if a competitor partners with them?)
- [ ] Technology changes that could make the partnership obsolete are tracked
- [ ] Customer demand for the joint offering validated

---

## SECTION 7: PARTNERSHIP HEALTH CHECK (For Active Partnerships - QBR)

### 7.1 Performance Against Metrics

- [ ] Revenue targets on track (within 20% of plan)
- [ ] Lead generation targets on track
- [ ] Integration usage metrics reviewed (for technology partners)
- [ ] Customer satisfaction with the partnership solution measured
- [ ] Cost of partnership within budget
- [ ] ROI is positive or on trajectory to become positive

### 7.2 Relationship Assessment

- [ ] Partner satisfaction measured (survey or direct feedback)
- [ ] Communication cadence maintained as agreed
- [ ] Issues resolved within SLA timeframes
- [ ] Executive sponsors still engaged
- [ ] No unresolved conflicts or grievances
- [ ] Both teams enjoy working together

### 7.3 Strategic Relevance

- [ ] Partnership still aligns with current company strategy
- [ ] Partner's strategic direction still compatible
- [ ] Market conditions still support the partnership
- [ ] No better alternatives have emerged
- [ ] Growth potential remains for the partnership
- [ ] Both parties want to continue and expand

### 7.4 Improvement Plan

- [ ] Areas for improvement identified and documented
- [ ] Action items from previous QBR completed or on track
- [ ] New initiatives planned for next quarter
- [ ] Escalation items for executive attention identified
- [ ] Resource needs for the next quarter identified
- [ ] Renewal or expansion timeline confirmed

---

## SECTION 8: PARTNERSHIP EXIT CRITERIA (Advisory)

### 8.1 When to Consider Exit

- [ ] Partnership has failed to meet revenue targets for 2+ consecutive quarters
- [ ] Partner activation rate is below 20% after 6 months
- [ ] Strategic alignment has fundamentally shifted
- [ ] Partner's reputation or business practices have deteriorated
- [ ] Cost of partnership exceeds value delivered for 2+ quarters
- [ ] Better alternatives exist and switching cost is manageable

### 8.2 Exit Process

- [ ] Customer transition plan created (no customer left without support)
- [ ] Technical integration wind-down plan created
- [ ] Data migration and deletion plan created
- [ ] Contractual obligations for exit fulfilled
- [ ] Communication plan for customers, teams, and market
- [ ] Post-exit relationship defined (can we still work together informally?)

## ENFORCEMENT RULE

No partnership advances to active status without passing this review.
Critical section failures block the partnership entirely.
Important section failures require remediation plans with deadlines.
Every active partnership is reviewed quarterly.
This checklist is not optional.

# Social Media Advertising Fundamentals

## Purpose

This module provides comprehensive knowledge of paid social media advertising across all major platforms. Organic social media builds relationships; paid social media scales reach and drives measurable business outcomes. A mature social media operation requires both. This module covers campaign objectives, audience targeting, creative best practices, budget allocation, bidding strategies, and cross-platform paid media coordination.

---

## 1. Paid Social Fundamentals

### The Paid Social Value Proposition

Paid social advertising exists to solve three problems organic social cannot:

1. **Scale**: Organic reach is algorithmically limited; paid reach is budget-limited
2. **Targeting precision**: Organic reaches followers and algorithmic recommendations; paid reaches specific demographics, behaviors, and intent signals
3. **Measurement**: Organic attribution is fuzzy; paid social provides clear cost-per-action measurement

### Campaign Objective Framework

Every paid social campaign must begin with a clear objective that maps to a business outcome:

| Objective Category | Campaign Goal | Key Metric | Funnel Stage |
|-------------------|---------------|------------|-------------|
| Awareness | Maximize reach and brand recall | CPM, estimated ad recall lift | Top of funnel |
| Consideration | Drive traffic to website/app | CPC, CTR, landing page views | Middle of funnel |
| Consideration | Video views | CPV, ThruPlay rate, view-through rate | Middle of funnel |
| Consideration | Engagement | CPE, engagement rate | Middle of funnel |
| Consideration | Lead generation | CPL, lead quality score | Middle of funnel |
| Conversion | Website conversions | CPA, ROAS, conversion rate | Bottom of funnel |
| Conversion | Catalog/product sales | ROAS, AOV, transaction volume | Bottom of funnel |
| Conversion | App installs/events | CPI, in-app event cost | Bottom of funnel |

### The Full-Funnel Paid Social Architecture

```
AWARENESS (Top of Funnel)
│ Objective: Reach, Brand Awareness
│ Targeting: Broad interest-based, lookalike audiences
│ Creative: Brand storytelling, thought leadership, entertainment
│ Budget: 20-30% of total paid social
│
├── CONSIDERATION (Middle of Funnel)
│   Objective: Traffic, Video Views, Engagement, Lead Gen
│   Targeting: Engaged audiences, website visitors, video viewers
│   Creative: Educational content, product demos, social proof
│   Budget: 30-40% of total paid social
│
└── CONVERSION (Bottom of Funnel)
    Objective: Conversions, Catalog Sales, App Events
    Targeting: Retargeting (website visitors, cart abandoners, past customers)
    Creative: Product-specific, offers, testimonials, urgency
    Budget: 30-40% of total paid social
```

### Budget Allocation by Funnel Stage

| Business Stage | Awareness | Consideration | Conversion |
|---------------|-----------|---------------|------------|
| New brand (0-6 months) | 50% | 30% | 20% |
| Growing brand (6-18 months) | 30% | 40% | 30% |
| Established brand (18+ months) | 20% | 35% | 45% |
| Product launch | 40% | 35% | 25% |
| Promotional period | 15% | 25% | 60% |

## 2. Audience Targeting

### Targeting Taxonomy

| Targeting Type | Definition | Use Case |
|---------------|-----------|----------|
| **Core/Interest targeting** | Demographics, interests, behaviors from platform data | Prospecting, top of funnel |
| **Custom audiences** | Your own data uploaded to platform (email, phone, website) | Retargeting, upselling |
| **Lookalike/Similar audiences** | Platform finds users similar to your custom audience | Expansion, prospecting |
| **Retargeting** | Users who interacted with your content, website, or app | Middle and bottom funnel |
| **Broad/AI-optimized** | Minimal targeting constraints; algorithm finds best audience | Performance campaigns with sufficient data |

### Custom Audience Types

| Source | Description | Quality | Typical Size |
|--------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Email list | CRM contacts uploaded to platform | Highest (known customers) | Varies |
| Phone number list | Phone contacts uploaded to platform | High (verified contacts) | Varies |
| Website visitors | Pixel/CAPI tracked visitors | High (demonstrated interest) | Depends on traffic |
| App users | SDK tracked app activity | High (active users) | Depends on installs |
| Video viewers | Users who watched your video content | Moderate (engagement signal) | Can be very large |
| Engagement audiences | Users who engaged with your social content | Moderate (interest signal) | Platform-specific |
| Shopping audiences | Users who interacted with shop/catalog | High (purchase intent) | Depends on activity |
| Lead form openers | Users who opened but may not have submitted lead forms | Moderate (consideration signal) | Depends on campaigns |

### Lookalike Audience Strategy

Lookalike audiences find new users who share characteristics with a source audience:

**Source Audience Quality Hierarchy**
1. Purchasers/converters (highest quality signal)
2. High-value customers (LTV-based)
3. Email subscribers who engage
4. Website visitors who viewed product pages
5. Video completers (watched 75%+)
6. Social media engagers
7. All website visitors (lowest quality but largest pool)

**Lookalike Size Selection**

| Size | Reach | Similarity | Use Case |
|------|-------|-----------|----------|
| 1% | Smallest | Most similar | Conversion campaigns, high-value targeting |
| 1-3% | Moderate | High similarity | Balanced performance and scale |
| 3-5% | Large | Moderate similarity | Awareness campaigns, testing |
| 5-10% | Very large | Lower similarity | Broad awareness, maximum reach |

**Best Practice**: Start with 1% lookalike for conversion campaigns; expand to 3-5% if delivery is limited or you need to scale.

### Retargeting Strategy

**Retargeting Windows**

| Audience | Window | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Cart abandoners | 1-3 days | Highest purchase intent, urgency |
| Product page viewers | 7-14 days | Active consideration |
| Blog/content visitors | 14-30 days | Interest demonstrated |
| Homepage visitors | 7-14 days | General interest |
| Past purchasers (upsell) | 30-90 days | Post-purchase engagement |
| Past purchasers (replenishment) | Product-dependent | Usage cycle timing |
| Social engagers | 30-60 days | Brand awareness established |
| Video viewers (75%+) | 14-30 days | Content engagement signal |

**Frequency Capping**
Retargeting without frequency caps leads to ad fatigue and negative brand sentiment:
- Awareness: 3-5 impressions per week
- Consideration: 5-7 impressions per week
- Conversion (retargeting): 7-10 impressions per week
- Maximum before fatigue: 15 impressions per week per user

## 3. Creative Best Practices by Platform

### Meta (Facebook + Instagram)

| Format | Specification | Creative Best Practice |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| Single image | 1080x1080 (1:1) or 1080x1350 (4:5) | Clear focal point, minimal text overlay (<20%), bold color |
| Carousel | 1080x1080 per card, 2-10 cards | Story arc across cards, first card hooks, last card CTAs |
| Video (Feed) | 1:1 or 4:5, <240 min, <4GB | Hook in first 3s, captions mandatory, brand in first 3s |
| Video (Reels) | 9:16, <90s recommended | Native-feel, UGC aesthetic outperforms polished |
| Stories | 1080x1920 (9:16), <120s | Full-screen, interactive elements, swipe-up CTA |
| Collection | Cover image/video + product catalog | Hero creative + shoppable products below |

### LinkedIn

| Format | Specification | Creative Best Practice |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| Single image | 1200x628 or 1080x1080 | Professional, clean, thought-provoking |
| Carousel | 1080x1080 per card, 2-10 cards | Educational frameworks, data visualization |
| Video | 16:9 or 1:1, <10 min | Professional tone, subtitles, value-first |
| Document | PDF upload, multiple pages | Gated content preview, professional design |
| Message ads | Subject + body text | Personalized, value proposition clear, single CTA |
| Conversation ads | Multi-path branching | Decision-tree format, multiple CTAs |

### TikTok

| Format | Specification | Creative Best Practice |
|--------|-------------|----------------------|
| In-feed video | 9:16, 5-60s optimal | Native TikTok aesthetic, not polished ad feel |
| Spark ads | Boost organic content | Proven organic performers, authentic feel |
| TopView | 9:16, 5-60s, auto-play | Brand story, maximum impact creative |
| Branded effect | AR filter/effect | Interactive, shareable, brand-integrated |

### Cross-Platform Creative Principles

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Mobile-first design | All creative designed for mobile viewing (large text, clear visuals, vertical formats) |
| Sound-off optimization | Captions and text overlays on all video (most social video viewed without sound initially) |
| First 3 seconds | Hook must arrest scrolling behavior within 3 seconds |
| Clear value proposition | What is this ad offering the viewer? Must be immediately obvious |
| Single CTA | One clear action per ad (not multiple competing CTAs) |
| Social proof | Reviews, testimonials, user counts, awards in creative when possible |
| Authentic aesthetic | UGC-style and creator-style content outperforms polished corporate creative |
| Creative diversity | Run 3-5 creative variations per ad set to combat fatigue |
| Refresh cadence | New creative every 2-4 weeks to prevent performance decay |

## 4. Budget Allocation

### Budget Framework

**Total Paid Social Budget Determination**

| Method | Calculation | Best For |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Percentage of revenue | 5-15% of revenue allocated to marketing; 20-40% of that to paid social | Established businesses |
| Objective-based | Calculate required spend to achieve target CPA at scale | Performance-focused |
| Competitive parity | Match or exceed competitor estimated spend | Market share defense |
| Test-and-learn | Start with test budget, scale based on ROAS | New channels or markets |

**Platform Budget Allocation**

Allocate across platforms based on audience presence, platform performance, and funnel stage:

| Platform | Strengths | Typical Allocation |
|----------|-----------|-------------------|
| Meta (Facebook + Instagram) | Broadest reach, most sophisticated targeting, best retargeting | 40-60% |
| LinkedIn | B2B targeting, decision-maker access, thought leadership | 15-30% (B2B) |
| TikTok | Younger audiences, creative engagement, low CPMs | 10-25% |
| YouTube | Video awareness, intent targeting, connected TV | 10-20% |
| X/Twitter | Real-time, events, niche communities | 5-10% |
| Pinterest | Purchase intent, visual discovery | 5-15% (e-commerce) |

### Budget Pacing and Optimization

| Pacing Strategy | Description | Use Case |
|----------------|-------------|----------|
| Even pacing | Spend evenly across campaign duration | Brand awareness, always-on |
| Accelerated pacing | Spend budget as quickly as possible | Time-sensitive promotions, launches |
| Front-loaded | Higher spend at beginning, taper off | Product launches, event promotion |
| Back-loaded | Lower spend initially, increase toward end | Seasonal campaigns building to peak |
| Dynamic | Adjust daily based on performance | Performance campaigns with ROAS targets |

## 5. Bidding Strategies

### Bid Strategy Selection

| Strategy | Mechanism | Best For | Risk |
|----------|-----------|----------|------|
| Lowest cost | Platform optimizes for lowest CPA within budget | New campaigns, learning phase | CPA may fluctuate |
| Cost cap | Set maximum CPA target | Mature campaigns with known CPA benchmarks | May underdeliver if cap too low |
| Bid cap | Set maximum bid per auction | Granular control, experienced advertisers | Requires active management |
| ROAS target | Optimize for return on ad spend target | E-commerce with clear revenue tracking | May limit scale |
| Highest value | Optimize for highest conversion value | E-commerce with varied product values | Requires value tracking |

### Learning Phase Management

Meta and most platforms have a "learning phase" where the algorithm optimizes delivery:

| Phase | Status | Behavior |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Learning | <50 conversion events per ad set per week | Performance volatile, CPA fluctuating |
| Active | 50+ conversion events per ad set per week | Stable performance, algorithm optimized |
| Learning limited | Insufficient conversion volume | Consider broader targeting, higher budget, broader conversion event |

**Best Practices for Learning Phase**
- Do not make significant edits during learning phase (resets learning)
- Ensure ad set budget supports 50+ conversions per week
- Use broader conversion events if primary conversion volume is too low
- Consolidate ad sets to concentrate conversion data

## 6. Campaign Structure Best Practices

### Meta Campaign Structure (Simplified Power 5)

```
Campaign (Objective: Conversions)
├── Ad Set 1: Prospecting - Lookalike 1% (Purchasers)
│   ├── Ad 1: Video (UGC style)
│   ├── Ad 2: Carousel (product showcase)
│   └── Ad 3: Image (social proof/testimonial)
│
├── Ad Set 2: Prospecting - Interest-Based
│   ├── Ad 1: Video (UGC style)
│   ├── Ad 2: Carousel (product showcase)
│   └── Ad 3: Image (social proof/testimonial)
│
└── Ad Set 3: Retargeting - Website Visitors 30d
    ├── Ad 1: Testimonial video
    ├── Ad 2: Product carousel with pricing
    └── Ad 3: Offer/discount creative
```

### Campaign Naming Convention

Consistent naming enables reporting and optimization:

```
[Platform]_[Objective]_[Funnel Stage]_[Audience]_[Date]

Example: META_CONV_BOF_RETARGET-WV30D_2025-Q1
         LI_LEADS_MOF_LAL-CUSTOMERS_2025-03
         TT_TRAFFIC_TOF_INTEREST-FITNESS_2025-Q1
```

## 7. Creative Testing Framework

### Testing Methodology

**Variable Isolation**
Test one variable at a time to produce actionable insights:

| Variable | Test Examples |
|----------|-------------|
| Hook | First 3 seconds: question vs. bold statement vs. visual pattern interrupt |
| Format | Video vs. carousel vs. static image for same message |
| CTA | "Shop now" vs. "Learn more" vs. "Get yours" |
| Social proof | With testimonial vs. without |
| Offer | Free shipping vs. 15% off vs. no offer |
| Talent | UGC creator vs. model vs. product-only |
| Length | 15s vs. 30s vs. 60s video |

**Testing Protocol**
1. Hypothesis: "Changing [variable] from [A] to [B] will improve [metric] by [amount]"
2. Create variations isolating only the test variable
3. Run in same ad set with equal budget distribution
4. Minimum 1,000 impressions per variation before evaluating
5. Statistical significance threshold: 95% confidence
6. Document winner and rationale in creative knowledge base
7. Apply learning to future creative production

### Creative Fatigue Detection

| Indicator | Threshold | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| CTR declining | >20% drop from peak | Introduce new creative variations |
| CPM increasing | >15% increase from average | Refresh creative, check frequency |
| Frequency >3 | Same user seeing ad 3+ times in 7 days | Add new creative, expand audience |
| Conversion rate declining | >15% drop from average | Test new creative angles, offers |
| Negative feedback increasing | Rising hide/report rates | Pause creative, review messaging |

## 8. Cross-Platform Paid Social Coordination

### Unified Reporting Framework

| Metric | Meta | LinkedIn | TikTok | YouTube | Standardize To |
|--------|------|----------|--------|---------|---------------|
| Impressions | Impressions | Impressions | Impressions | Impressions | Impressions |
| Clicks | Link clicks | Clicks | Clicks | Clicks | Clicks (standardize type) |
| Engagement | Post engagement | Social actions | Engagement | Engagements | Engagement rate |
| Video views | ThruPlay (15s) | Video views (2s) | Video views (6s) | Views (30s) | Normalize by threshold |
| Conversions | Custom conversions | Lead gen conversions | Pixel conversions | Conversion actions | Standardize events |
| Cost metrics | CPM, CPC, CPA | CPM, CPC, CPL | CPM, CPC, CPA | CPV, CPC, CPA | Compare CPA apples-to-apples |

### Attribution Considerations

| Model | Description | Platform Default |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Last click | Credit to last ad clicked before conversion | Google default |
| 7-day click, 1-day view | Credit to ad clicked within 7 days or viewed within 1 day | Meta default |
| 30-day click | Credit to ad clicked within 30 days | LinkedIn default |
| Data-driven | ML model distributes credit across touchpoints | Available on Meta, Google |

**Attribution Alignment**
- Use consistent attribution windows across platforms for valid comparison
- Implement UTM parameters on all ad links for GA4 cross-platform analysis
- Triangulate platform-reported conversions with GA4 and CRM data
- Accept that no attribution model is perfectly accurate; use directional guidance

## 9. Paid Social Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Consequence | Correction |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Boosting every post | Wasting budget on low-performing content | Only boost top organic performers |
| Over-narrow targeting | Insufficient audience for algorithm optimization | Minimum 500K-1M audience for prospecting |
| No creative rotation | Ad fatigue, declining performance | 3-5 creatives per ad set, refresh monthly |
| Ignoring learning phase | Poor optimization, volatile results | Allow 50+ conversions before major edits |
| Platform-reported ROAS only | Inflated attribution, overconfidence | Cross-reference with GA4 and CRM data |
| Set-and-forget campaigns | Performance decay without optimization | Weekly review, monthly strategy adjustment |
| Bottom-funnel only | Audience depletion, rising CPAs | Full-funnel approach with awareness investment |
| Identical creative everywhere | Platform mismatch, poor performance | Platform-native creative adaptation |

---

*Paid social advertising is a discipline of systematic testing, data-driven optimization, and creative innovation. The best advertisers treat every dollar as a learning investment and every campaign as an experiment.*

# Social Media Brain -- Review Checklist

Version: 1.0
Last Updated: 2026-02-03
Owner: Social Media Brain
Status: ACTIVE

---

## Purpose

This checklist is the definitive quality gate for all social media operations.
No campaign launches, no content publishes, no strategy shifts occur without
passing the relevant sections of this checklist. Skipping items is not
permitted. If an item does not apply, it must be explicitly marked "N/A" with
a written justification.

This is not a suggestion list. It is a mandatory review protocol.

---

## SECTION 1: Strategy Review (Quarterly)

### 1.1 Audience Definition

- [ ] Target personas documented with demographics, psychographics, and platform behavior
- [ ] Persona validation: survey or interview data from last 6 months supports personas
- [ ] Persona-platform mapping: each persona assigned to primary and secondary platforms
- [ ] Competitor audience overlap analysis completed
- [ ] Negative personas defined (who we are NOT targeting)
- [ ] Audience size estimates per platform documented with sources
- [ ] ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) alignment verified with Sales and Marketing brains

### 1.2 Platform Strategy

- [ ] Active platforms justified with audience data (not assumptions)
- [ ] Platforms explicitly deprioritized or exited are documented with rationale
- [ ] Each platform has a unique content strategy (not copy-paste)
- [ ] Platform-specific KPIs defined and differ by platform
- [ ] New platform evaluation criteria documented (when to adopt, when to ignore)
- [ ] Platform algorithm changes tracked and strategy adjusted within 14 days
- [ ] Resource allocation per platform documented (hours, budget, personnel)

### 1.3 Content Strategy

- [ ] Content pillars defined (minimum 3, maximum 6)
- [ ] Pillar distribution targets set (no pillar >40%, promotional <20%)
- [ ] Content calendar exists for minimum 2 weeks ahead
- [ ] Content themes aligned with business objectives and seasonal events
- [ ] User-generated content (UGC) strategy defined
- [ ] Content repurposing workflow documented (long-form to short-form pipeline)
- [ ] Evergreen vs timely content ratio defined
- [ ] Content approval workflow documented with turnaround time SLAs

### 1.4 Competitive Analysis

- [ ] Top 5 competitors identified per platform
- [ ] Competitor content audit completed (last 90 days minimum)
- [ ] Share of voice tracked monthly
- [ ] Competitor strategy changes logged and assessed
- [ ] Differentiation strategy documented (what we do that they do not)
- [ ] Competitor response protocol defined (how to handle competitor mentions)

### 1.5 Goal Alignment

- [ ] Social media goals ladder up to marketing goals
- [ ] Marketing goals ladder up to business goals
- [ ] Goal cascade documented and visible to team
- [ ] OKRs or KPIs defined per platform per quarter
- [ ] Attribution model agreed upon with marketing leadership
- [ ] Reporting cadence and format agreed upon with stakeholders

## SECTION 2: Content Production Review (Per Piece)

### 2.1 Pre-Production

- [ ] Content brief exists with objective, audience, platform, and CTA
- [ ] Brief approved by strategy lead before production begins
- [ ] Reference materials gathered (competitor examples, trending formats)
- [ ] Brand guidelines consulted (voice, tone, visual identity)
- [ ] Legal/compliance review required? If yes, initiated.
- [ ] Collaboration requirements identified (influencer, partner, customer)

### 2.2 Copy Review

- [ ] Hook is compelling (first line or first 3 seconds tested against alternatives)
- [ ] Copy matches platform tone (LinkedIn professional, TikTok casual, etc.)
- [ ] Character/length limits respected per platform
- [ ] Call to action is clear, specific, and singular (one CTA per post)
- [ ] Hashtags researched and relevant (not stuffed, not generic)
- [ ] Hashtag count within platform best practice (Instagram: 5-15, LinkedIn: 3-5)
- [ ] Spelling and grammar verified (automated tool + human review)
- [ ] No broken links or incorrect URLs
- [ ] Mentions and tags verified (correct handles, permission obtained)
- [ ] Emoji usage appropriate to brand voice and platform norms
- [ ] Accessibility: alt text written for all images
- [ ] Accessibility: captions/subtitles included for all video
- [ ] Localization: content appropriate for target geographic audience

### 2.3 Visual/Creative Review

- [ ] Visual matches brand guidelines (colors, fonts, logo placement)
- [ ] Image/video resolution meets platform requirements
- [ ] Aspect ratio correct for placement (feed, stories, reels)
- [ ] Text overlay readable on mobile (minimum 24pt equivalent)
- [ ] Thumbnail selected (not auto-generated) for video content
- [ ] Faces visible in thumbnail where applicable (higher CTR)
- [ ] No copyrighted music, images, or footage without license
- [ ] Watermarks from other platforms removed
- [ ] Safe zone compliance (no critical elements in crop zones)
- [ ] Dark mode tested (content readable in both light and dark mode)
- [ ] Color contrast meets WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 for text)

### 2.4 Technical Review

- [ ] UTM parameters appended to all outbound links
- [ ] UTM naming convention follows established taxonomy
- [ ] Tracking pixels firing correctly on landing pages
- [ ] Link destination tested and loads in <3 seconds
- [ ] Landing page matches content promise (no bait and switch)
- [ ] Platform-specific features utilized (polls, stickers, shops, etc.)
- [ ] Scheduling tool configured correctly (time zone, date, platform)
- [ ] Cross-posting settings verified (no accidental duplicate publishing)

## SECTION 3: Campaign Launch Review

### 3.1 Pre-Launch

- [ ] Campaign brief signed off by stakeholders
- [ ] Campaign goals quantified (specific numbers, not "increase awareness")
- [ ] Budget allocated and approved
- [ ] Timeline documented with milestones
- [ ] Roles and responsibilities assigned (who creates, who approves, who monitors)
- [ ] Paid and organic strategies aligned (organic supports paid, paid amplifies organic)
- [ ] Influencer contracts signed (if applicable)
- [ ] Creative assets produced for all planned placements
- [ ] A/B test plan documented (what variables, what success criteria)
- [ ] Measurement plan documented (what metrics, what tools, what cadence)
- [ ] Contingency plan exists (what if campaign underperforms at day 3?)

### 3.2 Paid Campaign Specifics

- [ ] Targeting parameters documented and reviewed
- [ ] Audience exclusions set (existing customers, employees, competitors)
- [ ] Frequency caps configured
- [ ] Budget pacing set (daily vs lifetime, accelerated vs standard)
- [ ] Bid strategy selected with rationale
- [ ] Creative variants minimum 3 per ad set
- [ ] Ad copy variants minimum 2 per creative
- [ ] Landing page A/B test running simultaneously
- [ ] Conversion tracking verified (test conversion fired)
- [ ] Negative keywords set (where applicable)
- [ ] Placement exclusions set (remove low-quality placements)
- [ ] Brand safety settings configured (content adjacency controls)
- [ ] Dayparting configured based on audience activity data
- [ ] Retargeting audiences built and active

### 3.3 Launch Day Protocol

- [ ] All scheduled posts verified (content, time, platform)
- [ ] Paid campaigns activated and confirmed serving
- [ ] Community management team briefed on campaign and FAQ
- [ ] Real-time monitoring dashboard active
- [ ] Escalation contacts confirmed available
- [ ] First-hour engagement plan active (team engages with early comments)

## SECTION 4: Ongoing Operations Review (Weekly)

### 4.1 Performance Monitoring

- [ ] Daily metrics reviewed against benchmarks
- [ ] Anomalies investigated within 24 hours
- [ ] Top-performing content analyzed (why did it work?)
- [ ] Bottom-performing content analyzed (why did it fail?)
- [ ] Competitor activity reviewed
- [ ] Trending topics/formats assessed for relevance
- [ ] Community sentiment pulse check completed
- [ ] DM backlog at zero or within SLA

### 4.2 Community Management

- [ ] All comments responded to within SLA (4 hours for questions, 24 hours for general)
- [ ] Negative comments triaged (legitimate complaint vs troll)
- [ ] Legitimate complaints escalated to support team
- [ ] Brand mentions monitored and engaged where appropriate
- [ ] UGC identified and permission requested for repurposing
- [ ] Community members recognized and appreciated
- [ ] Spam and bot comments removed
- [ ] Block/mute list updated

### 4.3 Content Calendar Maintenance

- [ ] Next 2 weeks of content scheduled or in production
- [ ] Content gaps identified and assigned
- [ ] Real-time/reactive content opportunities assessed daily
- [ ] Content performance data feeding back into calendar decisions
- [ ] Seasonal and cultural events cross-referenced
- [ ] Cross-functional content needs integrated (product launches, HR hiring, events)

## SECTION 5: Crisis Management Review

### 5.1 Preparedness (Monthly Verification)

- [ ] Crisis playbook accessible to all team members
- [ ] Escalation chain verified (phone numbers current, alternates listed)
- [ ] Holding statements reviewed and updated for relevance
- [ ] Monitoring keywords updated to reflect current risk landscape
- [ ] Social listening tools configured for crisis detection
- [ ] Legal team response time confirmed (<30 minutes for Tier 1)
- [ ] Post-crisis communication templates ready
- [ ] Team trained on crisis protocol within last 6 months

### 5.2 Active Crisis Checklist

- [ ] Crisis identified and classified by tier (1-5)
- [ ] Appropriate stakeholders notified per escalation chain
- [ ] All scheduled content paused pending review
- [ ] Holding statement deployed within response time SLA
- [ ] Single source of truth designated (one person controls messaging)
- [ ] All team members instructed to route inquiries to designated spokesperson
- [ ] Monitoring frequency increased to real-time
- [ ] Response log initiated (all actions timestamped)
- [ ] Cross-channel consistency verified (same message everywhere)
- [ ] Legal review obtained before substantive public statements
- [ ] Customer support team briefed and aligned
- [ ] Internal communications sent to broader organization

### 5.3 Post-Crisis Review

- [ ] After-action review scheduled within 72 hours
- [ ] Timeline of events documented
- [ ] Response effectiveness assessed (time, tone, outcome)
- [ ] Lessons learned documented
- [ ] Playbook updated with new learnings
- [ ] Stakeholder debrief completed
- [ ] Recovery content strategy developed
- [ ] Brand sentiment monitoring intensified for 30 days post-crisis

## SECTION 6: Influencer Partnership Review

### 6.1 Vetting

- [ ] Influencer audience demographics match brand target
- [ ] Audience authenticity verified (bot check completed)
- [ ] Content quality and consistency assessed (last 50 posts minimum)
- [ ] Brand alignment verified (values, tone, aesthetic)
- [ ] Controversy check completed (last 24 months of public activity)
- [ ] Previous brand partnerships reviewed for quality and disclosure compliance
- [ ] Engagement rate verified against tier benchmarks
- [ ] Rate card or pricing assessed against expected ROI

### 6.2 Contract and Compliance

- [ ] Contract includes deliverables, timeline, and payment terms
- [ ] Content approval workflow specified in contract
- [ ] FTC disclosure requirements specified (platform-specific guidance provided)
- [ ] Usage rights defined (duration, channels, modifications)
- [ ] Exclusivity clauses defined (competitors, category)
- [ ] Termination clauses included (brand safety, non-performance)
- [ ] Performance bonuses or penalties specified where appropriate

### 6.3 Campaign Execution

- [ ] Brief provided to influencer with clear objectives and guidelines
- [ ] Creative freedom balanced with brand guardrails
- [ ] Content reviewed and approved before publishing
- [ ] Tracking links and promo codes provided and tested
- [ ] Publishing schedule aligned with brand content calendar
- [ ] Real-time monitoring active during campaign
- [ ] Performance data collected and shared with influencer

### 6.4 Post-Campaign

- [ ] Performance report generated within 7 days of campaign end
- [ ] ROI calculated against investment
- [ ] Influencer feedback collected (process improvement)
- [ ] Relationship assessment: continue, modify, or end partnership
- [ ] Content assets archived with usage rights documentation
- [ ] Learnings documented for future influencer selection

---

## SECTION 7: Reporting and Analytics Review

### 7.1 Data Integrity

- [ ] All tracking pixels and UTM parameters firing correctly
- [ ] Platform analytics cross-referenced with Google Analytics (or equivalent)
- [ ] Discrepancies >10% investigated and documented
- [ ] Attribution model consistently applied across reports
- [ ] Historical data preserved and accessible for trend analysis
- [ ] Data retention policy followed (platform export schedule maintained)

### 7.2 Report Quality

- [ ] Reports include context, not just numbers (why, not just what)
- [ ] Benchmarks included for comparison (previous period, competitors, industry)
- [ ] Actionable recommendations included in every report
- [ ] Visualizations clear and accurate (no misleading scales or cherry-picked dates)
- [ ] Executive summary included for leadership reports
- [ ] Report delivered on schedule per agreed cadence

---

## SECTION 8: Brand Safety and Compliance

### 8.1 Ongoing Compliance

- [ ] All paid content compliant with platform advertising policies
- [ ] FTC disclosure guidelines followed on all sponsored content
- [ ] GDPR/CCPA compliance maintained for any data collection via social
- [ ] Industry-specific regulations reviewed (healthcare, finance, alcohol, etc.)
- [ ] Copyright and licensing verified for all creative assets
- [ ] Employee social media policy current and communicated
- [ ] Third-party tool access audited quarterly (who has admin access)

### 8.2 Brand Voice Consistency

- [ ] Brand voice guide referenced for all content creation
- [ ] Tone adaptation per platform documented (formal on LinkedIn, casual on TikTok)
- [ ] New team members trained on voice guide within first week
- [ ] Voice consistency audit conducted quarterly
- [ ] Customer-facing responses follow approved tone guidelines
- [ ] Automated responses (chatbots, auto-replies) reviewed monthly for tone

## Checklist Governance

### Mandatory Reviews

| Review Type | Frequency | Reviewer | Sign-off Required |
|------------|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| Strategy Review | Quarterly | Social Lead + Marketing Director | Yes |
| Content Review | Per piece | Content Creator + Reviewer | Yes |
| Campaign Launch | Per campaign | Social Lead + Stakeholders | Yes |
| Operations Review | Weekly | Social Team | No (but logged) |
| Crisis Preparedness | Monthly | Social Lead + PR | Yes |
| Influencer Vetting | Per partnership | Social Lead + Legal | Yes |
| Compliance Audit | Quarterly | Social Lead + Legal | Yes |

### Exceptions Process

Any checklist item bypass requires:
1. Written justification from the team lead
2. Approval from the next-level manager
3. Documentation in the exception log with expected risk
4. Review of exception outcome within 14 days

### Checklist Updates

This checklist is reviewed and updated quarterly. Proposed changes require:
- Evidence-based justification (data, incident, or industry change)
- Review by Social Media Brain lead
- Approval from Marketing leadership
- Version-controlled update with changelog

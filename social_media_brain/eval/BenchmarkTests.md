Last Updated: 2026-02-03

## Purpose

These benchmark tests evaluate the Social Media Brain's ability to handle
real-world social media scenarios ranging from routine optimization to crisis
management. Each test presents a specific situation, provides context data,
and demands a structured, actionable response. Passing requires demonstrating
both strategic thinking and tactical execution capability.

Tests are scored on a 10-point scale per the SocialScore framework.
Minimum passing score: 7.0. Below 7.0 triggers remediation.

---

## Scoring Criteria (Applied to Every Test)

| Criterion | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Problem Diagnosis | 25% | Accurate identification of root cause, not just symptoms |
| Strategic Response | 25% | Coherent strategy aligned with business objectives |
| Tactical Execution | 20% | Specific, actionable steps with owners and timelines |
| Measurement Plan | 15% | Clear KPIs and success criteria for proposed solution |
| Risk Awareness | 15% | Anticipation of what could go wrong and mitigation |

## TEST 1: Viral Negative Brand Association

**Severity: CRITICAL**
**Time Pressure: 2 hours**

### Scenario

A post from your brand's Instagram account goes viral (2M impressions in 3
hours) but for the wrong reasons. A product placement image inadvertently
includes a background element that users are interpreting as offensive. The
hashtag #BrandNameFail is trending with 15K mentions and growing. Screenshots
are spreading to X/Twitter and TikTok. Three news outlets have DM'd requesting
comment. Your CEO is in a board meeting.

### Context Data

- Original post engagement: 45K likes, 8K comments (95% negative), 12K shares
- Brand follower count: 850K on Instagram
- Normal daily mention volume: 200. Current: 15K and accelerating.
- No crisis playbook was tested in the last 12 months
- Two team members are available. Social media manager is on PTO.

### Required Response

1. Immediate actions (first 30 minutes)
2. Stakeholder communication plan
3. Public response strategy (platform-specific)
4. Media response framework
5. 48-hour recovery plan
6. Post-crisis analysis framework
7. Preventive measures to avoid recurrence

### Evaluation Criteria

- Response initiated within simulated 30-minute window
- Single spokesperson designated
- Scheduled content paused
- Response tone appropriate (not defensive, not dismissive)
- Media response does not create secondary crisis
- Recovery plan addresses both reputation and audience retention

## TEST 2: Organic Reach Collapse on Instagram

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 2 weeks to diagnose and pivot**

### Scenario

Organic reach on your brand's Instagram account has dropped 50% over the past
3 months. Your follower count has remained stable at 120K, but average reach
per post has declined from 18K to 9K. Engagement rate has dropped from 3.2%
to 1.8%. Stories views have declined 35%. The content strategy has not changed.
Your manager is asking for an explanation and action plan by Friday.

### Context Data

- Post frequency: 5 feed posts/week, 3 Reels/week, daily Stories
- Content mix: 40% educational carousels, 30% product photos, 20% Reels, 10% Stories
- Hashtag strategy: same 30 hashtags on every post for 6 months
- Last algorithm update: Instagram shifted Reels priority 2 months ago
- Competitor accounts in your niche show stable or growing reach
- Follower growth: +150/month (was +800/month 6 months ago)
- No paid promotion in last 90 days

### Required Response

1. Root cause analysis (minimum 5 potential factors ranked by likelihood)
2. Algorithm audit findings
3. Content audit findings with specific underperforming categories
4. Revised content strategy with platform-specific adjustments
5. Hashtag strategy overhaul
6. 30/60/90 day recovery plan with milestones
7. Metrics to track recovery and decision points for further pivots

## TEST 3: Cross-Platform Product Launch

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 3-week planning window**

### Scenario

Your company is launching a new product in 3 weeks. You have a $10K social
media budget (paid + influencer). You need to design a cross-platform content
strategy that maximizes awareness and drives pre-orders. The product is a
B2C consumer tech gadget priced at $79. Target audience is 25-40 year olds
interested in productivity and smart home tech.

### Context Data

- Active platforms: Instagram (120K followers), TikTok (45K), X/Twitter (30K), YouTube (8K)
- Past product launch conversion rate from social: 0.8%
- Average order value: $79
- Customer acquisition cost target: <$25
- Influencer relationships: 3 micro-influencers (20-50K followers) interested
- Creative team capacity: 2 designers, 1 videographer, 1 copywriter
- Organic content takes 48 hours from brief to publish

### Required Response

1. Budget allocation across platforms and paid vs organic (show math)
2. Content calendar for 3-week launch period
3. Platform-specific content strategy (format, tone, frequency)
4. Influencer activation plan with deliverables and timeline
5. Paid campaign structure (audiences, placements, bid strategy)
6. Pre-order funnel from social to conversion
7. Real-time optimization plan (what to adjust and when)
8. Success metrics with specific targets

## TEST 5: TikTok Trend Hijacking Decision

**Severity: MEDIUM**
**Time Pressure: 6 hours (trend lifecycle)**

### Scenario

A TikTok trend is blowing up that could be adapted for your brand. The trend
involves a sound clip and a visual format that is getting millions of views.
Your brand is a B2B SaaS company targeting mid-market CFOs. The trend is
humorous and slightly edgy but not offensive. Your TikTok account has 12K
followers and your last 10 posts averaged 2K views each.

### Context Data

- Trend has been active for 18 hours (likely 24-48 hours of relevance remaining)
- Top brand examples using the trend: fast food chains and fashion brands
- No B2B brands have used it yet
- Your internal approval process takes 24 hours minimum
- Your video team can produce a rough cut in 3 hours
- Your compliance team requires 4 hours for review

### Required Response

1. Go/no-go decision framework (criteria for when to jump on a trend)
2. If go: concept, script, and production plan within time constraints
3. If go: approval process acceleration strategy
4. If no-go: documentation of decision rationale
5. Alternative approach to capturing trend momentum without direct participation
6. General trend response protocol for future speed

## TEST 9: Competitor Social Takedown Attempt

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 48 hours**

### Scenario

A direct competitor has launched an aggressive social campaign that directly
names your product and makes comparison claims that are partially misleading.
Their campaign includes paid ads running on Instagram and YouTube, influencer
posts, and a comparison landing page. The campaign is generating significant
engagement and their sales team is reportedly using the content in pitches.

### Context Data

- Competitor has 2x your social following
- Their campaign budget appears to be $30-50K based on ad library data
- 3 of the comparison claims are misleading, 2 are accurate criticisms of your product
- Your legal team says a cease and desist would take 2-3 weeks and is uncertain
- Your social audience is asking you to respond

### Required Response

1. Response strategy decision tree (respond, ignore, or escalate)
2. If responding: tone, format, and channel strategy
3. Content plan that addresses accurate criticisms constructively
4. Legal coordination plan for misleading claims
5. Community management script for audience inquiries
6. Proactive campaign to reinforce brand strengths
7. Competitive monitoring escalation for the next 90 days

## TEST 13: UGC Campaign That Backfires

**Severity: HIGH**
**Time Pressure: 12 hours**

### Scenario

You launched a branded hashtag campaign encouraging customers to share how they
use your product. The hashtag is gaining traction, but 40% of posts using it
are sarcastic, mocking, or sharing negative experiences. Some of the negative
posts are going viral. The campaign has been live for 3 days and was planned
for 14 days.

### Context Data

- Hashtag volume: 2,500 posts total
- Positive posts: 800 (32%)
- Negative/sarcastic posts: 1,000 (40%)
- Neutral/unrelated: 700 (28%)
- Paid amplification of hashtag: $3K spent, $7K remaining budget
- Several industry publications have noticed the backfire

### Required Response

1. Continue, pause, or kill decision (with framework for deciding)
2. If continuing: course-correction strategy
3. If killing: graceful exit plan that does not draw more attention
4. Paid amplification adjustment
5. Negative post response strategy (engage, ignore, or report)
6. Media inquiry response
7. Positive UGC amplification plan (boost the good)
8. Post-mortem framework and preventive criteria for future UGC campaigns

## Test Administration Protocol

### Scheduling

- Full benchmark suite administered quarterly
- Minimum 4 tests per monthly spot-check (rotating selection)
- Crisis tests (1, 4, 9, 13) administered bi-annually as tabletop exercises
- New team members complete full suite within first 30 days

### Scoring

- Each test scored on 10-point scale using weighted criteria
- Composite benchmark score = average across all completed tests
- Individual test scores below 5.0 trigger immediate remediation
- Composite score below 7.0 triggers strategy review

### Remediation Protocol

| Score Range | Required Action | Timeline |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| 8.0-10.0 | Document approach for team playbook | 7 days |
| 6.0-7.9 | Identify gaps, create improvement plan | 14 days |
| 4.0-5.9 | Supervised remediation with mentor review | 21 days |
| <4.0 | Full retraining on failed domain | 30 days |

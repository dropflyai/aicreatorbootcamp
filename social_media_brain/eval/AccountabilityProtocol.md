# Social Media Brain -- Accountability Protocol

Version: 1.0
Last Updated: 2026-02-03
Owner: Social Media Brain
Status: ACTIVE

---

## Purpose

This protocol defines who is accountable for what, when accountability is
assessed, what happens when standards are not met, and how the Social Media
Brain maintains operational integrity. Accountability without consequences
is suggestion. This document ensures consequences exist, are proportional,
and are enforced consistently.

---

## Core Accountability Principles

1. **Every metric has an owner.** No metric exists without a named person responsible.
2. **Every failure has a root cause analysis.** "It didn't work" is not analysis.
3. **Every miss has a documented recovery plan.** Missing a target without a plan is negligence.
4. **Accountability is forward-looking.** The goal is not punishment. It is correction and prevention.
5. **Escalation is mandatory, not optional.** Hiding a miss is worse than the miss itself.

## SECTION 2: Escalation Framework

### Escalation Tiers

| Tier | Trigger | Who Escalates | Who Receives | Response Time |
|------|---------|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| 1 - Advisory | Metric misses target by <15% | Role owner | Social Lead | 48 hours |
| 2 - Warning | Metric misses target by 15-30% | Social Lead | Marketing Director | 24 hours |
| 3 - Intervention | Metric misses target by >30% | Marketing Director | VP Marketing | 12 hours |
| 4 - Crisis | Brand safety incident or public crisis | Anyone (no hierarchy gate) | Social Lead + PR + Legal | 30 minutes |
| 5 - Executive | Sustained underperformance (3+ months) | Marketing Director | C-suite | 7 days |

### Escalation Documentation Requirements

Every escalation must include:

1. **What happened:** Factual description of the miss or incident
2. **When it started:** Date the deviation was first detected
3. **Impact assessment:** Quantified business impact (revenue, reputation, reach)
4. **Root cause analysis:** Why it happened (not just what happened)
5. **Recovery plan:** Specific actions, owners, timelines
6. **Prevention plan:** What changes to prevent recurrence
7. **Resources needed:** Any additional budget, tools, or personnel required

### Escalation Anti-Patterns (Prohibited)

- Waiting for someone else to notice a miss before escalating
- Escalating without a proposed recovery plan
- Escalating to skip levels (Tier 1 goes to Social Lead, not VP)
- Using escalation as blame assignment rather than problem-solving
- Delaying escalation hoping the metric will self-correct

## SECTION 3: Performance Review Cadence

### Daily Accountability

**Who:** Entire social team
**Format:** 15-minute standup (async acceptable if distributed team)
**Required content:**
- Yesterday's key metrics vs targets
- Any anomalies detected
- Today's priorities and potential blockers
- Crisis monitoring status (green/yellow/red)

### Weekly Accountability

**Who:** Social Lead + direct reports
**Format:** 45-minute review meeting
**Required content:**
- Weekly scorecard review (all dimensions from SocialScore)
- Content performance analysis (top 3 and bottom 3 posts)
- Campaign performance updates
- Community sentiment pulse
- Competitor activity summary
- Upcoming content and campaign preview
- Resource and blocker discussion

**Output:** Written summary distributed within 24 hours.

### Monthly Accountability

**Who:** Social Lead + Marketing Director
**Format:** 60-minute strategic review
**Required content:**
- Full SocialScore composite (all 8 dimensions)
- Month-over-month trend analysis
- Budget vs actual spend
- Goal progress (quarterly OKRs)
- Team performance summary
- Strategic adjustments proposed
- Resource requests

**Output:** Monthly report archived. Action items tracked to completion.

### Quarterly Accountability

**Who:** Social Lead + Marketing Director + VP Marketing
**Format:** 90-minute strategic review
**Required content:**
- Quarterly SocialScore trend
- OKR results (met, partially met, missed with explanation)
- Competitive landscape analysis
- Platform strategy assessment (add, maintain, sunset decisions)
- Budget proposal for next quarter
- Team development plans
- Innovation initiatives and results
- Benchmark test results

**Output:** Quarterly strategy document. Budget allocation decision. OKRs for next quarter.

## SECTION 4: Failure Response Protocol

### Classification of Failures

| Category | Definition | Example |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Process Failure | Established process not followed | Checklist skipped, approval bypassed |
| Execution Failure | Process followed but outcome missed | Campaign launched correctly but underperformed |
| Strategic Failure | Strategy was wrong despite good execution | Content pillar mix was misaligned with audience |
| External Failure | Factors outside team control | Platform algorithm change, competitor action |
| Judgment Failure | Poor decision with available information | Approved controversial content despite red flags |

### Response by Failure Category

**Process Failure:**
1. Document the skip and its impact
2. Identify why process was bypassed (unclear, inconvenient, time pressure)
3. Fix the process barrier, not just the person
4. Retrain if knowledge gap; restructure if process gap
5. Second process failure in same area within 90 days triggers formal warning

**Execution Failure:**
1. Root cause analysis within 5 business days
2. Identify whether the miss was foreseeable with available data
3. Adjust tactics or resources as needed
4. Document lessons learned
5. Execution failures are learning opportunities, not disciplinary events (unless repeated)

**Strategic Failure:**
1. Full strategy review within 14 days
2. Identify what signals were missed or misinterpreted
3. Adjust strategy with explicit hypothesis for new direction
4. Increase measurement cadence during pivot period
5. Share learning across team to prevent similar strategic errors

**External Failure:**
1. Document the external factor and its impact
2. Assess whether the team could have anticipated or mitigated
3. Adjust strategy for new reality
4. Update risk register with new external risk factor
5. No individual accountability unless response to external event was inadequate

**Judgment Failure:**
1. Immediate review by Social Lead and Marketing Director
2. Assess decision-making process (what information was available, what was ignored)
3. Determine if judgment failure was isolated or pattern
4. Implement additional review gates for similar decisions
5. Repeated judgment failures trigger role reassessment

## SECTION 5: Accountability Metrics Dashboard

### Required Dashboard Components

Every team member must have access to a real-time dashboard showing:

1. **Personal metrics vs targets** (green/yellow/red status)
2. **Team composite SocialScore** (current and 90-day trend)
3. **SLA compliance** (response times, publishing cadence)
4. **Campaign performance** (active campaigns vs targets)
5. **Escalation status** (open escalations and their stage)
6. **Calendar compliance** (scheduled vs published content)

### Dashboard Review Requirements

- Team members review personal dashboard daily
- Social Lead reviews team dashboard daily
- Marketing Director reviews summary dashboard weekly
- Dashboard data must not be more than 4 hours stale
- Any dashboard showing RED status for >24 hours without an escalation is itself an escalation trigger

## SECTION 6: Continuous Improvement Protocol

### Retrospective Cadence

| Retrospective | Frequency | Scope | Duration |
|--------------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Post-campaign | After every campaign >$1K spend | Single campaign | 30 min |
| Post-crisis | After every crisis event | Single incident | 60 min |
| Monthly | Last week of each month | All operations | 45 min |
| Quarterly | End of quarter | Strategy and process | 90 min |

### Retrospective Format (Mandatory)

1. **What worked:** Specific actions that drove positive outcomes
2. **What did not work:** Specific actions that underperformed or failed
3. **What we learned:** Insights that change future behavior
4. **What we will change:** Concrete actions with owners and deadlines
5. **What we need:** Resources, tools, or support required

### Learning Documentation

All retrospective outputs must be:
- Documented in the Social Media Brain's knowledge base
- Tagged by topic (crisis, content, paid, influencer, platform, etc.)
- Searchable by the team
- Reviewed quarterly for pattern identification
- Used to update the Review Checklist and BenchmarkTests

### Improvement Velocity Tracking

Track the number of process improvements implemented per quarter:
- Target: Minimum 3 meaningful improvements per quarter
- Each improvement must have a measurable impact metric
- Improvements that fail to show impact within 60 days are reversed or modified

## SECTION 7: Cross-Brain Accountability

### Dependencies on Other Brains

| Dependency | Brain | Accountability |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| Visual assets and brand guidelines | Design Brain | SLA: 48-hour turnaround on asset requests |
| Product information for campaigns | Product Brain | SLA: 24-hour response to product queries |
| Legal review on campaigns | Legal Brain | SLA: 4-hour review for active campaigns |
| Blog content for social distribution | Content Brain | SLA: 48 hours before social publish date |
| Conversion data for attribution | Analytics Brain | SLA: Weekly data sync |
| Crisis coordination | PR / Communications | SLA: 30-minute response for Tier 1 crises |

### Accountability for Cross-Brain Failures

When a dependency failure impacts Social Media Brain performance:
1. Document the dependency failure and its impact on social metrics
2. Notify the dependent brain's lead within 24 hours
3. Propose a mitigation plan that does not require the dependency (if possible)
4. Escalate to CEO Brain if dependency failure persists beyond 7 days
5. Social Media Brain is not penalized for documented, escalated dependency failures
6. Social Media Brain IS penalized for not escalating dependency failures promptly

## SECTION 8: Accountability Governance

### Protocol Review

This accountability protocol is reviewed quarterly. Changes require:
- Evidence-based justification (data showing current protocol inadequacy)
- Approval from Social Lead and Marketing Director
- 14-day notice to team before changes take effect
- Version-controlled update with changelog

### Dispute Resolution

If a team member disagrees with an accountability assessment:
1. Written objection submitted within 5 business days of assessment
2. Review by Social Lead (or Marketing Director if Social Lead issued assessment)
3. Decision rendered within 5 business days of objection
4. Decision is final unless new evidence emerges
5. No retaliation for good-faith objections

### Protocol Violations

Failure to follow this accountability protocol is itself an accountability failure:
- First violation: Documented reminder with acknowledgment
- Second violation: Formal discussion with Social Lead and Marketing Director
- Third violation: Performance improvement plan
- Pattern of protocol avoidance indicates cultural misalignment, not just process failure

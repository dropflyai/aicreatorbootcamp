# Meta Platforms Strategy

## Purpose

This module provides deep operational knowledge for Meta's platform ecosystem: Instagram (Reels, Stories, Feed, Carousel), Facebook (Groups, Pages, Marketplace), Threads, and the unified Meta Business Suite infrastructure. Meta remains the largest social media company by total reach and advertising revenue. Mastery of its ecosystem is non-negotiable for any serious social media operation.

## 1. Instagram: Format-Specific Strategy

### Instagram Reels

**Algorithm Mechanics**
Instagram Reels operates on a hybrid social-graph and interest-graph model. Unlike the main feed, Reels are distributed significantly to non-followers through the Reels tab and Explore page.

**Ranking Signals (Weighted)**
1. **Watch completion rate**: The single strongest signal. Videos watched to completion (or rewatched) receive exponential distribution advantage
2. **Engagement velocity**: Likes, comments, shares, and saves in the first 30-60 minutes
3. **Shares**: Particularly to DMs and Stories. Shares are weighted more heavily than likes
4. **Saves**: High-intent signal indicating content has lasting value
5. **Audio usage**: Using trending audio provides discovery advantage through the audio page
6. **Originality**: Instagram's algorithm deprioritizes content with visible watermarks from other platforms (TikTok logo) and reposts of existing content

**Reels Best Practices**
- **Duration**: 15-30 seconds optimal for completion rate; 60-90 seconds for educational content
- **Hook**: First 0.5-1 second must arrest scrolling. Text overlay hooks outperform caption-only
- **Aspect ratio**: 9:16 full-screen vertical, mandatory
- **Text placement**: Keep within safe zones (avoid top 15% for UI overlay and bottom 20% for caption)
- **Audio**: Trending sounds for discovery; original audio for branded content and repurposing
- **Caption**: First line must hook; use line breaks; include CTA; hashtags in caption (3-5 relevant)
- **Cover image**: Custom cover for profile grid aesthetic consistency
- **Cross-posting**: Reels can be shared to Stories and Feed for additional distribution

**Content Frameworks for Reels**

| Framework | Structure | Best For |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Hook-Value-CTA | Attention grab → Deliver insight → Clear action | Educational, tips |
| Before/After | Show transformation | Product demos, tutorials |
| POV format | "POV: [relatable scenario]" | Humor, relatability |
| List format | "3 things about X" with text overlay | Quick value delivery |
| Trend adaptation | Brand take on trending format/sound | Discovery, cultural relevance |
| Day in the life | Behind-the-scenes narrative | Authenticity, parasocial bond |
| Tutorial | Step-by-step demonstration | How-to, product education |

### Instagram Stories

**Algorithm and Visibility**
Stories appear in the Stories tray ordered by relationship strength (interaction history) rather than chronological posting. Accounts with frequent DM exchanges, profile visits, and content interactions appear first.

**Story Strategy**

| Story Type | Purpose | Frequency |
|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Daily updates | Maintain presence, relationship building | Daily |
| Interactive stickers | Engagement boost (polls, questions, quizzes, sliders) | 3-5x/week |
| Behind-the-scenes | Authenticity, team personality | 2-3x/week |
| User-generated content | Social proof, community appreciation | When available |
| Product showcases | Soft selling with swipe-up/link sticker | 2-3x/week max |
| Story series | Multi-frame narrative or educational sequence | 1-2x/week |
| Countdown stickers | Event/launch hype building | As needed |

**Interactive Sticker Strategy**
- **Polls**: Binary choices drive easy engagement; use for audience research and content preferences
- **Questions**: Open-ended prompts for community voice; use responses as future content ideas
- **Quizzes**: Educational engagement; test audience knowledge on brand-relevant topics
- **Sliders**: Sentiment measurement; emotionally engaging
- **Link stickers**: Available to all accounts; track click-through with UTM parameters

**Story Highlights Strategy**
- Organize Highlights as a mini-website: About, Products/Services, Testimonials, FAQ, Culture
- Custom cover images aligned with brand visual identity
- Keep Highlights updated; remove outdated content quarterly
- Highlight naming: short, clear, emoji-optional (based on brand voice)

### Instagram Feed Posts

**Feed Algorithm (2024-2026)**
The feed algorithm combines chronological signals with engagement prediction:
- Content type preference (based on individual user's historical engagement patterns)
- Relationship strength between viewer and creator
- Post engagement velocity in the first 30-60 minutes
- Content format (carousels and Reels prioritized over single images)

**Carousel Strategy**
Carousels are Instagram's highest-performing feed format for engagement and saves:

| Carousel Element | Best Practice |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Slide count | 7-10 slides optimal (drives swipe-through, increases dwell time) |
| First slide | Hook slide: bold text, curiosity gap, value promise |
| Middle slides | One idea per slide, consistent visual template |
| Last slide | CTA slide: follow, save, share, comment, or visit link |
| Design consistency | Branded template with consistent fonts, colors, layout |
| Text density | Readable at mobile scale; 20-40 words per slide maximum |
| Aspect ratio | 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (portrait) for maximum feed real estate |

**Caption Strategy**
- First line is critical (visible before "more" truncation): hook or key insight
- Long captions (150-300 words) outperform short captions for engagement
- Structure with line breaks and formatting for readability
- End with a question or CTA to drive comments
- Hashtags: 3-5 relevant hashtags in caption body or first comment

## 2. Facebook Strategy

### Facebook Pages

**Organic Reach Reality**
Facebook Page organic reach has declined to approximately 2-5% of followers. This is a structural reality, not a temporary algorithm shift. Strategy must account for this limitation:

- Prioritize content types that still receive organic distribution (video, Reels, Groups content)
- Use paid amplification for high-performing organic content
- Shift community engagement from Pages to Groups
- Leverage employee advocacy to extend organic reach through personal profiles

**Content Format Performance (Ranked)**
1. Facebook Reels (highest organic reach)
2. Native video (uploaded directly, not linked from YouTube)
3. Photo posts with engaging captions
4. Link posts (lowest organic reach, but necessary for traffic driving)
5. Text-only posts (variable, can perform well for engagement)

### Facebook Groups

**Strategic Value**
Facebook Groups represent Meta's highest-engagement product. They provide:
- Direct community access without algorithmic suppression
- Notification delivery for new posts
- Community-driven content creation (members post, not just brand)
- Relationship building through meaningful interaction
- Customer support and peer-to-peer help

**Group Strategy Framework**

| Phase | Actions | Timeline |
|-------|---------|----------|
| Setup | Define purpose, create group rules, design visual identity | Week 1 |
| Seeding | Invite initial members (employees, loyal customers, beta testers) | Week 1-2 |
| Culture building | Daily moderated discussions, welcome rituals, member spotlights | Month 1-3 |
| Scaling | Organic growth through member invitations, cross-promotion | Month 3-6 |
| Sustaining | Member-led content, structured programming (weekly threads, events) | Ongoing |
| Monetizing | Exclusive content, early access, community-driven product development | Month 6+ |

**Group Moderation Best Practices**
- Establish clear rules (pinned post) covering acceptable behavior, promotional content, and conflict resolution
- Respond to membership requests within 24 hours
- Use screening questions to filter spam and ensure member quality
- Assign trusted members as moderators as community scales
- Schedule recurring engagement posts (weekly prompts, Monday motivation, Friday wins)
- Remove spam and off-topic content immediately; enforce rules consistently

### Facebook Marketplace

**Brand Relevance**
Facebook Marketplace generates significant local commerce activity. For brands with physical products or local services:
- Product listings with direct purchase capability
- Local discovery for brick-and-mortar businesses
- Inventory syndication from e-commerce catalog
- Lead generation for service-based businesses

## 3. Threads Strategy

### Platform Positioning

Threads occupies the text-based public conversation space, positioned as Meta's answer to X/Twitter. Its tight Instagram integration provides unique advantages:

- **Account migration**: Instagram followers receive notification when you join Threads
- **Cross-posting**: Content can be simultaneously shared to Instagram
- **Verification**: Instagram verified status carries over to Threads
- **Audience overlap**: Threads audience closely mirrors Instagram audience

### Content Strategy for Threads

**What Works on Threads (2024-2026)**
- Conversational, authentic text posts
- Hot takes and industry opinions
- Behind-the-scenes moments in text form
- Questions that spark discussion
- Real-time commentary on industry events
- Threaded (multi-post) narratives
- Repurposed tweet-style content

**What Does Not Work on Threads**
- Heavily promotional content (deprioritized algorithmically)
- Cross-posted content without platform adaptation
- Content that requires visual context without attached media
- Overly formal corporate messaging

### Algorithm Considerations

Threads uses a recommendation-based feed similar to TikTok's interest graph:
- Content is distributed based on topic relevance and predicted engagement
- Following feed available but algorithmic feed is default
- Engagement on posts (replies, reposts, likes) drives expanded distribution
- Posting consistency matters; the algorithm favors regular contributors

## 4. Meta Business Suite

### Unified Management Platform

Meta Business Suite is the central management hub for Facebook Pages, Instagram accounts, and Threads:

**Core Capabilities**
- Cross-platform content scheduling and publishing
- Unified inbox for messages and comments across platforms
- Performance analytics and audience insights
- Ad creation and management (integrated with Ads Manager)
- Catalog management for commerce features
- Team access management and role assignment

### Content Scheduling Best Practices

| Feature | Best Practice |
|---------|---------------|
| Scheduling window | Schedule 1-2 weeks ahead; leave room for real-time content |
| Optimal times | Use platform-specific audience active hours from Insights |
| Draft management | Use drafts for content in review/approval workflows |
| Cross-platform posting | Customize captions and hashtags per platform; never identical |
| Story scheduling | Available but Stories should maintain spontaneous feel |
| Reel scheduling | Schedule Reels with custom cover images and captions |

### Meta Pixel and Conversions API (CAPI)

**Pixel Implementation**
The Meta Pixel tracks website visitor behavior for:
- Retargeting (showing ads to website visitors on Meta platforms)
- Conversion tracking (measuring actions taken after seeing ads)
- Lookalike audience creation (finding new users similar to converters)
- Attribution (connecting ad exposure to business outcomes)

**Conversions API (CAPI)**
Server-side event tracking that supplements pixel data:
- Browser-independent tracking (unaffected by cookie blocking)
- iOS 14.5+ compliance (server-side data not subject to ATT)
- Event match quality scoring (deduplicate with pixel events)
- Enhanced measurement accuracy for conversion optimization

### Meta Advertising Infrastructure

**Campaign Objectives**

| Objective | Use Case | Key Metric |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| Awareness | Brand awareness, reach | CPM, estimated ad recall lift |
| Traffic | Website visits, landing page views | CPC, landing page views |
| Engagement | Post engagement, page likes, event responses | CPE, engagement rate |
| Leads | Lead form submissions | CPL, lead quality |
| App promotion | App installs, in-app events | CPI, ROAS |
| Sales | Conversions, catalog sales | CPA, ROAS |

**Audience Targeting**

| Targeting Type | Description | Application |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|
| Core audiences | Demographics, interests, behaviors | Prospecting |
| Custom audiences | Website visitors, email lists, app users, engagement | Retargeting |
| Lookalike audiences | Users similar to custom audience source | Expansion |
| Advantage+ audience | AI-optimized broad targeting | Performance campaigns |

### Meta Advantage+ Campaigns

Meta's AI-driven campaign automation (Advantage+) is increasingly the default:
- **Advantage+ shopping campaigns**: Automated e-commerce campaigns with AI-optimized targeting, placements, and creative
- **Advantage+ creative**: AI-powered creative optimization (text variations, image enhancements)
- **Advantage+ audience**: Broader targeting with AI optimization replacing manual audience selection
- **Advantage+ placements**: Cross-platform placement optimization across Facebook, Instagram, Audience Network, Messenger

**When to Use Advantage+ vs. Manual**
- Advantage+: High-volume conversions, e-commerce, established pixel data, broad audience
- Manual: Niche audiences, brand awareness, creative testing, early-stage campaigns with limited data

## 5. Meta Platform Integration Strategy

### Cross-Platform Content Flow

```
Long-form content (blog, YouTube) → Instagram Carousel (educational summary)
                                    → Instagram Reel (key takeaway, hook)
                                    → Facebook Group post (discussion prompt)
                                    → Threads post (hot take or key insight)
                                    → Instagram Story (behind-the-scenes of creation)
```

### Unified Community Strategy

| Platform | Community Role |
|----------|---------------|
| Instagram | Discovery, visual brand building, Reels for reach |
| Facebook Group | Deep community, peer-to-peer engagement, loyalty |
| Threads | Real-time conversation, thought leadership, text engagement |
| Messenger/Instagram DMs | Customer service, personal connection, sales support |

### Meta-Specific Metrics to Track

| Metric | Platform | Significance |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Reel plays + shares | Instagram | Algorithm amplification signal |
| Story completion rate | Instagram | Audience retention indicator |
| Group active members | Facebook | Community health metric |
| Threads replies | Threads | Conversation quality indicator |
| Message response time | Messenger/IG DMs | Customer service quality |
| Pixel event match quality | Cross-platform | Attribution accuracy |
| Frequency cap adherence | Ads | Ad fatigue prevention |

## 6. Meta Platform Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Consequence | Correction |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Cross-posting identical content | Low engagement, algorithm penalty | Platform-native content adaptation |
| Ignoring Reels | Missing largest organic reach surface | Minimum 3 Reels/week |
| Relying on Page organic reach | Reaching only 2-5% of audience | Groups + paid amplification |
| TikTok watermark on Reels | Algorithm deprioritization | Remove watermarks or use original |
| Over-reliance on link posts | Lowest organic distribution | Native content with link in comments/bio |
| Neglecting DMs and comments | Damaged relationship signals | Respond within 4 hours during business hours |
| Ignoring Threads | Missing early-mover advantage | Establish presence, post 3-5x/week |

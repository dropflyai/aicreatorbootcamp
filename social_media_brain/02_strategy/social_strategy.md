# Social Media Strategy Framework

## Purpose

This module provides the comprehensive strategic framework for developing, executing, and iterating on social media strategy. Strategy precedes tactics; every post, campaign, and platform investment must trace back to a documented strategic rationale. Ad hoc social media activity without strategic grounding is resource waste.

---

## 1. The Social Media Strategy Framework

### Seven-Phase Strategic Process

```
AUDIT → GOALS → AUDIENCE → PLATFORMS → CONTENT → EXECUTION → MEASUREMENT
  ↑                                                                    │
  └────────────────────── Continuous Loop ─────────────────────────────┘
```

Each phase produces a documented deliverable. No phase may be skipped. Strategy is revisited quarterly at minimum.

---

## 2. Phase 1: Social Media Audit

### Current State Assessment

Before defining strategy, conduct a comprehensive audit of existing social media presence:

**Account Inventory**

| Audit Element | Details to Capture |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Active accounts | Platform, handle, access credentials, owner |
| Dormant accounts | Accounts not posted to in 90+ days |
| Unauthorized accounts | Fan pages, former employee accounts, impersonators |
| Connected integrations | Scheduling tools, analytics, pixel/tag connections |
| Content archive | Volume, formats, themes from past 12 months |

**Performance Baseline**

For each active platform, document trailing 90-day metrics:

- Follower count and growth rate (absolute and percentage)
- Average engagement rate per post (by content type)
- Reach and impressions (organic vs. paid)
- Top-performing content (top 10 by engagement rate)
- Worst-performing content (bottom 10 by engagement rate)
- Audience demographics (age, gender, location, active hours)
- Profile visit to follow conversion rate
- Click-through rates to website/landing pages

**Competitive Audit**

Analyze 3-5 direct competitors and 2-3 aspirational brands:

- Platform presence and follower counts
- Posting frequency and content mix
- Engagement rates (benchmark against industry averages)
- Content themes and messaging patterns
- Audience overlap and differentiation
- Share of voice in relevant conversations

**SWOT Analysis**

Synthesize audit findings into a social media SWOT:

| Strengths | Weaknesses |
|-----------|-----------|
| What platforms/content types perform well? | Where is performance below benchmark? |
| What unique content capabilities exist? | What resource gaps limit execution? |

| Opportunities | Threats |
|--------------|---------|
| What platforms or formats are underexplored? | What competitor actions threaten position? |
| What audience segments are underserved? | What platform changes could impact strategy? |

## 3. Phase 2: Goal Setting

### Goal Hierarchy

Social media goals must cascade from business objectives:

```
Business Objective
  └→ Marketing Objective
       └→ Social Media Goal
            └→ Platform-Specific KPIs
                 └→ Tactical Metrics
```

### SMART Goal Framework for Social Media

Every goal must be Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound:

| Goal Category | Example SMART Goal |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Awareness | Increase Instagram reach by 40% from 50K to 70K average monthly reach by Q2 2025 |
| Engagement | Improve LinkedIn engagement rate from 2.1% to 3.5% within 6 months |
| Traffic | Drive 5,000 monthly website sessions from social media by end of Q3, up from 2,200 |
| Lead generation | Generate 200 qualified leads per month through LinkedIn and Meta lead forms |
| Sales/Revenue | Achieve $50K monthly attributed revenue through social commerce by Q4 |
| Community | Grow Discord server to 5,000 active members with 30% weekly active rate |
| Customer service | Reduce average social media response time from 4 hours to 1 hour within 90 days |

### Goal Prioritization Matrix

Not all goals are equal. Prioritize using impact vs. effort analysis:

| Priority | Goal Type | Typical Timeline |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| P0 (Critical) | Revenue, crisis readiness | Immediate |
| P1 (High) | Lead generation, community growth | 1-3 months |
| P2 (Medium) | Awareness, engagement improvement | 3-6 months |
| P3 (Low) | Experimental platforms, emerging formats | 6-12 months |

## 4. Phase 3: Audience Definition

### Audience Research Methodology

Audience understanding is the foundation of all content and platform decisions. Research inputs include:

1. **First-party data**: CRM data, website analytics, existing follower demographics
2. **Platform analytics**: Native insights from each social platform
3. **Social listening**: Conversation analysis around brand, category, and competitors
4. **Customer research**: Interviews, surveys, support ticket analysis
5. **Third-party research**: Industry reports (Pew Research, eMarketer, Statista)

### Social Media Persona Development

Develop 3-5 social media personas distinct from general marketing personas. Social personas emphasize:

| Persona Element | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Platform behavior | Which platforms they use, when, and how |
| Content preferences | Formats they engage with, topics they care about |
| Social identity | How they present themselves online |
| Motivation | What gratification they seek from social media |
| Pain points | What frustrates them on social platforms |
| Influence circle | Who they follow, trust, and share content from |
| Purchase journey | How social media influences their buying decisions |

### Community Identification

Beyond individual personas, identify communities where target audiences congregate:

- **Hashtag communities**: Active hashtags that define group membership
- **Platform communities**: Relevant subreddits, Facebook Groups, Discord servers
- **Creator audiences**: Influencers and creators whose audiences overlap with target
- **Event communities**: Conferences, webinars, live events with social presence
- **Professional communities**: Industry groups, associations, alumni networks

| Resource fit | 10% | ? | ? | ? | ? | ? | ? |
| **Weighted Score** | | ? | ? | ? | ? | ? | ? |

### Platform Tiering

Based on weighted scores, categorize platforms:

- **Tier 1 (Primary)**: Maximum investment, daily activity, full content calendar. Typically 1-2 platforms
- **Tier 2 (Secondary)**: Moderate investment, 3-5x weekly activity, repurposed content with platform adaptation. Typically 1-2 platforms
- **Tier 3 (Experimental)**: Minimal investment, testing new formats and audiences. 1-2x weekly. Typically 1 platform
- **Tier 4 (Monitoring)**: No active posting, social listening and competitor monitoring only

### Organic vs. Paid Strategy by Platform

| Strategy | When to Use | Budget Allocation |
|----------|------------|-------------------|
| Organic-first | Strong content capabilities, niche audiences, thought leadership | 70% content creation, 30% paid amplification |
| Paid-first | Mature platforms with low organic reach, direct response goals | 30% content creation, 70% paid media |
| Hybrid | Established presence with growth ambitions | 50% content, 50% paid |
| Community-first | Discord, Reddit, niche platforms | 80% community management, 20% content |

## 6. Phase 5: Content Strategy

### Content Pillar Framework

Content pillars are 3-5 thematic categories that structure all social content. Pillars must:

1. Align with brand positioning and expertise
2. Address audience needs and gratifications
3. Be sustainable over months/years (not trend-dependent)
4. Differentiate from competitor content
5. Support business goals (awareness, trust, conversion)

**Example Content Pillar Structure**

| Pillar | Purpose | Content Mix % | Formats |
|--------|---------|---------------|---------|
| Industry Expertise | Thought leadership, trust building | 30% | Carousels, threads, long-form video |
| Behind the Scenes | Authenticity, parasocial bond | 20% | Stories, Reels, casual video |
| Customer Stories | Social proof, community | 20% | UGC, testimonials, case studies |
| Culture and Values | Brand identity, recruitment | 15% | Employee content, values-driven posts |
| Product/Service | Direct value communication | 15% | Demos, tutorials, launches |

### Content Mix Rules

**The 80/20 Rule**
- 80% value-first content (educate, entertain, inspire, connect)
- 20% promotional content (product, services, offers, CTAs)

**The 4-1-1 Rule (Alternative)**
- 4 pieces of curated or educational content
- 1 piece of soft promotion (behind the scenes, culture)
- 1 piece of direct promotion (product, CTA)

### Governance Framework

Social media governance prevents brand risk and ensures consistency:

| Governance Element | Documentation Required |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| Voice and tone guidelines | Brand voice document with platform-specific adaptations |
| Approval workflow | Who approves what content types, escalation paths |
| Legal review triggers | Claims, testimonials, regulated content, partnerships |
| Crisis protocol | When to pause posting, who to notify, response templates |
| Employee social media policy | What employees can/cannot share about the company |
| Community guidelines | Moderation rules, response protocols, ban criteria |
| Content archiving | Retention requirements for regulated industries |

## 8. Phase 7: Measurement Framework

### Metric Hierarchy

```
Business Outcomes (Revenue, Leads, Retention)
     ↑ attributed to
Social Media KPIs (Traffic, Conversions, SOV)
     ↑ driven by
Platform Metrics (Reach, Engagement, Followers)
     ↑ produced by
Content Metrics (Views, Likes, Shares, Saves, Comments)
```

### Core Metrics by Objective

| Objective | Primary Metric | Secondary Metrics |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Awareness | Reach | Impressions, follower growth, share of voice |
| Engagement | Engagement rate | Comments, shares, saves, DMs |
| Traffic | Click-through rate | Sessions, bounce rate, pages per session |
| Lead gen | Cost per lead | Lead quality score, SQL conversion rate |
| Sales | ROAS | Revenue attributed, AOV from social, conversion rate |
| Community | Active member rate | Retention, contribution rate, NPS |
| Service | Response time | Resolution rate, CSAT from social |

### Reporting Cadence

| Report | Frequency | Audience | Content |
|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Performance dashboard | Real-time/daily | Social team | Platform metrics, alerts |
| Weekly report | Weekly | Marketing team | Content performance, trends, engagement |
| Monthly report | Monthly | Marketing leadership | KPI tracking, competitive analysis, recommendations |
| Quarterly business review | Quarterly | Executive team | Business impact, ROI, strategic recommendations |
| Annual strategy review | Annually | Executive + stakeholders | Year in review, next year strategy |

### Continuous Optimization Loop

```
Measure → Analyze → Hypothesize → Test → Learn → Optimize → Measure
```

Every measurement cycle should produce:
1. **What worked**: Content and tactics that exceeded benchmarks
2. **What did not work**: Underperformers and contributing factors
3. **Why**: Hypothesis for performance drivers
4. **So what**: Strategic implications and adjustments
5. **Now what**: Specific tactical changes for next period

## 9. Strategy Documentation

### Required Strategy Deliverables

A complete social media strategy document includes:

1. **Executive summary**: One-page overview for leadership
2. **Situation analysis**: Audit findings, competitive landscape, market context
3. **Goals and KPIs**: SMART goals with measurable KPIs per platform
4. **Audience profiles**: Social media personas with platform behaviors
5. **Platform strategy**: Selected platforms with tiering and rationale
6. **Content strategy**: Pillars, mix, formats, governance
7. **Paid media strategy**: Budget allocation, targeting approach, creative strategy
8. **Influencer strategy**: Partnership approach, budget, selection criteria
9. **Community strategy**: Community building approach by platform
10. **Crisis preparedness**: Monitoring, response protocols, escalation
11. **Resource plan**: Team, tools, budget, timeline
12. **Measurement plan**: Metrics, reporting cadence, optimization process

### Strategy Approval and Alignment

Before execution:
- Align strategy with broader marketing and business strategy
- Obtain stakeholder sign-off (marketing leadership, legal, communications)
- Brief execution team on strategy and expectations
- Establish baseline metrics for goal tracking
- Set up reporting infrastructure

## 10. Strategic Anti-Patterns

### Common Strategic Failures

| Anti-Pattern | Description | Correction |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Platform FOMO | Being on every platform without strategic rationale | Ruthless platform prioritization based on matrix |
| Vanity metrics | Optimizing for followers/likes instead of business outcomes | Tie every metric to business goal |
| Content factory | Prioritizing volume over quality and strategy | Reduce frequency, increase per-post investment |
| Copy-paste cross-posting | Identical content across platforms | Platform-native content adaptation |
| Trend chasing | Abandoning pillars to chase every trend | Trend evaluation framework within pillar structure |
| Dark social blindness | Ignoring untrackable sharing (DMs, messaging) | Dark social estimation, shareable content design |
| Organic-only dogma | Refusing paid amplification for high-performing organic | Paid amplification budget for top organic content |
| Set-and-forget | Publishing without monitoring or response | Real-time monitoring and community management |

*Strategy is a living document. Revisit quarterly. Test continuously. Let data, not assumptions, drive decisions.*

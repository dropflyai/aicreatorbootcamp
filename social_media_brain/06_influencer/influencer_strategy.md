# Influencer Marketing Strategy

## Purpose

This module provides the complete strategic framework for influencer marketing: planning, influencer identification and selection, outreach, contracting, relationship management, and compliance. Influencer marketing has evolved from a tactical experiment into a core marketing discipline representing $21+ billion in annual global spend. Effective influencer strategy requires the same rigor as any other marketing channel.

---

## 1. Influencer Marketing Fundamentals

### Strategic Rationale

Influencer marketing works because it leverages three theoretical principles simultaneously:

1. **Parasocial relationships**: Audiences trust influencers like personal acquaintances (see 01_foundations/social_theory.md)
2. **Social proof**: Influencer endorsement signals quality to their audience
3. **Content creation**: Influencers produce platform-native content that outperforms brand-created content

### When Influencer Marketing Is Appropriate

| Scenario | Influencer Marketing Fit | Alternative |
|----------|------------------------|-------------|
| Brand awareness in new market | Strong | Paid social awareness campaigns |
| Product launch | Strong | PR, paid media, email marketing |
| Social proof/credibility building | Very strong | Customer testimonials, reviews |
| Content production at scale | Very strong | In-house content team, UGC campaigns |
| Direct response/performance | Moderate | Paid social with conversion optimization |
| B2B lead generation | Moderate (thought leader partnerships) | LinkedIn ads, content marketing |
| Community building | Strong (community-integrated creators) | Community management, events |
| SEO/content marketing | Moderate (backlinks, content creation) | In-house content, guest posting |

## 2. Influencer Tier Classification

### Tier Definitions

| Tier | Follower Range | Characteristics | Cost Range (Per Post) |
|------|---------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Nano | 1K-10K | Highest engagement rates (5-10%), niche authority, personal relationships with followers, often local | $50-$500 |
| Micro | 10K-50K | Strong engagement (3-6%), defined niche, authentic voice, growing authority | $500-$5,000 |
| Mid-Tier | 50K-500K | Professional content, established brand partnerships, reliable deliverables | $5,000-$25,000 |
| Macro | 500K-1M | Broad reach, professional management, celebrity-adjacent, high production value | $25,000-$100,000 |
| Mega/Celebrity | 1M+ | Maximum reach, celebrity status, lowest engagement rates (1-3%), agency-represented | $100,000-$1M+ |

### Tier Selection by Objective

| Objective | Recommended Tiers | Rationale |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| Mass awareness | Macro, Mega | Maximum reach per partnership |
| Niche awareness | Micro, Mid-Tier | Relevant audience concentration |
| Engagement and community | Nano, Micro | Highest engagement rates, authentic connection |
| Content creation | Micro, Mid-Tier | Professional quality at reasonable cost |
| Conversion/sales | Nano, Micro | Highest trust, strongest purchase influence |
| Credibility/social proof | Mid-Tier, Macro | Recognized authority, professional association |
| Long-term partnership | Micro, Mid-Tier | Sustainable cost, authentic relationship building |

### The Volume vs. Reach Trade-Off

| Strategy | Approach | Example | Pro | Con |
|----------|----------|---------|-----|-----|
| Few large | 1-3 macro/mega influencers | One celebrity partnership | Maximum reach per post | High cost, single point of failure |
| Many small | 20-50 nano/micro influencers | Community seeding campaign | Diverse reach, authentic, testable | Management complexity |
| Pyramid | 1 macro + 5 mid + 20 micro | Layered launch campaign | Reach + depth + authenticity | Coordination complexity |

**Recommendation**: The pyramid approach or many-small strategy typically delivers superior ROI. Academic research (Valsesia, Nunes & Ordanini, 2020) confirms that micro-influencers generate higher engagement and conversion rates per dollar invested.

## 3. Influencer Selection Criteria

### Quantitative Selection Metrics

| Metric | What to Look For | Red Flag |
|--------|-----------------|----------|
| Engagement rate | 3-6% (micro), 2-4% (mid), 1-3% (macro) | Below 1% or above 15% (fake engagement) |
| Follower growth | Steady organic growth trajectory | Sudden spikes (purchased followers) |
| Audience demographics | Alignment with target demographic | Demographics misaligned with your target |
| Audience location | Concentrated in target markets | Audience in non-target geographies |
| Content consistency | Regular posting schedule, consistent quality | Long gaps, erratic quality |
| Audience authenticity | Low bot follower percentage (<10%) | High bot percentage (>20%) |
| View-to-follower ratio | Views proportional to follower count | Views significantly lower than follower count |
| Comment quality | Substantive comments from real accounts | Generic emoji comments, bot-like responses |

### Qualitative Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Assessment Method |
|-----------|------------------|
| Brand alignment | Does their personal brand, values, and aesthetic align with yours? |
| Content quality | Is their content consistently high quality and platform-native? |
| Authenticity | Do they genuinely use/need products in your category? |
| Audience trust | Do followers take their recommendations seriously? |
| Professionalism | Have they worked with brands before? Are they reliable? |
| Exclusivity | Are they currently partnered with a direct competitor? |
| Risk profile | Is there controversial content that could damage brand association? |
| Creative capability | Can they adapt brand messaging into their authentic voice? |

## 4. Influencer Outreach

### Outreach Process

**Step 1: Research**
Before reaching out, thoroughly review the influencer's:
- Last 20-30 posts across platforms
- Audience demographics (use verification tools)
- Previous brand partnerships (check for competitors)
- Content themes, tone, and production style
- Engagement patterns (which content types perform best)

**Step 2: Initial Contact**

| Channel | Best For | Response Rate |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| Email | Professional approach, mid-tier+ influencers | Highest for managed influencers |
| Instagram DM | Nano/micro influencers, casual initial contact | Moderate, often checked |
| Platform-specific DM | Where the influencer is most active | Variable |
| Agent/Manager | Macro/mega influencers | Required for represented creators |
| Influencer marketplace | Platform-facilitated connections | High (pre-qualified interest) |

**Outreach Message Framework**

```
Subject: Partnership Opportunity - [Brand Name] x [Influencer Name]

Hi [Name],

[1-2 sentences showing you genuinely follow and appreciate their content.
Reference a SPECIFIC recent post or series.]

I'm reaching out from [Brand Name]. We [brief description of what you do
and why it's relevant to their audience].

We'd love to explore a partnership with you because [specific reason --
their expertise, their audience alignment, their content style].

What we're thinking:
- [Brief overview of partnership concept]
- [Compensation transparency: "Paid partnership" or range]
- [Timeline if relevant]

Would you be open to a conversation about this?

Best,
[Your name]
[Title, Brand]
```

**Outreach Anti-Patterns**
- Generic template with no personalization
- Leading with "We'd love to send you free product" (devalues their work)
- No compensation mention (implies you expect free work)
- Mass DMs with identical messages
- Reaching out about products irrelevant to their content

### Negotiation Framework

| Element | Considerations |
|---------|---------------|
| Compensation model | Flat fee, performance-based, hybrid, product-only (nano only) |
| Deliverables | Number of posts, platforms, formats, Stories, usage rights |
| Timeline | Creation deadline, posting schedule, revision rounds |
| Exclusivity | Category exclusivity period (30-90 days typical) |
| Usage rights | Organic repurposing, paid amplification rights, duration |
| Revision rounds | Number of revision rounds included (1-2 standard) |
| Kill fee | Payment if brand cancels after creator invests work |
| Performance bonuses | Bonus for exceeding engagement/conversion benchmarks |

## 5. Influencer Contracts and Compliance

### Contract Essentials

Every influencer partnership must have a written agreement covering:

| Section | Key Terms |
|---------|-----------|
| Scope of work | Deliverables, platforms, formats, quantity, timeline |
| Compensation | Amount, payment schedule, performance bonuses, expenses |
| Content approval | Approval workflow, revision rounds, turnaround times |
| Usage rights | Where brand can use content, for how long, paid amplification rights |
| Exclusivity | Category exclusivity, competitor restrictions, duration |
| FTC compliance | Disclosure requirements, specific language required |
| Intellectual property | Who owns the content, licensing terms |
| Termination | Termination conditions, kill fee, notice period |
| Confidentiality | NDA for unreleased products, campaign details |
| Indemnification | Liability for claims arising from content |
| Morality clause | Behavior standards, grounds for termination |

### FTC Disclosure Requirements (US)

The Federal Trade Commission requires clear and conspicuous disclosure of material connections between influencers and brands:

**Required Disclosure**
- Any payment, free products, affiliate commissions, or other material benefit must be disclosed
- Disclosure must be clear and conspicuous (not buried in hashtags or fine print)
- Disclosure must be in the same format as the endorsement (video disclosure for video content)

**Acceptable Disclosure Methods**
| Platform | Acceptable | Not Acceptable |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Instagram | #ad or #sponsored at beginning of caption, or Paid Partnership tag | #ad buried after 50 hashtags, "sp" abbreviation |
| TikTok | Branded Content toggle, verbal "paid partnership" disclosure | Only in description, not in video itself |
| YouTube | Paid Promotion checkbox, verbal disclosure in first 30 seconds | Only in description box, small text |
| LinkedIn | "Sponsored" or "Paid partnership" clearly stated | Ambiguous language like "thanks to" without clarity |
| All platforms | "Paid partnership with [Brand]", "#ad", "#sponsored" prominently placed | Hidden, ambiguous, or absent disclosure |

**FTC Enforcement Actions**
The FTC has taken enforcement action against both brands and influencers. Brands are responsible for:
- Providing clear disclosure guidelines to influencers
- Monitoring that influencers comply with disclosure requirements
- Taking corrective action when non-compliance is identified
- Having compliance language in all influencer contracts

### International Disclosure Requirements

| Region | Regulation | Key Difference |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| UK | ASA/CAP Code | "Ad" or "Advertisement" required; stricter than US |
| EU | UCPD, national implementations | Varies by country; "Werbung" in Germany, "Publicite" in France |
| Canada | Competition Act, Ad Standards | Similar to US FTC requirements |
| Australia | AANA Code | "Sponsored", "Ad", or "Paid partnership" required |

## 6. Influencer Relationship Management

### Relationship Lifecycle

```
Discovery → Vetting → Outreach → Negotiation → Onboarding → Creation →
Review → Publication → Monitoring → Analysis → Renewal/Expansion
```

### Onboarding Best Practices

| Onboarding Element | Content |
|-------------------|---------|
| Brand kit | Logos, color palette, visual guidelines, brand voice guide |
| Product information | Key features, benefits, differentiators, talking points |
| Campaign brief | Objectives, key messages, creative direction, dos and don'ts |
| Content examples | Examples of good brand content, not to be copied but as inspiration |
| Compliance guide | FTC disclosure requirements, platform-specific guidelines |
| Timeline and workflow | Deadlines, submission process, approval workflow, contact person |
| Hashtags and mentions | Required tags, hashtags, mentions, and tracking links |

### Long-Term Relationship Building

| Tactic | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Regular check-ins | Maintain relationship between campaigns |
| Early product access | Make influencers feel valued and "insider" |
| Exclusive experiences | Events, trips, factory visits, executive access |
| Creative freedom | Trust influencer's understanding of their audience |
| Fair compensation | Pay market rate or above; never devalue their work |
| Performance sharing | Share campaign results so influencers see their impact |
| Public appreciation | Tag and thank influencers publicly, engage with their content |
| Feedback loops | Ask for their input on products and campaigns |

### Influencer Tiering for Brand Relationships

| Tier | Relationship | Engagement | Investment |
|------|-------------|------------|------------|
| Ambassador | Long-term exclusive partnership, brand representative | Monthly content, events, product development input | Premium compensation, retainer |
| Partner | Multi-campaign relationship, preferred creator | Quarterly campaigns, consistent collaboration | Above-market compensation |
| Collaborator | Campaign-by-campaign basis, tested and trusted | Per-campaign engagement | Market-rate compensation |
| Seeded | Product gifting with no obligation, building relationship | Product send, no deliverable requirement | Product cost only |

## 7. Campaign Types

### Campaign Framework Options

| Campaign Type | Description | Best Objectives | Timeline |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|----------|
| Product launch | Coordinated influencer content around new product | Awareness, trial, sales | 2-4 weeks |
| Always-on | Ongoing creator content throughout the year | Sustained awareness, content generation | 3-12 months |
| Seasonal | Aligned with seasonal moments (holidays, back-to-school) | Awareness, sales during peak periods | 4-6 weeks |
| Event | Content around brand event, conference, or experience | Awareness, FOMO, community | 1-2 weeks |
| Takeover | Influencer takes over brand's social account | Engagement, audience crossover | 1-3 days |
| Challenge/Hashtag | Influencers seed a branded challenge | Awareness, UGC generation | 2-4 weeks |
| Review/Unboxing | Authentic product reviews and first impressions | Credibility, purchase consideration | Ongoing |
| Affiliate | Performance-based, influencers earn commission | Sales, measurable ROI | Ongoing |

## 9. Influencer Strategy Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Consequence | Correction |
|-------------|-------------|------------|
| Vanity metric selection | Partnering with influencers based on follower count alone | Evaluate engagement quality, audience authenticity, brand fit |
| Over-scripting content | Inauthentic content that audience sees through | Creative brief with key messages, not scripts |
| One-off partnerships | No relationship depth, no audience familiarity | Long-term ambassador relationships |
| No performance tracking | Unknown ROI, inability to optimize | Unique links, codes, UTM tracking for every partnership |
| Ignoring FTC compliance | Legal risk, platform penalties, audience backlash | Compliance in every contract and monitoring |
| Underpaying creators | Damaged relationships, lower content quality | Market-rate compensation research, transparent negotiation |
| No exclusivity clause | Influencer posts competitor content immediately after | Category exclusivity in contracts (30-90 days) |
| Ghosting after campaign | Relationship destruction, negative word-of-mouth | Post-campaign follow-up, results sharing, relationship nurturing |

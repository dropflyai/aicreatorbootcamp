# Sales Coaching — Observe, Assess, Coach, Review

## Why Coaching Is the Highest-Leverage Activity

CSO Insights research consistently demonstrates that coaching is the single highest-
leverage activity a sales manager performs. Organizations with a formal coaching
cadence achieve 16.7% higher win rates than those without (CSO Insights 2019). Yet
most managers spend less than 5% of their time coaching. The gap between knowing
coaching matters and actually doing it is the largest execution gap in sales
leadership.

The reason coaching is under-invested is structural, not motivational. Managers are
promoted for selling, not coaching. They are measured on team revenue, not coaching
quality. They are trained in pipeline review (inspecting results) but not in coaching
(developing capability). This module provides the frameworks to close that gap.

## The OACR Coaching Model

### Observe — Assess — Coach — Review

```
OBSERVE: Watch the rep perform (live or recorded)
    ↓
ASSESS: Identify specific strengths and development areas
    ↓
COACH: Provide feedback, model the skill, practice together
    ↓
REVIEW: Track progress, reinforce improvement, repeat
```

### Phase 1: Observe

**What to Observe:**
- Discovery calls (diagnostic skill, questioning depth)
- Demo calls (presentation, relevance, audience reading)
- Negotiation conversations (composure, value defense)
- Pipeline reviews (deal strategy, qualification rigor)
- Internal meetings (collaboration, communication)

**Observation Methods:**

| Method | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| Live Ride-Along | Real-time context, body language visible | Observer effect changes behavior | Early ramp, complex deals |
| Call Recording (Gong) | Scalable, reviewable, no observer effect | Missing visual cues (phone/video) | Ongoing coaching, benchmarking |
| Pipeline Review | Tests strategic thinking, not just selling | Does not assess live execution | Deal strategy coaching |
| Role-Play | Controlled practice, can isolate skills | Artificial, may not transfer to live | Skill building, certification |

### Phase 2: Assess

**Assessment Framework (per interaction):**

| Dimension | What to Evaluate | Scoring (1-5) |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------|
| Preparation | Was the rep prepared? Research, pre-call plan, materials | |
| Opening | Did they establish context, agenda, and up-front contract? | |
| Discovery Depth | Did they move beyond surface questions to business impact? | |
| Active Listening | Did they mirror, label, follow threads, or talk over? | |
| Value Articulation | Did they connect capabilities to buyer-stated needs? | |
| Objection Handling | Did they acknowledge, explore, and resolve? | |
| Next Steps | Did they secure a specific, date-bound, attendee-confirmed next step? | |
| Overall Effectiveness | Holistic assessment of buyer engagement and deal advancement | |

**The One-Thing Rule:**
Do not overwhelm a rep with 8 development areas. Identify the ONE skill that, if
improved, would have the largest impact on results. Focus coaching energy there until
proficiency is achieved, then move to the next skill.

### Phase 3: Coach

**The Coaching Conversation Structure (15-20 minutes):**

1. **Rep Self-Assessment (3 min)**
   "How do you think the call went? What went well? What would you do differently?"
   Starting with self-assessment builds self-awareness and often the rep identifies
   the same issue you observed. This makes them more receptive.

2. **Strength Reinforcement (2 min)**
   "I noticed you did [specific behavior] really well. The effect it had on the buyer
   was [observable impact]. Keep doing that." Specific, behavior-level praise builds
   on strengths and creates psychological safety.

3. **Development Area (5 min)**
   "The area I want to work on with you is [specific skill]. On today's call, I noticed
   [specific behavior]. The impact was [effect on buyer/deal]. Here is what I would
   recommend instead: [specific alternative behavior]."

4. **Model and Practice (5-7 min)**
   "Let me demonstrate what that would sound like." (Manager role-plays the skill.)
   "Now you try. I will play the buyer." (Rep practices with feedback.)

5. **Commitment (2-3 min)**
   "On your next 3 calls, I want you to focus specifically on [skill]. I will review
   those calls and we will debrief next week. Sound good?"

### Phase 4: Review

**Review Cadence:**
- Daily: Manager reviews 1-2 Gong calls per rep (10-15 min each)
- Weekly: Formal 1:1 coaching session per direct report (30 min)
- Monthly: Skill progression assessment (5 min per rep in team meeting)
- Quarterly: Comprehensive performance and development review (60 min)

**Review Documentation:**
Maintain a coaching log for each rep:
```
Rep: [Name]
Date: [Date]
Calls Reviewed: [List]
Current Focus Skill: [Skill]
Observation: [What was observed]
Coaching Provided: [What feedback was given]
Practice: [What was role-played]
Progress Since Last Session: [Improvement or stagnation]
Next Focus: [Continue same skill or advance]
```

## Call Coaching with Conversation Intelligence (Gong)

### Leveraging Gong for Systematic Coaching

Conversation intelligence platforms transform coaching from subjective opinion to
data-informed development. Gong, Chorus, and similar tools provide:

**Quantitative Call Metrics:**
| Metric | Definition | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Talk Ratio | % of call where rep is speaking | 40-60% (discovery), 60-70% (demo) |
| Longest Monologue | Longest uninterrupted rep speech (seconds) | <120 sec (discovery), <180 sec (demo) |
| Question Rate | Questions asked per hour | 11-14 (optimal discovery) |
| Filler Words | "Um", "uh", "like" per minute | <3 per minute |
| Topic Tracking | Topics discussed and duration | Pain, pricing, competition, next steps |
| Patience | Seconds of silence after buyer finishes | >1.5 seconds |
| Interactivity | Speaker switches per meeting | >15 per 30 min (balanced conversation) |

### Gong-Based Coaching Workflow

**Step 1: Identify Coaching Opportunities (5 min/day)**
- Review Gong dashboard for outlier metrics (talk ratio >80%, no questions asked,
  pricing discussed without discovery)
- Check deal risk alerts (key topics not discussed, stakeholders not mentioned)
- Flag calls for coaching review

**Step 2: Review Flagged Calls (10-15 min/call)**
- Listen at 1.5x speed to the flagged segments
- Timestamp specific moments for coaching conversation
- Identify the behavior pattern (is this a one-time issue or recurring?)

**Step 3: Share Clips in Coaching Session**
- Share the specific Gong clip with the rep before the session
- Have them self-assess the clip
- Provide your observation and coaching guidance
- Role-play the improved approach

**Step 4: Track Progress Over Time**
- Monitor the coached metric over subsequent calls
- Compare pre-coaching and post-coaching averages
- Celebrate measurable improvement
- Adjust coaching focus as metrics improve

### Building a Call Library

Create a curated library of exemplary calls organized by skill:

| Skill | Call Example | What Makes It Great |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Discovery Depth | [Link to Gong call] | Rep asks 14 questions, reaches financial impact |
| Objection Handling | [Link to Gong call] | Rep labels emotion, explores root cause, resolves |
| Executive Presence | [Link to Gong call] | Rep speaks CFO language, concise, data-driven |

New hires should listen to 20+ library calls during onboarding.

## Deal Coaching

### What Is Deal Coaching?

Deal coaching differs from skill coaching. Skill coaching develops individual
capabilities (questioning, presenting, handling objections). Deal coaching develops
strategic thinking about specific opportunities (who to engage, what sequence, how
to position).

### Deal Coaching Framework

**The Five Questions:**
For every deal in S2+, the manager should ask:

1. **"Why will they buy?"** (Pain + impact + urgency)
   If the rep cannot articulate why the buyer will choose to buy anything, the deal
   is at risk of no-decision.

2. **"Why will they buy from us?"** (Differentiation + value)
   If the rep cannot articulate competitive advantage, the deal is at risk of
   competitive loss.

3. **"Why will they buy now?"** (Compelling event + timeline)
   If the rep cannot articulate time-bound urgency, the deal will slip.

4. **"Who decides and who influences?"** (Stakeholder map + champion)
   If the rep cannot name the economic buyer and champion, the deal is single-threaded.

5. **"What is the next verifiable buyer action?"** (Next step)
   If the next step is a seller action (send proposal, follow up), the buyer is not
   driving the process. Next step must be a BUYER action.

### Deal Coaching Anti-Patterns

**The Pipeline Review Disguised as Coaching:**
Manager asks: "What stage is this?" "When will it close?" "What is the amount?"
This is inspection, not coaching. Coaching asks: "Why?" and "How?"

**The Manager Takes Over the Deal:**
Manager joins every call, runs every meeting, closes every deal. The rep learns
nothing. Coaching requires letting the rep do the work with guidance.

**The One-Size-Fits-All Approach:**
Applying the same coaching to a veteran AE and a new hire. Experienced reps need
strategic coaching (deal strategy, competitive positioning). New reps need skill
coaching (discovery, demo, objection handling).

## Skill Coaching by Competency

### Coaching Discovery Skills

**Observation Indicators of Weak Discovery:**
- Talk ratio >60% during discovery
- Questions are all situation-level (no problem or implication)
- Rep presents solutions before completing diagnosis
- Buyer disengages (short answers, checking phone)

**Coaching Interventions:**
1. Role-play with Sandler pain funnel — practice drilling from surface to emotion
2. Assign "5 why" exercise: for every pain, ask "why does that matter?" 5 times
3. Review 3 great discovery calls from call library
4. Set a metric target: minimum 12 questions per discovery call

### Coaching Demo Skills

**Observation Indicators of Weak Demos:**
- Demo follows a standard script regardless of buyer's stated pain
- Rep shows every feature rather than relevant ones
- No buyer interaction during demo (monologue)
- No connection between feature shown and problem discussed

**Coaching Interventions:**
1. Pre-demo briefing: "Which 3 pain points from discovery will you address?"
2. Practice "tell-show-tell" format: explain the problem, show the solution, confirm impact
3. Time-boxed demo practice: 15-minute demo constraint forces prioritization
4. Demo debrief: "Which features did the buyer react to most? Which fell flat?"

### Coaching Negotiation Skills

**Observation Indicators of Weak Negotiation:**
- Rep concedes immediately when buyer pushes on price
- Rep does not ask for anything in return for concessions
- Rep panics during silence or aggressive buyer tactics
- Rep discounts before exhausting other negotiation levers

**Coaching Interventions:**
1. Negotiation simulation with scripted buyer tactics
2. Build personal concession matrix before every negotiation
3. Practice the "pause" technique — 3-second silence after every buyer demand
4. Post-negotiation debrief: "What did you give? What did you get? Fair trade?"

## Building a Coaching Culture

### Manager Coaching Certification

Before managers can coach reps, they must be coached themselves:

| Certification Element | Requirement |
|----------------------|-------------|
| Coaching Framework | Complete OACR training (1-day workshop) |
| Call Review Proficiency | Review 10 calls with accurate assessment |
| Coaching Conversation | Deliver 3 observed coaching sessions with feedback |
| Gong Certification | Demonstrate platform proficiency for data-driven coaching |
| Ongoing Requirement | Conduct minimum 4 coaching sessions per rep per month |

### Coaching Accountability

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Coaching Sessions/Rep/Month | 4+ | Calendar + coaching log |
| Gong Calls Reviewed/Week | 5+ per team | Gong activity tracking |
| Coaching Log Completion | 100% | Shared coaching doc |
| Rep Satisfaction (coaching quality) | 4+ out of 5 | Anonymous quarterly survey |
| Behavior Metric Improvement | Measurable trend | Gong metric tracking |

**Coaching is not what a manager does in spare time. It is the primary mechanism
by which individual capability becomes organizational capability. The manager who
coaches transforms a team. The manager who only inspects maintains one.**

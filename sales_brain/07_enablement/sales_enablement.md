# Sales Enablement — Onboarding, Content, Certification, and Impact

## Enablement as a Strategic Function

Sales enablement is the discipline of equipping revenue teams with the knowledge,
skills, content, and tools they need to effectively engage buyers at every stage of
the journey. Enablement is not training — training is an event, enablement is a
system. The distinction matters because training decays (Hermann Ebbinghaus' forgetting
curve shows 70% knowledge loss within 24 hours without reinforcement), while
enablement systems sustain capability through continuous reinforcement, coaching, and
content delivery.

The charter of a world-class enablement function: reduce ramp time, increase win
rates, improve deal sizes, and ensure methodology adherence — all measurable, all
connected to revenue outcomes.

## Onboarding: The 30-60-90 Day Framework

### Why Onboarding Matters

The average B2B SaaS AE takes 4-6 months to ramp to full productivity. During this
period, the company pays full OTE (or close to it) while receiving sub-quota
performance. Structured onboarding reduces ramp time by 30-40% (Bridge Group data),
which translates directly to faster revenue and lower cost per hire.

### 30-60-90 Day Onboarding Plan

#### Days 1-30: LEARN

**Week 1: Company and Product**
| Day | Focus | Activities | Assessment |
|-----|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Company orientation | Vision, values, org chart, tools setup | Setup completion |
| 2 | Product deep dive | Core product demo, architecture overview | Product quiz (80%+) |
| 3 | Market and competition | ICP definition, competitive landscape, market position | Competitor matrix |
| 4 | Customer stories | Listen to 5 customer calls (Gong), read 3 case studies | Written summary |
| 5 | Sales process overview | Stages, exit criteria, CRM walkthrough | CRM scavenger hunt |

**Week 2: Methodology**
| Day | Focus | Activities | Assessment |
|-----|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 6-7 | Discovery methodology | SPIN/Challenger training, discovery role-play | Role-play score |
| 8-9 | Qualification framework | MEDDPICC training, deal scoring practice | MEDDPICC quiz |
| 10 | Objection handling | Top 15 objections, response frameworks | Objection role-play |

**Week 3: Shadowing**
| Day | Focus | Activities | Assessment |
|-----|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 11-12 | Discovery call shadowing | Observe 4-6 senior AE discovery calls | Written debrief |
| 13-14 | Demo shadowing | Observe 3-4 demos, note techniques | Demo structure outline |
| 15 | Pipeline review shadowing | Sit in on manager pipeline reviews | Pipeline review notes |

**Week 4: Controlled Practice**
| Day | Focus | Activities | Assessment |
|-----|-------|-----------|-----------|
| 16-17 | First discovery calls | Supervised live calls with manager observing | Call review score |
| 18-19 | First demos | Supervised demos with SE support | Demo score |
| 20 | 30-day assessment | Manager evaluation, gap identification | Written assessment |

**30-Day Checkpoint Criteria:**
- [ ] Can articulate company value proposition in 60 seconds
- [ ] Can navigate CRM and update opportunities correctly
- [ ] Can run a discovery call with supervision
- [ ] Scored 80%+ on product knowledge assessment
- [ ] Completed 10+ call shadow sessions

#### Days 31-60: PRACTICE

**Weeks 5-6: Live Selling with Support**
- Take ownership of initial pipeline (SDR-sourced or marketing leads)
- Run discovery calls independently (manager reviews via Gong)
- Deliver demos with SE support (transition from co-pilot to observer)
- Begin building personal pipeline through outbound

**Weeks 7-8: Expanding Skills**
- Advanced objection handling workshops
- Negotiation fundamentals training
- Proposal creation and pricing walkthrough
- Enterprise selling basics (if relevant to role)

**60-Day Checkpoint Criteria:**
- [ ] Has 3-5 active opportunities in pipeline
- [ ] Discovery call quality score >70% (Gong scorecard)
- [ ] Demo quality score >70%
- [ ] CRM hygiene compliance >90%
- [ ] First meeting/proposal sent (at minimum)

#### Days 61-90: OWN

**Weeks 9-12: Full Ownership**
- Managing full pipeline independently
- Running complete sales cycles (discovery through close)
- Participating in pipeline reviews with manager feedback
- Building repeatable outbound prospecting motion
- First deal closed (target: at least 1 deal or advanced pipeline)

**90-Day Checkpoint Criteria:**
- [ ] Pipeline coverage at 2x+ ramp quota
- [ ] Discovery call quality score >80%
- [ ] At least one deal in S3+ or closed
- [ ] MEDDPICC completion rate >80% for all opportunities
- [ ] Forecast accuracy within 20% of actual

## Content Management

### The Content Problem

Most sales organizations have too much content (hundreds of assets) with too little
usage (CSO Insights: 65% of sales content goes unused). The problem is not creation —
it is organization, discoverability, and relevance.

### Content Architecture

Organize content by buyer journey stage and buyer persona:

```
                    AWARENESS    CONSIDERATION    DECISION    EXPANSION
C-Suite/EB         [Insight      [Business       [ROI         [QBR
                    Report]       Case Study]      Calculator]  Template]

VP/Director        [Industry     [Product        [Proposal    [Expansion
                    Brief]        Comparison]      Template]    Playbook]

Manager/User       [Blog         [Technical      [POC         [Training
                    Post]         Deep Dive]       Plan]        Materials]

Technical          [Whitepaper   [Architecture   [Integration [API
                    ]             Diagram]         Guide]       Docs]
```

### Content Types by Stage

| Stage | Content Type | Purpose | Format |
|-------|-------------|---------|--------|
| Prospecting | Insight reports, industry data | Open doors | PDF, email, LinkedIn |
| Discovery | Case studies, ROI calculators | Build credibility | Interactive, PDF |
| Evaluation | Product comparisons, technical docs | Prove capability | Doc, video, live demo |
| Proposal | Business cases, proposals, references | Justify investment | Deck, doc, spreadsheet |
| Negotiation | Pricing guides, contract templates | Facilitate process | Doc, spreadsheet |
| Post-Sale | Onboarding guides, training materials | Ensure adoption | Video, doc, in-app |

### Content Effectiveness Measurement

| Metric | Formula | Benchmark |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Content Usage Rate | Content pieces used / Total content available | >35% |
| Content-to-Win Correlation | Win rate for deals where content X was shared | Content X vs. baseline |
| Content Engagement | Views, downloads, time spent per asset | Asset-specific |
| Content Currency | Assets updated in last 6 months / Total assets | >80% |
| Rep Content Satisfaction | Survey score (1-5) for content quality and findability | >3.5 |

## Certification Programs

### Certification Architecture

Certifications validate that a rep has achieved a defined competency level. They
are not participation trophies — they require demonstrated proficiency.

### Certification Framework

| Certification | Requirement | Assessment Method | Validity |
|--------------|-------------|-------------------|----------|
| Product Certified | Score 85%+ on product exam | Written exam (50 questions) | 6 months |
| Discovery Certified | Pass live discovery role-play | Manager + peer evaluation | 6 months |
| Demo Certified | Pass live demo assessment | SE + manager evaluation | 6 months |
| Methodology Certified | Pass MEDDPICC/Challenger assessment | Case study + role-play | 12 months |
| Enterprise Certified | Pass complex deal strategy exercise | Panel review (VP + peers) | 12 months |
| Negotiation Certified | Pass negotiation simulation | Recorded simulation scoring | 12 months |

### Certification Scoring Rubric (Discovery Example)

| Competency | Weight | Criteria | Score (1-5) |
|-----------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Up-Front Contract | 10% | Sets clear agenda, time, and outcome expectations | |
| Situation Questions | 10% | Efficient, research-informed, not excessive | |
| Problem Questions | 20% | Surfaces explicit pain, goes beyond surface level | |
| Implication Questions | 25% | Connects pain to business impact, quantifies | |
| Need-Payoff Questions | 15% | Co-creates vision of future state with buyer | |
| Active Listening | 10% | Mirrors, labels, paraphrases, follows threads | |
| Next Steps | 10% | Specific, date-bound, attendee-confirmed | |

**Passing Threshold:** Weighted average score of 3.5+ (out of 5.0)
**Distinction:** 4.5+ earns "Senior Certified" designation

## Measuring Enablement Impact

### The Enablement ROI Model

Enablement must prove its value in business terms, not training completions.

**Level 1: Activity Metrics (Did they participate?)**
- Training attendance rate
- Content consumption rate
- Certification completion rate
These are necessary but insufficient measures.

**Level 2: Knowledge Metrics (Did they learn?)**
- Pre/post assessment score improvement
- Certification pass rate
- Knowledge retention at 30/60/90 days
These confirm learning occurred but do not confirm behavior change.

**Level 3: Behavior Metrics (Did they change?)**
- Methodology adherence in Gong call analysis
- CRM hygiene compliance
- Discovery depth improvement (time in discovery phase)
- Multi-threading improvement (contacts per deal)
These confirm behavior change but do not confirm revenue impact.

**Level 4: Revenue Metrics (Did it drive results?)**
- Ramp time reduction (months to first deal, months to full productivity)
- Win rate improvement (cohort comparison: trained vs. not trained)
- Deal size improvement (average ACV before/after enablement intervention)
- Cycle time reduction (days to close before/after)
- Quota attainment lift (% of trained reps hitting quota vs. baseline)

### Impact Measurement Framework

```
Enablement ROI = (Revenue Lift from Trained Reps - Enablement Program Cost)
                  / Enablement Program Cost
```

**Example:**
- 20 reps trained in advanced discovery methodology
- Pre-training average win rate: 22%
- Post-training average win rate: 28% (6 point improvement)
- Average pipeline per rep: $2M annually
- Revenue lift per rep: $2M x 0.06 = $120K
- Total revenue lift: 20 x $120K = $2.4M
- Enablement program cost: $150K (content creation, trainer, tools)
- ROI = ($2.4M - $150K) / $150K = 15x

## Enablement Program Types

### Evergreen Programs (Ongoing)

| Program | Cadence | Format | Audience |
|---------|---------|--------|----------|
| New Hire Onboarding | Continuous | Blended (live + async) | New hires |
| Product Update Training | Monthly | 30-min live session | All reps |
| Competitive Intelligence | Monthly | Battle card updates + 15-min briefing | All reps |
| Call Review Sessions | Weekly | 30-min Gong clip review | Team-based |
| Certification Renewal | Semi-annual | Assessment + role-play | All certified reps |

### Campaign Programs (Time-Bound)

| Program | Trigger | Duration | Format |
|---------|---------|----------|--------|
| New Product Launch | Product release | 2-3 weeks | Training + content + certification |
| Methodology Rollout | Process change | 6-8 weeks | Workshop + coaching + certification |
| Market Entry | New segment/geo | 4-6 weeks | Market briefing + content + playbook |
| Competitive Response | Competitor launch | 1-2 weeks | Battle card + talk track + role-play |
| Skill Bootcamp | Performance gap | 2-3 days | Intensive workshop + practice |

# Sales Process — Methodology Implementation and Call Frameworks

## Process vs. Methodology: The Critical Distinction

A sales process is the sequence of stages a deal moves through from identification
to close. It is company-specific, buyer-aligned, and stage-gated. A sales methodology
is the HOW — the techniques, frameworks, and skills used within each stage to advance
the deal. Process without methodology produces a pipeline full of stalled deals.
Methodology without process produces heroic sellers who cannot be replicated.

The most effective sales organizations implement a structured process AND embed
one or more methodologies at each stage. This module covers methodology selection,
implementation, and the call frameworks that operationalize methodology in practice.

## Methodology Selection Framework

### When to Use Which Methodology

| Methodology | Best For | ACV Range | Buyer Complexity | Core Mechanism |
|------------|---------|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| SPIN Selling | Discovery-intensive, problem-led sales | $25K-$500K | Medium-High | Question sequence (Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff) |
| Challenger Sale | Commodity displacement, teaching-led sales | $50K-$1M | High | Teach-Tailor-Take Control |
| Sandler | Qualification-intensive, disqualification-focused | $10K-$250K | Medium | Pain funnel, Up-Front Contract |
| MEDDPICC | Enterprise qualification, multi-stakeholder | $100K-$10M+ | Very High | 8-element qualification scoring |
| Solution Selling | Custom/configured solutions, value-led | $50K-$2M | High | Pain-Power-Vision-Value-Control |
| Gap Selling | Current state/future state focused | $25K-$500K | Medium | Gap analysis methodology |
| Command of the Message | Messaging consistency across team | All | All | Value drivers, required capabilities |

### Methodology Layering (Recommended)

Most organizations should not choose one methodology. They should layer:

**Layer 1: Qualification Framework (MEDDPICC)**
Applied to EVERY deal to score qualification completeness. This is the deal
management layer — it tells you where you stand.

**Layer 2: Discovery Methodology (SPIN or Gap Selling)**
Applied during discovery and early-stage conversations. This is the diagnostic
layer — it tells you what the buyer needs.

**Layer 3: Engagement Methodology (Challenger or Solution Selling)**
Applied during mid-to-late-stage conversations. This is the value creation
layer — it differentiates you from competition.

**Layer 4: Negotiation Framework (Getting to Yes / Voss)**
Applied during commercial negotiation. This is the closing layer — it creates
agreements that stick.

## Methodology Deep Dive: Challenger Implementation

### The Challenger Insight

CEB/Gartner's research across 6,000+ sales reps identified five seller profiles:
1. **Hard Worker** (21% of high performers) — outworks everyone
2. **Lone Wolf** (25% of high performers) — self-directed, hard to manage
3. **Relationship Builder** (7% of high performers) — invest in rapport
4. **Problem Solver** (12% of high performers) — detail-oriented, thorough
5. **Challenger** (39% of high performers) — teaches, tailors, takes control

The Challenger was the highest-performing profile in complex sales by a significant
margin. Critically, the Relationship Builder — the traditional "best seller" archetype —
was the LOWEST performer in complex environments.

### Challenger Implementation Framework

**Teach:**
Lead with an insight the buyer did not have. This insight must:
- Challenge an assumption the buyer currently holds
- Be connected to a unique capability you provide
- Be backed by data or research (not opinion)
- Create a "reframe" moment where the buyer rethinks their approach

**Example Insight:**
"Most companies in your industry benchmark their sales cycle against direct
competitors. Our research across 400 SaaS companies shows that the real benchmark
should be the buyer's alternative evaluation time — and for your category, that is
35% longer than the industry average. The companies closing faster are not selling
better — they are reducing evaluation complexity."

**Tailor:**
Customize the insight and value proposition to the buyer's specific context:
- Industry-specific metrics and challenges
- Company-size-appropriate solutions
- Role-specific value propositions (CTO cares about different things than CFO)
- Geography or regulatory considerations

**Take Control:**
Maintain productive tension throughout the deal:
- Push back respectfully when the buyer's criteria are misaligned
- Challenge requests for unnecessary discounts with value evidence
- Propose next steps assertively (not passively)
- Set expectations for the evaluation process and timeline

## Methodology Deep Dive: Sandler Implementation

### The Sandler Submarine

Sandler's methodology follows a seven-step "submarine" (each compartment must be
closed before moving to the next):

```
[Bonding & Rapport] → [Up-Front Contract] → [Pain] → [Budget] →
[Decision] → [Fulfillment] → [Post-Sell]
```

### Key Sandler Concepts

**The Up-Front Contract:**
Before EVERY meeting, establish a mutual agreement on:
- Purpose: "The purpose of today's meeting is..."
- Agenda: "Here is what I would like to cover..."
- Time: "We have 45 minutes — is that still good?"
- Outcome: "At the end, one of three things will happen: you will say 'yes, let us
  move forward,' 'no, this is not a fit,' or 'I need more information on X.' All
  three are perfectly okay. Does that work?"

**The Pain Funnel:**
A questioning technique that drills from surface-level to emotional pain:

```
Surface:   "Tell me more about that..."
            "Can you be more specific?"
            "How long has that been a problem?"
            "What have you tried to do about it?"
            "Did that work?"
            "How much do you think that has cost you?"
            "How do you feel about that?"
Emotional: "Have you given up trying to solve this?"
```

**Reversing:**
Answer questions with questions to maintain control:
Buyer: "Can your product do X?"
Sandler Response: "That is a great question. Why is X important to you?"
(This prevents premature product pitching and deepens discovery)

**Negative Reverse:**
Use counter-intuitive statements to lower resistance:
"I am not sure we can help you with that..." (Buyer instinctively argues FOR the sale)
"You probably should not change what you are doing..." (Buyer explains why they should)

## Call Frameworks

### The Discovery Call Framework (60 minutes)

| Segment | Duration | Objective | Techniques |
|---------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Opening | 5 min | Establish agenda, up-front contract | Sandler up-front contract |
| Context | 10 min | Understand current situation | SPIN situation questions |
| Pain Exploration | 20 min | Uncover and quantify problems | SPIN problem/implication, Sandler pain funnel |
| Vision | 10 min | Co-create desired future state | SPIN need-payoff, solution selling vision |
| Mutual Fit | 5 min | Assess alignment openly | "Based on what we have discussed, here is where I see fit..." |
| Next Steps | 10 min | Secure specific next action | Date-bound, attendee-confirmed, agenda-set |

### The Demo Call Framework (60 minutes)

| Segment | Duration | Objective | Techniques |
|---------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Re-Establish Context | 5 min | Confirm pain, set expectations | "Last time we discussed [pain]. Has anything changed?" |
| Tailored Demo | 25 min | Show solution to THEIR problems | Demo only features that map to stated pain |
| Impact Discussion | 10 min | Connect demo to business impact | "How would this capability impact [their metric]?" |
| Technical Q&A | 10 min | Address technical concerns | SE-led, document all questions |
| Objection Surface | 5 min | Proactively invite concerns | "What concerns you most about what you have seen?" |
| Next Steps | 5 min | Advance the deal | Propose specific next step (POC, exec meeting, proposal) |

### The Proposal Review Framework (45 minutes)

| Segment | Duration | Objective | Techniques |
|---------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Recap Journey | 5 min | Remind buyer of diagnosed pain | "We started this conversation because of [pain]..." |
| Value Summary | 10 min | Reinforce business case | Present ROI model, customer proof points |
| Proposal Walk-Through | 15 min | Present terms and structure | Go section by section, gauge reaction at each point |
| Objection Handling | 10 min | Address concerns | LAER model (Listen, Acknowledge, Explore, Respond) |
| Next Steps | 5 min | Close or advance | "What happens next on your side to move this to approval?" |

### The Executive Briefing Framework (30 minutes)

| Segment | Duration | Objective | Techniques |
|---------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Industry Insight | 5 min | Demonstrate expertise | Challenger teach moment, data-backed |
| Their Strategic Context | 5 min | Show you understand their world | Reference public filings, news, strategy |
| Value Proposition | 10 min | Connect solution to strategy | Executive-level language (revenue, risk, margin) |
| Partnership Vision | 5 min | Position as strategic partner | Reference customer outcomes, roadmap alignment |
| Ask | 5 min | Secure executive commitment | "Based on our discussion, what would it take to move forward?" |

## Process Implementation Playbook

### Phase 1: Design (Weeks 1-4)

1. Audit current sales process (interview top performers and new reps)
2. Map buyer journey stages to seller process stages
3. Define exit criteria for each stage
4. Select methodology layers (qualification, discovery, engagement, negotiation)
5. Design call frameworks for each stage

### Phase 2: Build (Weeks 5-8)

1. Create methodology training materials (not slides — practice exercises)
2. Build CRM automation (stage gates, required fields, alerts)
3. Design call recording review criteria
4. Create manager coaching guides
5. Build measurement dashboards

### Phase 3: Train (Weeks 9-12)

1. Instructor-led methodology training (2-day workshop)
2. Role-play certification (each rep must pass live role-play assessment)
3. Manager certification (each manager must demonstrate coaching capability)
4. CRM workflow training (process enforcement)
5. Baseline metric measurement

### Phase 4: Reinforce (Ongoing)

1. Weekly call review sessions (Gong/Chorus clips)
2. Monthly methodology refresh (15-min micro-training)
3. Quarterly certification renewal
4. Pipeline review integration (use methodology language in reviews)
5. New hire onboarding with methodology from day one

## Process Measurement

### Leading Indicators (Predict Future Revenue)

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Discovery-to-Opportunity Rate | Discoveries / Opportunities Created | 30-50% |
| Stage 1 to 2 Conversion | S1→S2 / Total S1 | 60-70% |

| MEDDPICC Score at S3+ | Avg MEDDPICC score for S3+ deals | 18+ (of 24) |
| Mutual Action Plan Adoption | Deals with MAP / Total S3+ deals | 70%+ |

### Lagging Indicators (Confirm Process Effectiveness)

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Win Rate | Closed Won / (Closed Won + Closed Lost) | 25-35% (enterprise) |
| Average Sales Cycle | Median days from S0 to Closed Won | Segment-dependent |
| Average Deal Size | Total bookings / Number of deals | Growing over time |
| Forecast Accuracy | 1 - ABS(Forecast - Actual) / Actual | 85%+ |
| Quota Attainment | Reps at/above quota / Total reps | 60-70% |

## Methodology Adoption Maturity

### Level 1: Awareness
Reps have been trained, can name the methodology, but do not consistently apply it.
*Indicator: <30% of calls show methodology adherence in Gong analysis.*

### Level 2: Compliance
Reps follow the methodology when reminded or observed, but revert to old habits
under pressure.
*Indicator: 30-60% methodology adherence. Improvement during coaching weeks.*

### Level 3: Competence
Reps consistently apply methodology across deal stages. Managers reinforce in pipeline
reviews.
*Indicator: 60-80% adherence. Pipeline metrics improving.*

### Level 4: Mastery
Methodology is internalized — reps adapt it fluidly to different buyer contexts.
Top performers innovate within the framework.
*Indicator: 80%+ adherence. Win rates measurably above pre-methodology baseline.*

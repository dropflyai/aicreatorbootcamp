# Sales Score -- Quality Enforcement (Authoritative)

This document defines how sales execution quality is evaluated.
Every deal, every discovery call, every proposal must be scored before advancing.

If sales quality is not measurable, it is not enforced.
If it is not enforced, pipeline is fiction.

---

## SCORING RULES (MANDATORY)

Each deal or sales activity must be scored across the following dimensions.
Score each category from **1 (poor)** to **5 (excellent)**.

### Hard Fail Dimensions

These dimensions are critical. Score <3 = immediate intervention required:
- **Qualification Rigor**
- **Pipeline Integrity**
- **Forecast Accuracy**

### Passing Criteria

- Average score across all dimensions >= 4.0
- No hard-fail dimension < 3
- No single dimension < 2
- All failure conditions checked and cleared

### Weighted Scoring

Some dimensions carry more weight in final scoring:
- Qualification Rigor: 1.5x weight
- Pipeline Integrity: 1.5x weight
- Forecast Accuracy: 1.5x weight
- All others: 1.0x weight

Weighted average must be >= 4.0 to pass.

## 1. QUALIFICATION RIGOR (MEDDPICC)

**Question:**
Has the deal been qualified using complete MEDDPICC criteria with verifiable evidence?

### MEDDPICC Completeness Requirements

| Element | Minimum Standard | Evidence Required |
|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Metrics** | Quantified business impact in $ or % | Customer-stated numbers, not rep estimates |
| **Economic Buyer** | Named individual with budget authority identified | Title, name, confirmed in org chart |
| **Decision Criteria** | Written list of evaluation factors | Customer email or meeting notes confirming |
| **Decision Process** | Step-by-step approval flow documented | Timeline with named approvers |
| **Paper Process** | Legal, procurement, security steps mapped | Procurement contact identified |
| **Identify Pain** | Business pain quantified with urgency | Cost of inaction calculated |
| **Champion** | Internal advocate actively selling for you | Evidence of champion actions (forwarded emails, scheduled meetings) |
| **Competition** | Named competitors with known positioning | Intel on competitor pricing, features, relationships |

### Scoring Guide

- **5** -- All 8 MEDDPICC elements complete with verified evidence. No gaps.
- **4** -- 7 of 8 complete. Missing element has a documented plan to fill within 5 days.
- **3** -- 6 of 8 complete. Gaps identified but not yet addressed.
- **2** -- 4-5 of 8 complete. Significant qualification gaps.
- **1** -- Fewer than 4 elements complete. Deal is unqualified.

Score <3 = deal MUST NOT advance to next stage.

## 2. DISCOVERY QUALITY (SPIN Depth)

**Question:**
Has discovery uncovered real pain, quantified in dollars, using structured methodology?

### SPIN Framework Requirements

| Phase | What Must Be Documented | Quality Bar |
|-------|------------------------|-------------|
| **Situation** | Current state, tools, process, team size, budget | Factual, not assumed |
| **Problem** | Explicit problems stated by the prospect | Direct quotes captured |
| **Implication** | Business impact of unsolved problems | Quantified in $ or time |
| **Need-Payoff** | Prospect articulates value of solving | Prospect's own words, not rep projection |

### Pain Quantification Requirements

Every qualified deal must have:
- **Annual cost of the problem** in dollars
- **Who bears the cost** (which department/role)
- **What happens if they do nothing** for 12 months
- **Source of the number** (prospect-stated vs. calculated together)

### Scoring Guide

- **5** -- Full SPIN executed. Pain quantified in $ with prospect confirmation. Need-payoff articulated by prospect. Direct quotes documented.
- **4** -- SPIN complete. Pain quantified but source is rep-calculated, not prospect-confirmed. Minor gaps in implication exploration.
- **3** -- Situation and Problem documented. Implication explored but not quantified. Need-payoff assumed, not prospect-stated.
- **2** -- Surface-level discovery. Questions were asked but answers are thin. No dollar quantification.
- **1** -- Discovery was a product demo disguised as a call. No structured methodology.

Score <3 = discovery must be repeated before advancing.

## 3. PIPELINE INTEGRITY

**Question:**
Does every deal in pipeline have verifiable outcomes, accurate stage placement, and validated next steps?

### Stage Gate Requirements

| Stage | Entry Criteria | Verifiable Outcome |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| **S1: Prospecting** | ICP match confirmed, outreach initiated | Meeting booked with decision-involved contact |
| **S2: Discovery** | Discovery call completed | Pain quantified, MEDDPICC started |
| **S3: Evaluation** | Solution presented, technical validation | Champion confirmed, EB aware |
| **S4: Proposal** | Proposal delivered, pricing discussed | Mutual action plan signed |
| **S5: Negotiation** | Terms being finalized | Redlines received, legal engaged |
| **S6: Closed Won** | Contract signed | Handoff to CS/implementation |
| **S7: Closed Lost** | Deal dead | Post-mortem completed |

### Pipeline Hygiene Rules

- No deal sits in a stage for more than 2x the average stage duration without review
- Every deal must have a "next step" with a date and a named person
- "Following up" is not a next step. Specific action required.
- Deals with no activity for 14 days must be reviewed or moved to nurture
- Close dates cannot be pushed more than twice without manager review

### Scoring Guide

- **5** -- Every deal in correct stage with verified entry criteria. All next steps are specific, dated, and assigned. No stale deals.
- **4** -- 90%+ of deals meet criteria. 1-2 deals need stage adjustment. All have next steps.
- **3** -- 75-89% of deals meet criteria. Some stale deals identified. Some vague next steps.
- **2** -- Below 75% compliance. Multiple deals in wrong stages. Pipeline is unreliable.
- **1** -- Pipeline is fiction. Deals placed by hope, not evidence.

Score <3 = pipeline freeze until audit complete.

## 4. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING

**Question:**
Has the rep set strategic traps, controlled the narrative, and positioned against competitors effectively?

### Competitive Intelligence Requirements

- Named competitors identified for every deal >$25K
- Competitor strengths and weaknesses documented
- Trap questions prepared (questions that expose competitor weaknesses)
- Landmine features identified (capabilities competitors cannot match)
- Competitive battle card referenced and adapted to specific deal

### Trap Question Framework

A trap question is a question you coach your champion to ask competitors that exposes a weakness:
- Must be specific and technical enough that the competitor cannot bluff
- Must relate to a genuine buyer need, not a gotcha
- Must have a clear "right answer" that only your solution delivers
- Must be documented with expected competitor response

### Scoring Guide

- **5** -- All competitors identified. Battle cards customized. Trap questions set and confirmed with champion. Competitor weaknesses exploited without disparaging.
- **4** -- Competitors identified. Standard battle card used. Some trap questions prepared but not all confirmed with champion.
- **3** -- Main competitor identified. Generic positioning used. No trap questions set.
- **2** -- Competitor mentioned but no strategy developed. Reactive positioning only.
- **1** -- No competitive awareness. Selling in a vacuum.

Score <3 = competitive strategy required before proposal stage.

## 5. NEGOTIATION SKILL

**Question:**
Has the rep mapped the ZOPA, prepared concessions strategically, and maintained deal value?

### ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement) Requirements

| Element | Must Document | Quality Bar |
|---------|-------------- |-------------|
| **Walk-away point** | Minimum acceptable terms | Approved by management |
| **Target outcome** | Ideal terms | Realistic and justified |
| **Buyer's ZOPA** | Estimated range of acceptable terms | Based on intel, not guess |
| **Concession plan** | Ordered list of tradeable items | Each concession has a corresponding ask |
| **Non-negotiables** | Terms that cannot change | Pre-approved by leadership |

### Concession Trading Rules

- Never give a concession without getting something in return
- Concessions must decrease in value over time (start big, shrink)
- Payment terms, not price, should be the first lever
- Every discount must be tied to a specific justification (volume, term, timing)
- Document every concession and the corresponding "get"

### Discount Governance

| Discount Level | Approval Required | Justification Required |
|---------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 0-10% | Rep authority | Standard deal note |
| 11-20% | Manager approval | Written business case |
| 21-30% | VP approval | Executive justification + competitive evidence |
| >30% | CRO/CEO approval | Strategic deal designation required |

### Scoring Guide

- **5** -- ZOPA mapped with evidence. Concession plan executed perfectly. Zero unnecessary discounting. Every give had a get. Deal value protected or expanded.
- **4** -- ZOPA estimated with reasonable basis. Most concessions traded. Discount within rep authority. Minor value leakage.
- **3** -- Basic negotiation. Some concessions given without reciprocation. Discount within policy but not strategically deployed.
- **2** -- Reactive negotiation. Discounted to close. No concession strategy. ZOPA not considered.
- **1** -- Capitulated on price. Gave concessions freely. No negotiation discipline.

Score <3 = negotiation coaching required before closing.

## 6. FORECAST ACCURACY

**Question:**
Does the rep's forecast match reality within acceptable variance?

### Forecast Accuracy Targets

| Category | Acceptable Variance | Red Flag |
|----------|-------------------|----------|
| **Commit deals** | +/- 10% of forecast | Any commit that slips = accuracy failure |
| **Best case deals** | +/- 20% of forecast | >30% miss = pipeline quality issue |
| **Pipeline total** | +/- 15% of forecast | >25% miss = systemic issue |
| **Close date accuracy** | Within 2 weeks | >4 weeks off = staging problem |

### Forecast Categories (Strict Definitions)

- **Commit**: Will close this period. Rep would bet their commission. EB has verbally agreed.
- **Best Case**: Strong probability. All stakeholders positive. Minor items remain.
- **Upside**: Possible but requires things to go right. Not in commit.
- **Pipeline**: In play but not predictable enough for forecast.

### Scoring Guide

- **5** -- Quarterly forecast within +/- 5%. Every commit deal closed. Close dates accurate within 1 week. Zero surprises.
- **4** -- Quarterly forecast within +/- 10%. One commit slip with valid reason. Close dates within 2 weeks.
- **3** -- Quarterly forecast within +/- 15%. Multiple commit slips. Some deals pulled forward. Close dates off by 2-4 weeks.
- **2** -- Quarterly forecast off by 15-25%. Significant commit misses. Regular date pushing.
- **1** -- Quarterly forecast off by >25%. Forecast is unreliable. Commits are wishes.

Score <3 = forecast methodology review required.

## 7. DEAL STRATEGY

**Question:**
Has the rep mapped the org chart, multi-threaded relationships, and built a strategic deal plan?

### Org Chart Requirements

Every deal >$25K must have:
- Org chart with key stakeholders mapped
- Role of each stakeholder (Champion, EB, Influencer, Blocker, Coach)
- Sentiment of each stakeholder (Strong positive, Positive, Neutral, Negative, Hostile)
- Access status (Direct, Through champion, No access)
- Engagement plan for each stakeholder

### Multi-Threading Requirements

| Deal Size | Minimum Threads | Required Contacts |
|-----------|----------------|-------------------|
| <$25K | 2 threads | Champion + 1 other |
| $25K-$100K | 3 threads | Champion + EB + 1 influencer |
| $100K-$500K | 4 threads | Champion + EB + 2 influencers/users |
| >$500K | 5+ threads | Champion + EB + multiple levels/departments |

### Deal Strategy Document Requirements

- Executive summary of the opportunity
- Why they will buy (compelling event, pain, timeline)
- Why they will buy from us (differentiation, relationships, proof)
- Why now (urgency drivers, cost of delay)
- Risks and mitigation plan
- Resource requirements (SE, exec sponsor, legal)

### Scoring Guide

- **5** -- Full org chart mapped with sentiment. Multi-threaded per requirements. Deal strategy document complete. Risks identified with mitigation. Executive sponsor engaged.
- **4** -- Org chart complete. Multi-threading adequate. Deal strategy exists but minor gaps. Most risks identified.
- **3** -- Key stakeholders identified but not all mapped. Single or double-threaded. Basic deal strategy.
- **2** -- Champion identified only. No org chart. Single-threaded. No deal strategy document.
- **1** -- No stakeholder mapping. Entire deal depends on one contact. No strategy.

Score <3 = deal strategy session required with manager.

## 8. POST-SALE HANDOFF

**Question:**
Is the implementation plan complete, expectations set, and CS/onboarding team properly briefed?

### Handoff Document Requirements

| Section | Contents | Quality Bar |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Deal summary** | What was sold, pricing, terms | Matches contract exactly |
| **Success criteria** | Customer's definition of success | In customer's own words |
| **Implementation plan** | Phased rollout with milestones | Dates, owners, dependencies |
| **Key contacts** | Customer stakeholders for implementation | Names, roles, communication preferences |
| **Risk register** | Known risks from sales process | Mitigation plans included |
| **Promises made** | Every commitment during sales | Verified against contract |
| **Technical requirements** | Integration needs, data migration | SE-validated |

### Handoff Meeting Requirements

- Sales, CS, and implementation team all present
- Customer success criteria reviewed and confirmed
- Implementation timeline agreed by all parties
- Escalation path established
- First 30-day milestones defined

### Scoring Guide

- **5** -- Complete handoff document. Handoff meeting conducted. All promises verified. Implementation plan customer-approved. Risk register complete. CS team confident.
- **4** -- Handoff document complete with minor gaps. Meeting conducted. Most promises documented. Implementation plan exists.
- **3** -- Basic handoff. Key information transferred but gaps exist. No formal meeting. Some promises undocumented.
- **2** -- Minimal handoff. CS team has to rediscover deal context. Implementation plan incomplete.
- **1** -- No handoff. CS team discovers deal terms from contract. No implementation plan.

Score <3 = handoff must be completed before deal is recognized.

## FAILURE CONDITIONS (AUTOMATIC TRIGGERS)

These conditions trigger immediate action regardless of overall score:

### Critical Failures (Deal Stop)

| Condition | Trigger | Required Action |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| Champion hasn't intro'd to EB by Stage 3 | Deal at risk | Escalate to manager. Develop EB access plan within 48 hours or downgrade deal. |
| Single-threaded deal >$50K | Escalation required | Manager must approve continuation. Multi-thread plan required within 1 week. |
| Discount >20% without VP approval | Policy violation | Deal frozen. Approval must be obtained retroactively. Rep coaching required. |
| No mutual action plan by proposal stage | Deal stop | Cannot send proposal until MAP is agreed with customer. |
| Close date pushed 3+ times | Forecast integrity failure | Deal must be restaged. Root cause analysis required. |
| No competitive strategy for deal >$50K | Strategy gap | Competitive workshop required before advancing. |
| Champion leaves company mid-deal | Critical risk | 48-hour new champion development plan. Deal restaged. |
| No technical validation by Stage 4 | Process violation | Technical win required before commercial discussion. |

### Warning Conditions (Monitoring Required)

| Condition | Trigger | Required Action |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| Deal velocity below average by >50% | Slow deal | Review in weekly pipeline meeting |
| Single point of contact for 30+ days | Threading risk | Multi-thread plan due within 1 week |
| No customer communication in 10+ days | Engagement gap | Outreach plan due within 48 hours |
| Proposal sent without discovery score >= 3 | Process skip | Discovery must be completed retroactively |

## FINAL SALES SCORE DECISION

**Hard Fail Dimensions (Qualification, Pipeline, Forecast):**
- Score <3 = IMMEDIATE INTERVENTION REQUIRED

**All Dimensions:**
- Weighted average >= 4.0 = DEAL/ACTIVITY MAY ADVANCE
- Weighted average 3.0-3.9 = IMPROVEMENT PLAN REQUIRED
- Weighted average < 3.0 = DEAL REVIEW / RESTRUCTURE REQUIRED

**Failure Conditions:**
- Any critical failure = DEAL STOPPED regardless of score
- Two or more warning conditions = MANAGER REVIEW

Scores must be stated explicitly before deal advances.

## ENFORCEMENT RULE

Sales quality is enforced, not assumed.
Hope is not a strategy. Pipeline is not a wish list.
Do not justify low scores. Fix the gaps.
Refactor deal strategy until standards are met.

Deals that do not meet scoring thresholds do not advance.
Reps that consistently score below 3.0 require a performance improvement plan.
Forecast misses are tracked and trend-analyzed quarterly.

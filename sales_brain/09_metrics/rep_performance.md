# Rep Performance — Quota Attainment, Ramp Analysis, and Capacity Modeling

## Why Rep Performance Analysis Matters

Individual rep performance analysis is the bridge between organizational revenue
targets and individual development. Without it, organizations treat all reps the
same — giving top performers too little attention and struggling performers too
much tolerance. The goal of rep performance analysis is not ranking — it is
diagnosis: understanding WHY a rep is performing at their level and WHAT intervention
will improve outcomes.

---

## Quota Attainment Analysis

### Attainment Distribution

The healthiest sales organizations show a roughly normal distribution of quota
attainment centered around 100%:

```
Healthy Distribution:
  <50% attainment:   5-10% of reps (performance issue)
  50-80% attainment: 15-20% of reps (developing or struggling)
  80-100% attainment: 25-30% of reps (on-track)
  100-120% attainment: 25-30% of reps (strong performers)
  120-150% attainment: 10-15% of reps (top performers)
  >150% attainment:  5-10% of reps (exceptional / windfall)
```

**Unhealthy Distributions and Their Diagnoses:**

**Bimodal (two clusters — many at 0-50% and many at 120%+):**
Diagnosis: Territory or quota imbalance. Some reps have easy territories or low
quotas; others have impossible ones. The problem is the system, not the people.

**Right-Skewed (most reps above 100%):**
Diagnosis: Quotas are too low. The organization is not stretching performance.
Increase quotas or raise targets. Alternatively, the market is expanding faster
than the plan assumed.

**Left-Skewed (most reps below 80%):**
Diagnosis: Quotas are too high, the product has a market problem, or the sales
process is broken. Investigate root cause before assuming rep performance is the issue.

### Attainment Cohort Analysis

Track attainment by cohort to identify systemic patterns:

| Cohort | Average Attainment | Variance | Insight |
|--------|-------------------|----------|---------|
| Tenure <6 months | 40-60% | High | Expected — ramp effect |
| Tenure 6-12 months | 70-90% | Moderate | Should be approaching full productivity |
| Tenure 12-24 months | 90-110% | Low | Peak productivity zone |
| Tenure >24 months | 85-105% | Moderate | Watch for plateau or territory exhaustion |
| Enterprise segment | 75-95% | High | Longer cycles = quarterly variance |
| Mid-market segment | 90-110% | Low | Shorter cycles = more predictable |
| SMB segment | 85-100% | Moderate | Volume-driven, activity-dependent |

### Attainment Trend Analysis

Single-period attainment is a snapshot. Trend analysis reveals trajectory:

**Rep Trend Categories:**
- **Ascending:** Below quota early, improving each quarter. Invest in development.
- **Consistent:** At or near quota each quarter. Stable performer, retain and develop.
- **Declining:** Was at quota, now trending down. Investigate: territory change,
  personal issue, skill gap, market shift.
- **Volatile:** Wild swings between over and under. Pipeline management issue —
  needs process coaching.
- **Stagnant-Low:** Consistently below 70% for 3+ quarters. Performance plan or exit.

## Ramp Analysis

### Ramp Curve Definition

The ramp curve describes how quickly a new hire reaches full productivity:

```
Month 1-2:  0% of quota (training, onboarding)
Month 3:    25% of quota
Month 4:    50% of quota
Month 5:    75% of quota
Month 6:    100% of quota (fully ramped)
```

**Reality Check:** These are targets. Actual ramp curves vary by:
- Rep experience level (experienced hires ramp faster)
- Sales motion complexity (enterprise ramps slower than SMB)
- Onboarding quality (structured programs ramp 30-40% faster)
- Market conditions (hot market = faster, down market = slower)
- Product complexity (simple product = faster, platform = slower)

### Ramp Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Benchmark |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Time to First Activity | Days from start to first outbound prospecting | <14 days |
| Time to First Meeting | Days from start to first buyer meeting | <30 days |
| Time to First Pipeline | Days from start to first qualified opportunity | <45 days |
| Time to First Deal | Days from start to first closed-won deal | <90 days (SMB), <180 days (enterprise) |
| Time to Full Quota | Months from start to first full-quota month | 4-6 months (SMB), 6-9 months (enterprise) |
| Ramp Quota Hit Rate | % of new hires who hit ramp quotas | >70% (good), >85% (great) |

### Ramp Cost Analysis

New hires are the most expensive investment in a sales organization:

```
Ramp Cost Calculation:
  Months 1-2 (0% productivity):
    Salary + Benefits = $20K/month x 2 = $40K
    Revenue Generated: $0
    Net Cost: -$40K

  Month 3 (25% productivity):
    Cost: $20K
    Revenue: 25% of $67K monthly quota = $16.7K
    Net Cost: -$3.3K

  Month 4 (50% productivity):
    Cost: $20K
    Revenue: 50% of $67K = $33.5K
    Net Contribution: +$13.5K

  Month 5 (75% productivity):
    Cost: $20K
    Revenue: 75% of $67K = $50.3K
    Net Contribution: +$30.3K

  Month 6 (100% productivity):
    Cost: $20K
    Revenue: $67K
    Net Contribution: +$47K

  Cumulative to Breakeven: ~4.5 months
  Total Ramp Investment: ~$40K net cost before breakeven
```

Add recruiting cost ($20-30K), onboarding materials ($2-5K), and tools ($500/month),
and a single new AE hire costs $60-80K before producing positive ROI. This is why
retention of productive reps is more valuable than aggressive hiring.

### Ramp Improvement Levers

| Lever | Impact | Implementation |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| Structured onboarding | 30-40% ramp reduction | 30-60-90 day program |
| Pipeline seeding | 15-25% faster first deal | Transfer warm leads to new rep |
| Buddy/mentor program | 10-20% quality improvement | Pair with top performer |
| Call shadowing | 15-20% skill acceleration | 10+ observed calls in first month |
| Role-play certification | 10-15% competency acceleration | Certified before first solo call |
| Territory optimization | 20-30% faster pipeline | Pre-qualified accounts in territory |

## Rep Scoring Models

### Multi-Dimensional Performance Score

Single-metric evaluation (quota attainment only) is incomplete. A comprehensive
scoring model evaluates across multiple dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Components | Scoring Method |
|-----------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| Revenue Results | 35% | Quota attainment, deal count, ACV growth | % of target |
| Pipeline Management | 20% | Coverage ratio, pipeline created, velocity | % of target |
| Process Adherence | 15% | MEDDPICC completion, CRM hygiene, forecast accuracy | Compliance rate |
| Skill Development | 15% | Call quality (Gong), certification, coaching engagement | Scorecard (1-5) |
| Strategic Contribution | 15% | New logo acquisition, strategic account growth, mentoring | Manager assessment (1-5) |

### Scoring Calculation

```
Performance Score = (Revenue x 0.35) + (Pipeline x 0.20) + (Process x 0.15)
                    + (Skill x 0.15) + (Strategic x 0.15)

Example:
  Revenue: 95% attainment = 95 points
  Pipeline: 110% of target = 100 points (capped)
  Process: 85% compliance = 85 points
  Skill: 4.0/5.0 = 80 points
  Strategic: 3.5/5.0 = 70 points

  Score = (95 x 0.35) + (100 x 0.20) + (85 x 0.15) + (80 x 0.15) + (70 x 0.15)
       = 33.25 + 20.00 + 12.75 + 12.00 + 10.50
       = 88.5 → "A Player"
```

### Performance Categories and Actions

| Category | Score Range | % of Team | Action |
|----------|-----------|-----------|--------|
| A+ | 95-100 | 5% | Promote, equity, leadership opportunities |
| A | 85-94 | 15-20% | Retain aggressively, develop for leadership |
| B+ | 75-84 | 25-30% | Targeted coaching on specific dimensions |
| B | 65-74 | 25-30% | Increased coaching cadence, skill development plan |
| C | 50-64 | 10-15% | Performance improvement plan (30-60 days) |
| D | <50 | 5-10% | Accelerated PIP or role change/exit |

## Capacity Modeling

### What Is Capacity Modeling?

Capacity modeling determines how many quota-carrying reps are needed to achieve a
revenue target, accounting for ramp, attrition, and productivity variance.

### Basic Capacity Model

```
Required Revenue:                   $20,000,000
Average Quota per Rep:              $    800,000
Average Attainment:                         80%
Effective Revenue per Rep:          $    640,000
Raw Headcount Needed:               20M / 640K = 31.25 → 32 reps

Attrition Adjustment (20% annual):
  Reps lost per year:              32 x 0.20 = 6.4 → 7 reps
  Reps in ramp at any time:        7 reps (new hires replacing attrition)
  Ramp productivity:               50% average for first 6 months
  Ramp revenue loss:               7 x ($640K x 0.50 x 0.50) = $1,120,000
  Additional reps needed:          $1.12M / $640K = 1.75 → 2 reps

Total Headcount Plan:               32 + 2 = 34 quota-carrying reps

Plus:
  SDR/BDRs (2:1 AE ratio):         17 SDRs
  Sales Managers (6:1 span):        6 managers
  Sales Ops:                        2-3 FTE
  Enablement:                       1-2 FTE
  Total Sales Org:                  ~60 FTE
```

### Advanced Capacity Model (Segment-Based)

| Segment | Quota | Attainment | Effective | Target Revenue | Reps Needed |
|---------|-------|-----------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Enterprise | $1.2M | 70% | $840K | $8M | 10 |
| Mid-Market | $800K | 80% | $640K | $8M | 13 |
| SMB | $500K | 85% | $425K | $4M | 10 |
| **Total** | | | | **$20M** | **33** |

### Capacity Model Sensitivity Analysis

Test the model against different scenarios:

| Scenario | Attainment | Reps Needed | Variance |
|----------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| Optimistic (90% attain) | 90% | 28 | -5 reps |
| Base Case (80% attain) | 80% | 33 | Baseline |
| Pessimistic (70% attain) | 70% | 38 | +5 reps |
| New Market (60% attain) | 60% | 45 | +12 reps |

### Hiring Timeline Planning

Work backward from the revenue target to determine when hires must start:

```
Q4 Revenue Target: $5M
Reps needed at full productivity by Q4: 8 new reps
Average ramp: 6 months to full productivity
Required hire date: Q2 (6 months before Q4)
Recruiting lead time: 60-90 days
Recruiting must begin: Q1

Timeline:
  Jan-Mar: Recruit 8 AEs
  Apr-Jun: Onboard and ramp (25-75% productivity)
  Jul-Sep: Approaching full productivity (75-100%)
  Oct-Dec: Full productivity contributing to Q4 target
```

## Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)

### When to PIP

A PIP is appropriate when:
- Rep has been fully ramped for 2+ quarters
- Performance is consistently below 65% of quota
- Coaching and support have been provided and documented
- Root cause is skill or effort (not territory, quota, or product issue)

### PIP Structure

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Duration | 30-60 days (30 for clear underperformance, 60 if there is ambiguity) |
| Metrics | Specific, measurable targets (not just "improve performance") |
| Support | Documented coaching, additional resources, reduced admin burden |
| Check-ins | Weekly written progress reviews |
| Outcome | Clear binary: meets targets = exits PIP, does not meet = role change or separation |

### PIP Metrics Example

```
Current Performance: 45% quota attainment
PIP Target: 65% quota attainment (not full quota — realistic improvement)

30-Day Metrics:
  - Pipeline created: $200K minimum
  - Discovery calls completed: 20 minimum
  - Proposal meetings: 4 minimum
  - CRM compliance: 95%+
  - Coaching session attendance: 100%

Weekly Check-In:
  Week 1: Activity metrics on track? If not, daily check-in.
  Week 2: Pipeline building? Quality assessment.
  Week 3: Deal progression? Are proposals being delivered?
  Week 4: Revenue outlook. Will targets be met?
```

---

**Rep performance analysis is not judgment — it is diagnosis. The goal is to
understand what drives individual performance and build systems that elevate
the entire team, not just celebrate the top and punish the bottom.**

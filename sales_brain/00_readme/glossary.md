# Sales Brain — Glossary

## Purpose

This glossary defines terms used throughout the Sales Brain. Definitions are grounded
in academic and practitioner literature. When terms have multiple industry definitions,
the Sales Brain canonical definition is provided.

---

## A

**ABC (Always Be Closing)** — Outdated transactional sales philosophy popularized by
Glengarry Glen Ross. Empirically disproven by Rackham (1988) who showed that in
complex sales, traditional closing techniques decrease win rates by 17%. The Sales
Brain explicitly rejects ABC in favor of consultative methodologies.

**Account Executive (AE)** — Full-cycle seller responsible for pipeline creation
(in some models), opportunity advancement, negotiation, and closing. Carries a
quota measured in ARR, ACV, or bookings. Distinguished from SDRs who handle
prospecting and CSMs who handle post-sale.

**Account Plan** — Strategic document mapping an account's organizational structure,
buying committee, whitespace opportunities, competitive landscape, and expansion
strategy. Updated quarterly at minimum. See Templates/account_plan_template.md.

**ACV (Annual Contract Value)** — The annualized revenue value of a subscription
contract. For multi-year deals: TCV / contract length in years. Distinct from ARR
which measures run-rate across the entire customer base.

**ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** — The annualized value of all active recurring
subscription contracts. ARR = MRR x 12. Includes new, expansion, and contraction.
Excludes one-time fees, professional services, and churned revenue.

**ASP (Average Selling Price)** — Mean deal size across closed-won opportunities in
a given period. Segmented by motion (inbound vs outbound), segment (SMB vs Enterprise),
and product line for meaningful analysis.

---

## B

**BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)** — Legacy qualification framework
(IBM, 1960s). Criticized for being seller-centric and insufficient for complex B2B.
Superseded by MEDDIC/MEDDPICC in enterprise contexts. Still useful for transactional
and SMB motions where buying complexity is low.

**BATNA (Best Alternative to a Negotiated Agreement)** — Fisher & Ury (Getting to
Yes, 1981). The most favorable outcome a party can achieve without negotiation. A
seller's BATNA is typically the next-best deal in pipeline. A buyer's BATNA is the
next-best vendor or status quo. The party with the stronger BATNA holds leverage.

**Bluebird** — An unexpected, unforecasted deal that closes with minimal effort.
Bluebirds are excluded from pipeline velocity calculations and forecast models
because they are not repeatable or predictable.

**Bookings** — The total value of signed contracts in a period. Can be measured as
ACV (annual), TCV (total), or net-new (excluding renewals). The Sales Brain standard
is new-logo ACV + expansion ACV unless otherwise specified.

**Buyer's Journey** — The process a buyer follows from problem recognition through
vendor selection and purchase. Gartner (2019) identifies six "jobs" buyers must
complete: problem identification, solution exploration, requirements building,
supplier selection, validation, and consensus creation. The sales process must
map to this journey, not impose a seller-centric sequence.

---

## C

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — Total cost to acquire a customer. Fully loaded
CAC includes: sales compensation, marketing spend, sales tools, overhead, and
allocated management cost. CAC = total S&M spend / new customers acquired.

**Champion** — An internal advocate within the buyer organization who has power,
influence, and active motivation to advance the deal. Per MEDDIC, a true champion
must pass three tests: (1) they have personal win (vested interest), (2) they have
access to Economic Buyer, (3) they are willing to sell on your behalf internally.
A contact who is merely friendly is a Coach, not a Champion.

**CHAMP (Challenges, Authority, Money, Prioritization)** — Buyer-centric alternative
to BANT. Starts with buyer's challenges rather than seller's budget question.

**Churned Revenue** — ARR lost from customers who cancel subscriptions. Gross churn
excludes expansion; net churn (net revenue retention) includes expansion. Net
revenue retention >100% indicates expansion exceeds churn.

**Close Rate / Win Rate** — Percentage of qualified opportunities that result in
closed-won. Calculated as: closed-won / (closed-won + closed-lost). Exclude
open opportunities. Segment by source, ACV band, and rep cohort for actionable
analysis. Industry benchmarks: 20-30% for new logo, 60-80% for expansion.

**Commercial Teaching** — Core Challenger Sale concept. The seller teaches the
buyer something new about their business that reframes the problem and leads
naturally to the seller's differentiated solution. Distinguished from product
pitching by its focus on buyer insight rather than seller capabilities.

**Constructive Tension** — Challenger concept where the seller respectfully pushes
back on the buyer's assumptions or stated requirements. Research shows top
performers create productive discomfort that moves buyers from status quo. Not
confrontation — it is informed disagreement backed by insight.

**Conversion Rate** — Percentage of opportunities advancing from one pipeline stage
to the next. Stage-by-stage conversion rates are the atomic units of pipeline math.
Overall win rate = product of all stage conversion rates.

**CPQ (Configure, Price, Quote)** — Software that automates product configuration,
pricing rules, and quote generation. Reduces quote errors, enforces pricing
governance, and accelerates deal velocity.

---

## D

**Dark (Gone Dark)** — When a prospect stops responding to all communication
channels. Common causes: internal priority shift, champion departure, competitive
loss without notification, or deal deprioritization. Requires multi-thread
reactivation strategy.

**Deal Desk** — Cross-functional team (Sales, Finance, Legal, Product) that reviews
and approves non-standard deal structures. Typical triggers: >X% discount, custom
terms, multi-year commitments, or bundled pricing.

**Decision Criteria** — The formal and informal standards by which a buyer evaluates
vendors. In MEDDIC, you must know (not guess) the decision criteria and have
influenced them to favor your solution's differentiation. Criteria you cannot win
on must be neutralized or reweighted.

**Decision Process** — The sequence of steps, approvals, and reviews a buyer follows
to make a purchase decision. Includes: evaluation process, approval chain,
procurement requirements, legal review, security review, and budget approval.
In MEDDIC, you must map this process with dates for each step.

---

## E

**Economic Buyer** — The person with final authority to approve the purchase and
allocate budget. In MEDDIC, there is always exactly one Economic Buyer. They can
say yes when everyone else says no, and can say no when everyone else says yes.
Access to the Economic Buyer is a critical qualification criterion.

**Expansion Revenue** — Additional ARR from existing customers through upsell
(higher tier), cross-sell (additional products), or seat expansion. Expansion is
typically 3-5x more efficient than new-logo acquisition (lower CAC, shorter cycle).

---

## F

**Forecast Categories** — Standard pipeline classification for forecasting:
- **Commit:** >90% probability, deal will close this period. Miss = rep credibility issue.
- **Best Case:** 60-80% probability, strong signal but risk factors remain.
- **Upside:** 30-50% probability, possible but not relied upon.
- **Pipeline:** <30% probability, early stage opportunities.
- **Omit:** Deals removed from forecast due to disqualification.

**FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt)** — Competitive tactic of introducing anxiety about
a competitor's viability, roadmap, or risk profile. The Sales Brain permits
fact-based competitive differentiation but prohibits fabricated or misleading FUD.
Ethical alternative: "trap setting" with genuine differentiators.

---

## L

**Land and Expand** — GTM strategy where initial deal is intentionally small (the
"land") with a planned expansion path. Reduces buyer risk, shortens initial sales
cycle, and creates proof points for larger investment. Requires deliberate
expansion triggers and account planning.

**LTV (Lifetime Value)** — Total revenue expected from a customer over the
relationship lifetime. LTV = ARPA x gross margin x (1 / churn rate). LTV:CAC
ratio >3:1 indicates healthy unit economics. <1:1 indicates unsustainable
acquisition.

---

## M

**Magic Number** — Sales efficiency metric. Net new ARR / prior quarter S&M spend.
>1.0 = efficient, invest more. 0.5-1.0 = acceptable. <0.5 = efficiency problem.
Measures how much ARR each dollar of S&M spend produces.

**MEDDIC** — Enterprise deal qualification framework: Metrics, Economic Buyer,
Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion. Each element is
scored to assess deal health and identify gaps.

**MEDDPICC** — Extended MEDDIC adding Paper Process (procurement, legal, security)
and Competition. Reflects the reality that enterprise deals face procurement
friction and competitive dynamics that MEDDIC underweights.

**MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)** — Lead meeting marketing-defined criteria for
sales follow-up. MQL definitions vary; the Sales Brain requires explicit SLA
defining MQL criteria, response time, and feedback loop.

**Multi-Threading** — Strategy of building relationships with multiple stakeholders
in the buying organization. Reduces single-point-of-failure risk if one contact
leaves, goes dark, or loses influence. Minimum 3 contacts across 2 departments
for enterprise deals.

---

## N

**NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** — Percentage of ARR retained from existing customers
including expansion and contraction. NRR = (beginning ARR + expansion - contraction
- churn) / beginning ARR. Elite SaaS: >120%. Good: 100-120%. Concerning: <100%.

---

## O

**OTE (On-Target Earnings)** — Total expected compensation when a rep achieves 100%
quota. OTE = base salary + target variable (commission at quota). Standard splits:
50/50 for hunters, 60/40 or 70/30 for farmers/AM roles.

**Outbound** — Proactive prospecting where sellers initiate contact with target
accounts. Contrasted with inbound (buyer initiates). Outbound typically has
higher CAC, longer cycle, but larger ACV. Channels: email, phone, social,
events, direct mail.

---

## P

**Pain (Identified Pain)** — In MEDDIC, the business problem the buyer is actively
trying to solve. Pain must be quantified (cost of inaction) and connected to
organizational priorities. Latent pain (buyer unaware) requires commercial
teaching to activate. Admitted pain (buyer acknowledges) enables solution
positioning.

**Pipeline Coverage** — Ratio of total pipeline value to quota for a period.
Formula: total pipeline / quota target. Standard benchmark: 3x for enterprise,
4x for mid-market, 5x for SMB. Accounts for conversion rates and deal attrition.

**Pipeline Velocity** — Rate at which pipeline converts to revenue. Formula:
(number of opportunities x average deal value x win rate) / average sales cycle
in days. Measures daily revenue generation capacity of the pipeline.

---

## Q

**QBR (Quarterly Business Review)** — Structured review of account performance,
health, and expansion opportunities. Conducted with customers (external QBR)
and internally (internal QBR). See Templates/qbr_template.md.

**Quota** — Revenue target assigned to a seller for a defined period. Quota should
be achievable by 60-70% of ramped reps. Quota:OTE ratio (quota / OTE) typically
4-6x, meaning reps should generate 4-6x their OTE in revenue.

---

## S

**Sales Cycle** — Duration from opportunity creation to closed-won. Measured in
days. Varies dramatically by ACV: <$10K = 14-30 days, $10-50K = 30-60 days,
$50-250K = 60-120 days, >$250K = 120-270 days. Segment by source and motion.

**SLA (Service Level Agreement)** — In sales context, the agreement between
marketing and sales defining lead quality, follow-up time, and feedback
requirements. Marketing commits to X qualified leads; Sales commits to Y-minute
response time and Z-touch follow-up sequence.

**SPIN** — Rackham's questioning methodology: Situation (context), Problem
(difficulties), Implication (consequences), Need-Payoff (value of solving).
Empirically validated sequence that moves buyers from problem awareness to
solution urgency.

**SQL (Sales Qualified Lead)** — Lead accepted by sales as meeting qualification
criteria after initial discovery. Stricter than MQL. Typically requires: confirmed
budget or business case, identified decision-maker, defined timeline, and
articulated pain.

---

## T

**TCV (Total Contract Value)** — Full value of a contract over its entire term.
For a 3-year deal at $100K/year, TCV = $300K, ACV = $100K.

---

## W

**Weighted Pipeline** — Pipeline value adjusted by probability of close. Each stage
has a default probability: Discovery (10%), Qualification (20%), Demo/Eval (40%),
Proposal (60%), Negotiation (80%), Verbal Commit (90%). Weighted pipeline = sum
of (deal value x stage probability) for all deals.

**Whitespace** — Untapped revenue potential within an existing account. Identified
by mapping current product usage against the full product portfolio and the
account's total addressable spend. Whitespace analysis drives expansion strategy.

---

## Z

**ZOPA (Zone of Possible Agreement)** — The range between the seller's minimum
acceptable terms and the buyer's maximum willingness to pay. If ZOPA exists,
negotiation can succeed. If no ZOPA exists, the deal cannot close at mutually
acceptable terms. Identifying ZOPA requires understanding both parties' BATNA.

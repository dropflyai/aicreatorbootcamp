# Partner Management — Onboarding, Enablement, Planning, and Co-Selling

## The Partner Lifecycle

Partner management follows a lifecycle analogous to customer success: onboard,
enable, activate, grow, and renew. Just as customer churn reflects failure in
the post-sale experience, partner inactivation reflects failure in the partner
experience. The partner manager's job is to make partners successful — because
when partners succeed, your revenue through the channel grows.

```
Recruit → Onboard → Enable → Activate → Co-Sell → Grow → Renew/Upgrade
```

## Partner Onboarding

### Why Partner Onboarding Fails

Most partner programs onboard with a login to a portal and a "welcome" webinar.
This is insufficient. Partners have their own businesses to run, their own products
to sell, and limited bandwidth to invest in learning yours. Onboarding must be
structured, time-bound, and value-demonstrating from day one.

### Partner Onboarding Program (30-Day Framework)

#### Week 1: Foundation

| Day | Activity | Format | Outcome |
|-----|----------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Welcome and program overview | 1:1 with partner manager | Expectations set |
| 2 | Product fundamentals training | Self-paced LMS module (2 hours) | Product quiz passed |
| 3 | Target customer and ICP alignment | 1:1 workshop (60 min) | Shared ICP documented |
| 4 | Demo environment setup | Self-serve with support | Partner has working demo |
| 5 | Portal walkthrough | Recorded training + live Q&A | Portal proficiency |

#### Week 2: Sales Readiness

| Day | Activity | Format | Outcome |
|-----|----------|--------|---------|
| 6-7 | Value proposition and positioning | Live training (90 min) | Can articulate value prop |
| 8-9 | Competitive positioning | Battle card review + role-play | Understands competitive landscape |
| 10 | Pricing and packaging | Self-paced + 1:1 review | Can structure deals |

#### Week 3: Technical Readiness

| Day | Activity | Format | Outcome |
|-----|----------|--------|---------|
| 11-12 | Technical deep dive | Live SE-led session (2 hours) | Understands architecture |
| 13 | Integration and implementation | Documentation review + Q&A | Can scope implementations |
| 14-15 | Demo certification | Practice demo + certification assessment | Demo certified |

#### Week 4: Activation

| Day | Activity | Format | Outcome |
|-----|----------|--------|---------|
| 16-17 | Joint account mapping | 1:1 workshop (90 min) | 10+ target accounts identified |
| 18-19 | First co-marketing activity | Joint webinar planning or co-branded email | Marketing activity scheduled |
| 20 | Onboarding graduation and plan | 1:1 with partner manager | 90-day activation plan documented |

### Onboarding Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Onboarding completion rate | >85% | % completing all modules |
| Time to first deal registration | <60 days | Days from onboarding start |
| Demo certification pass rate | >90% | Assessment score |
| Account mapping completion | 100% | Target accounts identified |
| Partner satisfaction with onboarding | >4.0/5.0 | Post-onboarding survey |

## Partner Enablement

### Ongoing Enablement Framework

Onboarding is the beginning. Ongoing enablement ensures partners maintain competence
and grow capability over time. Without continuous enablement, partner effectiveness
decays as products evolve, markets shift, and competitors adjust.

### Enablement Content Library

| Content Type | Purpose | Update Frequency | Delivery |
|-------------|---------|-----------------|----------|
| Product Update Briefs | Keep partners current on new features | Monthly | Email + portal |
| Battle Cards | Competitive positioning for active competitors | Monthly | Portal + Slack |
| Sales Playbooks | How to sell specific use cases or to specific verticals | Quarterly | Portal |
| Technical Guides | Implementation, integration, and configuration | As needed | Portal |
| Case Studies | Customer success stories partners can share | Monthly | Portal + email |
| Pricing Calculator | Dynamic pricing tool for deal structuring | Real-time | Portal tool |
| ROI Calculator | Value quantification tool for buyer conversations | Quarterly | Portal tool |
| Demo Scripts | Guided demo paths for common use cases | Quarterly | Portal |

### Partner Certification Tiers

| Tier | Requirements | Benefits |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Registered | Complete onboarding, sign partner agreement | Portal access, basic margin |
| Silver | 2+ certified reps, $50K+ annual revenue | Enhanced margin (+5%), co-marketing fund |
| Gold | 4+ certified reps, $200K+ annual revenue, 2+ customer references | Premium margin (+10%), dedicated partner manager, MDF |
| Platinum | 8+ certified reps, $500K+ annual revenue, 5+ references, joint business plan | Maximum margin (+15%), strategic co-selling, executive alignment, product advisory board seat |

### Certification Requirements

**Sales Certification:**
- Complete product training modules (4 hours self-paced)
- Pass written assessment (85%+ score)
- Deliver certified demo (live evaluation by your SE)
- Complete competitive positioning assessment

**Technical Certification:**
- Complete architecture and integration training (8 hours)
- Pass technical assessment (80%+ score)
- Complete a practice implementation (sandbox environment)
- Demonstrate proficiency in common integration scenarios

**Recertification:** Required annually. If partner falls below tier requirements
for two consecutive quarters, downgrade with 30-day notice.

reviewed quarterly and updated annually.

## Co-Selling

### Co-Sell Motion Types

| Motion | Description | Your Role | Partner Role |
|--------|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Partner-Led | Partner owns the deal, you support | SE support, pricing, references | Discovery, demo, negotiation, close |
| Partner-Sourced | Partner creates the opportunity, you co-sell | Joint discovery, demo, close | Introduction, relationship leverage |
| Your-Sourced Co-Sell | You identify the opportunity, partner adds value | Lead seller, deal management | Services, integration, trust bridge |
| Co-Execute | Both parties actively sell together | Product positioning, technical | Services positioning, relationship |

### Co-Sell Engagement Protocol

**Step 1: Opportunity Identification**
Partner registers deal or your AE identifies partner-fit opportunity. Both
parties agree to engage.

**Step 2: Joint Discovery**
Schedule joint discovery call with both partner and your team. Align on buyer
pain, decision process, and roles. Document in shared CRM or deal sheet.

**Step 3: Role Definition**
Agree on:
- Who leads each meeting?
- Who manages the relationship with each stakeholder?
- Who delivers the demo?
- Who handles pricing and negotiation?
- Who manages post-sale implementation?

**Step 4: Joint Execution**
Execute the deal according to defined roles. Weekly check-ins between partner
and your team. Shared deal tracker.

**Step 5: Deal Close**
Coordinate on contract structure (direct sale + partner services, or resale).
Align on pricing, margin, and customer expectations.

**Step 6: Joint Handoff**
Both teams participate in customer handoff. Implementation plan includes both
partner and your CS team responsibilities.

### Co-Sell Rules of Engagement

1. **Transparency:** Both parties share relevant deal information openly
2. **Respect:** Neither party poaches the other's customer or undermines position
3. **Accountability:** Both parties deliver on commitments made to the customer
4. **Communication:** Weekly deal sync for all active co-sell opportunities
5. **Conflict Resolution:** Escalation to partner manager within 24 hours of conflict
6. **Customer First:** Disagreements are resolved based on what is best for the customer

|-----------|--------|---------|-------------|
| Revenue | 30% | Actual vs. plan, growth rate | |

| Strategic Alignment | 10% | Business plan adherence, executive engagement | |

### Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Agenda

| Segment | Duration | Content |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Performance Review | 15 min | Scorecard review, revenue vs. plan |
| Pipeline Review | 15 min | Active deals, co-sell status, new opportunities |
| Market Insights | 10 min | Competitive dynamics, customer feedback, market trends |
| Enablement Update | 10 min | New product features, content, certification updates |
| Business Plan Alignment | 10 min | Next quarter activities, investment, targets |
| Open Discussion | 15 min | Issues, ideas, requests, strategic alignment |
| Action Items | 5 min | Documented commitments with owners and dates |

### Partner Health Indicators

**Healthy Partner:**
- Registering 2+ deals per month
- Closing 1+ deal per quarter
- Maintaining certifications
- Participating in co-marketing activities
- Responsive to communications within 24 hours

**At-Risk Partner:**
- No deal registrations in 60+ days
- No closed revenue in 2+ quarters
- Certifications expired or not maintained
- Declining meeting attendance
- Slow or no response to communications

**At-Risk Intervention:**
1. Schedule health check meeting with partner principal
2. Identify barriers (internal changes, competitive pressure, product issues)
3. Develop 90-day recovery plan with specific milestones
4. Increase partner manager touchpoint frequency
5. If no improvement in 90 days, evaluate tier downgrade or program exit

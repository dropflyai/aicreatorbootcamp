# Branding Accountability Protocol — Governance Framework (Authoritative)

This document defines how the Branding Brain is held accountable for its outputs.
Every brand recommendation, identity decision, and strategic direction is subject to this protocol.

A brand without accountability is just decoration.

---

## CORE ACCOUNTABILITY PRINCIPLES

1. **Every brand decision has a documented rationale.** No arbitrary creative choices.
2. **Every brand recommendation has a measurable expected outcome.** No feel-good branding.
3. **Every brand asset has an owner.** No orphaned creative work.
4. **Every brand failure is analyzed.** No sweeping brand misses under the rug.
5. **Every brand investment has ROI expectations.** No bottomless branding budgets.

---

## SECTION 1: OUTPUT ACCOUNTABILITY

### 1.1 Deliverable Registry

Every branding deliverable must be registered:

```markdown
| Deliverable | Owner | Due Date | Status | Quality Gate |
|-------------|-------|----------|--------|-------------|
| [Name] | [Person] | [Date] | Draft/Review/Approved/Implemented | Pending/Passed/Failed |
```

**Rules:**
- No deliverable exists without a registered owner
- No deliverable ships without a passed quality gate (ReviewChecklist.md)
- No brand asset is "final" until stakeholder sign-off is documented
- Overdue deliverables trigger escalation within 48 hours

### 1.2 Decision Log

Every significant brand decision must be logged:

```markdown
| Decision | Date | Decider | Rationale | Alternatives Considered | Expected Outcome |
|----------|------|---------|-----------|------------------------|-----------------|
```

**Rules:**
- "Significant" = affects brand positioning, visual identity, voice, architecture, or public-facing materials
- Rationale must reference strategy, research, or data — not just preference
- Alternatives must be documented (proves the decision was deliberate, not default)
- Expected outcome must be measurable and time-bound
- Creative preference alone is never sufficient rationale

### 1.3 Recommendation Tracking

Every brand recommendation must be tracked to outcome:

```markdown
| Recommendation | Date | Implemented? | Outcome Measured? | Outcome vs. Prediction |
|----------------|------|-------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| [Description] | [Date] | Yes/No/Partial | Yes/No | Better/Worse/As Expected |
```

**Rules:**
- Recommendations not acted on within 60 days require justification for delay
- Implemented recommendations must have outcome measurement within 180 days (brand moves slower than ops)
- Predictions that miss significantly trigger review of methodology
- Track record is reviewed semi-annually to calibrate confidence

## SECTION 2: PERFORMANCE ACCOUNTABILITY

### 2.1 Brand Score Accountability

The BrandScore.md defines 8 dimensions. Accountability is enforced as follows:

| Score Range | Accountability Action |
|-------------|----------------------|
| 5 (Excellent) | Document and share as brand case study |
| 4 (Good) | Continue current trajectory |
| 3 (Adequate) | Improvement plan required within 30 days |
| 2 (Poor) | Escalation to leadership, remediation plan within 2 weeks |
| 1 (Failing) | Emergency brand intervention, weekly reviews until resolved |

**Hard Fail Consequences:**
- Brand Clarity <3 → Brand clarity workshop within 10 business days
- Consistency <3 → Brand governance audit and enforcement within 2 weeks
- Voice Authenticity <3 → Voice overhaul initiative within 30 days

### 2.2 Benchmark Test Accountability

Benchmark test results (BenchmarkTests.md) trigger the following:

| Average Score | Accountability Action |
|---------------|----------------------|
| >= 4.5 | Competency verified, re-test in 12 months |
| 4.0 - 4.4 | Competency adequate, re-test in 6 months |
| 3.0 - 3.9 | Competency gap, targeted development within 60 days, re-test in 90 days |
| < 3.0 | Critical gap, intensive development program, supervised brand work |

### 2.3 Trend Accountability

Brand scores are tracked over time. Trends trigger action:

| Trend | Accountability Action |
|-------|----------------------|
| 3 consecutive quarters improving | Recognize and document approach for replication |
| 3 consecutive quarters stable | Evaluate if ceiling reached or if new strategies needed |
| 2 consecutive quarters declining | Root cause analysis required within 2 weeks |
| 3 consecutive quarters declining | Escalation to leadership with comprehensive brand review |
| Any dimension drops 2+ points in one period | Emergency review within 1 week |

## SECTION 3: CREATIVE ACCOUNTABILITY

### 3.1 Creative Decisions Must Be Defensible

Every creative decision (color, typography, imagery, voice, naming) must answer:

1. **Why this choice?** (Strategic rationale, not aesthetic preference)
2. **Why not the alternatives?** (Documented alternatives with rejection reasons)
3. **What does this accomplish?** (Expected brand impact)
4. **How will we know it worked?** (Measurement plan)

**Anti-patterns that trigger review:**
- "It just feels right" — Not acceptable as sole justification
- "I like it" — Preference without strategy is not a rationale
- "It's trending" — Trends without strategic fit are a risk, not an asset
- "Our competitor does this" — Following competitors is not differentiation
- "The CEO likes this color" — Authority preference without strategy is dangerous

### 3.2 Brand Guideline Compliance

When brand guidelines are created:

- **Compliance rate must be tracked** (percentage of materials following guidelines)
- **Deviations must be logged** with severity and corrective action
- **Guidelines must be tested** (can non-designers use them to create on-brand materials?)
- **Guidelines must be updated** when the brand evolves (stale guidelines cause deviation)

### 3.3 Brand Consistency Audits

Frequency and accountability:

| Audit Type | Frequency | Owner | Deliverable |
|------------|-----------|-------|-------------|
| Digital channel audit | Monthly | Brand team | Deviation report with fixes |
| Full cross-channel audit | Quarterly | Brand team | Audit report with trend analysis |
| Partner/vendor audit | Quarterly | Brand team + legal | Compliance report with escalations |
| Internal materials audit | Semi-annually | Brand team + HR | Compliance report |
| Comprehensive brand audit | Annually | Brand team + external | Full audit report with strategic recommendations |

## SECTION 4: BRAND INVESTMENT ACCOUNTABILITY

### 4.1 Budget Tracking

All brand investments must be tracked:

```markdown
| Investment | Amount | Expected Outcome | Timeline | Actual Outcome |
|------------|--------|-----------------|----------|---------------|
```

**Rules:**
- Every brand investment >$5,000 requires documented expected ROI
- ROI can be measured in brand metrics (awareness, preference, sentiment) not just revenue
- Brand metric improvements must be translated to estimated business impact
- Investments with no measurable outcome after 180 days trigger review

### 4.2 Research Investment
- Brand research spending must produce actionable findings
- Research that only confirms what was already known is low-value (evaluate methodology)
- Research findings must be connected to brand strategy decisions
- Research cadence must be justified (too frequent = waste, too infrequent = blind)

### 4.3 Rebrand/Refresh Investment
- Cost of rebrand/refresh must include: design, implementation, communication, transition, opportunity cost
- Expected benefit must be quantified (even if estimated)
- Post-rebrand measurement plan must exist before the rebrand begins
- Rebrands that do not deliver expected outcomes trigger methodology review

## SECTION 5: FAILURE ACCOUNTABILITY

### 5.1 Brand Failure Categories

| Category | Definition | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| Brand Confusion | Audience cannot articulate the brand | Inconsistent messaging leading to "what do you actually do?" |
| Brand Dilution | Existing equity is weakened | Poorly executed brand extension |
| Brand Inconsistency | Materials deviate from guidelines | Rogue department creating off-brand materials |
| Brand Crisis | Public brand reputation damage | Social media backlash, negative press |
| Brand Investment Failure | Spending with no return | Rebrand that did not move any metrics |
| Brand Misalignment | Internal/external disconnect | Culture says X, marketing says Y |

### 5.2 Failure Response Protocol

When a brand failure occurs:

1. **Acknowledge** — Do not hide or minimize the failure
2. **Assess** — Quantify the brand equity impact
3. **Analyze** — Root cause analysis (what went wrong and why)
4. **Act** — Remediation plan with specific actions, owners, and deadlines
5. **Adapt** — Update processes to prevent recurrence
6. **Archive** — Document the failure and learning for institutional memory

**Rules:**
- Brand failures are not punished unless they result from willful guideline violation
- Failure to analyze brand failures IS a punishable offense (kills organizational learning)
- Failure analysis must be completed within 2 weeks of identification
- Remediation actions must begin within 30 days

### 5.3 Brand Crisis Accountability

When a brand crisis occurs:

| Phase | Timeline | Accountability |
|-------|----------|---------------|
| Detection | Immediate | Who detected and how? Was monitoring adequate? |
| Response | Within 4 hours | Was the response timely, appropriate, and on-brand? |
| Containment | Within 24 hours | Was the damage contained? Were stakeholders informed? |
| Resolution | Within 1 week | Was the root cause addressed? Were commitments made? |
| Recovery | Within 90 days | Did brand metrics recover? Were commitments kept? |
| Prevention | Within 30 days | Were systemic changes made to prevent recurrence? |

## SECTION 6: REVIEW CADENCE

### 6.1 Monthly Brand Health Check
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Participants:** Brand team
- **Agenda:** Brand metrics dashboard, consistency audit results, active projects status
- **Accountability:** Action items from previous month reviewed for completion

### 6.2 Quarterly Brand Review
- **Duration:** 90 minutes
- **Participants:** Brand team + marketing leadership
- **Agenda:** Brand Score review, benchmark comparison, brand investment ROI, competitive landscape update
- **Accountability:** Brand Score must be presented with evidence and trend data

### 6.3 Semi-Annual Strategic Brand Review
- **Duration:** Half day
- **Participants:** Brand team + cross-functional leadership
- **Agenda:** Brand strategy alignment, market positioning, architecture review, voice and messaging effectiveness
- **Accountability:** Strategic recommendations must have data support and resource requirements

### 6.4 Annual Brand Audit
- **Duration:** Comprehensive (multi-week process)
- **Participants:** Brand team + external perspective (if available)
- **Agenda:** Full audit across all dimensions (BrandScore.md), competitive analysis, customer research
- **Accountability:** Audit findings must produce a prioritized action plan with owners and deadlines

## SECTION 7: ESCALATION FRAMEWORK

### 7.1 Escalation Triggers

| Trigger | Escalation Level | Response Time |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|
| Hard-fail dimension <3 | Brand leadership | 1 week |
| Average Brand Score <3.0 | Marketing leadership | 2 weeks |
| Brand crisis (public) | Executive team | Immediate |
| Brand guideline compliance <70% | Brand leadership | 2 weeks |
| Benchmark score <3.0 | Brand leadership | 30 days |
| Failed brand investment >$25K | Marketing leadership + finance | 2 weeks |
| Customer brand perception declining | Brand + product leadership | 30 days |

### 7.2 Escalation Protocol

1. **Document** — Describe the issue with data and brand equity impact
2. **Assess** — Determine severity and urgency (brand equity at risk?)
3. **Notify** — Alert the appropriate escalation level
4. **Propose** — Present options with brand equity implications for each
5. **Decide** — Obtain decision from appropriate authority
6. **Execute** — Implement the decision with tracking
7. **Verify** — Confirm brand equity is protected or recovering
8. **Learn** — Conduct post-escalation review

---

## SECTION 8: TRANSPARENCY REQUIREMENTS

### 8.1 What Must Be Visible

- Brand Score across all dimensions with trend direction
- Brand guideline compliance rate
- Brand investment spending and ROI
- Active brand projects and their status
- Brand research findings and implications
- Brand failure log and remediation status
- Competitive brand landscape updates

### 8.2 Anti-Hiding Rules

- Brand perception data must be shared even when negative
- Creative decisions must show rejected alternatives (not just the chosen option)
- Brand investment failures must be reported with the same transparency as successes
- Declining metrics must be highlighted, not buried in dashboards
- Qualitative feedback (customer complaints about brand) must be surfaced alongside quantitative data

---

## SECTION 9: CONTINUOUS CALIBRATION

### 9.1 Protocol Review

This accountability protocol itself is subject to review:

- Semi-annually: Are the thresholds appropriate for the brand's maturity?
- Annually: Full protocol review and revision
- Ad-hoc: After any significant brand failure or market shift

### 9.2 Calibration Criteria

- **Too Strict:** If brand team spends more time on compliance than creation, thresholds need adjustment
- **Too Lenient:** If brand issues are only discovered by customers (not caught internally), thresholds are too relaxed
- **Right-Sized:** Brand team catches and corrects most issues before they reach customers


# Employer Brand Execution — Activating EVP Across Every Talent Touchpoint

## From Strategy to Activation

Employer brand strategy without execution is a PowerPoint. Execution transforms the Employee Value Proposition from an internal document into a living, breathing experience that candidates and employees encounter at every touchpoint. Mosley (2014) identifies that the "experience gap" — the distance between what the employer brand promises and what talent actually experiences — is the single largest predictor of employer brand failure.

## 1. Career Site Design and Optimization

### Career Site as Employer Brand Hub

The career site is the single most important employer brand touchpoint. 77% of candidates will visit a company's career site before applying (Glassdoor). Yet most career sites are optimized for ATS efficiency, not candidate experience.

### Career Site Architecture

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    CAREERS HOME                     │
│  Hero: EVP headline + authentic team video/image   │
│  Quick: Open roles count + search                  │
│  Social proof: Awards, ratings, employee quotes    │
├──────────┬─────────────┬──────────┬────────────────┤
│  LIFE AT │  TEAMS &    │  OPEN    │  EARLY         │
│  [CO]    │  FUNCTIONS  │  ROLES   │  CAREER        │
├──────────┼─────────────┼──────────┼────────────────┤
│ Culture  │ Engineering │ Search + │ Internships    │
│ Values   │ Design      │ Filter   │ New grad       │
│ Benefits │ Sales       │ Role     │ Apprentice     │
│ DEI      │ Marketing   │ detail   │ Rotational     │
│ Locations│ etc.        │ pages    │ programs       │
│ Blog/    │             │          │                │
│ Stories  │ Team-level  │ Easy     │ Campus         │
│          │ EVP content │ apply    │ partnerships   │
└──────────┴─────────────┴──────────┴────────────────┘
```

### Career Site Best Practices

**Content Requirements:**
- Authentic photography (real employees, real offices, not stock photos)
- Employee stories in their own words (video preferred, written acceptable)
- Team-specific content: what it is like to work on this team specifically
- Transparent benefits and perks information
- DEI data and commitments (with specifics, not vague promises)
- Interview process transparency: what to expect, how to prepare
- Location-specific content for multi-office companies

**Technical Requirements:**
- Mobile-first responsive design (60%+ of career site traffic is mobile)
- Page load <3 seconds
- Accessibility: WCAG AA minimum
- SEO optimized (structured data for job postings, schema.org/JobPosting)
- Analytics: track funnel from landing page to application submission
- ATS integration that does not destroy the candidate experience
- Personalization: surface relevant roles based on browsing behavior

### Career Site Metrics

| Metric | Benchmark | Target |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Bounce rate | 60-70% (industry avg) | <45% |
| Average session duration | 2-3 minutes | >4 minutes |
| Application conversion rate | 8-12% | >15% |
| Mobile application completion | 5-8% | >12% |
| Return visitor rate | 15-20% | >25% |
| Career blog engagement | N/A | >2 min avg read time |

## 2. Job Descriptions as Brand Content

### The Job Description Problem

Most job descriptions are written by hiring managers in a hurry, reviewed by legal for compliance, and posted as-is. They are often filled with jargon, unrealistic requirements, and zero employer brand signal. Research by Textio shows that the language in job descriptions has a measurable impact on applicant quality, diversity, and volume.

### Job Description Framework

```
Structure:
1. Hook (2-3 sentences): Why this role matters, impact the person will have
2. About Us (3-4 sentences): EVP-aligned company description
3. What You'll Do (5-8 bullet points): Responsibilities framed as impact
4. What You Bring (5-8 bullet points): Requirements with clear must-have vs nice-to-have
5. What We Offer (5-8 bullet points): EVP pillars with specifics
6. Our Hiring Process (4-6 steps): What to expect
7. Equal Opportunity Statement: Legal + genuine commitment
```

### Language Optimization

| Instead of... | Write... | Why |
|---------------|----------|-----|
| "Rockstar developer" | "Experienced software engineer" | Exclusionary, vague |
| "Must have 10+ years" | "Deep expertise in..." | Years =/= competence; deters diverse candidates |
| "Fast-paced environment" | "You'll ship features that reach millions" | Specific impact, not euphemism for chaos |
| "Competitive salary" | "$150K-$180K base + equity" | Transparency builds trust |
| "Other duties as assigned" | [Remove entirely] | Signals disorganization |
| "Culture fit" | "Values alignment" | Culture fit enables bias |
| "Work hard, play hard" | "We believe in sustainable pace and meaningful work" | Mature employer positioning |

## 3. Recruitment Marketing

### Channel Strategy

| Channel | Audience | Content Type | Cadence |
|---------|----------|-------------|---------|
| LinkedIn | Professional, all levels | Employee stories, thought leadership, culture content | 3-5x/week |
| Instagram | Early career, creative, lifestyle | Behind-the-scenes, events, team spotlights | 4-5x/week |
| Twitter/X | Tech, startup ecosystem | Hot takes, engineering blogs, company news | Daily |
| TikTok | Gen Z, early career | Day-in-the-life, fun culture moments, tips | 3-4x/week |
| YouTube | All segments | Long-form: office tours, team deep-dives, tech talks | 2-4x/month |
| Glassdoor | Active job seekers | Response to reviews, company profile optimization | Ongoing |
| Engineering blog | Technical talent | Technical deep-dives, architecture decisions | 2-4x/month |
| Podcast | Niche talent segments | Industry conversations, employee journeys | 2-4x/month |

### Recruitment Marketing Content Pillars

Build a content calendar around 4-6 recurring themes aligned to EVP pillars:

1. **Impact Stories:** Concrete examples of employee impact on customers, product, business
2. **Growth Narratives:** Career progression stories, internal mobility, learning
3. **Culture Windows:** Authentic glimpses into daily work life, team rituals, values in action
4. **Technical Depth:** Engineering blog posts, design case studies, data science papers
5. **Community Contribution:** Open source, conference talks, volunteering, DEI initiatives
6. **Leadership Voice:** Founder/executive perspectives on company direction and values

### Paid Recruitment Advertising

| Format | Platform | Best For | Cost Model |
|--------|----------|----------|------------|
| Sponsored job posts | LinkedIn, Indeed | Active candidates | CPC ($3-8 avg) |
| Display retargeting | Programmatic | Career site visitors who didn't apply | CPM ($5-15) |
| Social ads | LinkedIn, Meta, TikTok | Passive candidates, brand awareness | CPM/CPC |
| Programmatic job ads | Appcast, Joveo, PandoLogic | High-volume hiring | CPA ($15-50) |
| Talent community nurture | Email, owned channels | Pipeline candidates | Minimal |

## 4. Employee Advocacy

### The Advocacy Multiplier

Employee networks collectively reach 10x more people than corporate brand channels (LinkedIn). Employee-shared content receives 8x more engagement than brand-shared content. Advocacy is the highest-ROI employer brand channel.

### Building an Employee Advocacy Program

**Program Architecture:**
1. **Voluntary participation:** Mandate destroys authenticity; 15-25% participation is excellent
2. **Content enablement:** Provide shareable content but do not script employees
3. **Training:** Social media best practices, personal branding, what not to share
4. **Recognition:** Celebrate advocates, tie to performance recognition (not compensation)
5. **Measurement:** Track reach, engagement, referrals generated

**Content for Advocates:**
- Company news and milestones (easy shares)
- Team achievements and product launches
- Personal story prompts ("Share your growth story this week")
- Industry insights and thought leadership
- Job openings with personal referral context

**Advocacy Tools:**
- LinkedIn Elevate (sunset, but similar platforms exist)
- Bambu, Haiilo, EveryoneSocial, PostBeyond
- Simple approach: Slack channel with shareable content and suggested copy

### Advocacy Guardrails

- Clear social media policy (what's shareable, what's confidential)
- No enforcement mechanism for non-participation
- Opt-in, opt-out at any time
- Personal voice encouraged; corporate voice optional
- Legal review of guidelines, not individual posts

## 5. Employer Brand on Social Media

### Platform-Specific Best Practices

**LinkedIn:**
- Company page: EVP-aligned banner, about section, featured content
- Life tab: Populated with culture content, employee perspectives, office photos
- Employee profiles: Headline and about section mention company (encourage, don't mandate)
- Content mix: 40% culture, 30% thought leadership, 20% jobs, 10% company news

**Instagram:**
- Grid aesthetic consistent with consumer brand (or complementary employer brand visual)
- Stories: Day-in-the-life, Q&A, event coverage
- Reels: Short-form employee spotlights, office tours, fun team moments
- Highlights: Organized by EVP pillar (Culture, Growth, Benefits, Teams)

**Glassdoor:**
- Respond to every review (positive and negative) within 48 hours
- Responses should be authentic, empathetic, and non-defensive
- Update company profile quarterly with current photos and EVP content
- CEO approval rating and "recommend to a friend" as key metrics to monitor

## 6. Onboarding as Employer Brand Delivery

### The Onboarding Moment of Truth

Onboarding is where the employer brand promise meets reality. Research by the Brandon Hall Group shows that organizations with strong onboarding improve new hire retention by 82% and productivity by 70%.

### Onboarding Experience Design

**Pre-boarding (Offer Acceptance to Day 1):**
- Welcome email from hiring manager (personal, not template)
- Onboarding portal access with logistics, culture content, team introductions
- Welcome package (physical or digital): branded swag, handbook, personal note
- Equipment/access provisioned before day 1
- Buddy/mentor assigned and introduced

**First Week:**
- Day 1: Culture immersion, meet leadership, EVP narrative delivered in person
- Day 2-3: Team integration, tools/systems training
- Day 4-5: First meaningful work assignment (people want impact, not more onboarding)
- Social event or team lunch within first week
- Daily check-in with manager and buddy

**First 90 Days:**
- 30-day check-in: Manager 1:1, "Is this what you expected?"
- 60-day check-in: Goal setting, feedback exchange
- 90-day check-in: Formal onboarding completion, survey, celebration
- Ongoing buddy/mentor relationship
- Integration into relevant ERGs, social groups, learning programs

### Onboarding Metrics

| Metric | Measurement Point | Target |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| New hire satisfaction | 30/60/90 day surveys | >4.2/5.0 |
| Time to productivity | Manager assessment at 90 days | Meet role expectations |
| 90-day retention | Tracked by cohort | >95% |
| EVP gap score | "Was the reality what you expected?" | >4.0/5.0 |
| Buddy/mentor satisfaction | Survey at 90 days | >4.0/5.0 |
| Referral likelihood | "Would you refer a friend?" at 90 days | >8/10 |

## 7. Employer Brand Content Production

### Content Operations Model

**Monthly Content Calendar:**
- 8-12 social media posts (across platforms)
- 2-4 blog/article pieces
- 1-2 video pieces
- 1-2 employee spotlights
- Ongoing Glassdoor response
- Event coverage as applicable

**Content Production Workflow:**
1. Ideation (aligned to EVP pillars and content calendar)
2. Story sourcing (identify employees willing to participate)
3. Creation (interview, shoot, write, design)
4. Review (brand compliance, legal if needed, employee approval of their content)
5. Publish (scheduled across channels)
6. Amplify (employee advocacy, paid promotion if warranted)
7. Measure (engagement, traffic, applications attributed)

# Content Review Checklist -- Mandatory Pre-Publication Verification

Every content piece must pass this checklist before publication.
Every content campaign must pass this checklist before launch.
No exceptions. No "we will fix it after publishing."

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

1. Complete the relevant section based on content type
2. Mark each item as PASS, FAIL, or N/A
3. Any FAIL in a critical item = content cannot be published
4. Reviewer must sign and date each section
5. This checklist is retained as part of the content audit trail

---

## SECTION 1: PRE-PRODUCTION CHECKLIST

Complete before writing begins. This prevents wasted effort.

### Content Brief Verification

- [ ] **Business objective declared**: Content serves a specific business goal (awareness, consideration, decision, retention)
- [ ] **Target persona defined**: Role, seniority, industry, pain points documented
- [ ] **Funnel stage assigned**: TOFU, MOFU, BOFU, or Post-Funnel
- [ ] **Primary keyword selected**: Based on keyword research with volume and difficulty data
- [ ] **Secondary keywords listed**: 3-5 related terms with search intent alignment
- [ ] **Topic cluster identified**: Content belongs to an existing cluster or starts a new one with justification
- [ ] **Competitive content analyzed**: Top 3 ranking pages for the keyword reviewed for gaps
- [ ] **Unique angle defined**: What makes this piece different from what already exists
- [ ] **Target word count set**: Based on competitive analysis and content depth requirements
- [ ] **CTA strategy defined**: Primary and secondary CTAs selected before writing
- [ ] **Distribution plan outlined**: Where and how this content will be promoted

### VoC Integration

- [ ] **Customer language researched**: Reviewed support tickets, sales calls, reviews for relevant language
- [ ] **Customer quotes available**: At least 2 relevant customer quotes or data points ready
- [ ] **Objections identified**: Common reader objections for this topic listed
- [ ] **Examples sourced**: Real-world examples relevant to the target persona gathered

### Critical Items (Must Pass)

- [ ] Business objective is specific and measurable
- [ ] Primary keyword has been researched (not guessed)
- [ ] Unique angle is articulated (not "we should write about X")

## SECTION 2: WRITING QUALITY CHECKLIST

Complete during editing, before publication.

### Structure and Flow

- [ ] **Title is compelling**: Passes the "so what?" test. Specific, benefit-driven, curiosity-creating.
- [ ] **Opening hook works**: First 2 sentences establish relevance and reason to continue
- [ ] **Logical structure**: Sections flow in a logical order that builds understanding
- [ ] **Section transitions**: Each section connects to the next with a transition or bridge
- [ ] **Subheadings are informative**: A reader scanning only headers understands the main argument
- [ ] **Conclusion is actionable**: Ends with a clear takeaway or next step, not a vague summary
- [ ] **Length is appropriate**: Not padded with filler, not cut short before fully covering the topic

### Craft Standards

- [ ] **Readability score checked**: Flesch-Kincaid matches target audience
- [ ] **Sentences are concise**: Average sentence length under 25 words. No sentence over 35 words.
- [ ] **Paragraphs are short**: Maximum 5 sentences per paragraph. Most are 2-3.
- [ ] **Active voice dominant**: Passive voice used only for intentional emphasis
- [ ] **Filler words removed**: Very, really, basically, actually, just, quite -- all removed or justified
- [ ] **Weasel words eliminated**: Some, many, often, generally replaced with specific data or claims
- [ ] **Jargon defined or eliminated**: Technical terms defined on first use or replaced with plain language
- [ ] **Consistent tense**: No unintentional tense shifts
- [ ] **Parallel structure**: Lists and comparisons use consistent grammatical structure

### Accuracy and Ethics

- [ ] **Facts verified**: Every claim checked against primary sources
- [ ] **Statistics sourced**: Every number has a citation
- [ ] **Quotes attributed**: All quotes linked to named, verifiable sources
- [ ] **No plagiarism**: Content is original. Any borrowed ideas are properly attributed.
- [ ] **No misleading claims**: Content does not overstate capabilities or benefits
- [ ] **Disclosures included**: Sponsored content, affiliate links, or conflicts of interest disclosed

### Visual and Formatting

- [ ] **Images are relevant**: Every image supports the content (not decorative filler)
- [ ] **Alt text present**: All images have descriptive alt text
- [ ] **Formatting aids scanability**: Bold, bullet points, and headers used to break up text
- [ ] **Code examples formatted**: If present, code is syntax-highlighted and tested
- [ ] **Links are working**: All internal and external links tested
- [ ] **Mobile-friendly**: Content renders properly on mobile devices

### Critical Items (Must Pass)

- [ ] Title passes the "so what?" test
- [ ] Opening hook establishes value within the first 2 sentences
- [ ] All facts and statistics are sourced
- [ ] No plagiarism or unattributed content
- [ ] All links are working

## SECTION 3: SEO CHECKLIST

Complete before publication. SEO is not optional.

### Keyword Optimization

- [ ] **Primary keyword in H1**: Title contains the exact primary keyword
- [ ] **Primary keyword in URL**: URL slug includes the primary keyword
- [ ] **Primary keyword in first 100 words**: Keyword appears naturally in the opening
- [ ] **Primary keyword in meta title**: Title tag contains the keyword (50-60 characters)
- [ ] **Primary keyword in meta description**: Description includes keyword and compelling CTA (150-160 chars)
- [ ] **Secondary keywords used**: 3-5 related terms used naturally throughout
- [ ] **Keyword density appropriate**: Not stuffed (1-2% density), reads naturally

### Technical SEO

- [ ] **URL structure clean**: Short, descriptive, hyphenated, lowercase
- [ ] **Header hierarchy correct**: H1 > H2 > H3, no skipped levels
- [ ] **Schema markup added**: Appropriate structured data for content type (Article, FAQ, HowTo)
- [ ] **Canonical tag set**: Self-referencing canonical or pointing to original if syndicated
- [ ] **Open Graph tags set**: Title, description, and image for social sharing
- [ ] **Page speed acceptable**: Core Web Vitals passing (LCP, FID, CLS)
- [ ] **Mobile-responsive**: Content passes mobile-friendly test

### Internal Linking

- [ ] **Links to pillar page**: Content links to its topic cluster pillar page
- [ ] **Links from pillar page**: Pillar page updated to link to this content
- [ ] **3+ internal links**: Content links to at least 3 other relevant pages
- [ ] **Anchor text is descriptive**: Not "click here" but descriptive of the destination
- [ ] **No orphan pages**: Content is reachable from at least 3 other pages

### External Linking

- [ ] **2+ authoritative external links**: Content cites authoritative external sources
- [ ] **External links open in new tab**: Readers are not navigated away from the site
- [ ] **No broken external links**: All external URLs tested and working
- [ ] **No links to competitors**: Unless strategically intentional (comparison content)

### Critical Items (Must Pass)

- [ ] Primary keyword in H1, URL, and meta title
- [ ] Meta description is written (not auto-generated)
- [ ] Internal links to related content present
- [ ] Header hierarchy is correct

## SECTION 4: BRAND VOICE CHECKLIST

Complete before publication. Voice consistency builds trust.

### Voice Alignment

- [ ] **Tone matches guidelines**: Content tone aligns with documented brand voice
- [ ] **Formality level consistent**: First/second/third person used per guidelines
- [ ] **Vocabulary on-brand**: Preferred terms used, avoided terms eliminated
- [ ] **Personality present**: Content reads as "the brand" not "generic company"
- [ ] **Humor appropriate**: If used, matches brand guidelines for type and frequency

### Brand-Specific Language

- [ ] **Product name correct**: Product/company name spelled and capitalized correctly every time
- [ ] **Trademarked terms proper**: TM and R symbols used correctly on first use
- [ ] **Competitor references appropriate**: Competitors referred to factually, without disparagement
- [ ] **Legal-sensitive language reviewed**: Claims about performance, guarantees, or comparisons verified
- [ ] **Inclusive language used**: No gendered, ableist, or culturally insensitive language

### Consistency Across Pieces

- [ ] **Consistent with recent publications**: Tone and style match the last 5-10 published pieces
- [ ] **No contradiction with existing content**: Claims and positions align with other published content
- [ ] **Design elements aligned**: Formatting, image style, and visual presentation match brand standards

### Critical Items (Must Pass)

- [ ] Product and company names are correct throughout
- [ ] Tone matches brand voice guidelines
- [ ] No insensitive or exclusionary language
- [ ] No contradiction with existing published content

## SECTION 5: CONVERSION CHECKLIST

Complete before publication. Content without conversion strategy is a missed opportunity.

### CTA Verification

- [ ] **Primary CTA exists**: One clear, prominent call-to-action
- [ ] **Primary CTA is relevant**: Matches the content's funnel stage and audience
- [ ] **CTA copy is specific**: States the benefit, not just the action ("See your savings" not "Learn more")
- [ ] **CTA placement is strategic**: After value delivery, at natural decision point
- [ ] **CTA is visually distinct**: Stands out from surrounding content
- [ ] **Secondary CTA exists**: Lower-commitment alternative for readers not ready
- [ ] **CTA links work**: All CTA buttons and links tested and functioning

### Conversion Path

- [ ] **Landing page ready**: CTA leads to a page that continues the conversion journey
- [ ] **Form is appropriate**: If CTA leads to a form, fields match the value of the offer
- [ ] **Thank you page exists**: Confirmation page with next steps after conversion
- [ ] **Nurture sequence connected**: If lead capture, follow-up emails are queued
- [ ] **Attribution tracking in place**: UTM parameters or tracking pixels configured

### Content-Specific Conversion Elements

- [ ] **Social proof present**: Customer logos, testimonials, or case study references where appropriate
- [ ] **Risk reducers included**: "Free," "No credit card," "Cancel anytime" where applicable
- [ ] **Urgency (if genuine)**: Time-bound offers or limited availability noted when real
- [ ] **Objection handlers present**: Common hesitations addressed near the CTA

### Critical Items (Must Pass)

- [ ] Primary CTA exists and links work
- [ ] Conversion tracking is in place
- [ ] CTA matches content funnel stage

## SECTION 6: DISTRIBUTION CHECKLIST

Complete before and immediately after publication.

### Pre-Distribution

- [ ] **Social media posts drafted**: Platform-specific posts ready for LinkedIn, X/Twitter, etc.
- [ ] **Email promotion planned**: Newsletter inclusion or dedicated email scheduled
- [ ] **Internal team notified**: Sales, CS, and product teams alerted about new content
- [ ] **Partner/customer sharing**: Relevant partners or featured customers notified
- [ ] **Syndication plan**: If applicable, syndication partners identified and contacted
- [ ] **Paid promotion budget**: If applicable, paid amplification budget allocated

### Post-Publication (Within 24 Hours)

- [ ] **Published URL verified**: Content renders correctly at final URL
- [ ] **Social shares executed**: Posts published on all planned platforms
- [ ] **Email sent**: Newsletter or promotional email deployed
- [ ] **Internal Slack/Teams notification**: Team notified with link and suggested share language
- [ ] **Google Search Console**: URL submitted for indexing
- [ ] **Analytics verified**: Page views, events, and goals tracking correctly

### Post-Publication (Within 1 Week)

- [ ] **Community engagement**: Responding to comments and shares
- [ ] **Backlink outreach**: If applicable, reaching out to sites that might link to the content
- [ ] **Performance baseline**: Initial traffic, engagement, and conversion metrics captured
- [ ] **Paid performance check**: If promoted, ad performance reviewed and optimized

### Critical Items (Must Pass)

- [ ] Content renders correctly at published URL
- [ ] Analytics tracking is confirmed working
- [ ] At least 2 distribution channels activated within 24 hours

## SECTION 7: CONTENT REFRESH CHECKLIST

Complete when auditing existing content for updates.

### Staleness Assessment

- [ ] **Date check**: Content published >6 months ago flagged for review
- [ ] **Accuracy check**: All facts, statistics, and claims still accurate
- [ ] **Link audit**: All internal and external links still working
- [ ] **Screenshot/image audit**: Product screenshots and images still current
- [ ] **Competitive check**: Content still competitive for target keyword?
- [ ] **Performance check**: Traffic trending up, flat, or down? Conversion rate?

### Refresh Decision

- [ ] **Update**: Minor changes (stats, links, screenshots) -- keep URL, update content
- [ ] **Rewrite**: Major changes needed -- keep URL, rewrite substantially
- [ ] **Consolidate**: Multiple pieces covering the same topic -- merge into one stronger piece
- [ ] **Redirect**: Content no longer relevant -- 301 redirect to best alternative
- [ ] **Remove**: Content harmful to brand or SEO -- unpublish with redirect

### Post-Refresh Verification

- [ ] **Updated date reflected**: Publication date updated to refresh date
- [ ] **Editor's note**: If significant changes, note what was updated and why
- [ ] **Resubmit to search**: URL resubmitted to Google Search Console
- [ ] **Redistribution**: Updated content shared through distribution channels

### Critical Items (Must Pass)

- [ ] All facts and statistics verified as current
- [ ] All links tested and working
- [ ] Performance data reviewed to inform the refresh strategy

---

## SECTION 8: CAMPAIGN CHECKLIST

Complete before launching a multi-piece content campaign.

### Campaign Planning

- [ ] **Campaign goal defined**: Specific, measurable outcome (leads, signups, pipeline)
- [ ] **Content pieces mapped**: All pieces in the campaign listed with type, topic, and sequence
- [ ] **Timeline set**: Publication dates for each piece with dependencies noted
- [ ] **Resources allocated**: Writers, designers, editors assigned with deadlines
- [ ] **Budget approved**: Paid promotion and production costs approved

### Campaign Execution

- [ ] **Each piece passes individual checklist**: Every content piece in the campaign goes through the standard review
- [ ] **Cross-linking complete**: Campaign pieces link to each other where appropriate
- [ ] **Landing page ready**: Campaign landing page live and converting
- [ ] **Nurture sequence built**: Follow-up emails for campaign leads configured
- [ ] **Distribution plan activated**: Multi-channel promotion executing per plan

### Campaign Measurement

- [ ] **Baseline metrics captured**: Pre-campaign performance documented
- [ ] **Campaign-specific tracking**: UTM parameters and tracking unique to this campaign
- [ ] **Weekly performance reviews**: During campaign, weekly check on key metrics
- [ ] **Post-campaign analysis**: Within 2 weeks of campaign end, full analysis completed
- [ ] **Learnings documented**: What worked, what did not, what to do differently

### Critical Items (Must Pass)

- [ ] Campaign goal is specific and measurable
- [ ] Every content piece passes its individual review checklist
- [ ] Conversion tracking is campaign-specific and verified

---

## CHECKLIST ENFORCEMENT

- This checklist is mandatory for all content publications
- "The deadline was yesterday" is not an acceptable reason for skipping items
- Critical items must ALL pass before publication
- Completed checklists are retained for quarterly content audits
- Repeated checklist failures trigger an editorial process review

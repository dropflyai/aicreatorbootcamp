# Content Accountability Protocol -- Enforcement and Governance

This document defines how content performance is tracked, reviewed, and improved.
Content accountability is not about punishment. It is about systematic learning.
Without accountability, content is a cost center. With it, content is a revenue engine.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE

Every piece of content is an investment. Investments must be measured.
If content cannot demonstrate its contribution to the business, it cannot justify its cost.
The goal is not to punish underperformance but to learn from it and improve.

---

## 1. 30/60/90 CONTENT PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

### The Review Cadence

Every content piece published goes through three performance checkpoints:

| Review | Timing | Focus | Action Threshold |
|--------|--------|-------|-----------------|
| **30-Day Review** | 30 days after publication | Early signals: traffic, engagement, initial conversions | Course correct if significantly underperforming |
| **60-Day Review** | 60 days after publication | Trend confirmation: SEO ranking trajectory, lead quality | Optimize or escalate if underperforming |
| **90-Day Review** | 90 days after publication | Full attribution: pipeline influenced, revenue attributed | Decision: sustain, refresh, remove |

### 30-Day Review Protocol

**Metrics to evaluate:**
- Page views (vs. similar content average)
- Average time on page (target: >2 minutes for long-form)
- Bounce rate (target: <65% for blog content)
- Scroll depth (target: >50% of readers reach mid-point)
- Social shares and engagement
- Keyword ranking movement (if SEO content)
- Initial conversions (email signups, downloads, demo requests)

**Action framework:**
| Performance | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Above average on 5+ metrics | Document what worked. Apply to future content. |
| Average (within 20% of benchmarks) | Monitor. No immediate action needed. |
| Below average on 3+ metrics | Diagnose. Is it the topic, the headline, the content quality, or the distribution? |
| Significantly below (50%+ below benchmarks) | Immediate action: refresh headline, add CTAs, redistribute, or consider takedown. |

### 60-Day Review Protocol

**Additional metrics to evaluate (beyond 30-day):**
- SEO ranking trajectory (improving, stable, declining)
- Backlinks acquired
- Conversion rate trend (improving or declining)
- Lead quality assessment (are converted leads qualified?)
- Sales team feedback (is the content useful in deals?)

**Action framework:**
| Performance | Action |
|-------------|--------|
| Strong SEO trajectory + conversions | Invest more: build cluster content, increase promotion. |
| Good traffic, poor conversion | CTA and conversion path optimization. |
| Poor traffic, good conversion rate | Distribution and promotion investment. |
| Poor traffic and poor conversion | Content refresh or removal decision. |

### 90-Day Review Protocol

**Full attribution metrics:**
- Total organic traffic driven
- Total leads generated (attributed)
- Pipeline influenced ($)
- Revenue attributed ($)
- Cost to produce (writer, editor, design, promotion)
- Content ROI (revenue / cost)
- Customer feedback or sales usage data

**Final decision:**
| Result | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Positive ROI | Evergreen maintenance: refresh annually. Consider expanding into series. |
| Neutral ROI but strategic value | Maintain. Content supports brand or authority even if direct attribution is limited. |
| Negative ROI and no strategic value | Deprioritize. Do not invest in similar content. Redirect URL if removing. |
| Significant underperformance | Full post-mortem. Document learnings. |

### Batch Review Process

Do not review 200 pieces individually. Batch by:
- Content type (blog, guide, case study)
- Funnel stage (TOFU, MOFU, BOFU)
- Topic cluster
- Author

Look for patterns within batches: "Our BOFU content converts 3x better than TOFU. All content by [Author] outperforms. Cluster [X] is underperforming across the board."

## 2. SEO RANKING TRACKING

### Keyword Tracking Cadence

| Tracking Activity | Frequency | Tool | Owner |
|-------------------|-----------|------|-------|
| Primary keyword rankings | Weekly | SEO tool (Ahrefs, Semrush, etc.) | SEO Specialist |
| Keyword ranking changes (alerts) | Daily (automated) | SEO tool | Content Lead |
| New keyword opportunities | Monthly | SEO tool + Google Search Console | SEO Specialist |
| Competitor ranking comparison | Monthly | SEO tool | Content Lead |
| Topic cluster authority assessment | Quarterly | Manual + SEO tool | Content Lead |

### Ranking Performance Standards

| Metric | Target | Watch | Alert |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| Primary keyword in top 10 | Within 90 days of publication | Not in top 20 after 60 days | Not in top 50 after 90 days |
| Primary keyword in top 3 | Within 6 months | Stuck at positions 4-10 for 3+ months | Declining from page 1 |
| Organic traffic growth | MoM increase | Flat for 2+ months | Declining for 2+ months |
| Domain authority | Increasing or stable | Flat for 6+ months | Declining |

### Ranking Drop Response Protocol

When a key page drops 5+ positions:

**Day 1: Identify the cause**
- Algorithm update? (Check SEO news and community reports)
- Technical issue? (Crawl errors, page speed degradation, broken canonicals)
- Content freshness? (Competitor published better content)
- Link loss? (Key backlinks removed or broken)

**Day 2-3: Diagnose and plan**
- If algorithm update: assess if site-wide or page-specific. Wait 2 weeks if site-wide before reacting.
- If technical: fix immediately.
- If content freshness: schedule a content refresh within 2 weeks.
- If link loss: initiate link recovery or new link building.

**Day 4-14: Execute recovery**
- Content refresh: update stats, add new sections, improve depth
- Technical fixes: resolve all identified issues
- Link building: outreach to relevant sites for new links
- Internal linking: add more internal links to the affected page

**Day 30: Evaluate recovery**
- If ranking recovered: document what worked
- If not recovered: escalate to content lead for strategic decision

## 3. CONTENT AUDIT CADENCE

### Scheduled Content Audits

| Audit Type | Frequency | Scope | Owner |
|-----------|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Quick performance audit** | Monthly | Top 20 and bottom 20 pages by traffic | Content Lead |
| **SEO audit** | Quarterly | All SEO-targeted content | SEO Specialist |
| **Conversion audit** | Quarterly | All MOFU and BOFU content | Content Lead + Growth |
| **Freshness audit** | Semi-annually | All content >6 months old | Content Lead |
| **Comprehensive audit** | Annually | Entire content library | Content Lead + CMO |

### Monthly Quick Audit

**Top 20 pages: Protect and optimize**
- Are they still accurate and current?
- Are CTAs performing? Can they be improved?
- Are there new internal linking opportunities?
- Any technical issues (slow load, broken elements)?

**Bottom 20 pages: Evaluate and act**
- Why are they underperforming? (Traffic source, content quality, keyword competition)
- Can they be refreshed and improved?
- Should they be consolidated with other content?
- Should they be removed (with redirect)?

### Quarterly SEO Audit

For all SEO-targeted content:
- Current ranking position vs. target
- Traffic trend (last 3 months)
- Click-through rate from SERP (via Search Console)
- Content freshness assessment
- Keyword cannibalization check (are multiple pages competing for same keyword?)
- Featured snippet opportunities identified

### Semi-Annual Freshness Audit

For all content >6 months old:
- [ ] Facts and statistics still accurate
- [ ] Screenshots and images still current
- [ ] Links still working (internal and external)
- [ ] Product features and pricing still accurate
- [ ] Industry references still relevant
- [ ] CTA still appropriate and functional

### Annual Comprehensive Audit

Complete inventory of all content:
- Total content pieces by type, funnel stage, and cluster
- Performance distribution (what % of content drives what % of traffic/leads)
- Portfolio balance assessment (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU ratio)
- Topic cluster completeness assessment
- Content gap analysis vs. competitors
- ROI analysis by content type and channel
- Recommendations for next year's content strategy

## 4. STYLE GUIDE COMPLIANCE AUDITS

### Compliance Review Cadence

| Review | Frequency | Sample | Reviewer |
|--------|-----------|--------|---------|
| New content compliance | Every piece (before publication) | 100% of new content | Editor |
| Published content spot-check | Monthly | 10 randomly selected pieces | Editor |
| Author-level compliance review | Quarterly | 5 pieces per author | Editor + Content Lead |
| Full style guide compliance audit | Semi-annually | 20% of published content | Editor + Brand Manager |

### Style Guide Compliance Checklist

| Category | Standard | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Voice and tone** | Matches brand voice guidelines | Consistent throughout piece |
| **Formatting** | Follows formatting standards (headers, lists, emphasis) | No deviations from template |
| **Terminology** | Uses approved terms, avoids banned terms | Zero banned terms, approved terms used |
| **Product references** | Product/company names correct | 100% accuracy required |
| **Grammar standards** | Oxford comma, active voice, etc. per style guide | Consistent adherence |
| **Image standards** | Alt text, resolution, branding guidelines | All images compliant |
| **CTA standards** | CTA language and placement per guidelines | Meets CTA requirements |
| **Legal/compliance** | Required disclaimers, proper claims language | All legal requirements met |

### Non-Compliance Response

| Severity | Example | Response |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Minor | Inconsistent formatting, missing Oxford comma | Fix in next update cycle |
| Moderate | Wrong product name, off-brand tone in section | Fix within 48 hours |
| Major | Legal compliance issue, misleading claim | Fix immediately, escalate to legal |
| Critical | Plagiarism, unattributed content, brand-damaging error | Unpublish immediately, investigate |

## 7. PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PROTOCOL

### Content Underperformance Triggers

A content piece is flagged for intervention when:
- Traffic is 50%+ below average for its content type after 60 days
- Zero conversions after 30 days (for MOFU/BOFU content)
- Bounce rate >85% for 30+ days
- Keyword ranking drops 10+ positions from peak
- Sales team reports content is not useful

### Intervention Steps

**Step 1: Diagnose (Day 1-2)**
- Is it the topic? (Low search volume, misaligned intent)
- Is it the headline? (Poor CTR from SERP or social)
- Is it the content? (High bounce, low time on page)
- Is it the CTA? (Good engagement, no conversion)
- Is it the distribution? (Good content, no traffic)

**Step 2: Prescribe (Day 3)**
Based on diagnosis, one of:
- Headline refresh (if CTR issue)
- Content update (if quality/depth issue)
- CTA optimization (if conversion issue)
- Redistribution (if traffic issue)
- Removal and redirect (if topic is wrong)

**Step 3: Execute (Day 4-14)**
Implement the prescribed change with tracking in place to measure improvement.

**Step 4: Evaluate (Day 30 post-change)**
Did the intervention work? If yes, document the learning. If no, escalate to content lead for strategic decision.

## 9. CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT LOOP

### Monthly Content Retrospective

**When:** Last Thursday of each month, 30 minutes
**Attendees:** Content team

**Questions:**
1. What content performed best and why?
2. What content underperformed and what did we learn?
3. What process improvements would help us work better?
4. What skills or tools does the team need?

### Quarterly Strategy Review

**When:** First week of each quarter, 90 minutes
**Attendees:** Content Lead + CMO + Sales Lead + Product Lead

**Agenda:**
1. Quarterly content performance review (30 min)
2. Audience and market changes (15 min)
3. Product and business changes affecting content (15 min)
4. Next quarter content strategy and priorities (30 min)

### Annual Framework Review

Once per year, review the entire accountability framework:
- Are we measuring the right things?
- Are our benchmarks appropriate for our stage?
- Has the content landscape changed in ways that require new approaches?
- What should be added, removed, or adjusted?
- Benchmark against best-in-class content operations

---

## ENFORCEMENT RULE

Content accountability is a system, not a suggestion.
Every protocol in this document is mandatory.
Non-compliance is tracked and addressed.
The goal is not content for content's sake. The goal is content that drives measurable business outcomes.

Publishing without measurement is spending without budgeting.
Creating without strategy is movement without progress.
Reporting without insight is data without value.

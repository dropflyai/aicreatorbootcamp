# Link Building — Digital PR, HARO, Broken Link Building, Internal Linking

## Overview

Link building is the practice of earning hyperlinks from external
websites to your own, thereby increasing domain authority, referral
traffic, and topical relevance signals. Google's ranking algorithm
treats links as votes of confidence—a page with high-quality inbound
links will outrank a page with superior content but no links. This
module covers four link building disciplines: digital PR, reactive
media pitching (HARO/Connectively), broken link building, and internal
linking strategy. Each approach has distinct economics, scalability
characteristics, and risk profiles.

---

## Pillar 1: Digital PR

### Definition and Strategic Value

Digital PR is the practice of creating newsworthy content assets
specifically designed to earn editorial links from journalists,
bloggers, and online publications. Unlike traditional PR (which targets
brand mentions), digital PR optimizes for followed hyperlinks from
high-authority domains.

**Why Digital PR Works**
- Editorial links from news sites carry maximum authority weight
- A single placement in a DR 80+ publication can be worth hundreds
  of lower-quality links
- Branded data studies create citation networks that compound over time
- PR-driven links are algorithmically safer than outreach-based links
  because they are editorially earned

### Content Formats That Earn Links

**Data Studies and Original Research**
The highest-ROI digital PR format. Original data creates a citation
imperative—journalists need to link to the source.
- Survey-based studies (sample size 500+ for credibility)
- Data analysis of publicly available datasets (government, APIs)
- Industry benchmarks derived from proprietary product data
- Annual "State of X" reports that become recurring citation sources

**Interactive Tools and Calculators**
Utility-driven content that provides ongoing value earns passive links
from content creators who reference it.
- ROI calculators, cost-of-living tools, assessment quizzes
- Self-hosted (not third-party embeds) to capture link equity
- Must provide genuine utility, not thinly veiled lead generation

**Visual Assets**
Infographics have declined in link-earning power since their 2012 peak,
but data visualizations, maps, and interactive charts still perform
well when they present original data.
- Static infographics: Must contain original data (not repackaged stats)
- Interactive visualizations: Higher shareability and link potential
- Maps: Geographic data visualizations earn local and national press

**Expert Commentary and Thought Leadership**
Positioning company experts as media sources for their domain. This
requires a media relationship strategy, not just content creation.

### The Digital PR Pitch Process

**Step 1: Angle Development**
Every successful pitch begins with a newsworthy angle. Newsworthiness
is determined by:
- Timeliness (tied to current events, trends, or seasonal moments)
- Novelty (never-before-seen data, contrarian finding, first-of-kind)
- Magnitude (affects many people, involves large numbers)
- Proximity (relevant to the journalist's beat and audience)
- Conflict or tension (surprising findings, industry debates)

**Step 2: Journalist Targeting**
Build targeted media lists by:
- Analyzing who covers your industry vertical (use BuzzSumo, Muck Rack)
- Identifying journalists who have linked to similar studies
- Checking recent bylines for topical relevance and recency
- Segmenting lists by tier (national, trade, niche, blogger)
- Avoiding mass-blast pitching—personalization is mandatory

**Step 3: Pitch Crafting**

```
Subject: [Data Point] — New [Study/Report] from [Brand]

Hi [First Name],

I noticed your recent piece on [specific article]. We just published
[study name], which found that [most surprising finding with specific
number].

Key findings:
- [Finding 1 with data]
- [Finding 2 with data]
- [Finding 3 with data]

Full study with methodology: [URL]

Happy to provide additional data cuts or expert commentary if useful.

[Name]
```

**Step 4: Follow-Up Protocol**
- Wait 3–5 business days before first follow-up
- Maximum two follow-ups per journalist per pitch
- Provide additional value in follow-up (new data angle, expert quote)
- Never guilt-trip, demand coverage, or threaten exclusivity withdrawal

### Measuring Digital PR Performance

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Links earned per campaign | 20–50 for data studies | Ahrefs new referring domains |
| Average linking domain DR | 50+ | Ahrefs domain rating of linking sites |
| Dofollow ratio | 70%+ of earned links | Manual classification |
| Referral traffic | Measurable spike post-placement | Google Analytics referral report |
| Brand search lift | 5–15% increase post-campaign | Google Search Console brand queries |

## Pillar 2: HARO and Reactive Media Pitching

### How Reactive Pitching Works

HARO (Help a Reporter Out), now rebranded as Connectively, is a
platform where journalists post source requests and subject matter
experts respond with quotes, data, and commentary. When a journalist
uses your response, they typically link to your website.

**Platform Landscape**
- Connectively (formerly HARO): Largest platform, 3 daily digests
- Qwoted: Growing alternative with better journalist verification
- Featured.com: AI-assisted matching (higher cost, higher hit rate)
- #JournoRequest on Twitter/X: Real-time journalist requests
- Source of Sources (SOS): UK-focused journalist request service
- ProfNet: PR Newswire's expert matching service

### Response Strategy

**Speed Is Everything**
Journalists receive dozens to hundreds of responses per query. The first
3–5 quality responses have the highest selection probability. Target
response time: under 60 minutes from query publication.

**Response Quality Framework**
- Lead with credentials (why you are qualified to comment)
- Answer the specific question asked (do not pitch tangentially)
- Provide a quotable sound bite (20–40 words, self-contained)
- Include supporting data or specific examples
- Offer availability for follow-up questions
- Keep total response under 300 words

**Response Template**

```
Hi [Journalist Name],

[One sentence establishing expertise and relevance.]

[Direct answer to the question in 2-3 sentences with specific
data, example, or actionable insight.]

"[Quotable sound bite that can be used verbatim.]"

[One additional supporting point or counterintuitive insight.]

Bio: [Name], [Title] at [Company]. [One sentence credibility
statement with relevant experience or publication credits.]

Website: [URL to link to]
```

### Scaling HARO Operations

**Filtering for Relevance**
Set up keyword alerts for your expertise areas. Ignore queries outside
your domain—irrelevant responses damage your reputation with journalists.

**Response Tracking**
Maintain a log of all responses with fields:
- Date, journalist, publication, query topic
- Response sent (yes/no), response time
- Published (yes/no), link earned (yes/no), link type (follow/nofollow)
- Estimated domain rating of linking publication

**Hit Rate Benchmarks**
- Beginner: 5–10% of responses result in placement
- Intermediate: 15–25% with refined targeting and quality
- Expert: 30–40% with journalist relationship building

## Pillar 3: Broken Link Building

### The Broken Link Building Method

Broken link building identifies dead links on external websites and
offers your content as a replacement. It works because you provide value
to the webmaster (fixing their broken links) while earning a link.

**Step 1: Prospecting for Broken Links**
Methods for finding broken link opportunities:
- Ahrefs Broken Backlinks report: Find broken pages in your niche that
  had inbound links
- Check My Links browser extension: Scan resource pages for 404 links
- Wayback Machine: Review archived versions of dead content
- Competitor analysis: Find competitors' broken pages that earned links

**Step 2: Content Replacement Creation**
Create content that serves the same purpose as the dead page:
- Use the Wayback Machine to review the dead content's original scope
- Create a superior version (more current, more comprehensive)
- Match the format and depth the linking pages expected
- Ensure your replacement genuinely serves the linking page's audience

**Step 3: Outreach**

```
Subject: Broken link on [Page Title]

Hi [Name],

I was reading your [page type] on [topic] and noticed that the link
to [dead page description] in the [section name] section is returning
a 404.

We recently published [your content title], which covers the same
topic: [URL]

It might make a good replacement if you're updating the page.

Either way, thought you'd want to know about the broken link.

[Name]
```

**Step 4: Follow-Up and Relationship Building**
- Wait 7 days before first follow-up
- Provide the exact anchor text and URL of the broken link for easy fix
- Offer to check for other broken links on their site (builds goodwill)
- Track outreach-to-link conversion rate (benchmark: 5–10%)

### Broken Link Building at Scale

**Resource Page Mining**
Resource pages (curated lists of links on a topic) have the highest
density of broken links and the highest receptivity to replacement
suggestions. Find them with search operators:
- `intitle:"resources" + [topic]`
- `inurl:resources + [topic]`
- `intitle:"useful links" + [topic]`

**Link Reclamation**
A variant of broken link building focused on your own lost links:
- Monitor Ahrefs Lost Backlinks report weekly
- Identify links lost due to content removal, URL changes, or site
  migrations on the linking site
- Reach out to webmasters with updated URLs or offer new content

## Pillar 4: Internal Linking Strategy

### Why Internal Links Matter

Internal links are the most underutilized and highest-leverage SEO
tactic. Unlike external link building, internal linking is entirely
within your control, requires no outreach, and can be implemented
immediately.

**Internal Link Functions**
- Distribute PageRank (link equity) from high-authority pages to
  pages that need ranking support
- Establish topical relationships between pages (clustering signal)
- Guide crawlers to discover and prioritize new content
- Improve user navigation and reduce bounce rates
- Create contextual relevance signals through anchor text

### Internal Linking Architecture

**Hub-and-Spoke Linking**
Every topic cluster should have a defined linking pattern:
- Pillar page links to all cluster pages (comprehensive hub)
- Each cluster page links back to the pillar page (authority flow)
- Cluster pages link to 2–3 related cluster pages (lateral discovery)
- Breadcrumbs reinforce hierarchical navigation

**Contextual Link Placement**
Links within body content carry more weight than links in sidebars,
footers, or navigation menus. Best practices:
- Place links in the first 50% of the content when possible
- Use descriptive anchor text (3–7 words, keyword-inclusive)
- Limit to 3–5 contextual internal links per 1,000 words
- Avoid linking the same target page more than once per article

**PageRank Sculpting**
Intentionally direct link equity toward pages with the highest
business value:
- Identify high-authority pages (most backlinks, most traffic)
- Add internal links from those pages to priority pages that need
  ranking support
- Use Screaming Frog's internal link analysis to visualize equity flow
- Monitor changes in rankings after internal link additions

### Internal Link Audit Process

**Step 1: Crawl and Map**
Use Screaming Frog or Sitebulb to crawl the entire site and generate
an internal link report showing:
- Number of internal links pointing to each page
- Orphan pages (zero internal links)
- Click depth (how many clicks from homepage)
- Anchor text distribution

**Step 2: Identify Opportunities**
- Pages ranking positions 5–15 that could benefit from more internal
  links (the "striking distance" opportunity)
- High-authority pages with few outbound internal links (equity hoarding)
- New content with no internal links yet (common oversight)
- Over-linked pages (navigation clutter, equity dilution)

**Step 3: Implement and Monitor**
- Add 5–10 new internal links per week (sustainable cadence)
- Prioritize by business impact (revenue pages, conversion pages)
- Track ranking changes for target pages over 4–8 weeks
- Document all internal link additions in the keyword-URL map

## Link Building Ethics and Risk Management

### Google's Link Spam Policies

Google's SpamBrain algorithm detects and penalizes manipulative link
building. Activities that violate Google's policies:
- Buying or selling links that pass PageRank
- Excessive link exchanges ("link to me and I'll link to you")
- Large-scale article marketing or guest posting with keyword anchors
- Automated link building through bots or scrapers
- Private Blog Networks (PBNs) and link farms

### Risk Mitigation

- Prioritize editorially earned links (digital PR, original research)
- Diversify anchor text profiles (branded, URL, generic, keyword mix)
- Avoid over-optimization of exact-match anchor text
- Monitor backlink profile for toxic links (disavow if necessary)
- Build links gradually (sudden spikes trigger algorithmic scrutiny)
- Focus on relevance over raw authority (a DR 40 niche site link may
  outperform a DR 80 generic directory link)

## Summary

Link building remains one of the most impactful ranking factors in
search. Digital PR earns the highest-authority links through newsworthy
content assets and journalist relationships. Reactive pitching via
HARO/Connectively provides a steady stream of mid-authority editorial
links. Broken link building converts dead pages into linking
opportunities through value-driven outreach. Internal linking—the most
accessible and controllable tactic—distributes existing authority to
the pages that need it most. A mature link building program uses all
four approaches in combination, measuring performance by both link
quality metrics and organic ranking impact.

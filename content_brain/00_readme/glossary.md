# Content Brain — Glossary

## Purpose

This glossary defines key terms used throughout the Content Brain. All
module authors and content practitioners must use these definitions
consistently. Ambiguous terminology leads to misaligned execution.

Terms are organized by domain. Cross-references use `->` notation.

---

## Content Strategy Terms

### Content Pillar
A core topic area that a brand commits to owning through sustained,
authoritative content. Typically 3-5 pillars form the foundation of a
content strategy. Each pillar maps to audience needs and business goals.
Example: A project management tool might have pillars of "remote work,"
"team productivity," and "agile methodology."
Related: -> Topic Cluster, -> Editorial Mission

### Content-Market Fit
The state in which content produced by a brand consistently matches the
needs, questions, and interests of its target audience. Analogous to
product-market fit. Validated through engagement metrics, organic traffic
growth, and audience feedback.
Source: Inspired by Marc Andreessen's product-market fit concept.

### Editorial Mission
A one-sentence statement defining who the content serves, what it covers,
and what outcome it produces. Format: "We help [audience] do [action]
through [content type]."
Source: Joe Pulizzi, Content Inc.

### Content Audit
A systematic inventory and evaluation of all existing content assets.
Includes cataloging URLs, assessing quality scores, identifying gaps,
and recommending actions (keep, update, merge, retire).
Related: -> Gap Analysis, -> Content Scoring

### Gap Analysis
The process of mapping existing content against the buyer journey and
identifying stages, topics, or formats where content is missing or weak.
Gaps represent opportunities for new content creation.

### Content Governance
The rules, workflows, and standards that control how content is created,
reviewed, approved, published, and maintained. Includes style guides,
approval chains, and archival policies.

### Editorial Calendar
A planning document that schedules content creation, review, and
publication across a defined time period. Includes themes, assignments,
deadlines, channels, and status tracking.
Related: -> Content Cadence

### Content Cadence
The rhythm and frequency of content publication. Determined by audience
expectations, team capacity, and channel requirements. Consistency matters
more than volume.

## Copywriting Terms

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
A classic copywriting framework attributed to E. St. Elmo Lewis (1898).
Structures persuasive copy in four stages: grab attention, build interest,
create desire, prompt action. Best for: landing pages, sales emails.

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)
A copywriting formula that names the reader's problem, intensifies the
emotional stakes, then presents the solution. More emotionally driven
than AIDA. Best for: email copy, ad copy.

### BAB (Before-After-Bridge)
A framework showing the reader's current state (before), their desired
state (after), and how to get there (bridge = your product/solution).
Best for: case studies, testimonials, transformation narratives.

### Hook
The opening element of any content piece designed to capture attention
and compel continued reading. Can be a question, statistic, story,
contradiction, or bold claim. First 8 words determine if the rest
gets read. Source: Ogilvy on Advertising.

### CTA (Call to Action)
A directive that tells the reader exactly what to do next. Effective
CTAs are specific, benefit-driven, and create urgency without
manipulation. Example: "Start your free trial" vs. "Submit."
Related: -> Microcopy, -> Conversion Copy

### Conversion Copy
Writing specifically designed to move readers toward a measurable action:
signup, purchase, download, or other conversion event. Every word is
tested and justified by its impact on conversion rate.

### Social Proof
A Cialdini principle: people follow the actions of others. In copy,
social proof manifests as testimonials, case studies, user counts,
trust badges, and media mentions.
Source: Cialdini, R. (2006). *Influence*.

### Power Words
Words with outsized emotional impact that increase click-through and
conversion rates. Categories: urgency (now, limited), exclusivity
(secret, insider), safety (proven, guaranteed), curiosity (surprising,
unexpected). Use sparingly to maintain credibility.

### Above the Fold
Content visible without scrolling on initial page load. Critical for
landing pages: headline, value proposition, and primary CTA must all
appear above the fold. Origin: newspaper terminology.

## SEO Terms

### Search Intent
The underlying goal behind a search query. Four types:
- **Informational** — seeking knowledge ("how to write a headline")
- **Navigational** — seeking a specific page ("NN/g homepage")
- **Commercial** — comparing options ("best CMS 2024")
- **Transactional** — ready to act ("buy Grammarly premium")
Content must match intent or it will not rank.

### Topic Cluster
An SEO architecture where a comprehensive pillar page links to multiple
related cluster pages, and all cluster pages link back to the pillar.
Creates topical authority and improves internal linking.
Related: -> Pillar Page, -> Content Pillar

### Pillar Page
A comprehensive, long-form page (2000-5000 words) covering a broad topic
at a high level, with links out to detailed cluster content. Serves as
the hub in a topic cluster architecture.

### Featured Snippet
A highlighted search result box that appears at position zero in Google
SERPs. Content structured with clear headers, concise definitions, lists,
or tables has the best chance of earning featured snippets.

### Keyword Cannibalization
When multiple pages on the same site compete for the same keyword, diluting
ranking power. Prevented by clear content architecture and topic mapping.
Related: -> Topic Cluster

### E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness)
Google's quality rater guidelines for evaluating content. Content must
demonstrate first-hand experience, subject expertise, authoritative
sourcing, and trustworthy presentation.
Source: Google Search Quality Evaluator Guidelines.

## UX Writing Terms

### Microcopy
Short, functional text in user interfaces: button labels, error messages,
tooltips, placeholder text, confirmation dialogs. Despite being "micro,"
these words have outsized impact on user experience and conversion.
Source: Kinneret Yifrah, *Microcopy: The Complete Guide*.

### Empty State
The screen users see when no content or data exists yet. Effective empty
states explain what will appear, why it matters, and how to populate it.
Anti-pattern: blank screens with no guidance.

### Progressive Disclosure
Revealing information gradually as users need it, rather than presenting
everything at once. Reduces cognitive load. Applied in onboarding flows,
tooltips, expandable sections, and multi-step forms.
Source: Nielsen Norman Group research.

### Voice Attributes
The 3-4 adjective pairs that define a brand's communication personality.
Format: "We are [X] but not [Y]." Example: "We are confident but not
arrogant. We are helpful but not patronizing."
Related: -> Tone Modulation

### Tone Modulation
How brand voice shifts based on context and user emotion. Voice stays
constant; tone adapts. A brand might be playful in onboarding but
serious in error states. Mapped on a spectrum per context.
Related: -> Voice Attributes

### Content Design
The practice of using data and evidence to give the audience the content
they need, at the time they need it, in a way they expect. Goes beyond
writing words to designing content experiences.
Source: Sarah Winters (Richards), *Content Design*.

## Measurement Terms

### Content Scoring
A systematic method of rating content quality on defined criteria. The
Content Brain uses a 10-point scale across dimensions: clarity, accuracy,
engagement, SEO alignment, brand voice, and conversion potential.
Related: -> eval/ContentScore.md

### Content Attribution
The process of connecting content consumption to business outcomes
(leads, pipeline, revenue). Models: first-touch, last-touch, multi-touch,
and content-influenced attribution.

### Engagement Rate
A composite metric measuring how audiences interact with content. Includes
time on page, scroll depth, comments, shares, and click-through rate.
Higher engagement correlates with content-audience fit.

### Content Decay
The gradual decline in a content piece's performance over time as
information becomes outdated, competitors publish competing content, or
search algorithms evolve. Addressed through content refresh programs.
Related: -> Patterns/content_refresh_pattern.md

## Narrative and Storytelling Terms

### Hero's Journey
Joseph Campbell's monomyth: a universal narrative structure where a hero
leaves the ordinary world, faces trials, achieves transformation, and
returns changed. In content marketing, the customer is the hero and the
brand is the guide.
Source: Campbell, J. (1949). *The Hero with a Thousand Faces*.

### Narrative Arc
The structural shape of a story: exposition, rising action, climax,
falling action, resolution. Even non-fiction content benefits from
narrative arc — it creates tension and resolution that sustains attention.

### Story Framework (StoryBrand)
Donald Miller's framework: a character (customer) has a problem, meets
a guide (brand), who gives them a plan, calls them to action, that ends
in success (and avoids failure).
Source: Miller, D. (2017). *Building a StoryBrand*.

## Operations Terms

### Content Management System (CMS)
Software for creating, managing, and publishing digital content. Categories:
traditional (WordPress), headless (Contentful, Strapi), static site
generators (Hugo, Astro). Choice impacts content workflow and scalability.

### Content Model
The structured definition of content types, fields, relationships, and
taxonomies within a CMS. A well-designed content model enables content
reuse, omnichannel publishing, and API-driven delivery.

### Content Workflow
The defined process content follows from ideation to publication:
brief -> draft -> review -> revision -> approval -> publish -> measure.
Each stage has clear owners, criteria, and handoff protocols.

### Content Brief
A document that gives a content creator everything needed to write a
piece: topic, audience, intent, keywords, outline, examples, voice
notes, and success criteria. Prevents revision cycles.
Related: -> Templates/content_brief.md

## Rhetorical Terms

### Ethos
Aristotle's appeal to credibility. In content, ethos is established
through expertise, credentials, transparency, and consistent quality.
A brand's ethos is its reputation made visible through content.

### Pathos
Aristotle's appeal to emotion. In content, pathos connects through
stories, vivid language, empathy, and shared values. Effective pathos
is authentic, not manufactured.

### Logos
Aristotle's appeal to logic. In content, logos manifests as data,
evidence, structured arguments, and clear reasoning. The backbone of
technical writing and thought leadership.

### Kairos
The rhetorical concept of timeliness — the right message at the right
moment. In content, kairos drives newsjacking, seasonal content, and
real-time marketing. Content published at the right moment multiplies
its impact.
Source: Aristotle, *Rhetoric*.

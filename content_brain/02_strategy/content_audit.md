# Content Audit — Inventory, Gap Analysis, Competitive Content Analysis

## Overview

A content audit is a systematic evaluation of all content assets an
organization owns. It answers: What do we have? Is it any good? What
is missing? What should we kill? Content audits are the diagnostic
phase of content strategy — you cannot improve what you have not measured.

Source: Halvorson, K. & Rach, M. (2012). *Content Strategy for the Web*.

---

## When to Conduct a Content Audit

### Mandatory Triggers

- Before launching a new content strategy
- During a website redesign or migration
- When organic traffic declines for 3+ consecutive months
- When a new content lead or team joins
- Annually as part of content governance

### Warning Signs That Demand an Audit

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|-------------|
| Duplicate content ranking against itself | Keyword cannibalization |
| Old content getting more traffic than new | Content decay in new content |
| Support tickets asking questions your docs answer | Content discoverability failure |
| Sales team creating their own content | Content-sales misalignment |
| Multiple pages targeting the same keyword | No content architecture |
| Declining organic CTR despite stable rankings | Stale meta descriptions |

## Phase 1: Content Inventory

### What to Catalog

A content inventory is a complete, quantitative catalog of every content
asset. No qualitative judgment yet — just documentation.

**Required fields for each content asset:**

| Field | Description | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| URL | Page address | /blog/content-strategy-guide |
| Title | Page title / H1 | "The Complete Content Strategy Guide" |
| Content type | Blog, landing page, doc, etc. | Blog post |
| Topic / Pillar | Primary topic area | Content strategy |
| Word count | Total words on page | 2,847 |
| Author | Content creator | Jane Smith |
| Publish date | Original publication | 2023-03-15 |
| Last updated | Most recent revision | 2024-01-10 |
| Primary keyword | Target keyword | "content strategy" |
| Organic traffic | Monthly organic visits | 1,240 |
| Backlinks | Referring domains | 23 |
| Conversion events | Leads/signups from page | 15/month |
| Content score | Quality rating (1-10) | 7 |
| Action | Keep, update, merge, retire | Update |

### Inventory Tools and Methods

**Automated crawling:**
- Screaming Frog (crawl up to 500 URLs free)
- Ahrefs Site Audit (crawl + SEO data)
- Google Search Console (index coverage, performance data)
- Google Analytics (traffic, engagement, conversion data)

**Manual review additions:**
- Off-site content (social posts, guest articles, PR)
- Gated content (PDFs, ebooks, white papers)
- Email content (sequences, newsletters)
- Internal content (process docs, wikis)

### Inventory Scale Guidelines

| Content Volume | Approach | Timeline |
|---------------|----------|----------|
| Under 100 pages | Full manual review of every page | 1-2 weeks |
| 100-500 pages | Automated crawl + sample manual review | 2-4 weeks |
| 500-2000 pages | Automated crawl + top performers manual review | 4-8 weeks |
| 2000+ pages | Automated + statistical sampling + priority triage | 8-12 weeks |

## Phase 2: Content Quality Assessment

### The Content Scoring Matrix

Score each content piece across six dimensions (1-5 each, 30 points max):

**1. Accuracy (1-5)**
- Is the information current and factually correct?
- Are sources cited and verifiable?
- Has anything changed since publication?
- Score 1: Contains factual errors
- Score 5: Thoroughly researched, fully current

**2. Relevance (1-5)**
- Does this serve a current audience need?
- Is the topic still strategically important?
- Does search demand still exist?
- Score 1: Topic is obsolete or off-strategy
- Score 5: Core pillar content with strong demand

**3. Quality (1-5)**
- Is the writing clear, engaging, and well-structured?
- Does it meet readability standards?
- Is it better than competing content on the same topic?
- Score 1: Poorly written, unstructured
- Score 5: Best-in-class execution

**4. SEO Performance (1-5)**
- Does it rank for target keywords?
- Is organic traffic growing, stable, or declining?
- Does it have quality backlinks?
- Score 1: No rankings, no traffic
- Score 5: Page 1 rankings, growing traffic

**5. Conversion Impact (1-5)**
- Does it contribute to business goals?
- Are CTAs present and effective?
- Can attribution be demonstrated?
- Score 1: No measurable business impact
- Score 5: Directly drives pipeline/revenue

**6. Brand Alignment (1-5)**
- Does it match current brand voice and tone?
- Does it reflect current positioning and messaging?
- Does it represent the brand well?
- Score 1: Off-brand, outdated messaging
- Score 5: Perfect brand representation

### Action Decision Matrix

| Total Score | Action | Priority |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| 25-30 | Keep — flagship content, protect and promote | Monitor quarterly |
| 20-24 | Update — refresh and optimize | Schedule within 30 days |
| 15-19 | Overhaul — major rewrite needed | Schedule within 60 days |
| 10-14 | Merge — combine with stronger content | Schedule within 90 days |
| Below 10 | Retire — redirect or remove | Execute within 30 days |

## Phase 3: Gap Analysis

### Content Gap Types

**1. Topic Gaps**
Topics your audience cares about that you have not covered.
- Detection: Keyword research revealing untargeted terms
- Detection: Customer questions with no content answers
- Detection: Competitor coverage of topics you lack

**2. Funnel Gaps**
Missing content at specific buyer journey stages.
- Detection: Map existing content to funnel stages
- Detection: Find stages with fewer than 3 content pieces
- Common gap: Decision-stage content (comparisons, pricing, demos)

**3. Format Gaps**
Topics covered in one format but not others.
- Detection: All blog posts, no video or email
- Detection: Technical docs but no tutorials
- Impact: Different audiences prefer different formats

**4. Persona Gaps**
Audience segments not served by existing content.
- Detection: Map content to personas
- Detection: Find personas with fewer than 5 content pieces
- Common gap: Advanced users (most content targets beginners)

**5. Freshness Gaps**
Content that was once strong but has decayed.
- Detection: Pages with declining traffic over 6+ months
- Detection: Content older than 18 months without updates
- Impact: Outdated content erodes trust and rankings

### Gap Analysis Template

```
GAP TYPE: [Topic / Funnel / Format / Persona / Freshness]
DESCRIPTION: [What is missing?]
EVIDENCE: [How was this gap identified?]
AUDIENCE IMPACT: [Who is underserved?]
BUSINESS IMPACT: [What opportunity is lost?]
PRIORITY: [High / Medium / Low]
PROPOSED CONTENT: [What to create to fill the gap]
ESTIMATED EFFORT: [Hours / complexity]
SUCCESS METRIC: [How will we know the gap is filled?]
```

## Phase 4: Competitive Content Analysis

### What to Analyze

Examine 3-5 direct competitors' content programs:

**Quantitative analysis:**
- Total indexed pages (site: search operator)
- Blog post frequency (posts per week/month)
- Domain authority / domain rating
- Total organic keywords ranking
- Top-performing content (by traffic, backlinks, shares)
- Content types distribution (blog vs. video vs. tools)

**Qualitative analysis:**
- Content pillars and topic focus areas
- Writing quality and depth
- Unique angles or frameworks they own
- Voice and tone characteristics
- CTA strategy and conversion approach
- Content differentiation (what makes them different?)

### Competitive Content Matrix

```
| Dimension        | Your Brand | Comp A | Comp B | Comp C |
|-----------------|-----------|--------|--------|--------|
| Blog frequency   | 2/week    | 4/week | 1/week | Daily  |
| Avg word count   | 1,500     | 800    | 2,500  | 500    |
| Video content    | None      | Weekly | None   | Daily  |
| Email newsletter | Weekly    | None   | Bi-wkly| Daily  |
| Gated content    | 3 ebooks  | 12     | 2      | None   |
| Original research| Quarterly | None   | Annual | None   |
| Domain rating    | 45        | 62     | 38     | 71     |
| Organic keywords | 5,200     | 12,400 | 3,100  | 18,000 |
```

### Finding Competitive Content Gaps

1. **What they cover that you don't** — Topics to potentially add
2. **What you cover that they don't** — Your defensible advantages
3. **What nobody covers well** — Blue ocean content opportunities
4. **What everyone covers the same way** — Differentiation opportunities
5. **Their top-performing content** — Proven demand you can serve better

## Phase 5: Audit Report and Recommendations

### Audit Report Structure

1. **Executive Summary**
   - Total content assets audited
   - Overall content health score
   - Top 3 findings
   - Top 3 recommendations

2. **Inventory Summary**
   - Content by type, pillar, age, and performance tier
   - Distribution charts and trend data

3. **Quality Assessment**
   - Score distribution across the portfolio
   - Flagged content requiring immediate action
   - Top-performing content to protect

4. **Gap Analysis**
   - Prioritized gaps with business impact estimates
   - Recommended new content to fill gaps

5. **Competitive Landscape**
   - Positioning relative to competitors
   - Opportunities for differentiation

6. **Action Plan**
   - Prioritized content actions (keep, update, merge, retire)
   - Timeline and resource requirements
   - Success metrics and review dates

## Content Audit Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Never auditing | Content accumulates like clutter | Annual minimum, quarterly ideal |
| Audit without action | Inventory is useless without decisions | Every piece gets an action label |
| Only auditing blog posts | Ignores product, support, sales content | Audit all content types |
| Traffic-only scoring | Popular != good, Low traffic != bad | Multi-dimensional scoring matrix |
| Keeping everything | "Just in case" hoarding wastes crawl budget | Retire sub-10 scores aggressively |
| No competitive context | Your content exists in a competitive landscape | Always include competitor analysis |
| Auditing alone | One person cannot assess all dimensions | Cross-functional audit team |

## Audit Cadence

| Audit Type | Frequency | Scope |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Full audit | Annually | All content assets |
| Performance review | Quarterly | Top 50 pages + bottom 20 |
| Freshness check | Monthly | Content older than 12 months |
| Competitive scan | Quarterly | Top 3 competitors |
| New content review | Bi-weekly | Content published in last 2 weeks |

## Post-Audit Workflow

```
AUDIT COMPLETE
     |
     v
CATEGORIZE (Keep / Update / Merge / Retire)
     |
     v
PRIORITIZE (Impact x Effort matrix)
     |
     v
SCHEDULE (Add to editorial calendar)
     |
     v
EXECUTE (Update, merge, redirect, or remove)
     |
     v
MEASURE (Track performance post-action)
     |
     v
REPEAT (Next scheduled audit)
```

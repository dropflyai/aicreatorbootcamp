# Email Copywriting — Subject Lines, Sequences, Nurture Flows, Segmented Messaging

## Overview

Email remains the highest-ROI marketing channel: $36 returned for every
$1 spent (Litmus, 2023). Yet the average professional receives 121 emails
per day. Your email must earn its open, earn its read, and earn its click
in a brutally competitive inbox. This module covers the craft of email
copy from subject line to conversion.

---

## Subject Line Mastery

### The 7-Second Decision

Recipients decide to open or delete in approximately 7 seconds based on:
1. Sender name (most important — is this from someone I know/trust?)
2. Subject line (does this seem valuable/relevant/urgent?)
3. Preview text (does this reinforce the subject line's promise?)

### Subject Line Formulas

**Formula 1: The Curiosity Gap**
Open an information gap the reader needs to close.
- "The content metric we stopped tracking (and why)"
- "Something's wrong with your landing page"
- "I was wrong about email frequency"

**Formula 2: The Specific Benefit**
Promise a clear, quantified outcome.
- "Get 3x more blog traffic with this one change"
- "Save 5 hours/week on content production"
- "Your Q1 content plan in 15 minutes"

**Formula 3: The Personal Question**
Ask something the reader will answer internally.
- "Are you making this SEO mistake?"
- "When did you last audit your content?"
- "What's your content team's biggest bottleneck?"

**Formula 4: The Social Proof**
Lead with credible external validation.
- "How Buffer grew to 4M readers with 3 content pillars"
- "The email strategy Shopify uses for onboarding"
- "73% of content leaders do this differently"

**Formula 5: The Urgency/Timeliness**
Create genuine time-sensitivity.
- "Last day: Early pricing ends tonight"
- "Google's algorithm update: what it means for your content"
- "Your trial expires in 48 hours — here's what you'd lose"

**Formula 6: The Storytelling Tease**
Begin a narrative the reader must open to complete.
- "She published one blog post. It generated $2.3M."
- "We almost killed our blog. Then this happened."
- "The worst content mistake I've made (so far)"

### Subject Line Technical Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Length | 30-50 characters (mobile-optimized) |
| Preview text | Extend the subject line, don't repeat it |
| Personalization | First name increases opens 10-14% (use selectively) |
| Emoji | 1 max, test per audience (B2B: cautious, B2C: more acceptable) |
| CAPS | Never all-caps, occasional single-word emphasis okay |
| Spam triggers | Avoid: FREE, ACT NOW, LIMITED TIME, excessive punctuation |
| A/B testing | Test 2 variants, 15% sample, 4-hour wait, deploy winner |

### Subject Line Scoring (Pre-Send)

Score each subject line candidate (1-5 per dimension):

| Dimension | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Relevance | Does this match the segment's current needs? |
| Curiosity | Does this create an information gap? |
| Clarity | Is it immediately understood? |
| Benefit | Is a clear value promised? |
| Differentiation | Does it stand out from typical inbox noise? |

Minimum passing score: 18/25.

## Email Body Copy

### The First Line Rule

The first line of email body copy must accomplish one of three things:
1. Deliver on the subject line's promise immediately
2. Build empathy by naming the reader's situation
3. Create a micro-story that hooks continued reading

**Never** open with:
- "Hope this finds you well" (wasted space)
- "I wanted to reach out" (self-centered)
- "As you may know" (presumptuous)
- Company history or background (nobody asked)

### Email Copy Structure

**Short-form email (under 150 words):**
```
[Hook - 1 sentence that earns the next sentence]
[Context - 1-2 sentences expanding the hook]
[Value - The main content/offer/insight]
[CTA - Single, clear action]
[P.S. - Optional secondary hook or social proof]
```

**Long-form email (150-500 words):**
```
[Hook - Story, question, or startling fact]
[Bridge - Connect the hook to the reader's problem]
[Content - The main value (tips, insight, story)]
[Transition - Pivot from value to offer]
[CTA - Clear action with benefit framing]
[P.S. - Reinforce CTA or add urgency/proof]
```

### The P.S. Line

Research shows the P.S. is one of the most-read elements of any email.
People who skim the body will often read the P.S. Use it for:
- Reinforcing the primary CTA
- Adding a time-sensitive element
- Including a compelling testimonial
- Introducing a secondary benefit

## Email Sequence Design

### Welcome Sequence (5-7 emails)

The most important email sequence. Sets expectations, builds trust,
and establishes the relationship.

**Email 1 (Day 0): The Promise Delivery**
- Deliver whatever was promised (lead magnet, trial access)
- Set expectations for future emails
- One clear next step
- Send immediately upon signup

**Email 2 (Day 1): The Quick Win**
- Provide an immediate, actionable tip
- Demonstrate value before asking for anything
- Build the reciprocity principle (Cialdini)

**Email 3 (Day 3): The Story**
- Share origin story or customer transformation story
- Build emotional connection and liking (Cialdini)
- Reinforce why the reader joined

**Email 4 (Day 5): The Deep Value**
- Provide the most valuable piece of content you have
- No selling — pure value delivery
- Establish authority (Cialdini)

**Email 5 (Day 7): The Soft Pitch**
- Introduce your product/service naturally
- Frame as the solution to problems discussed in emails 2-4
- Low-pressure CTA ("See how it works" not "Buy now")

**Email 6 (Day 10): The Social Proof**
- Customer testimonial or case study
- Specific results and transformation
- Addresses the "will it work for me?" objection

**Email 7 (Day 14): The Direct Ask**
- Clear, confident pitch
- Summarize the value already delivered
- Direct CTA with urgency if genuine
- Risk reversal (guarantee, free trial)

### Nurture Sequence (Ongoing)

For subscribers who did not convert from the welcome sequence:

**Cadence:** Weekly or bi-weekly
**Content mix:**
- 80% value (educational content, insights, tips)
- 20% promotional (product mentions, offers, CTAs)

**Nurture email types:**
1. Educational — Teach something actionable
2. Curated — Share the best resources on a topic
3. Story — Customer success story or behind-the-scenes
4. Insight — Original data or contrarian perspective
5. Promotional — Direct pitch (earned through value delivery)

### Re-Engagement Sequence (3 emails)

For subscribers who haven't opened in 60-90 days:

**Email 1: "We miss you" + best content piece**
Subject: "Your best resource this quarter (you missed it)"

**Email 2: "Is this still relevant?" + preferences update**
Subject: "Should we keep sending these?"

**Email 3: "Last email unless you say otherwise"**
Subject: "Removing you from the list on Friday"

If no engagement after email 3, remove from active list. A clean list
with 5,000 engaged subscribers outperforms a bloated list of 50,000
that tanks your deliverability.

## Segmentation and Personalization

### Segmentation Variables

| Variable | Examples | Copy Impact |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| Role/Title | Marketer vs. Developer | Different pain points, language |
| Company size | Startup vs. Enterprise | Different scale, budget, complexity |
| Funnel stage | Awareness vs. Decision | Different content depth and CTA |
| Behavior | Active vs. Dormant | Different urgency and approach |
| Content interest | SEO vs. Social vs. Email | Different topic focus |
| Customer status | Prospect vs. Customer | Different relationship tone |

### Personalization Beyond First Name

| Level | Technique | Impact |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Basic | First name in subject/body | 10-14% open rate lift |
| Moderate | Segment-specific content | 20-30% click rate lift |
| Advanced | Behavior-triggered content | 50%+ relevance increase |
| Expert | Dynamic content blocks per segment | Highest engagement |

**Rule:** Personalization must feel natural, not surveillance-like.
"Hi Sarah, since you read our SEO guide last week..." = helpful.
"Hi Sarah, we noticed you visited our pricing page 3 times..." = creepy.

## Email Copy Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Wall of text | Nobody reads long email paragraphs | 2-3 sentences per paragraph max |
| Multiple CTAs | Paradox of choice | One primary CTA per email |
| No CTA at all | Engagement without direction | Every email has a purpose and action |
| Self-centered opener | "We're excited to announce..." | Lead with reader benefit |
| HTML-heavy design | Triggers spam filters, slow loading | Simple design, text-focused |
| No preview text | Default shows "View in browser" | Write custom preview text always |
| Same email to everyone | Irrelevant to most recipients | Segment by at least 3 variables |
| Sending without testing | Broken links, typos, rendering issues | Test on 3 email clients minimum |
| Over-emailing | Unsubscribes spike, trust erodes | Respect stated frequency preferences |
| Under-emailing | "Who is this?" when you finally send | Maintain consistent, expected cadence |

## Email Metrics and Benchmarks

### Key Metrics

| Metric | Good | Great | Investigate |
|--------|------|-------|------------|
| Open rate | 20-25% | 30%+ | Below 15% |
| Click-through rate | 2-3% | 5%+ | Below 1% |
| Click-to-open rate | 10-15% | 20%+ | Below 8% |
| Unsubscribe rate | Under 0.3% | Under 0.1% | Above 0.5% |
| Bounce rate | Under 2% | Under 0.5% | Above 3% |
| List growth rate | 2-5%/month | 5%+/month | Negative |

### Diagnosing Email Problems

| Problem | Likely Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Low opens | Weak subject lines or poor sender reputation | A/B test subjects, clean list |
| Opens but no clicks | Body copy not compelling or CTA weak | Rewrite body, strengthen CTA |
| Clicks but no conversion | Landing page mismatch | Align email promise to landing page |
| High unsubscribes | Too frequent, not relevant, or misleading | Segment better, send less, be honest |
| Going to spam | Technical issues or spam trigger words | Check authentication, audit copy |

**Every email is a promise in the subject line and a delivery in the body.
Break that promise once and the inbox door closes forever.**

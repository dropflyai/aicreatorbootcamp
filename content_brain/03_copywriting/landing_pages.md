# Landing Pages — Conversion Copy, Above-the-Fold, Social Proof, CTAs

## Overview

A landing page is a single page with a single purpose: convert visitors
into a specific action. Every word, every element, every pixel serves
conversion. Landing pages are the proving ground of copywriting —
where persuasion theory meets measurable results.

Average landing page conversion rate across industries: 2.35%.
Top 25% convert at 5.31%+. Top 10% convert at 11.45%+.
Source: Unbounce Conversion Benchmark Report.

The difference between 2% and 11% is almost always the copy.

## Above the Fold: The Critical 5 Seconds

### What Must Appear Above the Fold

Research from NN/g shows that 57% of page-viewing time is spent above
the fold. The above-the-fold section must answer three questions instantly:

1. **What is this?** (Headline)
2. **Why should I care?** (Subheadline + value prop)
3. **What do I do next?** (CTA)

If any question goes unanswered, the visitor bounces.

### Writing the Headline

The headline is your value proposition compressed into 6-12 words.

**Formula: [End result] + [Time period] + [Address objection]**

Examples:
- "Build Landing Pages in Minutes. No Code Required."
- "Hire Senior Developers in 48 Hours. Vetted and Ready."
- "Reduce Churn by 30% With Automated Health Scoring."

**Headline testing criteria:**
- Does it communicate the core benefit?
- Is it specific (numbers, timeframes, outcomes)?
- Does it address the primary objection?
- Would the reader's reaction be "tell me more" (not "so what")?
- Can a 12-year-old understand it? (Ogilvy's readability standard)

### Writing the Subheadline

The subheadline expands on the headline with supporting detail. It can:
- Explain the mechanism ("Our AI analyzes your content...")
- Add specificity ("Used by 10,000+ marketing teams")
- Address the "how" ("Connect your CMS in 60 seconds")
- Reinforce the benefit ("So you can focus on strategy, not operations")

**Rule:** If someone reads only the headline and subheadline, they should
understand what you do, who it's for, and why it matters.

### The Primary CTA

Above-the-fold CTA rules:
- Action-oriented verb: "Start," "Get," "Try," "Create" (not "Submit")
- Benefit-driven: "Start saving time" vs. "Sign up"
- Low-friction: "Start free trial" (not "Buy now")
- Visually prominent: Contrasting color, sufficient size
- One CTA only: Multiple CTAs above the fold splits attention

**CTA copy spectrum (low to high commitment):**
```
Learn More -> See Demo -> Start Free Trial -> Get Started -> Buy Now
```

Match CTA commitment level to where visitors are in the buyer journey.
Cold traffic landing pages: low commitment. Retargeting pages: higher.

## Writing Benefit-Driven Copy

### Features vs. Benefits vs. Outcomes

Most landing pages make the fatal error of listing features. Visitors
do not care about features. They care about what features do for them.

**Feature:** What the product does.
**Benefit:** What the feature means for the user.
**Outcome:** The end result in the user's life.

| Feature | Benefit | Outcome |
|---------|---------|---------|
| AI content analysis | Find content gaps automatically | Publish the right content every time |
| Real-time dashboard | See performance instantly | Make data-driven decisions faster |
| One-click integrations | Connect your tools in seconds | No engineering help needed |
| 99.9% uptime SLA | Your site stays live | Never lose a customer to downtime |

**The copy progression:** Feature -> "so that" -> Benefit -> "which means" -> Outcome.

Example: "Our AI scans your content library (feature) so that you can
identify gaps in minutes instead of weeks (benefit), which means you
always publish content your audience is actively searching for (outcome)."

### The "So What?" Test

After writing every sentence, ask "So what?" If you cannot answer with
a clear benefit to the reader, rewrite or delete.

- "We use machine learning algorithms." So what?
- "Our algorithm identifies your highest-impact content opportunities." Better.
- "Publish content that ranks — our AI shows you exactly what to write." Best.

## Social Proof on Landing Pages

### Types of Social Proof (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Specific customer results** (highest impact)
   "Acme Corp increased organic traffic 340% in 6 months using [product]."

2. **Named testimonials with photos and titles**
   "This tool saved our content team 20 hours per week." — Sarah Chen,
   VP of Marketing, Notion

3. **Aggregate metrics**
   "Trusted by 12,847 content teams" / "4.8 out of 5 on G2"

4. **Customer logos**
   Recognizable brand logos provide instant credibility.

5. **Media mentions**
   "As seen in TechCrunch, Forbes, Product Hunt"

6. **Awards and certifications**
   Third-party validation badges.

### Social Proof Copy Rules

- **Name names:** "A Fortune 500 company" is weaker than "Salesforce"
- **Quantify results:** "Improved our process" is weaker than "Reduced
  onboarding time from 3 weeks to 3 days"
- **Match the audience:** Testimonials from similar companies to the
  target audience are 3x more persuasive (Cialdini's similarity principle)
- **Place strategically:** After the value proposition, after the pricing
  section, and immediately before the final CTA
- **Refresh regularly:** Testimonials older than 18 months lose credibility

### Writing Effective Testimonials

**Format for maximum impact:**
```
"[Specific result or transformation achieved]."

[Full name], [Title], [Company]
[Optional: relevant detail — company size, industry, time as customer]
```

**Before (weak):**
"Great product! Really helped our team. Would recommend."

**After (strong):**
"We went from publishing 2 blog posts a month to 8 per week — without
adding headcount. Our organic traffic grew 210% in the first quarter."
— Marcus Rodriguez, Director of Content, Scale AI (Series C, 200 employees)

## Objection Handling in Landing Page Copy

### The Top 5 Universal Objections

Every landing page must address these, either explicitly or implicitly:

1. **"Is this right for me?"** — Address with specificity about who
   the product serves (and who it doesn't).

2. **"Is it worth the price?"** — Address with ROI framing, comparison
   to alternatives, or risk reversal (money-back guarantee).

3. **"Will it actually work?"** — Address with social proof, case studies,
   and specific results.

4. **"Is it hard to implement?"** — Address with "how it works" section
   (3 simple steps), time-to-value claims, and onboarding support.

5. **"What if I don't like it?"** — Address with free trial, guarantee,
   easy cancellation, no long-term contract.

### FAQ Section Copy

The FAQ is one of the most-read sections on any landing page. Use it
strategically to handle objections disguised as questions:

**Structure each FAQ answer:**
1. Acknowledge the concern (validates the reader)
2. Answer directly (no hedging)
3. Provide evidence (data, example, or guarantee)

**Example:**
Q: "How long does setup take?"
A: "Most teams are up and running in under 15 minutes. We've designed the
onboarding to require zero engineering support. If you get stuck, our
support team responds in under 2 hours during business hours."

## The How-It-Works Section

### The Three-Step Plan

Donald Miller's StoryBrand framework recommends a simple 3-step plan
that shows the path from current state to desired outcome:

```
Step 1: [Easy first action]  ->  Step 2: [Core experience]  ->  Step 3: [Desired outcome]
```

**Example:**
1. **Connect** — Import your existing content in one click
2. **Analyze** — Our AI identifies gaps, opportunities, and priorities
3. **Grow** — Publish data-driven content that ranks and converts

**Why 3 steps:** Three is the optimal number for cognitive processing.
It is comprehensive enough to feel like a real plan but simple enough
to feel achievable. Source: "The Rule of Three" in communication theory.

## Landing Page Copy Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Fix |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Feature lists without benefits | Readers don't connect features to their life | Feature -> "so that" -> Benefit |
| "We" focused copy | "We built..." "Our team..." = self-centered | Use "you" 3x for every "we" |
| No CTA above the fold | 57% of attention is above the fold | Primary CTA always visible |
| Multiple competing CTAs | Choice paralysis = no action | One primary CTA per section |
| Generic headline | "The Future of Content" = scroll past | Specific value + specific audience |
| Wall of text | Nobody reads paragraphs on landing pages | Bullets, icons, short paragraphs |
| No social proof | Claims without evidence = skepticism | Testimonials, logos, metrics |
| "Submit" as CTA | Zero motivation, institutional language | Action verb + benefit |
| Ignoring mobile | 50%+ traffic is mobile | Test mobile layout and copy length |
| No urgency (when genuine) | No reason to act today | "Start your 14-day free trial" |

## Landing Page Copy Checklist

- [ ] Headline communicates the core value proposition in under 10 words
- [ ] Subheadline expands with specificity (who, how, result)
- [ ] Above-the-fold CTA uses action verb + benefit
- [ ] Problem section names the pain the audience actually feels
- [ ] Solution section leads with benefits, not features
- [ ] How-it-works section has exactly 3 steps
- [ ] Social proof includes named individuals, specific results
- [ ] Objections addressed in FAQ or body copy
- [ ] CTA repeated at least 3 times on the page
- [ ] Copy uses "you/your" 3x more than "we/our"
- [ ] Readability is grade 8 or lower
- [ ] Every sentence passes the "so what?" test
- [ ] Mobile layout reviewed and copy adjusted

# Business Plan Template

## Purpose

A comprehensive business plan template for startups and new ventures. Adapt sections based on stage and audience (investors, internal planning, banking).

---

## 1. Executive Summary (1-2 pages)

### Company Overview
- Company name, legal structure, founding date
- Mission statement (one sentence)
- Problem being solved (2-3 sentences)
- Solution (2-3 sentences)
- Current stage and traction

### Key Metrics
- Revenue (current and projected)
- Customers (number and growth rate)
- Key unit economics (CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC)
- Team size

### The Ask
- Funding amount sought
- Use of funds (top 3 categories)
- Key milestones the funding will achieve

---

## 2. Problem (1-2 pages)

### Problem Statement
- What specific problem are you solving?
- Who experiences this problem? (target customer description)
- How painful is this problem? (frequency, intensity, willingness to pay)

### Current Solutions
- How do customers solve this problem today?
- What are the limitations of current solutions?
- What is the "hair on fire" aspect — why is the status quo unacceptable?

### Supporting Data
- Market research, surveys, or interviews supporting the problem's significance
- Size of the affected population
- Economic cost of the problem (quantified)

---

## 3. Solution (2-3 pages)

### Product/Service Description
- What you offer (features and capabilities)
- How it solves the problem
- Key differentiators from existing solutions

### Value Proposition
- Primary benefit to the customer (quantified if possible)
- Secondary benefits
- Jobs to Be Done that your product addresses

### Product Roadmap
- Current state of the product
- Near-term roadmap (6-12 months)
- Long-term vision (2-3 years)

### Technology and IP
- Key technology components
- Proprietary technology or IP
- Build vs. buy decisions

---

## 6. Go-to-Market Strategy (2 pages)

### Target Customer Profile
- Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
- Decision-making unit (who buys, who uses, who influences)

### Go-to-Market Plan

```
Phase 1 (Months 1-6):   [Beachhead market, initial channel]
Phase 2 (Months 6-12):  [Expand channels, refine messaging]
Phase 3 (Months 12-24): [Scale proven channels, add new segments]
```

### Marketing Strategy
- Positioning statement
- Key messaging by persona
- Channel strategy with budget allocation

### Sales Strategy
- Sales process and methodology
- Sales team structure and hiring plan
- Target quotas and ramp time

## Application Guidelines

1. **Start with diagnosis before prescription.** Gather data on the current state of funnel math and conversion before proposing changes. Misdiagnosis leads to wasted effort and organizational resistance.

2. **Use the 80/20 rule aggressively.** Identify the 20% of funnel math and conversion activities that drive 80% of value. Focus resources there first before optimizing the long tail.

3. **Build for reversibility when possible.** Prefer decisions that can be unwound cheaply over irreversible commitments, especially in early stages or high-uncertainty environments.

4. **Document decisions and rationale.** Future operators (including your future self) need to understand not just what was decided, but why. This prevents costly re-litigation of settled questions.

5. **Create accountability without bureaucracy.** Assign clear owners for each funnel math and conversion initiative. Use lightweight check-ins (weekly standup, async updates) rather than heavy governance.

- **Analysis paralysis**: Spending too long evaluating funnel math and conversion options when a quick experiment would provide more information than any amount of analysis.

- **Copying without context**: Adopting another company's funnel math and conversion approach without understanding the differences in scale, stage, market, and team that make it work for them.

- **Ignoring second-order effects**: Optimizing funnel math and conversion along one dimension while creating problems in adjacent areas. Always ask "and then what?"

- **Premature optimization**: Building sophisticated funnel math and conversion systems before product-market fit is established. Keep things simple until you have evidence that complexity is justified.

- **Single point of failure**: Concentrating funnel math and conversion knowledge or capability in one person or system. Build redundancy into critical paths.

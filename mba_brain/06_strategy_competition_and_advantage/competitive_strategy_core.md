## Frameworks

### Decision Matrix for Competitive Strategy Core

When evaluating competitive strategy core options, use this structured approach:

1. **Define the decision boundary** — What exactly are you deciding? What is out of scope?
2. **Identify stakeholders** — Who is affected? Who has veto power? Who has information you need?
3. **Enumerate options** — List at least 3 distinct approaches, including "do nothing"
4. **Evaluate against criteria** — Score each option on impact, cost, speed, reversibility, and risk
5. **Decide and document** — Record the decision, rationale, and expected outcomes for future reference

## Application Guidelines

1. **Start with diagnosis before prescription.** Gather data on the current state of competitive strategy core before proposing changes. Misdiagnosis leads to wasted effort and organizational resistance.

2. **Use the 80/20 rule aggressively.** Identify the 20% of competitive strategy core activities that drive 80% of value. Focus resources there first before optimizing the long tail.

3. **Build for reversibility when possible.** Prefer decisions that can be unwound cheaply over irreversible commitments, especially in early stages or high-uncertainty environments.

4. **Document decisions and rationale.** Future operators (including your future self) need to understand not just what was decided, but why. This prevents costly re-litigation of settled questions.

5. **Create accountability without bureaucracy.** Assign clear owners for each competitive strategy core initiative. Use lightweight check-ins (weekly standup, async updates) rather than heavy governance.

- **Ignoring second-order effects**: Optimizing competitive strategy core along one dimension while creating problems in adjacent areas. Always ask "and then what?"

- **Premature optimization**: Building sophisticated competitive strategy core systems before product-market fit is established. Keep things simple until you have evidence that complexity is justified.

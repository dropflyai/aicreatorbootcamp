# Customer Discovery Playbook

## Overview

Customer Discovery Playbook is a critical component within the Mba Brain's Entrepreneurship And New Ventures domain. This document provides frameworks, principles, and actionable guidance for applying customer discovery playbook concepts in real-world business and operational contexts.

Understanding customer discovery playbook enables leaders and operators to make better-informed decisions, reduce risk, and create sustainable competitive advantage.

## Key Concepts

- **First Principles Thinking**: Break down customer discovery playbook into fundamental truths before building up solutions. Avoid cargo-culting industry "best practices" without understanding why they work.

- **Systems Perspective**: Every customer discovery playbook decision exists within a larger system. Consider upstream inputs, downstream effects, and feedback loops before acting.

- **Measurement Framework**: Define leading and lagging indicators specific to customer discovery playbook. What you measure shapes behavior — choose metrics that drive the outcomes you want.

- **Risk-Reward Calibration**: Evaluate customer discovery playbook decisions through expected value calculations, not just upside potential. Account for tail risks and reversibility.

- **Iterative Refinement**: Treat customer discovery playbook as an evolving practice, not a one-time setup. Build review cycles and feedback mechanisms into every process.

## Frameworks

### Decision Matrix for Customer Discovery Playbook

When evaluating customer discovery playbook options, use this structured approach:

1. **Define the decision boundary** — What exactly are you deciding? What is out of scope?
2. **Identify stakeholders** — Who is affected? Who has veto power? Who has information you need?
3. **Enumerate options** — List at least 3 distinct approaches, including "do nothing"
4. **Evaluate against criteria** — Score each option on impact, cost, speed, reversibility, and risk
5. **Decide and document** — Record the decision, rationale, and expected outcomes for future reference

### The Entrepreneurship And New Ventures Playbook

Applying customer discovery playbook within the entrepreneurship and new ventures context requires:

- **Context awareness**: Understand the current state before prescribing changes
- **Constraint mapping**: Identify binding constraints (time, money, people, technology)
- **Sequencing**: Determine the order of operations that minimizes risk and maximizes learning
- **Feedback loops**: Build mechanisms to detect whether your approach is working within 1-2 cycles

## Application Guidelines

1. **Start with diagnosis before prescription.** Gather data on the current state of customer discovery playbook before proposing changes. Misdiagnosis leads to wasted effort and organizational resistance.

2. **Use the 80/20 rule aggressively.** Identify the 20% of customer discovery playbook activities that drive 80% of value. Focus resources there first before optimizing the long tail.

3. **Build for reversibility when possible.** Prefer decisions that can be unwound cheaply over irreversible commitments, especially in early stages or high-uncertainty environments.

4. **Document decisions and rationale.** Future operators (including your future self) need to understand not just what was decided, but why. This prevents costly re-litigation of settled questions.

5. **Create accountability without bureaucracy.** Assign clear owners for each customer discovery playbook initiative. Use lightweight check-ins (weekly standup, async updates) rather than heavy governance.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Analysis paralysis**: Spending too long evaluating customer discovery playbook options when a quick experiment would provide more information than any amount of analysis.

- **Copying without context**: Adopting another company's customer discovery playbook approach without understanding the differences in scale, stage, market, and team that make it work for them.

- **Ignoring second-order effects**: Optimizing customer discovery playbook along one dimension while creating problems in adjacent areas. Always ask "and then what?"

- **Premature optimization**: Building sophisticated customer discovery playbook systems before product-market fit is established. Keep things simple until you have evidence that complexity is justified.

- **Single point of failure**: Concentrating customer discovery playbook knowledge or capability in one person or system. Build redundancy into critical paths.

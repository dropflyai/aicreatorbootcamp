## Dimension 1: Strategy (Weight: 15%)

Ability to analyze competitive landscapes, define positioning, build sustainable advantage, and make strategic tradeoffs.

| Score | Description | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | No strategic thinking. Reactive, tactical decisions only. No awareness of competitive dynamics. | Recommendations ignore market context and competitive forces. |
| 2 | Basic awareness of competition but no framework applied. Confuses tactics with strategy. Generic recommendations. | Can name competitors but cannot articulate differentiation or moat. |
| 3 | Applies standard frameworks (Porter's 5 Forces, SWOT) correctly. Identifies competitive position. Makes explicit tradeoffs. | Analysis uses at least one strategic framework. Tradeoffs are documented. |
| 4 | Synthesizes multiple frameworks. Considers second-order effects and dynamic competition. Identifies sustainable advantage. | Multi-framework analysis with competitive dynamics. Anticipates rival responses. |
| 5 | Creates novel strategic insights. Identifies non-obvious opportunities. Thinks in systems with feedback loops. Defines kill criteria. | Original strategic thinking that goes beyond standard frameworks. Clear moat thesis with falsifiable assumptions. |

### Assessment Questions
- What is the company's sustainable competitive advantage and why is it durable?
- How would the strongest competitor respond to this strategy?
- What are the second-order effects of this strategic choice?
- Under what conditions should we abandon this strategy (kill criteria)?
- How does this strategy create compounding returns over time?

## Dimension 2: Finance (Weight: 15%)

Financial literacy, capital allocation discipline, unit economics understanding, and ability to use financial data for decisions.

| Score | Description | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Cannot read financial statements. No understanding of unit economics. Makes decisions without financial analysis. | Recommendations have no financial basis or contain basic errors (revenue vs. profit confusion). |
| 2 | Understands revenue and costs at a surface level. Cannot construct financial models. Ignores cash flow. | Can discuss revenue but cannot articulate CAC, LTV, or margin structure. |
| 3 | Reads financial statements fluently. Understands unit economics, burn rate, runway. Can evaluate basic financial tradeoffs. | Analysis includes unit economics. Financial statements are correctly interpreted. Burn rate and runway are calculated. |
| 4 | Builds financial models. Understands DCF, comparables, capital allocation. Can evaluate fundraising terms. Scenario analysis included. | Financial models with assumptions documented. Multiple scenarios (base, upside, downside). NPV/IRR analysis where appropriate. |
| 5 | CFO-level financial strategy. Optimizes capital structure. Sophisticated valuation with sensitivity analysis. Understands tax implications and financial engineering. | Advanced financial modeling with Monte Carlo or sensitivity analysis. Capital allocation framework with hurdle rates. Tax-aware strategy. |

### Assessment Questions
- What are the unit economics of this business and are they sustainable?
- How should the company allocate its next $1M of capital and why?
- Build a 3-year financial forecast with explicit assumptions.
- Evaluate this term sheet: what are the hidden costs and implications?
- What is the implied valuation from these comparable companies?

## Dimension 3: Operations (Weight: 10%)

Process design, supply chain thinking, operational efficiency, scaling execution, and quality management.

| Score | Description | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | No process awareness. Ad-hoc execution. Cannot identify bottlenecks. | Work output shows no systematic approach. No repeatable processes defined. |
| 2 | Recognizes the need for process but cannot design them. Identifies obvious bottlenecks but no solutions. | Can point to problems but proposed solutions are impractical or incomplete. |
| 3 | Designs functional processes. Identifies constraints and bottlenecks using TOC. Implements basic quality controls. | Processes documented with clear steps. Bottleneck analysis present. KPIs defined for operational health. |
| 4 | Optimizes processes for throughput and quality. Implements feedback loops. Designs for scale (10x current load). Uses data to drive improvements. | Process optimization with measured results. Capacity planning included. Automation of repetitive operations. |
| 5 | Systems-level operational design. Builds self-improving processes. Anticipates failure modes and builds resilience. World-class operational discipline. | Operations design with built-in feedback loops, failure modes addressed, and continuous improvement mechanisms. Anti-fragile process design. |

### Assessment Questions
- What is the binding constraint in this operation and how would you relieve it?
- Design a process that scales 10x without proportional headcount increase.
- How do you measure operational health? What are your leading indicators?
- What are the failure modes in this operation and how do you mitigate them?
- How would you implement continuous improvement in this process?

## Dimension 4: Marketing (Weight: 10%)

Market understanding, positioning, growth strategy, channel selection, and customer acquisition effectiveness.

| Score | Description | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | No marketing awareness. "Build it and they will come" mentality. Cannot articulate target customer. | No customer definition. No distribution strategy. Product-only focus. |
| 2 | Basic customer awareness but vague segmentation. Generic marketing tactics. No measurement. | Can describe customers loosely but no ICP. Marketing plan is a list of channels with no strategy. |
| 3 | Clear ICP definition. Positioning statement articulated. Channel strategy with CAC targets. Funnel metrics tracked. | Written ICP with demographics and psychographics. Positioning statement. Funnel with conversion metrics. CAC < LTV verified. |
| 4 | Multi-channel strategy with attribution. Growth loops identified. Retention strategy alongside acquisition. Pricing integrated with positioning. | Growth model with channel-level CAC and attribution. At least one growth loop identified. Retention metrics tracked alongside acquisition. |
| 5 | Creates category or redefines positioning. Network effects or viral growth engineered. Brand moat as competitive advantage. Predictable, scalable growth engine. | Evidence of category creation or redefinition. Viral coefficient > 1 or strong network effects. Brand equity measurable. Growth engine with predictable unit economics. |

### Assessment Questions
- Define the ICP: who is the ideal customer and why will they buy?
- What is the positioning statement and how is it differentiated?
- Which channels will drive acquisition and what is the expected CAC for each?
- Describe a growth loop that creates compounding returns.
- How do you retain customers after acquisition?

## Dimension 5: Leadership (Weight: 15%)

Team building, decision-making, stakeholder management, communication, and organizational design.

| Score | Description | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | No leadership awareness. Cannot delegate or communicate vision. Micromanages or abdicates. | Recommendations ignore team and organizational dynamics entirely. |
| 2 | Recognizes leadership matters but cannot operationalize it. Generic advice ("hire good people"). No organizational design. | Leadership recommendations are platitudes without actionable structure. |
| 3 | Designs functional org structures. Defines roles and responsibilities clearly. Communicates decisions with rationale. Delegates with accountability. | Org chart with clear reporting lines. RACI for key decisions. Decision communication template used. |
| 4 | Builds high-performing teams. Designs incentive systems aligned with goals. Manages up and across. Navigates organizational politics effectively. | Incentive design aligned with strategy. Stakeholder management plan. Evidence of cross-functional coordination. |
| 5 | Creates organizational culture as competitive advantage. Develops leaders who develop leaders. Manages through ambiguity with clarity. Inspires action without authority. | Culture design with reinforcing mechanisms. Leadership development framework. Evidence of leading through influence in ambiguous situations. |

### Assessment Questions
- Design an org structure for a 25-person startup scaling to 100.
- How do you align incentives between engineering, sales, and product?
- Describe how you would communicate a difficult strategic pivot to the team.
- What is your framework for making irreversible decisions under uncertainty?
- How do you build a culture that survives the departure of any single person?

## Dimension 6: Analytics (Weight: 10%)

Data-driven decision making, metric design, experimentation rigor, and insight extraction.

| Score | Description | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Ignores data. Decisions based on intuition or anecdote. No metrics tracked. | Recommendations cite no data. No KPIs defined. |
| 2 | Tracks vanity metrics. No statistical rigor. Confuses correlation with causation. | References data but uses misleading metrics (total signups vs. active users). No hypothesis testing. |
| 3 | Defines meaningful KPIs with leading and lagging indicators. Basic experimentation (A/B tests). Understands statistical significance. | KPI dashboard with leading/lagging indicators. A/B test designed with sample size calculation. Results interpreted with confidence intervals. |
| 4 | Designs metric hierarchies (North Star -> input metrics). Bayesian reasoning applied. Cohort analysis and segmentation. Distinguishes signal from noise. | Metric tree from North Star to input metrics. Cohort analysis with retention curves. Bayesian updating on beliefs with new data. |
| 5 | Creates data-informed culture. Designs measurement systems that drive behavior. Advanced causal inference. Quantifies uncertainty explicitly. | Measurement system design. Causal inference methodology (diff-in-diff, RCT, instrumental variables). Decision frameworks with explicit probability estimates. |

### Assessment Questions
- What is the North Star metric for this business and what are its input metrics?
- Design an A/B test for this feature: hypothesis, sample size, success criteria, duration.
- This metric went up 20% last week. What are five possible explanations and how would you determine which is correct?
- How do you avoid Goodhart's Law when designing incentive metrics?
- Quantify your confidence in this recommendation with explicit probability.

## Dimension 7: Ethics (Weight: 10%)

Ethical reasoning, stakeholder consideration, long-term thinking, regulatory awareness, and responsible business practices.

| Score | Description | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | No ethical consideration. Pure profit maximization. Ignores externalities and stakeholders beyond shareholders. | Recommendations ignore regulatory, social, or environmental implications entirely. |
| 2 | Aware that ethics matter but treats compliance as the ceiling. Reactive to ethical issues rather than proactive. | Mentions regulations but only as constraints to work around. No stakeholder analysis beyond customers and shareholders. |
| 3 | Considers multiple stakeholders. Applies ethical frameworks (utilitarian, deontological, virtue). Regulatory compliance as floor not ceiling. Transparency valued. | Stakeholder analysis includes employees, community, and environment. At least one ethical framework applied. Regulatory requirements met proactively. |
| 4 | Ethical reasoning integrated into strategy. Anticipates ethical dilemmas before they arise. Builds trust as strategic asset. Long-term reputation management. | Ethics integrated into decision framework (not an afterthought). Trust-building actions documented. Anticipatory ethics analysis for new initiatives. |
| 5 | Creates ethical competitive advantage. Designs incentive systems that align profit with positive impact. Industry leadership on responsible practices. Builds ethical culture. | Business model with aligned profit and impact incentives. Industry leadership examples. Ethical culture design with reinforcing mechanisms. |

### Assessment Questions
- Identify the top 3 ethical risks in this business and how to mitigate them.
- A feature would increase revenue 15% but may harm a vulnerable user segment. What do you recommend?
- How do you build a culture where employees raise ethical concerns?
- What stakeholders beyond shareholders should influence this decision and why?
- Design an incentive system that aligns profit with positive social impact.

## Dimension 8: Execution (Weight: 15%)

Ability to translate strategy into action, manage projects, hit deadlines, and deliver measurable results.

| Score | Description | Evidence Required |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Cannot translate strategy into tasks. No project management. Chronic missed deadlines. No accountability. | Plans are vague ("we should do marketing"). No milestones, no deadlines, no owners. |
| 2 | Creates task lists but poor prioritization. Underestimates complexity. No risk management. Inconsistent follow-through. | Task lists exist but are not prioritized. Timelines are unrealistic. No contingency planning. |
| 3 | Structured project plans with milestones, owners, and deadlines. Prioritization using frameworks (ICE, RICE). Risk register maintained. Regular progress tracking. | Project plan with milestones and owners. Prioritization framework applied. Risk register with mitigations. Weekly status updates. |
| 4 | High-velocity execution with quality. Parallel workstreams managed. Dependencies identified and managed. Scope creep controlled. Retrospectives drive improvement. | Evidence of parallel execution. Dependency map. Scope management examples. Retrospective action items completed. |
| 5 | Execution as competitive advantage. Speed without sacrificing quality. Builds execution muscle into team DNA. Predictable delivery with continuous improvement. | Track record of on-time, on-quality delivery. Execution playbooks that enable team-wide consistency. Velocity metrics improving over time. |

### Assessment Questions
- Take this strategy and create a 90-day execution plan with milestones.
- How do you prioritize when everything is "urgent"?
- What is your risk management framework for this project?
- How do you maintain execution quality while increasing velocity?
- Describe your approach to managing scope creep.

## How to Use This Rubric

### Self-Assessment
1. Score each dimension honestly using the criteria above.
2. Answer at least 2 assessment questions per dimension to verify your score.
3. Calculate weighted total.
4. Identify your 2 weakest dimensions for focused development.

### Output Evaluation
1. Score the strategic output across all 8 dimensions.
2. Any dimension scoring 2 or below is a blocking issue -- output should not be delivered.
3. Overall score below 3.0 means the output needs fundamental rework.
4. Document scores and rationale for accountability.

### Continuous Improvement
- Reassess monthly.
- Track score trends over time.
- Set target: improve weakest dimension by 1 point per quarter.
- Use `Memory/FrameworkApplications.md` to log patterns that improve scores.

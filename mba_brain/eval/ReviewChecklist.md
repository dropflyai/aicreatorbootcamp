# Strategic Review Checklist

Comprehensive quality gate for all strategic deliverables produced by the MBA Brain. Every recommendation, analysis, or plan MUST pass this checklist before delivery.

---

## How to Use

1. Complete each section by checking all applicable items.
2. Mark items as N/A only with written justification.
3. Any section with unchecked blocking items (marked with [B]) means the deliverable is not ready.
4. Non-blocking items (marked with [NB]) should be addressed but do not prevent delivery.
5. Record the review in the Review Log at the bottom.

---

## Section 1: Problem Definition [B]

The deliverable must clearly define what problem it solves.

- [ ] Problem statement is explicit and specific (not vague or overly broad)
- [ ] Stakeholders affected by the problem are identified
- [ ] Scope is defined: what is in scope and what is explicitly out of scope
- [ ] Success criteria are defined: how will we know the problem is solved?
- [ ] Urgency and importance are assessed (Eisenhower matrix placement)
- [ ] Root cause vs. symptom distinction is made

### Red Flags
- Problem statement includes the solution ("We need to build X" is a solution, not a problem)
- Problem is so broad it cannot be meaningfully addressed
- No success criteria defined

---

## Section 2: Market Analysis [B]

The deliverable must demonstrate understanding of the market landscape.

- [ ] Total Addressable Market (TAM) estimated with methodology explained
- [ ] Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM) narrowed with reasoning
- [ ] Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM) realistic with bottleneck identified
- [ ] Market trends identified (growing, shrinking, shifting) with data sources
- [ ] Customer segments defined with distinct needs and willingness to pay
- [ ] Market timing assessed: why now? What has changed?
- [ ] Regulatory environment considered

### Red Flags
- TAM is "everyone" or a single large number with no derivation
- No customer segmentation
- Market timing not addressed

---

## Section 3: Competitive Analysis [B]

The deliverable must account for competitive dynamics.

- [ ] Direct competitors identified (same product, same customer)
- [ ] Indirect competitors identified (different product, same job-to-be-done)
- [ ] Competitive advantages articulated for each major competitor
- [ ] Competitive vulnerabilities identified for each major competitor
- [ ] Own positioning clearly differentiated from top 3 competitors
- [ ] Competitor likely responses to our strategy anticipated
- [ ] Barriers to entry / switching costs analyzed

### Red Flags
- "We have no competitors" (everyone has competitors or substitutes)
- Competitive analysis is surface-level (features only, no strategic depth)
- No anticipation of competitive response

---

## Section 4: Financial Health [B]

The deliverable must include sound financial reasoning.

- [ ] Unit economics defined (CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, payback period)
- [ ] Revenue model clearly articulated (pricing, packaging, monetization)
- [ ] Cost structure mapped (fixed vs. variable, direct vs. indirect)
- [ ] Gross margin calculated and benchmarked against industry
- [ ] Burn rate and runway calculated (if pre-revenue or cash-burning)
- [ ] Break-even analysis completed
- [ ] Financial projections include base, upside, and downside scenarios
- [ ] Assumptions explicitly documented and falsifiable

### Red Flags
- No unit economics
- Single scenario financial projection
- LTV:CAC ratio below 3:1 without explanation
- Assumptions hidden or unrealistic ("we'll get 10% of the market")

---

## Section 5: Customer Understanding [B]

The deliverable must demonstrate deep customer empathy.

- [ ] Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) defined with specificity
- [ ] Customer jobs-to-be-done identified (functional, social, emotional)
- [ ] Customer pains and gains mapped
- [ ] Customer acquisition journey mapped (awareness -> consideration -> decision -> retention)
- [ ] Customer willingness to pay validated or estimated with methodology
- [ ] Customer feedback or research cited (interviews, surveys, behavioral data)
- [ ] Churn drivers identified (if existing product)

### Red Flags
- ICP is vague or describes everyone
- No evidence of customer interaction or research
- Jobs-to-be-done not articulated

---

## Section 6: Strategy and Positioning [B]

The deliverable must articulate a coherent strategy.

- [ ] Strategic choice is explicit: what we ARE doing and what we are NOT doing
- [ ] Positioning statement follows format: For [target], who [need], [product] is a [category] that [benefit], unlike [alternative], we [differentiator]
- [ ] Sustainable competitive advantage (moat) articulated with durability assessment
- [ ] Value chain position identified: where do we capture value?
- [ ] Strategic tradeoffs made explicit: what are we giving up?
- [ ] Win condition defined: what does winning look like in 1/3/5 years?
- [ ] Kill criteria defined: under what conditions do we abandon this strategy?

### Red Flags
- Strategy is "be the best at everything" (no tradeoffs)
- No moat thesis or moat is temporary (features, not systems)
- Kill criteria absent

---

## Section 7: Go-to-Market [NB]

If the deliverable includes a GTM plan, it must be actionable.

- [ ] Launch strategy defined (big bang, phased, soft launch, beta)
- [ ] Channel strategy with expected CAC per channel
- [ ] First 100 customers acquisition plan (specific, not generic)
- [ ] Messaging framework tied to customer pains
- [ ] Sales process defined (self-serve, sales-assisted, enterprise)
- [ ] Partnership or distribution leverage identified
- [ ] Timeline with milestones and owners

---

## Section 8: Team and Organization [NB]

If the deliverable addresses team or org design, it must be realistic.

- [ ] Key roles identified with hiring priority
- [ ] Skill gaps between current team and strategy requirements documented
- [ ] Org structure supports strategy (not the other way around)
- [ ] Decision-making authority clarified (RACI or equivalent)
- [ ] Culture considerations addressed
- [ ] Advisor or board composition considered

---

## Section 9: Operations and Execution [B]

The deliverable must be actionable, not just analytical.

- [ ] 90-day execution plan with milestones
- [ ] Owners assigned for each workstream
- [ ] Dependencies between workstreams identified
- [ ] Resource requirements (people, money, tools) quantified
- [ ] Risk register with top 5 risks, likelihood, impact, and mitigations
- [ ] Progress metrics defined (leading indicators, not just lagging)
- [ ] Review cadence specified (weekly, biweekly, monthly)

### Red Flags
- No execution plan (analysis without action)
- No owners (accountability gap)
- No risk assessment

---

## Section 10: Risk Assessment [B]

The deliverable must address what could go wrong.

- [ ] Top 5 risks identified with likelihood and impact scoring
- [ ] Mitigation strategy for each risk
- [ ] Contingency plan for top 2 risks (what if mitigation fails?)
- [ ] Reversibility of key decisions assessed (one-way vs. two-way doors)
- [ ] Dependencies on external factors identified
- [ ] Worst-case scenario described with response plan

---

## Section 11: Decision Quality [B]

The deliverable must demonstrate rigorous decision-making.

- [ ] Decision Quality Framework applied (from CLAUDE.md):
  - [ ] Incentives analyzed
  - [ ] Second-order effects considered
  - [ ] Reversibility assessed
  - [ ] Tradeoff clarity achieved
  - [ ] Kill criteria defined
- [ ] Alternatives considered (minimum 2 options evaluated)
- [ ] Evidence-based reasoning (not just opinion)
- [ ] Assumptions documented and falsifiable
- [ ] Confidence level stated explicitly

---

## Section 12: Communication Quality [NB]

The deliverable must be clear and actionable.

- [ ] Executive summary present (3-5 sentences a CEO can act on)
- [ ] Structure follows: Situation -> Complication -> Resolution
- [ ] Jargon defined or avoided
- [ ] Visual aids (charts, diagrams) where they add clarity
- [ ] Action items clearly stated with owners and deadlines
- [ ] Appendix for supporting detail (keep main body concise)

---

## Section 13: Ethical Considerations [B]

The deliverable must consider ethical implications.

- [ ] Stakeholder impact assessed beyond shareholders
- [ ] Potential negative externalities identified
- [ ] Regulatory and compliance requirements checked
- [ ] Long-term reputation impact considered
- [ ] Data privacy implications addressed (if applicable)

---

## Section 14: Cross-Brain Consultation [NB]

Has the deliverable consulted relevant specialist brains?

- [ ] Engineering Brain consulted for technical feasibility (if applicable)
- [ ] Design Brain consulted for UX implications (if applicable)
- [ ] Finance Brain consulted for financial modeling (if applicable)
- [ ] Legal Brain consulted for compliance (if applicable)
- [ ] Other specialist brains consulted as needed

---

## Section 15: MBA Score Verification [B]

Final quality gate using the 8-dimension scoring rubric.

- [ ] MBAScore.md rubric applied to deliverable
- [ ] Overall score meets minimum threshold (3.5 for strategic, 4.0 for executive)
- [ ] No individual dimension scores below 3
- [ ] Weakest dimensions identified with improvement plan

- Track pass/fail rates by section to identify systemic gaps.

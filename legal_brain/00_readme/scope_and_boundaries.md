# Legal Brain -- Scope and Boundaries

> **DISCLAIMER:** This content is for educational and informational purposes only. It does not constitute legal advice. Consult qualified legal counsel for specific legal matters.

---

## Scope Definition

The Legal Brain covers **business law for technology companies** with particular depth in the following domains. Each domain specifies its jurisdictional focus, limitations, and escalation triggers.

---

## Domain 1: Contract Law

### In Scope
- Contract formation, interpretation, and enforcement under common law and UCC
- Drafting and reviewing commercial agreements (SaaS, vendor, licensing, services)
- Employment-related agreements (offer letters, NDAs, non-competes, IP assignment)
- Standard contract provisions: representations, warranties, indemnification, limitation of liability, termination, force majeure
- Contract negotiation strategy and redline analysis
- Boilerplate clause analysis: entire agreement, severability, assignment, governing law, dispute resolution

### Jurisdictional Focus
- **Primary:** United States (federal and state, with emphasis on Delaware, California, New York)
- **Secondary:** United Kingdom, European Union (for cross-border transactions)
- **UCC coverage:** Articles 1 and 2 (sale of goods); Article 2A (leases) referenced where relevant

### Out of Scope
- Real property transactions (deeds, mortgages, real estate closings)
- Family law agreements (prenuptial, custody, divorce settlements)
- Government contracts (FAR, DFARS, specialized procurement law)
- Construction contracts (AIA, mechanic's liens, retainage)

### Escalation Triggers
- Contract value exceeds $5 million
- Cross-border transaction involving more than 3 jurisdictions
- Government entity is a counterparty
- Settlement of existing litigation

## Domain 2: Intellectual Property

### In Scope
- Patent strategy: provisional and utility applications, continuation practice, freedom-to-operate analysis
- Copyright: software copyrightability, work-for-hire, fair use, DMCA, licensing
- Trademark: selection, clearance, registration (USPTO), enforcement, domain disputes (UDRP)
- Trade secrets: identification, reasonable protective measures, DTSA, state UTSA adoption
- Open source: license identification (GPL, MIT, Apache, BSD, MPL), compatibility analysis, compliance programs
- IP assignment and work-for-hire agreements
- IP due diligence for M&A transactions

### Jurisdictional Focus
- **Primary:** United States (USPTO, Copyright Office, federal courts)
- **Secondary:** WIPO (PCT, Madrid Protocol), European Patent Office, EU trademarks (EUIPO)

### Out of Scope
- Patent prosecution (actual filing and prosecution before the USPTO requires a registered patent attorney/agent)
- Design patent analysis requiring visual inspection
- Plant patents and plant variety protection
- Semiconductor mask work protection (detailed SCPA analysis)

### Escalation Triggers
- Patent infringement allegation (send or receive)
- Cease and desist letters (trademark or copyright)
- Open source license violation discovery
- Trade secret misappropriation claim
- IP litigation of any kind

---

## Domain 3: Privacy and Data Protection

### In Scope
- GDPR: principles, lawful bases, data subject rights, controller/processor obligations, DPIAs, breach notification, cross-border transfers (SCCs, adequacy decisions, BCRs)
- CCPA/CPRA: consumer rights, business obligations, service provider requirements, opt-out mechanisms, CPPA enforcement
- Other US state laws: Virginia (VCDPA), Colorado (CPA), Connecticut (CTDPA), and emerging state legislation
- Sector-specific: HIPAA (health), FERPA (education), COPPA (children), GLBA (financial)
- Privacy engineering: privacy by design, data mapping, consent management, data minimization
- International frameworks: APEC CBPR, Brazil LGPD, Canada PIPEDA, UK Data Protection Act 2018
- Data processing agreements, sub-processor management, vendor privacy due diligence

### Jurisdictional Focus
- **Primary:** EU/EEA (GDPR), California (CCPA/CPRA), United States (federal sector laws)
- **Secondary:** UK, Canada, Brazil, Australia, select APAC jurisdictions

### Out of Scope
- National security and intelligence community data handling (FISA, EO 12333)
- Classified information handling procedures
- Law enforcement data access (detailed CLOUD Act analysis)
- Country-specific privacy laws for jurisdictions not listed above (consult local counsel)

### Escalation Triggers
- Data breach affecting more than 500 individuals
- Regulatory inquiry or investigation from any DPA
- Cross-border data transfer to a jurisdiction without adequacy decision
- DPIA revealing high residual risk under GDPR Article 35
- Class action or representative action related to privacy

## Domain 4: Corporate Law and Governance

### In Scope
- Entity formation: C-corp, S-corp, LLC, LP, benefit corporations
- Delaware incorporation: certificate of incorporation, bylaws, registered agent
- Foreign qualification in other states
- Board of directors: composition, fiduciary duties (care, loyalty, good faith), committee structures
- Stockholder agreements: voting, information rights, ROFR, co-sale, drag-along
- Equity: authorized shares, stock issuance, stock options (ISOs, NSOs), restricted stock, RSUs
- 83(b) elections, QSBS qualification (Section 1202), cap table management
- Corporate minutes, written consents, annual compliance

### Jurisdictional Focus
- **Primary:** Delaware (DGCL), California, New York
- **Secondary:** Model Business Corporation Act (MBCA) states

### Out of Scope
- Public company governance (SEC reporting, SOX compliance, proxy solicitation)
- Banking and insurance company formation and regulation
- Non-profit formation and tax-exempt status (501(c)(3) applications)
- Cooperative and mutual organization governance

### Escalation Triggers
- Potential breach of fiduciary duty by officer or director
- Stockholder derivative action or threatened litigation
- Restatement of financial statements
- Change of control transaction
- Dissolution or wind-down proceedings

---

## Domain 5: Employment Law

### In Scope
- Hiring: offer letters, background checks, I-9 compliance, at-will employment
- Compensation: wage and hour (FLSA), overtime, exempt vs. non-exempt classification, equal pay
- Workplace policies: anti-discrimination (Title VII, ADA, ADEA), anti-harassment, accommodations
- Leave: FMLA, state-specific leave laws, parental leave, disability accommodations
- Termination: at-will doctrine, wrongful termination exceptions, WARN Act, severance agreements
- Contractor classification: IRS 20-factor test, ABC test (California AB5), DOL guidance
- Non-competes: enforceability by state, FTC developments, garden leave provisions
- Workplace safety: OSHA general duty clause, industry-specific standards

### Jurisdictional Focus
- **Primary:** United States (federal: FLSA, Title VII, ADA, FMLA, NLRA; state: California, New York, Texas)
- **Secondary:** EU employment directive framework, UK Employment Rights Act

### Out of Scope
- Immigration law (visa sponsorship, PERM labor certification -- consult immigration counsel)
- Workers' compensation claims and adjudication
- Union organizing and collective bargaining (detailed NLRA strategy)
- ERISA and pension plan administration

### Escalation Triggers
- EEOC charge or state agency complaint
- Wage and hour class action
- Whistleblower retaliation claim
- Workplace safety incident with injury
- Executive termination involving disputed equity

---

## Domain 6: Regulatory Compliance

### In Scope
- Fintech: money transmission licensing, payment processing regulation, SEC/CFTC digital asset guidance
- Health tech: HIPAA compliance, FDA software regulation (SaMD), clinical trial data
- Ed tech: FERPA, COPPA, state student privacy laws
- Compliance programs: written policies, training, monitoring, reporting, remediation
- Anti-corruption: FCPA, UK Bribery Act, due diligence for international operations
- Export controls: EAR, ITAR (overview-level; detailed analysis requires export control counsel)
- Sanctions: OFAC SDN list, sectoral sanctions, screening obligations

### Jurisdictional Focus
- **Primary:** United States (federal regulatory agencies: SEC, FTC, HHS, DOE, FinCEN)
- **Secondary:** EU regulatory frameworks, UK FCA

### Out of Scope
- Environmental law (EPA, Clean Air Act, CERCLA)
- Energy regulation (FERC, state public utility commissions)
- Telecommunications regulation (detailed FCC analysis)
- Agricultural and food safety regulation (FDA food, USDA)
- Tax law (federal and state income tax, transfer pricing -- consult tax counsel)

### Escalation Triggers
- Regulatory investigation or subpoena
- Voluntary self-disclosure to any regulatory agency
- Material compliance failure discovered during audit
- Change in regulation that materially affects business operations

---

## Domain 7: Dispute Resolution

### In Scope
- Pre-litigation strategy: demand letters, cease and desist, settlement negotiation
- Mediation: process, mediator selection, settlement agreement drafting
- Arbitration: clause drafting, institutional rules (AAA, JAMS, ICC), enforcement of awards
- Litigation overview: pleading standards, discovery obligations, motion practice, trial preparation
- E-discovery: legal hold obligations, ESI protocols, proportionality, privilege review
- Settlement: evaluation, negotiation strategy, release drafting, confidentiality provisions

### Jurisdictional Focus
- **Primary:** United States (federal and state courts, AAA, JAMS)
- **Secondary:** International arbitration (ICC, LCIA, SIAC, UNCITRAL)

### Out of Scope
- Criminal defense or prosecution
- Bankruptcy and insolvency proceedings (Chapter 7, 11, 13)
- Administrative agency hearings (detailed procedural guidance)
- Appellate practice (detailed brief writing and oral argument strategy)

### Escalation Triggers
- ANY active litigation (the Legal Brain provides educational frameworks; active case strategy requires retained litigation counsel)
- Service of process (complaint, subpoena, court order)
- Threat of regulatory enforcement action
- Discovery deadline approaching without adequate response

## General Boundaries

### What the Legal Brain IS
- An educational resource encoding legal frameworks, principles, and best practices
- A drafting assistant that produces template-quality documents requiring attorney review
- A risk identification system that flags issues for human legal review
- A compliance framework that helps build legal infrastructure

### What the Legal Brain IS NOT
- A licensed attorney providing legal advice
- A substitute for jurisdiction-specific legal counsel
- A litigation strategy tool for active disputes
- A regulatory filing system

### Universal Escalation Rules

The following situations ALWAYS require escalation to qualified human counsel:

1. **Active litigation** -- Any matter where a lawsuit has been filed or is imminent
2. **Regulatory investigation** -- Any contact from a regulatory agency
3. **Criminal exposure** -- Any situation involving potential criminal liability
4. **Material transactions** -- M&A, IPO, or financing above $10 million
5. **Cross-border complexity** -- Operations in jurisdictions not covered by this brain
6. **Novel legal questions** -- Issues where the law is unsettled or rapidly evolving
7. **Privileged communications** -- Situations requiring attorney-client privilege protection

As laws change and new legal domains become relevant, the scope of the Legal Brain should be updated. Any scope expansion must:

1. Identify the new domain and its jurisdictional coverage
2. Specify in-scope and out-of-scope boundaries
3. Define escalation triggers
4. Create corresponding module files in the appropriate directory
5. Update this document and the `CLAUDE.md` module map

**The Legal Brain operates within these boundaries to provide maximum value while maintaining the integrity and limitations appropriate for educational legal guidance.**

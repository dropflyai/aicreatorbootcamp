# Legal Score — Quality Enforcement (Authoritative)

> **DISCLAIMER:** This brain provides educational guidance. It is not a substitute
> for licensed legal counsel. All outputs should be reviewed by a qualified
> attorney before being relied upon for legal decisions.

This document defines how Legal Brain output quality is evaluated.
Every legal analysis must be scored before it is considered complete.

If quality is not measurable, it is not enforced.

---

## SCORING RULES (MANDATORY)

Each analysis must be scored across the following dimensions.
Score each category from **1 (poor)** to **5 (excellent)**.

### Hard Fail Dimensions
These dimensions are critical. Score <3 = immediate rework required:
- **Risk Identification**
- **Compliance Posture**
- **Privacy Compliance**

### Passing Criteria
- Average score across all dimensions >= 4.0
- No hard-fail dimension < 3
- No dimension < 2
- Disclaimer must be present on all outputs

### Weighting
Risk Identification and Compliance Posture carry 1.5x weight in the
final average. Missing a risk or compliance gap is the worst possible failure.

## 1. RISK IDENTIFICATION

**Question:**
Has every material legal risk been identified, categorized, and assessed?

### What to Evaluate
- Comprehensive issue spotting: are all relevant legal issues identified?
- Materiality assessment: are risks ranked by severity and likelihood?
- Hidden risk detection: are non-obvious risks surfaced (IP, regulatory, contractual)?
- Jurisdiction awareness: are jurisdiction-specific risks flagged?
- Temporal awareness: are time-sensitive risks (statutes of limitation, deadlines) flagged?
- Interconnected risks: are cascading and compound risks identified?
- Stakeholder-specific risks: are risks mapped to affected parties?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — All material risks identified with severity and likelihood assessment.
        Hidden and non-obvious risks surfaced. Jurisdiction-specific issues
        flagged. Temporal urgency noted. Risk interactions mapped.
        Comparable to senior associate at a top-tier firm.
- **4** — Most material risks identified. Severity assessed. Some non-obvious
        risks noted. Jurisdiction mostly addressed. Minor gaps in temporal
        or interaction analysis.
- **3** — Major risks identified but materiality assessment is incomplete.
        Non-obvious risks missed. Jurisdiction awareness partial.
        Risk interactions not considered.
- **2** — Only obvious risks identified. No materiality ranking. Jurisdiction
        not considered. Significant blind spots.
- **1** — Critical risks missed. No systematic issue spotting. Analysis would
        expose the client to unidentified liability.

### Hard Fail Criteria
- Score < 3 = **IMMEDIATE REWORK REQUIRED**
- Missing a risk that would result in material liability = automatic score cap at 2
- Failing to flag jurisdiction-specific requirements = deduct 1 point
- No materiality assessment = automatic score cap at 3

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Risk coverage (vs. expert audit) | > 90% | Expert comparison |
| Materiality accuracy | > 85% | Post-hoc validation |
| Hidden risk detection rate | > 70% | Expert comparison |
| Jurisdiction accuracy | 100% | Legal review |
| False positive rate | < 20% | Expert review |
| Time-sensitive risk flagging | 100% | Deadline audit |

## 2. CONTRACT QUALITY

**Question:**
Are contracts clear, balanced, enforceable, and commercially reasonable?

### What to Evaluate
- Clarity of terms: can a non-lawyer understand the core obligations?
- Risk allocation: is risk distributed fairly between parties?
- Enforceability: are all provisions enforceable in the relevant jurisdiction?
- Completeness: are all necessary provisions included (termination, IP, liability)?
- Ambiguity: are there provisions that could be interpreted multiple ways?
- Commercial reasonableness: do terms reflect market standards?
- Protective provisions: are appropriate protections included (indemnity, limitation of liability, insurance)?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Terms are crystal clear. Risk allocation is balanced and commercially
        reasonable. All provisions enforceable. Complete coverage of necessary
        terms. No ambiguity. Protective provisions are comprehensive.
        Contract could withstand aggressive counterparty review.
- **4** — Terms are mostly clear. Risk allocation reasonable with minor imbalances.
        Provisions enforceable. Minor gaps in coverage. Limited ambiguity.
        Good protective provisions.
- **3** — Terms require some interpretation. Risk allocation skewed but workable.
        Most provisions enforceable. Noticeable gaps. Some ambiguous language.
        Basic protections present.
- **2** — Terms are unclear in material respects. Risk allocation significantly
        imbalanced. Some provisions may be unenforceable. Major gaps.
        Multiple ambiguities. Weak protections.
- **1** — Terms are confusing or contradictory. Risk allocation is one-sided.
        Key provisions unenforceable. Missing critical terms. Contract would
        not withstand dispute.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Clarity score (plain language) | > 4.0 | Readability assessment |
| Risk balance rating | Balanced | Risk matrix comparison |
| Enforceability confidence | > 95% | Jurisdiction review |
| Provision completeness | 100% of standard terms | Checklist audit |
| Ambiguity count | 0 material ambiguities | Legal review |
| Negotiation turnaround | < 3 rounds | Process tracking |

## 3. COMPLIANCE POSTURE

**Question:**
Are all applicable regulatory requirements mapped, assessed, and addressed?

### What to Evaluate
- Regulatory mapping: are all applicable regulations identified?
- Gap analysis: are current practices compared against requirements?
- Remediation planning: are gaps prioritized with remediation timelines?
- Monitoring: is there a system for tracking regulatory changes?
- Documentation: is compliance posture documented and auditable?
- Cross-border compliance: are international requirements addressed?
- Industry-specific requirements: are sector-specific regulations covered?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — All applicable regulations identified across all relevant jurisdictions.
        Complete gap analysis with prioritized remediation plan. Monitoring
        system for regulatory changes. Full documentation. Cross-border
        requirements addressed. Industry requirements covered.
- **4** — Most regulations identified. Gap analysis mostly complete. Remediation
        plan exists with some prioritization gaps. Basic monitoring.
        Good documentation. Most cross-border issues addressed.
- **3** — Major regulations identified but minor ones missed. Partial gap
        analysis. Remediation plan incomplete. No systematic monitoring.
        Partial documentation. Cross-border gaps.
- **2** — Significant regulatory gaps. Incomplete gap analysis. No remediation
        plan. No monitoring. Poor documentation. Cross-border ignored.
- **1** — Regulatory landscape not assessed. No gap analysis. No remediation.
        No monitoring. No documentation. Would fail an audit.

### Hard Fail Criteria
- Score < 3 = **IMMEDIATE REWORK REQUIRED**
- Missing a regulation that applies to the business = automatic score cap at 2
- No gap analysis for identified regulations = automatic score cap at 3
- Missing cross-border requirements for international operations = deduct 1 point

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Regulatory coverage | 100% | Expert audit |
| Gap analysis completeness | > 95% | Checklist audit |
| Remediation plan coverage | 100% of identified gaps | Plan review |
| Regulatory change monitoring | < 7 day lag | Monitor audit |
| Audit readiness | Always audit-ready | Mock audit quarterly |
| Cross-border coverage | All operating jurisdictions | Jurisdiction review |

## 4. IP PROTECTION

**Question:**
Are all intellectual property assets identified, protected, and monitored?

### What to Evaluate
- Asset identification: are all IP assets catalogued (patents, trademarks, copyrights, trade secrets)?
- Registration status: are registerable assets filed in appropriate jurisdictions?
- Protection strategy: are assets protected through appropriate mechanisms?
- Monitoring: are potential infringements being detected?
- Enforcement readiness: can the company enforce its IP rights effectively?
- Employee/contractor IP: are assignment agreements in place?
- Open source compliance: are open source licenses respected?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Complete IP inventory. All registerable assets filed. Comprehensive
        protection strategy. Active monitoring for infringement. Enforcement
        playbook ready. All assignment agreements current. Open source
        compliance program in place.
- **4** — Mostly complete inventory. Key assets filed. Good protection strategy.
        Basic monitoring. Enforcement capability exists. Most assignments
        in place. Open source awareness.
- **3** — Partial inventory. Some assets filed. Protection strategy for key
        assets only. No systematic monitoring. Limited enforcement
        capability. Some assignment gaps. Open source tracking informal.
- **2** — Incomplete inventory. Few filings. Weak protection. No monitoring.
        No enforcement capability. Assignment gaps. Open source risks.
- **1** — No IP inventory. No filings. No protection strategy. No monitoring.
        No enforcement. Assignment agreements missing. Open source violations likely.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| IP inventory completeness | 100% | Annual audit |
| Registration coverage | All key assets | Filing tracker |
| Monitoring coverage | All registered assets | Monitor report |
| Assignment agreement coverage | 100% of team | HR audit |
| Open source compliance | Zero violations | License audit |
| Enforcement response time | < 30 days from detection | Incident log |

## 5. PRIVACY COMPLIANCE

**Question:**
Is personal data handling compliant with all applicable privacy regulations?

### What to Evaluate
- GDPR compliance: for EU data subjects (lawful basis, rights, DPIAs, DPO)
- CCPA/CPRA compliance: for California residents (disclosure, opt-out, deletion)
- Other jurisdiction compliance: PIPEDA, LGPD, POPIA, etc.
- Data Processing Agreements: are DPAs in place with all processors?
- Consent management: is consent collected, stored, and revocable?
- Data mapping: is all personal data mapped (collection, storage, transfer, deletion)?
- Breach response: is there a breach notification plan?
- Privacy by design: are privacy considerations built into product development?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Full compliance with all applicable privacy regulations. DPIAs completed
        for all high-risk processing. DPAs with all processors. Consent
        management is robust and auditable. Complete data map. Breach plan
        tested. Privacy by design embedded in development process.
- **4** — Mostly compliant. DPIAs for high-risk processing. Most DPAs in place.
        Consent management functional. Data map mostly complete. Breach plan
        exists. Privacy considered in development.
- **3** — Partially compliant. Some DPIAs done. DPA gaps. Basic consent
        management. Incomplete data map. Breach plan drafted but untested.
        Privacy is an afterthought.
- **2** — Significant compliance gaps. No DPIAs. Few DPAs. Consent management
        weak. No data map. No breach plan. Privacy not considered.
- **1** — Non-compliant. No privacy program. No DPIAs. No DPAs. No consent
        management. No data map. No breach plan. Active violation risk.

### Hard Fail Criteria
- Score < 3 = **IMMEDIATE REWORK REQUIRED**
- No lawful basis identified for data processing = automatic score cap at 1
- No breach notification plan = automatic score cap at 2
- DPA missing for a processor handling sensitive data = deduct 2 points
- No consent mechanism for consent-dependent processing = automatic score cap at 2

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| DPIA completion | 100% of high-risk | DPIA register |
| DPA coverage | 100% of processors | Vendor audit |
| Consent validity | 100% auditable | Consent audit |
| Data map accuracy | > 95% | Quarterly validation |
| Breach drill frequency | 2x/year | Drill log |
| DSR response time | < 30 days (GDPR) | Request log |

## 6. PRACTICAL JUDGMENT

**Question:**
Is advice business-aware, proportionate, and enabling rather than purely risk-averse?

### What to Evaluate
- Business context: does the advice consider commercial objectives?
- Proportionality: is the recommended action proportionate to the risk?
- Alternative approaches: are multiple options presented with trade-offs?
- Cost-benefit analysis: are costs of compliance vs. non-compliance considered?
- Speed awareness: does the advice consider business timelines?
- Risk tolerance calibration: is advice calibrated to the company's risk appetite?
- Enabling language: does the advice help the business move forward, not just say "no"?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Advice is deeply business-aware. Multiple options presented with clear
        trade-offs. Cost-benefit analysis included. Recommendations are
        proportionate and enable business objectives. Advice helps the
        business move faster while managing risk.
- **4** — Advice considers business context. Some options presented. Basic
        cost-benefit awareness. Mostly proportionate. Generally enabling.
- **3** — Advice is technically correct but not business-aware. Single option
        presented. No cost-benefit analysis. May be disproportionate.
        Tends toward "no" without alternatives.
- **2** — Advice is overly conservative without justification. No alternatives.
        No business context. Blocks business progress without offering paths.
- **1** — Advice is purely risk-averse. Just says "no" or "consult a lawyer"
        without analysis. No business awareness. Not actionable.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Business satisfaction | > 4.0/5.0 | Internal survey |
| Options per recommendation | >= 2 | Output audit |
| Turnaround time | Meeting business timeline | Process tracking |
| "No without alternative" rate | 0% | Output audit |
| Cost-benefit inclusion rate | > 80% | Output audit |
| Risk calibration accuracy | Matches company appetite | Leadership review |

## 7. COMMUNICATION CLARITY

**Question:**
Is legal analysis communicated in plain language with actionable recommendations?

### What to Evaluate
- Plain language: can a non-lawyer understand the core message?
- Structure: is the analysis organized logically (issue, analysis, recommendation)?
- Actionability: does every analysis end with specific next steps?
- Risk quantification: are risks described in terms of probability and impact?
- Executive summary: is there a clear summary for decision-makers?
- Audience calibration: is the communication level appropriate for the reader?
- Visual aids: are complex relationships shown visually when helpful?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Crystal clear plain language. Well-structured IRAC format. Every
        analysis has specific actionable next steps. Risks quantified with
        probability and impact. Executive summary provided. Perfectly
        calibrated to audience. Visual aids where helpful.
- **4** — Mostly plain language with minor legal jargon. Good structure.
        Actionable recommendations. Basic risk quantification. Summary
        provided. Mostly audience-appropriate.
- **3** — Mix of plain language and unexplained legal terms. Adequate structure.
        Some actionable items. Risks described qualitatively only. No
        summary. Generic communication level.
- **2** — Heavy legal jargon. Poor structure. Vague recommendations. Risks
        not quantified. No summary. Communication not calibrated.
- **1** — Impenetrable legal language. No structure. No actionable items.
        Risks not communicated clearly. Would confuse the reader.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Readability score | Grade 10 or below | Flesch-Kincaid |
| Actionable recommendation rate | 100% | Output audit |
| Executive summary inclusion | 100% | Output audit |
| Risk quantification rate | > 80% | Output audit |
| Stakeholder comprehension | > 90% | Follow-up survey |
| Jargon-free rate | > 90% of content | Readability audit |

## 8. PRECEDENT AWARENESS

**Question:**
Is the analysis informed by relevant case law, statutes, and regulatory guidance?

### What to Evaluate
- Relevant case law: are applicable precedents identified and cited?
- Statutory basis: are relevant statutes identified with specific sections?
- Regulatory guidance: are agency interpretations and guidance considered?
- Jurisdiction appropriateness: are precedents from the correct jurisdiction?
- Recency: are the most current precedents and interpretations used?
- Trend awareness: are emerging legal trends and pending legislation noted?
- Distinguishing factors: are material differences from precedent acknowledged?

### Scoring Guide
- **5** — Comprehensive precedent research. All relevant case law cited with
        specific holdings. Statutory sections cited precisely. Regulatory
        guidance from correct agencies. Jurisdiction-specific. Current as
        of analysis date. Emerging trends noted. Distinguishing factors
        acknowledged.
- **4** — Good precedent research. Key cases cited. Relevant statutes
        identified. Most regulatory guidance included. Correct jurisdiction.
        Mostly current. Some trend awareness.
- **3** — Basic precedent research. Some cases cited. Statutes identified at
        the act level (not section). Limited regulatory guidance. Jurisdiction
        mostly correct. May be slightly dated.
- **2** — Minimal precedent research. Few or no cases. Statutes mentioned
        generally. No regulatory guidance. Jurisdiction unclear. Outdated.
- **1** — No precedent research. No cases cited. No statutory basis. No
        regulatory guidance. Analysis based on general knowledge only.

### Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| Case law citation rate | > 80% of analyses | Output audit |
| Statutory specificity | Section-level | Output audit |
| Regulatory guidance inclusion | > 70% | Output audit |
| Jurisdiction accuracy | 100% | Legal review |
| Precedent recency | Within 2 years | Citation audit |
| Trend identification rate | > 60% | Expert comparison |

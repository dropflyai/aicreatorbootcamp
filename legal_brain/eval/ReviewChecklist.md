# Legal Review Checklist — Pre-Ship Verification (Authoritative)

> **DISCLAIMER:** This brain provides educational guidance. It is not a substitute
> for licensed legal counsel.

This checklist must be completed before any Legal Brain output ships.
Every section must pass. Partial completion is not acceptable.

If an item cannot be verified, it has not been done.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS CHECKLIST

1. Complete every section relevant to the matter type
2. Mark each item: PASS / FAIL / N/A
3. All FAIL items must be resolved before shipping
4. Reviewer must sign off with name, date, and matter reference
5. Archive completed checklists for audit trail

## SECTION 1: RISK IDENTIFICATION VERIFICATION

### Issue Spotting
- [ ] All relevant legal domains identified (contract, IP, privacy, employment, regulatory)
- [ ] Each identified issue has a materiality assessment (high/medium/low)
- [ ] Likelihood of each risk is assessed (probable/possible/remote)
- [ ] Risks are categorized by type (legal, regulatory, reputational, financial)
- [ ] Time-sensitive risks flagged with specific deadlines
- [ ] Cascading risks identified (if X happens, then Y follows)
- [ ] Stakeholders affected by each risk are identified

### Jurisdiction Analysis
- [ ] All applicable jurisdictions identified
- [ ] Jurisdiction-specific requirements noted for each issue
- [ ] Choice of law and forum provisions evaluated
- [ ] Cross-border implications assessed
- [ ] Local counsel requirement flagged where applicable
- [ ] Regulatory authority for each jurisdiction identified

### Risk Matrix
- [ ] Risk matrix completed with severity and likelihood
- [ ] Top 5 risks clearly highlighted
- [ ] Mitigation strategy identified for each high-severity risk
- [ ] Residual risk assessed after mitigation
- [ ] Risk owner assigned for each material risk
- [ ] Risk monitoring plan defined

---

## SECTION 2: CONTRACT REVIEW VERIFICATION

### Term Analysis
- [ ] All defined terms are clear and consistently used
- [ ] Obligations of each party are explicitly stated
- [ ] Performance standards and deliverables are measurable
- [ ] Payment terms are clear (amount, timing, conditions, currency)
- [ ] Term and renewal provisions are unambiguous
- [ ] Termination rights are balanced and clearly defined
- [ ] Post-termination obligations are specified

### Risk Allocation
- [ ] Representations and warranties are appropriate for both parties
- [ ] Indemnification obligations are balanced and capped
- [ ] Limitation of liability is commercially reasonable
- [ ] Insurance requirements are appropriate
- [ ] Force majeure provisions are current and fair
- [ ] Consequential damages treatment is explicit
- [ ] Warranty disclaimers are enforceable in the jurisdiction

### IP and Confidentiality
- [ ] IP ownership is clearly assigned
- [ ] License grants are precisely scoped (exclusive/non-exclusive, territory, term)
- [ ] Background IP is carved out
- [ ] Confidentiality obligations are mutual where appropriate
- [ ] Confidentiality exceptions are standard (public knowledge, independent development)
- [ ] Trade secret protection provisions are adequate
- [ ] Open source obligations are addressed

### Dispute Resolution
- [ ] Dispute resolution mechanism is specified (litigation, arbitration, mediation)
- [ ] Governing law is appropriate
- [ ] Forum is reasonable for both parties
- [ ] Escalation process is defined
- [ ] Injunctive relief carve-out is preserved
- [ ] Statute of limitations implications are considered
- [ ] Fee-shifting provisions are explicit

### Compliance Provisions
- [ ] Regulatory compliance obligations are clear
- [ ] Anti-corruption provisions are included (FCPA, UK Bribery Act)
- [ ] Sanctions and export control provisions are adequate
- [ ] Data protection obligations are addressed
- [ ] Audit rights are included where appropriate
- [ ] Compliance certification requirements are specified

---

## SECTION 3: LITIGATION AND DISPUTE VERIFICATION

### Claim Assessment
- [ ] All potential claims identified (for and against)
- [ ] Elements of each claim analyzed
- [ ] Strengths and weaknesses of each claim assessed
- [ ] Potential damages range estimated
- [ ] Statute of limitations verified for each claim
- [ ] Standing and jurisdiction confirmed
- [ ] Counterclaim exposure assessed

### Evidence Preservation
- [ ] Litigation hold issued (if applicable)
- [ ] Key documents and communications identified
- [ ] Preservation obligations communicated to relevant parties
- [ ] Electronic discovery requirements assessed
- [ ] Third-party evidence sources identified
- [ ] Chain of custody requirements documented

### Strategy Assessment
- [ ] Settlement range analyzed
- [ ] Litigation cost estimate prepared
- [ ] Timeline for resolution estimated
- [ ] Alternative dispute resolution options evaluated
- [ ] Reputational considerations assessed
- [ ] Precedent implications considered
- [ ] Insurance coverage reviewed

---

## SECTION 4: COMPLIANCE VERIFICATION

### Regulatory Mapping
- [ ] All applicable regulations identified by jurisdiction
- [ ] Regulatory requirements documented at the provision level
- [ ] Regulatory authority and enforcement approach noted
- [ ] Penalty ranges documented for non-compliance
- [ ] Pending regulatory changes flagged
- [ ] Industry-specific regulations included

### Gap Analysis
- [ ] Current practices documented against each requirement
- [ ] Gaps identified and classified by severity
- [ ] Remediation actions specified for each gap
- [ ] Timeline for remediation is realistic and prioritized
- [ ] Resource requirements estimated
- [ ] Interim risk mitigation measures identified

### Privacy-Specific Compliance
- [ ] Data processing activities mapped
- [ ] Lawful basis identified for each processing activity
- [ ] Data subject rights mechanisms verified (access, deletion, portability)
- [ ] Data Protection Impact Assessments completed for high-risk processing
- [ ] Data Processing Agreements in place with all processors
- [ ] Cross-border transfer mechanisms validated (SCCs, adequacy, BCRs)
- [ ] Cookie consent implementation reviewed
- [ ] Privacy notice accuracy verified
- [ ] Data retention schedule documented and enforced
- [ ] Breach notification procedures tested

### Employment Compliance
- [ ] Employment classifications verified (employee vs. contractor)
- [ ] Wage and hour compliance confirmed
- [ ] Anti-discrimination policies current and communicated
- [ ] Leave policies compliant with local requirements
- [ ] Termination procedures legally defensible
- [ ] Non-compete and non-solicitation provisions enforceable

---

## SECTION 5: PRIVACY-SPECIFIC VERIFICATION

### GDPR Compliance (If Applicable)
- [ ] Lawful basis documented for all processing of EU data
- [ ] Data Protection Officer appointed (if required)
- [ ] Records of Processing Activities maintained
- [ ] DPIAs completed for high-risk processing
- [ ] Data subject rights procedures operational
- [ ] Breach notification procedures compliant (72-hour window)
- [ ] International transfer safeguards in place
- [ ] Processor agreements include Article 28 requirements
- [ ] Consent mechanisms meet GDPR standards (freely given, specific, informed)
- [ ] Children's data processing safeguards (if applicable)

### CCPA/CPRA Compliance (If Applicable)
- [ ] "Do Not Sell or Share" mechanism available
- [ ] Privacy notice includes all required disclosures
- [ ] Consumer rights procedures operational (know, delete, correct, opt-out)
- [ ] Service provider agreements include CCPA requirements
- [ ] Sensitive personal information handling compliant
- [ ] Financial incentive programs (if any) disclosed and non-discriminatory
- [ ] Contractor and service provider distinctions clear

### Data Security
- [ ] Technical safeguards appropriate for data sensitivity
- [ ] Administrative safeguards documented
- [ ] Physical safeguards in place
- [ ] Access controls implemented and audited
- [ ] Encryption at rest and in transit
- [ ] Incident response plan documented and tested
- [ ] Vendor security assessments completed

---

## SECTION 6: IP VERIFICATION

### Asset Inventory
- [ ] All trademarks inventoried with registration status
- [ ] All patents and patent applications tracked
- [ ] Copyright registrations catalogued
- [ ] Trade secrets identified and protection measures documented
- [ ] Domain names inventoried
- [ ] Software licenses tracked (inbound and outbound)

### Protection Status
- [ ] Trademark registrations current in all operating jurisdictions
- [ ] Patent maintenance fees current
- [ ] Copyright notices properly applied
- [ ] Trade secret protection measures adequate (NDAs, access controls)
- [ ] Employee invention assignment agreements current
- [ ] Contractor IP assignment agreements in place
- [ ] Open source license compliance verified

### Monitoring and Enforcement
- [ ] Trademark monitoring service active
- [ ] Patent watch in place for competitor filings
- [ ] Domain name monitoring active
- [ ] Infringement detection process documented
- [ ] Enforcement response playbook ready
- [ ] Cease and desist templates prepared
- [ ] Outside counsel identified for enforcement actions

## SECTION 7: CORPORATE VERIFICATION

### Entity Matters
- [ ] Corporate formation documents current
- [ ] Good standing maintained in all jurisdictions
- [ ] Annual filings current
- [ ] Board and officer records up to date
- [ ] Capitalization table accurate
- [ ] Stock option plan administered correctly

### Governance
- [ ] Board meeting minutes maintained
- [ ] Board resolutions properly documented
- [ ] Conflict of interest policy enforced
- [ ] D&O insurance adequate
- [ ] Corporate bylaws current
- [ ] Stockholder agreements enforced

## SECTION 8: COMMUNICATION QUALITY VERIFICATION

### Plain Language
- [ ] Core message understandable by non-lawyers
- [ ] Legal jargon is explained or replaced with plain language
- [ ] Acronyms are defined on first use
- [ ] Complex concepts are illustrated with examples
- [ ] Reading level is appropriate for the audience (Grade 10 or below)

### Structure and Actionability
- [ ] Analysis follows IRAC format (Issue, Rule, Analysis, Conclusion)
- [ ] Executive summary is provided for analyses longer than 1 page
- [ ] Recommendations are specific and actionable
- [ ] Next steps are clearly listed with owners and timelines
- [ ] Risk is quantified (probability and impact) where possible
- [ ] Alternatives are presented with trade-offs

### Disclaimer
- [ ] Disclaimer is present on every output
- [ ] Disclaimer clearly states this is educational guidance, not legal advice
- [ ] Recommendation to consult licensed counsel is explicit

---

## SECTION 9: PRECEDENT AND AUTHORITY VERIFICATION

### Legal Research
- [ ] Relevant case law cited with specific holdings
- [ ] Statutes cited at the section level
- [ ] Regulatory guidance referenced (agency opinions, letters, FAQs)
- [ ] All cited authorities are from the correct jurisdiction
- [ ] All cited authorities are current (not overruled or superseded)
- [ ] Distinguishing factors from unfavorable precedent acknowledged
- [ ] Emerging trends and pending legislation noted

### Source Quality
- [ ] Primary sources cited (not secondary summaries)
- [ ] Multiple authorities support key conclusions
- [ ] Contrary authority acknowledged and addressed
- [ ] Authorities are accessible and verifiable
- [ ] No reliance on outdated or overruled precedent
